## About This Policy

This privacy policy explains how A Curious Tractor ("ACT", "we", "us") collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information through the Empathy Ledger platform.

The Empathy Ledger is a community storytelling platform that prioritizes **storyteller ownership** and **Indigenous data sovereignty**.

## 2. OCAP Principles (Indigenous Data Sovereignty)

The Empathy Ledger adheres to the **OCAP** principles for all Indigenous data:

### Ownership
- **Storytellers own their stories.** ACT does not claim ownership of any content contributed by storytellers.
- Indigenous communities own data about their communities.

### Control
- Storytellers control how their stories are used, shared, and displayed.
- Stories can be made public, restricted to community, or kept private.
- Consent can be withdrawn at any time.

### Access
- Storytellers have full access to all their data at any time.
- Communities can request access to aggregate data about their community.
- Data export is available on request.

### Possession
- Data is stored in Australian data centers (Supabase Sydney region).
- ACT acts as a custodian, not an owner, of storyteller data.
- Upon request, all data can be transferred to community-controlled storage.

## 3. How We Use Information

We use personal information to:

1. **Operate the platform** — display stories, connect storytellers, measure impact
2. **Measure impact** — ALMA scores (Agency, Legacy, Momentum, Accountability)
3. **Support community programs** — connect storytellers with relevant projects
4. **Improve the platform** — understand usage patterns (anonymized)
5. **Comply with legal obligations** — grant reporting, regulatory requirements

### We Do NOT:

- Sell personal information to third parties
- Use stories for commercial purposes without explicit consent
- Share Indigenous data without community consent
- Use automated decision-making that affects individuals
- Transfer data outside Australia without consent

## 5. Data Storage and Security

### Storage

- **Primary database:** Supabase (Sydney, Australia)
- **File storage:** Supabase Storage (Sydney, Australia)
- **Backups:** Encrypted, Australian data centers only

### Security Measures

- Row-level security (RLS) on all database tables
- Encrypted connections (TLS 1.2+)
- Access logging and audit trails
- Regular security reviews
- Principle of least privilege for staff access

### Data Breach Response

In the event of a data breach:
1. Assess scope and impact within 24 hours
2. Notify affected individuals within 72 hours (if serious harm likely)
3. Notify OAIC (Office of the Australian Information Commissioner) if required
4. Document incident and remediation steps

## 6. Storyteller Rights

As a storyteller on the Empathy Ledger, you have the right to:

| Right | How to Exercise |
|-------|----------------|
| **Access** your data | View in platform or request export |
| **Correct** your data | Edit in platform or contact us |
| **Delete** your data | Request via platform or email |
| **Withdraw consent** | Contact us; story removed within 7 days |
| **Export** your data | Request data export (provided within 30 days) |
| **Restrict** use | Set story visibility (public/community/private) |
| **Object** to processing | Contact us to discuss |

### Story Deletion

When you request story deletion:
- Story content removed from platform within 7 days
- Story removed from search/aggregation immediately
- Backup copies removed within 30 days
- ALMA scores recalculated excluding your data
- We retain only a minimal audit record (consent withdrawal date)
