## How a transfer works (the shape to expect)

1. Between related entities, assets move by **sale at market value**. The new entity owns them; the seller accounts for the difference between sale price and written-down value.
2. **The catch:** gear already written off (instant asset write-off) has a written-down value near zero, so selling it at market value creates assessable income for the seller (recapture). That is why transferring is usually a cost to Nic, not a saving.
3. **The timing lever:** recapture lands in the seller's return for the year of sale. In FY26 Nic's income sits at top rates; from FY27 his sole trader income winds down. Default expectation: **transfer effective 1 July 2026 or later**, so recapture lands in his low year. SL confirms per asset class.
4. **The payment trick:** the company does not need cash. The purchase price is credited to a **director loan account** (Nic for sole trader assets; Ben for Knight Photography assets). Both founders gain a tax-free repayment lane, which also helps square the seed-loan imbalance.
5. **The company side is where the value is:** fresh cost base to depreciate, possible instant write-off on eligible items, and for assets used in R&D activities, the decline in value attracts the 43.5% offset in proportion to R&D use (track a use percentage per asset).
6. **Where assets land depends on Decision 1.** Classic protection pattern: the holding company owns the assets and leases them to the operating subsidiary, so trading liabilities cannot reach them. If the holdco structure is adopted, ask SL whether plant sits in holdco or opco.

3. Best **effective date** per asset class (default 1 July) and the FY27 instant write-off position for the buyer.
4. **Vehicles:** transfer vs keep personal and charge the company per logbook. Company cars with private use trigger FBT; sometimes keeping them out is cheaper.
5. **Goods production plant** (containers, shredder, press): R&D use percentage, feedstock implications if experimental output is sold, and whether intended future community or Butterfly ownership means it should not enter the trading company at all.
6. Consideration via **director loan credit**: confirm documentation (asset sale agreement + loan account entries).

## What does NOT transfer

- **Subscriptions** are not assets: rebill them to the company from 1 July (checklist §5 already tracks this).
- **Rented or hired things**: nothing to sell; the rental contracts novate to the new entity like any other contract (checklist §1 pattern).
- **IP, brands, codebases**: already covered by the IP assignment deed work-stream (checklist §6); CGT mechanics differ from plant, SL + lawyer handle.
