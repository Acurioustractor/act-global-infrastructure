# Insurance review (2026-06-17)

> Not insurance advice; Austcover is the licensed broker. This maps the quote against ACT's documented needs (migration checklist §8 + EOFY tracker insurance rows) and lists the checks to make before binding.

## Bottom line

The quote is one policy: a Public Liability / Business Package. It is one of ~10 covers the ACT business needs. The market review is sound and Blue Zebra is competitively priced, but at less than half of AIG / Allianz / Zurich, with CGU, Chubb and Vero declining, the price is the thing to interrogate. Confirm the limit, the named entity, and the covered activities before binding. Do not treat this single quote as "ACT is insured."

## Four checks before binding

1. **Named insured.** Mid-cutover: sole trader (ABN 21 591 780 066) to 30 June, A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676) from 1 July, Harvest a separate entity not yet incorporated. The Harvest $20M PL must sit in the lease-holding entity. Confirm who is named and how Harvest is covered (interim under ACT vs its own/endorsement once incorporated).
2. **Limit.** Confirm Blue Zebra is $20M, not a lower limit. The quote as supplied does not state it. The Harvest lease likely requires $20M; the broker flags it as the floor for child-injury exposure.
3. **Activities.** PL only responds to the declared business. Confirm cafe/food, events/venue hire, workshops, work with children/youth, remote on-country work, and product sales are all declared and not excluded or sub-limited.
4. **Why so cheap, why the declines.** Ask what Blue Zebra covers differently (sub-limits, exclusions, activity list). Three declines signal a real risk read (food + events + children + remote + products).

## UPDATE 2026-06-17: read the actual quote documents. DO NOT BIND AS-IS.

Read the insurer schedule (QF112606330BI), the quotation, and the "What You Told Us" form. The quote is mis-described for the wrong business and the wrong entity name. Instructing the broker to place it would leave ACT effectively uninsured for its real activities and would lock in inaccurate disclosure. The cheap premium is the symptom: it is rating a performing artist, not a community enterprise.

### Showstoppers

1. **Rated as the wrong business: "Performing Artist (incl. Plays/Musicals/Busking) (excl. Child Entertainment) (excl. Event Management) (excl. Stage/Lighting Installation-Erection)".** ACT and Harvest are a cafe, workshops, events, venue hire, community and youth work, remote on-country projects, and product manufacture and sale (Goods). The policy only responds to performing-artist liability, and it expressly excludes Event Management, which is core to Harvest. A cafe, event, workshop, product, or community claim would very likely be denied. Root cause of both the low price and the gaps.
2. **Molestation Exclusion (Endorsement 1).** No cover for any liability connected to molestation, interference, or mental/physical/sexual abuse of any person. ACT works with children, young people (JusticeHub), and vulnerable community members. Abuse liability is the catastrophic exposure for any organisation working with kids, and this removes exactly the protection the broker's own email flagged. ACT needs an insurer that offers abuse / sexual-misconduct liability, typically a not-for-profit or care-sector policy, not a performing-artist package.
3. **Performers Exclusion (Endorsement 2).** Excludes fire/flame, knives/animals, acrobatics/stunts, operating without council permits, and injury to performers/crew. Flame (cafe), animals (farm), and permit-required events could all bite.
4. **Wrong insured name: "A Curious Trader Pty Ltd".** Appears on the quote, the schedule, and the disclosure form. That entity does not exist. The real entity is A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676). No ACN recorded. A policy in a non-existent name fails at claim time.

### Material issues

5. **Liability only. Everything else Not Taken** (business property, contents, BI, theft, money, glass, machinery, electronic, transit). The Harvest fit-out, cafe equipment, gear and stock are uninsured. If intentional for now, make it a conscious choice.
6. **Disclosure answers look wrong, which is a duty-of-disclosure exposure.** Contractors/subcontractors answered NO (ACT engages Goods suppliers, build crews, Indigenous businesses). Hazardous substances and hot-work answered NO (Goods plastic shredding/melting/pressing and steel work may qualify). Turnover declared $1,000,000 (ACT is closer to $1.5M). Staff 2 full-time, 0 casual (Harvest plans gardener/caretaker/chef). Instructing to place the policy confirms these answers; if inaccurate, the insurer can reduce or refuse claims or void the policy.
7. **Interested parties: None.** The Harvest landlord is not noted. If the lease requires the lessor noted as an interested party or additional insured, it is missing, and the broker's own notice says liability policies do not cover other parties automatically.
8. **Single situation at the Witta farm (601 Maleny-Kenilworth Road).** Confirm activities at other locations (Mparntwe, Palm Island, Mount Isa, office) are covered; PL usually covers anywhere in Australia for the described business, but the description is the problem.

### What is actually fine

- Limit is $20M (general + products liability). Correct.
- Property in care/custody/control $250K sub-limit. Standard.
- No claims in 3 years. Fine.

### What this means for the 20 June Harvest event

This policy would not properly cover a Harvest event (Event Management excluded, performers and flame exclusions, cafe). Do not run the 20 June event assuming this is fit-for-purpose cover. Sort interim cover that actually matches the activity, or hold the event's risky elements.
