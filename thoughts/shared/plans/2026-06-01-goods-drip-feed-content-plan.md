## A) The two-track split

**Track A — Community / Storytellers (co-ownership, not a funnel).** Oonchiumpa, the Utopia and Ampilatwatja communities, Tennant Creek, Palm Island, and the Centrecorp-funded communities are not leads to warm. They are co-owners of the story. The only "sequence" here is a consent-first conversation: we show them what we hold (the photo sets, the videos, the quotes that came from them), and we ask **how they want to share it** — a gallery they can text around, a one-pager, a short video, a slide deck, or an audio cut. Nothing about them is published outward until they have seen it and said yes. The medium is the one they already use (WhatsApp / SMS), and the destination of any "yes" is the public-facing surface *they* nominate, not a Goods marketing asset. Track A's success metric is **shared authority and a clean consent trail**, never opens or conversions.

**Track B — Funders / Buyers / Supporters / Partners (the warm-up funnels).** These four audiences run as genuine drip sequences: ~5–9 touches over weeks, each touch = **one story beat + one usable thing** the recipient can lift for their own goal (a board paper, a procurement case, a repost, a co-design conversation). They ride the channel each audience already lives in — email for funders/partners, email + light social for supporters, a buyer-nurture email track for buyers. Every touch maps to a Goods pipeline stage and a GHL trigger tag, and any touch built on a consent-pending asset is held until the gate clears (we lead Track B with the **not-applicable** consent assets — product specs, aggregate data, founder-voice, governance — so the funnels can start *now* while community consent is still being sought).

## D) Track-A — Community co-ownership flow (consent-first, NOT a funnel)

This is a conversation, not a sequence. No `comms:` drip tag, no pipeline stage, no "conversion." The GHL role here is only to **log consent state and the chosen sharing format**, not to nurture.

**Step 1 — Show, don't send.** Take to each community (via Oonchiumpa as cultural authority, and directly to the people in frame) the things that came *from* them: the Mykel build recording, the Ampilatwatja OAM Elders' video, the door-to-bed before/after, the Day-1 photo set, their own quotes. Show on the channel they use (WhatsApp / SMS / in person on Country). Nothing is published before this.

**Step 2 — Ask how *they* want to share.** Offer the menu, theirs to pick or decline:
- a **gallery link** they can text around to family,
- a **short video** cut for their own socials,
- a **one-pager** with their words and photos,
- a **slide deck** for their own meetings/funders,
- an **audio cut**,
- or **nothing public** — internal record only.

**Step 3 — Consent-gate every element separately.** Run `consent-check` per name, per face, per quote, per place name. Verify spellings (Oonchiumpa not "Ntumba"; confirm Mykel, the Elders' names, Frank & Casey Holmes, the community name — never "the bush"). Youth (Mykel) needs family + Oonchiumpa under youth protocol. Verbal consent IS a valid audit trail — **record it, never fabricate a name or date to fill a gap.**

**Step 4 — Hand the asset to *them*, in their format, on their surface.** A "yes" produces the artefact *they* chose, delivered to *them* first. If they want it on a public surface, it goes where they nominate. ACT's role is turn-up-and-build, then step back — beautiful obsolescence applied to the storytelling itself.

**Step 5 — Close the loop into Track B (only with explicit permission).** Once a community has approved a specific element for external use, *that cleared element* (and only that one) unlocks the matching `BLOCKED` touch in the funnels above. The consent record is the bridge between the tracks. No approval, no unlock.

**Track-A success = a clean, contemporaneous consent trail and a community that controls its own story.** Opens, clicks, and conversions are not measured here and must not be.
