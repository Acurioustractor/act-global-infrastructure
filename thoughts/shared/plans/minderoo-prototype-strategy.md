# Minderoo — prototype strategy

## Part 1 — What the corpus is telling us

Six things the 25 stories, four decision records, and the governance pattern make visible that the original pitch cannot:

### 1. The method works in three cultural contexts already — that's a principle, not a model

Oonchiumpa (Mparntwe) leads with Country and Traditional-Owner authority. BG Fit (Mount Isa) leads with sport and Brodie's lived-experience relational work. Mounty Yarns (Mount Druitt) leads with youth running it themselves. Three Countries, three entry points, one underlying method. That's not a model to be franchised — it's a principle to be supported everywhere it's already emerging.

For Minderoo, this dissolves the biggest funding-risk question. "Will it travel?" is no longer a proposal. It's a fact with 16 named voices attached.

### 2. The young people have already named the system, in their own language

Laquisha, 16: *"Oppression."* Taleigha (Biripi young woman): *"Why are the older fellas making choices for the younger fellas when they're the ones going through it?"* Jackquann, 14: *"Looking after my family."* Polly, 13: *"I don't have to hide my personality."*

Minderoo is not being asked to listen to ACT analyse the problem. They are being asked to listen to the young people who already did. ACT's role in the pitch shifts from advocate to conduit. Their role shifts from funder of an idea to believer-in-evidence-that-is-already-there.

### 3. The trust infrastructure already exists — it's communities, not systems

Every approval flowed through a verbal conversation. Kristy said yes for Oonchiumpa. Brodie said yes for BG Fit, then separately for Jay, then Doomadgee, then Mount Isa Bush Camp. Daniel Daylight said yes for Mounty Yarns. No paperwork. No contracts. Relational trust, recorded as written precedent.

This is the OCAP framework operating *as communities do it*. ACT didn't invent it; ACT recorded it. Minderoo is being invited into a trust structure that already works.

### 4. The pitch material isn't documentation of a plan. It's artefacts of a working system.

Photos from Atnarpa trips. Xavier's Stretch Bed, physically built and used in ceremonies. Isabella and Polly in the Mounty Yarns documentary. The first BG Fit on-Country camp. Audio transcripts. The CONTAINED mobile shipping container already on tour. Artefact 001 (Murri Watch) printed. These exist. They are not promises.

The envelope is not a proposal document. It is a **sample of what is already happening** with a three-year frame for what more becomes possible.

### 5. The central platform is already live — JusticeHub + wiki + Empathy Ledger + CivicGraph

ALMA (1,155 interventions). CivicGraph (100K entities, 199K relationships). GrantScope (71K justice funding rows). AusTender (770K contracts). Foundations layer (10,779 foundations). The wiki with 25 consented stories. The Empathy Ledger with 13 Mounty storytellers + 13 BG Fit + 6 Oonchiumpa + hundreds more.

Minderoo isn't being asked to fund platform-building. They're being asked to fund the *relational and editorial work* that turns an already-running platform into a national advocacy artefact.

### 6. The artefact is the pitch — shipping the thing IS the ask

The Minderoo envelope contains Artefact 001 (printed), three Brave Ones portraits (printed), the JoC one-pager, the insert. That's not documentation of what ACT will do. That's a **physical example of the national collection** the three-year program produces, at Year 0 scale.

The ask translates as: *"Here is a one-envelope version of the thing. We want to make 3 × 10 × 365 of these — one per young person, one per community, a national volume — across three years. $2.9M is what that costs."*

## Part 2 — Reframe the pitch

From: *"Please fund a program called The Three Circles"*

To: *"Hold the circle with us for three years while the method that is already working becomes visible to the country at a scale that makes it impossible to ignore"*

This reframing comes from what the corpus shows. The method is already running. The young people are already leading. The platform is already live. The artefact already exists in miniature. What doesn't yet exist is the sustained, patient, three-year container that lets the method become national evidence.

Minderoo's role is not to fund a new thing. It is to hold the container of the existing thing at the scale needed for it to change sentencing, change policy, change the national story about Indigenous young people in the justice system.

## Part 3 — The Minderoo Prototype Package (what's in the envelope on 1 May)

Not a PDF stack. A physical, tactile package designed to communicate *this is alive, not proposed*. Every item serves a specific "you can see this, you can touch this, you can verify this" function.

### Core physical artefacts

1. **Canonical pitch document** — Three Circles proposal, $2.9M / 3 years, hand-bound or high-quality print. Cover photo from Judges on Country 21 April.
2. **Artefact 001 — Murri Watch** — A2 poster + 16-page saddle-stitched book. *This is what an issue of the national collection looks like.*
3. **Brave Ones insert v3** — three-site distributed cohort (now updated). Shows the method travels.
4. **Judges on Country one-pager** — real content from the 21 April trip, Kristy + Tanya sign-off before print.
5. **Three Brave Ones portraits** — physical A5 prints from JoC shoot. Not stock; real people.

### Sensory / memorable additions

6. **A physical object from Xavier's Stretch Bed work** — a small wooden offcut or sticker with the method's logo hand-stamped onto it. Or a recycled-plastic sample of the bed material. Something tactile that communicates "real young person built a real thing we already use." The most under-used move in philanthropy pitches is the physical object that can't be scrolled past.

7. **A QR-coded card** that opens on the Lucy-Stronach private dashboard URL (see Part 4). One card, printed on thick stock, matte, no logo clutter. *"Scan this to watch the program run."*

8. **90-second introduction video** — on the USB or via the QR code. Real footage from the 21 April trip + Mount Isa Tuesday gym + Polly or Isabella speaking + Brodie + Tanya. No voiceover. Just the voices already in the corpus, intercut with the sites. Ends on Tanya's *"Our young people are just collateral in a bigger issue."*

### Data-spine access

9. **Platform credentials** for Lucy and Minderoo's internal review team — specific accounts on JusticeHub + the Minderoo dashboard (see Part 4). *"The evidence this pitch cites is not in an appendix. It is a login."*

10. **Anchor community contact sheet** — the ten anchor communities, each with: named contact, relationship status (active / in-conversation / selection-pending), one line on the cultural authority leading the work, a photo if consented. This is the single item most funders never receive and most pitches gloss over.

### Optional / if capacity allows

11. **A short letter from one young person** — Polly, Jackquann, Isabella, or whoever gives consent — *"this is what the program means to me, please fund it"* in their own words. Handwritten scan preferred. Consent-gated via the Mounty Yarns / Oonchiumpa pattern that's already governed.

## Part 4 — The Loop-Back Dashboard (how Lucy stays in the loop)

Build a private dashboard specifically for Minderoo. URL: `minderoo.justicehub.com.au` or `act.place/minderoo-live`. Password-protected. Refreshed weekly or on significant events.

### Dashboard contents

| Section | What it shows | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Live story count** | Stories consented + flowing this week, per community | `wiki/output/story-sync/` manifests |
| **Youth voices** | Named young-people pool with direct quotes + consent status | `wiki/stories/` |
| **Anchor community tracker** | 10 communities, status per relationship | `wiki/campaigns/minderoo-pitch/brief.md` ten-anchors table |
| **Financial dashboard** | Spend to date on Brave Ones pilot, projected next quarter | JusticeHub finance layer |
| **Pitch artefact log** | Every iteration of pitch docs with dates | `wiki/output/narrative-drafts/` snapshots from JH reverse-sync |
| **Judges on Country tracker** | 55 judges + magistrates on the 21 April trip → what they did next | Post-trip capture, updated as judges engage publicly |
| **Live media** | Photos, audio, video as they're captured and consent-cleared | Empathy Ledger media library |
| **"What's missing"** | Explicit list of gaps the program is trying to close each quarter, with the named person responsible | Direct editorial |

### Why this is the undeniable move

Most philanthropic funders get quarterly reports that are post-hoc marketing documents. The Loop-Back Dashboard inverts this. Lucy sees what happened this week, not what was curated for her to see three months ago. The program is visible *as it runs*, not *as it was sold*.

That is a relational commitment that $2.9M cannot buy from most grantees. ACT is distinctive because the infrastructure for it already exists — we just point it at Minderoo and give them a login.

## Part 5 — How it all links up (the ecosystem diagram in words)

### Year 1 — the prototype scales

```
Community storytellers (Mparntwe, Mt Isa, Mt Druitt + 7 more)
        │
        │ captured with consent via Empathy Ledger v2
        ▼
Empathy Ledger storyteller records (with OCAP-governed consent flags)
        │
        │ sync-el-stories-to-wiki (per community approvals)
        ▼
Wiki stories (living library, hand-searchable + agent-readable)
        │
        ├─► autoreason → Brave Ones campaign material → Minderoo envelope
        ├─► JusticeHub platform → magistrate-facing service directory
        │                       → evidence for sentencing decisions
        ├─► Year 2 book chapters → national volume Year 3
        └─► Live dashboard → Minderoo weekly view into the program
```

### Tours — how stories earn movement

The Brave Ones exchange program turns journal practice into travel. *Journal kept = eligible for exchange.* Year 1: Brisbane domestic (Alice → Mt Isa → Brisbane, 8–13 June). Year 1: Mt Isa → Palm Island → Mt Druitt exchange. Year 2: international — Diagrama (Spain), YOPE (Amsterdam). Each trip is both a reward and a capture opportunity — young people travel, keep journaling, come back with footage and writing that becomes next year's book material. The tours feed the books. The books feed the platform. The platform feeds the pitches. The pitches feed the next community's relationship.

### Books — how the corpus becomes national evidence

Year 1: per-community zines. Each anchor community gets a printed book of its own young people's voices, drawings, photos. Distributed to the community, to local magistrates, to state ministers.

Year 2: cross-community chapters. Documentary-quality compilations that pair Mparntwe with Mt Isa, Mt Druitt with PICC.

Year 3: **The Country We're Building** — the national collection. One 400-page bound volume. Launches at a national event. Every magistrate, every state attorney-general, every First Nations MP gets a copy. The single most under-used funder asset is *a physical object that arrives in the post and can't be ignored*.

### JusticeHub — the central platform

JusticeHub (`justicehub.com.au`) is the public-facing surface — data, service directory, magistrate tool, story library, advocacy campaigns. CivicGraph (`civicgraph.app`) is the research surface — entity resolution, funding deserts analysis, relationship mapping. Together: the data spine that already exists, now with voice layer (25 stories flowing, 440+ more staged).

Minderoo's $2.9M *integrates the story layer into the data layer*. Right now they're parallel. By Year 2 they're linked — every ALMA intervention record has the community-voice evidence attached; every justice-funding analysis includes the counter-narrative from the storytellers; every magistrate query surfaces both the evidence *and* the voices.

### How the ACT ecosystem connects (what Minderoo is really investing in)

JusticeHub is not alone. It sits in ACT's regenerative ecosystem alongside:

- **Empathy Ledger** — story sovereignty (consent infrastructure)
- **Goods on Country** — economic sovereignty (Indigenous enterprise)
- **Black Cockatoo Valley** — ecological sovereignty (land + place)
- **The Harvest** — creative industry residency (cultural expression + practical skills)
- **ACT Farm** — food sovereignty
- **ACT Regenerative Studio** — design + planning

Minderoo investing $2.9M in The Three Circles is investing in the JusticeHub surface of a connected regenerative ecosystem. The three-year container funds the story+data+advocacy work specifically. But the ecosystem's other surfaces (Goods on Country's economic stories, The Harvest's creative stories, Black Cockatoo Valley's ecological stories) carry the same method and will compound the signal over the same three years — for free, from Minderoo's perspective.

That compounding effect is the reason the pitch is not just *"we need $2.9M for this program"*. It is *"we need $2.9M to hold the circle that turns an already-running ecosystem into national evidence."*

## Part 6 — Undeniable moves (the seven adds that make saying no hard)

1. **The physical object in the envelope** — Xavier's Stretch Bed offcut, or a polaroid from the Mt Isa Bush Camp with a young person's handwritten caption on the back. Something that isn't a document.

2. **The 90-second film** — real footage, real voices, cut to Tanya's *"collateral in a bigger issue"* line. Shareable URL. Delivers the pitch in under 2 minutes if Lucy only watches it.

3. **The live dashboard** — Lucy has credentials. Lucy watches the program run. No other funder has this from anyone.

4. **The young person's letter** — if consent-gated, a single handwritten note from Polly or Jackquann or Isabella to Lucy. "Please fund it. This is what it does for me."

5. **The 10-anchor contact sheet** — named, photographed, relationship-status-tagged. Every line a person a Minderoo researcher can call. The "ten anchors" claim becomes ten actual relationships, not ten concept slots.

6. **The "what's missing" section** — name what the program can't do without the $2.9M. Name which young people's stories don't flow because the editorial work isn't funded. Name which magistrates can't be reached because the tour doesn't scale. Specific, not guilting.

7. **The existing artefact pattern** — Artefact 001 (Murri Watch) ships whether or not Minderoo funds. Artefact 002 is planned. Lucy sees the pattern already running on its own momentum and decides whether to join at Year 1 (cheapest entry point) or try to catch up at Year 2.

## Part 8 — The question Lucy asks herself after the envelope

If the envelope lands as designed, Lucy is not asking *"should we fund this?"*

She is asking *"can we afford NOT to be the foundation that held the circle?"*

That shift — from "fund or don't fund" to "join or miss" — is the job of this strategy. The corpus shows it's already running. The artefacts show it already works. The platform shows the evidence is already visible. The young people's voices show the analysis is already correct. The only variable left is whether Minderoo is part of the story when it becomes national.

Everything else is documentation.
