### Two Core Problems

**Problem 1: The pipeline doesn't reflect reality.** $822K income came through Xero this FY, but only $112K is linked to "won" opportunities. $710K of income has no pipeline trail — it arrived as cash but nobody updated the opportunity. The pipeline shows $35.5M of hope but can't tell you what actually converted.

**Problem 2: Income isn't attributed to projects.** 98% of income ($806K of $822K) is tagged to ACT-HQ in Xero, not to the projects that earned it. Goods, Harvest, PICC, JusticeHub all show near-zero income in the books — but that can't be right. The money flows into the central entity and disappears into a single bucket. This makes it impossible to know which projects are revenue engines and which are cost centres.

**Problem 3: Feast-or-famine cash flow.** Nov: $245K. Dec: $0. Jan: $0. Income is lumpy and grant-dependent. One big grant hits, then nothing for months. Commercial/passive revenue smooths this out.

## The Compounding Effect

This is where it gets exciting. Each revenue stream reinforces the others:

```
GRANTS fund R&D → 43.5% back from ATO
     ↓
R&D builds TECHNOLOGY (GrantScope, Empathy Ledger, Fleet IoT)
     ↓
TECHNOLOGY generates COMMERCIAL revenue (SaaS, data products)
     ↓
COMMERCIAL revenue makes INVESTORS confident
     ↓
INVESTMENT funds INFRASTRUCTURE (farm stays, production facility)
     ↓
INFRASTRUCTURE generates PASSIVE income (accommodation, events)
     ↓
PASSIVE income funds OPERATIONS without grants
     ↓
INDEPENDENCE makes better GRANT applications (we don't need you, we want to partner)
     ↓
BETTER partnerships → BIGGER grants → MORE R&D → cycle compounds
```

**The flywheel:** Grant → R&D refund → Tech → Revenue → Investment → Infrastructure → Passive income → Independence → Better grants → repeat.

Every cycle, the grant dependency decreases and the compounding assets increase.
