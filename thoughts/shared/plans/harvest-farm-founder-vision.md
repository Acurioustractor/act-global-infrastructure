# The Harvest + ACT Farm — Founder Vision

*A place where people come to be amazed, to reconnect, and to remember what matters.*

---

## The Dream

Two properties. One hinterland. One mission.

**The Harvest Witta** sits at the heart of the village — the only gathering place in a community that's been waiting for one. A building that could be a cafe, or a gallery, or a workshop, or all three at once. A garden that feeds people and teaches them. A place where a kid from Palm Island can exhibit their art next to a programmer from Berlin, where an Elder's story fills the same room as a startup pitch, where the food comes from 200 metres away and the music comes from the mountains.

**ACT Farm** is the engine behind the dream. 40 acres of Jinibara Country that we're not building on — we're building *with*. Every sensor in the soil, every bird call recorded by a microphone in the trees, every data point from the weather station is part of a living artwork. The land is healing itself and we're making that healing visible, touchable, shareable. This is where the methodology becomes action — where Listen turns into Curiosity, where Curiosity becomes something you can walk through.

Together, they're one thing: **a destination that doesn't exist anywhere else in Australia.**

## What It Feels Like

You drive up the mountain from the coast. The air changes. The light changes. You turn off the highway into Witta and the first thing you see is The Harvest — warm timber, corrugated iron, native gardens spilling onto the footpath. You smell coffee and woodfire.

Inside, there's art on every wall — but it changes every month. Today it's photography from Palm Island. Last month it was generative AI landscapes of this exact valley. Next month it's a Contained exhibition that makes you cry and then makes you act.

You eat food that was growing 200 metres away this morning. The menu is small and perfect. The coffee is excellent. There are kids running through the garden centre. Someone's leading a yarn circle in the back room.

Then someone says: *"Have you been to The Farm?"*

You drive 5 minutes. A dirt road. You park. And then...

The landscape opens up and you realise you're standing in something that's part rewilded bush, part food forest, part living laboratory. Sensors in the soil are feeding data to a screen on the timber pavilion that shows you — in real time — what's happening beneath your feet. The soil is alive. The biodiversity count is ticking upward. There's a bird species counter. There are sounds you haven't heard since you were a child.

You walk the art trail. A light installation by a Jinibara artist pulses in a hollow. A sound sculpture catches the wind and turns it into music. A mirrored sphere reflects the whole valley back at you and for a second you feel like you're floating.

At night, the trees come alive with projected stories. Light paths guide you through the bush. A firepit crackles. Someone is playing guitar. The stars are insane.

You leave thinking: *I have to bring people here. I have to come back.*

## The Signature Moments

Every great destination has moments people travel specifically for. Ours:

### 1. The Living Dashboard
A beautiful timber-framed screen at the Farm entrance showing real-time data from the land. Soil moisture. Bird species detected today. Air quality. Carbon sequestered this month vs last. Temperature difference between the regenerated zone and the cleared paddock next door. It's science made visible. It's proof that healing works.

### 2. The Night Walk
Once a month, after dark. Projection mapping on the trees. Sound installations in the bush. Light paths that respond to footsteps. A firepit storytelling session with an Elder or artist. Limited to 50 people. $75/head. Sells out every time.

### 3. The Contained Wall
A permanent installation at The Harvest — the chalk question: *"What if we spent $1.55M on keeping a kid connected to family, culture, and community instead of locking them away?"* Visitors write their own questions on a communal chalk wall. It gets photographed more than anything else.

### 4. The Harvest Table
A long communal table under the trees at the Farm. Monthly dinners for 30 people. Farm-to-table. An artist or Elder speaks. No phones. Two hours. $120/head. People describe it as the best night they've had in years.

### 5. The Sunrise Spot
A simple timber platform at the highest point of the Farm, facing east over the hinterland. Two chairs. Nothing else. The most beautiful view you've ever seen. People come for the sunrise and stay for everything else.

## The Technology Layer

Not technology for technology's sake. Technology that makes the invisible visible.

- **IoT sensor mesh** across the Farm — soil, water, weather, acoustic, camera
- **Living dashboard** — real-time data visualisation for visitors and researchers
- **Empathy Ledger integration** — stories captured at The Harvest feed into the EL platform
- **Spatial computing trails** — AR overlays showing historical land use, Jinibara cultural sites (with permission), ecological data
- **Generative AI art engine** — visitors can create their own vision of the landscape, printed and taken home
- **Carbon tracking** — verifiable regeneration data for potential carbon credit revenue
- **Smart venue management** — automated lighting, sound, crowd flow for events

## The 5-Year Arc

**Year 1 (2026):** The Harvest opens as cafe + gallery + garden centre. First monthly events. Farm sensor network installed. First artist residency. First Night Walk.

**Year 2 (2027):** Glamping opens at the Farm (6 tents). Art trail commissioned. Monthly Harvest Tables. First corporate innovation retreat. Workshop program launches. Night Walk becomes signature event.

**Year 3 (2028):** $1.5M revenue target. First major festival (1,000+ people). Education partnerships with universities. International artist residency program. Empathy Ledger publicly launched from the Farm.

**Year 4 (2029):** The Farm becomes a registered research site. Carbon credit revenue begins. Accommodation expands. Second venue for events built. Begin planning for community ownership transition.

**Year 5 (2030):** Community trust established. The Harvest and Farm become community-owned assets. ACT designed itself out of operational control. The land belongs to the people who love it. We did what we came to do.
