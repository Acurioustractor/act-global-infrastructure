### Please tell us about your organisation (1,000 chars)

ACT (A Curious Tractor) is a regenerative innovation ecosystem founded by Benjamin Knight (systems designer) and Nicholas Marchesi OAM (2016 Young Australian of the Year). We build community-controlled technology that transfers institutional power to communities most affected by policy decisions.

We work across four live platforms: Empathy Ledger (consent-first storytelling with sovereign data), JusticeHub (youth justice transparency), GrantScope (open funding intelligence), and community data systems deployed with Palm Island Community Company and Oonchiumpa (Central Australia). We partner with First Nations communities to build tools that turn lived experience into evidence and make generational wealth — cultural knowledge, language, Country connection — visible and protected.

ACT is based on Jinibara Country, QLD. We design for our own obsolescence: communities own and operate the infrastructure independently.

### Project/initiative summary (3,000 chars)

Australia's most excluded communities — remote First Nations communities, youth in the justice system, communities disconnected from their homelands — are locked out of democratic participation. Not because they lack knowledge, but because they lack the infrastructure to be heard, to hold institutions accountable, and to access the resources that should serve them.

ACT has built and deployed four interconnected platforms that create sovereign civic infrastructure:

Empathy Ledger is a consent-first storytelling platform with data sovereignty enforced in code. Deployed with Palm Island Community Company (Bwgcolman, QLD) and Oonchiumpa (Mparntwe/Alice Springs, NT), it connects communities across 3,000km through shared cultural infrastructure while keeping each community's data sovereign. Palm Island Elders — descendants of 52+ nations forcibly removed — preserve and control cultural knowledge. Oonchiumpa's Traditional Owner leaders document youth mentorship and on-Country healing across seven language groups around Alice Springs.

JusticeHub is Australia's youth justice intelligence platform, built with the National Justice Project (justice.org.au). It catalogues 1,000+ interventions, making $1.5B in annual government spending transparent. It integrates NJP's community tools — Call It Out (racism reporting), Hear Me Out (complaint pathways), CopWatch (police encounters) — so communities report, track, and advocate with sovereign data. Community programs achieve 78% success at $150/day vs detention at 15.5% success, $3,320/day.

GrantScope is Australia's first open funding transparency platform — 14,000+ grants, 9,800+ foundations searchable. It reveals 94% of philanthropic funding goes to 10% of organisations; First Nations receive 0.5%. This is democratic infrastructure that levels access to generational wealth.

These platforms connect communities across geography through shared sovereign infrastructure. The cross-cultural connection is the proof: when communities control their own evidence, they build collective democratic power.

This project funds model documentation, platform interoperability, onboarding 2-3 new communities, and open-source publication so any excluded community can build sovereign civic infrastructure independently.

### What are your key goals? (1,000 chars)

1. Prove that sovereign data infrastructure is civic infrastructure — when excluded communities own their data, stories, and cultural knowledge, they gain capacity to participate in democratic processes that shape their lives and protect generational wealth.

2. Strengthen the cross-cultural civic connection between Palm Island (QLD) and Central Australian homeland communities through shared but sovereign storytelling infrastructure — communities connected across 3,000km through cultural exchange and collective advocacy.

3. Open access to funding intelligence through GrantScope — making visible who gets funded, who doesn't, and why. When First Nations communities receive 0.5% of philanthropic funding, transparency is a democratic act.

4. Document and publish the model as an open-source playbook so any community can adopt it without ongoing dependency on ACT.

5. Deploy with 2-3 additional communities, testing replication across remote, regional, and urban contexts.

### What other organisations are you collaborating with? (1,000 chars)

Palm Island Community Company (PICC) — Tier 1 Indigenous community-controlled org, 18+ years, 197 staff (95% local Indigenous). Led by Rachel Atkinson (Yorta Yorta, SNAICC Board Director) and Allan Palm Island (Manbarra Traditional Owner).

### Geographic scope (1,000 chars)

This work spans national scope built on deep local proof across two critical regions:

Palm Island (QLD) — remote island community, proof-of-concept for sovereign community intelligence. 18+ years of community-controlled operation.

Central Australia (NT) — Oonchiumpa works with homeland communities across a 150km radius of Alice Springs, seven language groups (Arrernte, Alyawarra, Luritja, Warlpiri and others), connecting youth to Country, culture, and civic agency.

JusticeHub and GrantScope operate nationally — youth justice data covers all jurisdictions; GrantScope indexes 14,000+ grants and 9,800+ foundations nationally.

The cross-cultural connection between Palm Island and Central Australia — communities 3,000km apart sharing sovereign civic infrastructure — demonstrates national scalability. Phase 3 replication targets 2-3 additional communities across different contexts. The open-source playbook is designed for adoption by any community organisation nationally.

### How is this work engaging with the broader community? (1,000 chars)

Our platforms serve communities most excluded from democratic institutions:

Remote First Nations communities: Palm Island and Central Australian homelands have deep reasons for institutional distrust. Our tools give communities sovereign infrastructure to hold institutions accountable with their own evidence and protect generational wealth — cultural knowledge, language, Country connection.

Youth in justice: JusticeHub + NJP make visible the 24x Indigenous overrepresentation and $1.5B spent with poor outcomes. Oonchiumpa's programs — mentorship, on-Country healing, True Justice with ANU — prove community solutions work.

Funding-excluded communities: GrantScope reveals 94% of funding goes to 10% of organisations. Searchable access levels the playing field.

We engage through everyday channels — SMS, WhatsApp, on Country — not institutional processes.

### How is this work led or informed by people it is seeking to engage? (1,000 chars)

Every platform was designed and is governed by the communities it serves:

Palm Island: 100% community control since 2021. Elders group meeting since 2016, designed the sovereign digital archive including four-tier cultural access (public/community/family/sacred). Community fundraised independently before first heritage journey. Presented at SNAICC national conference.

Oonchiumpa: 100% Aboriginal-led by Kristy Bloomfield (Traditional Owner) and Tanya Turner. 21 active young people in programs designed by community. Cultural camps at Atnarpa homestead on Bloomfield family's reclaimed ancestral Country. Aunty Bev and Uncle Terry provide Elder authority.

Empathy Ledger: Elder Review Workflow means cultural authorities approve content before publication. LCAA methodology (Listen, Curiosity, Action, Art) centres community voice. We design for obsolescence — communities own and operate independently.
