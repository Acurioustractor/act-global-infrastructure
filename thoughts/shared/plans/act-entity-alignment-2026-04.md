## 2. Key structural decisions and risks

### Decision made

- **Shareholding of the Pty**: Ben + Nic personal. Standard Pty. Mission lock is contractual via service agreement with A Kind Tractor, not structural via charity ownership.

### Risks and open questions

**Clarified 2026-04-24:** A Kind Tractor is effectively dormant. No operational role in current pitches. Kept available as a backstop vehicle if a future funder requires ACNC/DGR recipient, but nothing active depends on it.

**Risk 1: Mission lock is informal.**
The Pty is founder-owned, founder-directed. Mission lock lives in founder intent and board practice, not in entity structure or service agreements. Most impact-oriented Australian Pty Ltds operate this way; a sophisticated funder may or may not flag it. If it matters to a specific funder, the associated charity can be activated.

**Risk 2: A Kind Tractor is not DGR-endorsed.**
Not a blocker for the Minderoo grant (Pty-direct). Worth parking as a future option if ACT ever wants to unlock donor tax-deduction for individual giving, or to offer a charity-recipient backstop to specific PAFs. 6-12 week ATO process once pursued. Not in the critical path for the 30 June Pty cutover.

**Risk 3: Minderoo grant flow.**
Settled: grant lands directly in A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, matching the QBE Catalysing Impact contract location. Minderoo's Strategic Impact Fund does fund for-profit impact vehicles; Generation One's Dream Venture seed grants have gone to individual founders directly. Pre-decision conversation with Minderoo should verify their vehicle for this specific grant.
