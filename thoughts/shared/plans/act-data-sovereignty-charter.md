# ACT Data Sovereignty Charter

**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Status:** DRAFT
**Context:** ACT (A Curious Tractor) works alongside marginalised communities — especially First Nations communities — on projects involving deeply personal data: stories, cultural knowledge, relationships, and lived experience. This charter establishes our non-negotiable principles for handling that data.

---

## Preamble

ACT exists to transfer institutional power to community-led initiatives. Data is power. Therefore, data sovereignty is not a feature — it is the foundation.

We recognise that:
- **Data about communities belongs to those communities.** Not to ACT, not to funders, not to governments.
- **First Nations peoples have inherent rights** over their cultural heritage, traditional knowledge, and personal information.
- **The history of data extraction from Indigenous communities** — by researchers, governments, and corporations — is a history of harm. We will not repeat it.
- **Consent is not a checkbox.** It is an ongoing relationship of trust, maintained through transparency and reciprocity.

---

## Foundational Frameworks

This charter is informed by and aligns with:

### 1. CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance
- **Collective Benefit** — Data ecosystems shall be designed to enable Indigenous peoples to derive benefit
- **Authority to Control** — Indigenous peoples' rights and interests in Indigenous data must be recognised and their authority to control such data must be empowered
- **Responsibility** — Those working with Indigenous data have a responsibility to share how those data are used to support Indigenous peoples' self-determination
- **Ethics** — Indigenous peoples' rights and wellbeing should be the primary concern at all stages of the data life cycle

### 2. Maiam nayri Wingara — Australian Indigenous Data Sovereignty Principles
- Indigenous people have the right to exercise control of the data ecosystem (collection, ownership, application)
- Data should enable Indigenous peoples to tell their own stories
- Data must be collected in culturally appropriate ways
- Indigenous communities have the right to govern how their data is accessed and used

### 3. Te Hiku Media Kaitiakitanga License
- Originated from Aotearoa/New Zealand for te reo Maori language data
- Establishes that data collected from communities remains under community guardianship (kaitiakitanga)
- Commercial use requires community consent and benefit-sharing
- Forked to: https://github.com/Acurioustractor/Kaitiakitanga-License
- Adapting for Australian First Nations context as part of Empathy Ledger

### 4. OCAP Principles (First Nations Information Governance Centre, Canada)
- **Ownership** — community collectively owns its cultural knowledge and data
- **Control** — community controls all aspects of data management
- **Access** — community must have access to information about themselves
- **Possession** — physical control of data must rest with the community

## ACT's Data Sovereignty Commitments

### Principle 1: Community Ownership
- All stories, cultural knowledge, and personal narratives collected through Empathy Ledger, JusticeHub, or any ACT project **belong to the people and communities who shared them.**
- ACT is a custodian, not an owner. We hold data in trust.
- Communities can request deletion, export, or transfer of their data at any time.

### Principle 2: Informed and Ongoing Consent
- Consent is obtained in language and format accessible to each community.
- Consent is not perpetual — it is reviewed and renewed at agreed intervals.
- People can withdraw consent at any time, and we will action withdrawal within 30 days.
- We never collect data "just in case." Every data point has a stated purpose.

### Principle 3: Benefit Flows to Community
- Any commercial value derived from community data (research partnerships, publications, products) requires explicit community agreement and benefit-sharing.
- ACT Ventures' 40% profit-sharing model applies to any revenue generated from community-sourced data.
- Communities have first right of refusal on partnerships involving their data.

### Principle 4: AI and Algorithmic Transparency
- We disclose when AI is used to process, analyse, or generate insights from community data.
- AI models are never trained on Indigenous cultural knowledge without explicit community consent and oversight.
- Embedding/vectorising stories for search requires the same consent as sharing the story itself.
- We do not use community data for general-purpose AI training, benchmarks, or datasets — ever.

### Principle 5: Data Minimisation and Purpose Limitation
- We collect only what is needed for the stated purpose.
- Data is not repurposed without returning to the community for consent.
- When a project concludes, we work with the community to determine what data is retained, returned, or deleted.

### Principle 6: Security and Sovereignty Infrastructure
- Community data is stored in Australian data centres (Supabase AP-Southeast-2 / Sydney).
- Access controls are role-based with audit logging.
- No third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking on community-facing platforms.
- We do not sell, share, or trade community data with any third party.

### Principle 7: Design for Our Own Obsolescence
- All systems are built so communities can operate them independently.
- Data export is always available in standard, portable formats.
- We actively build local technical capacity so communities are not dependent on ACT.
- When a community is ready to self-govern their data infrastructure, we celebrate and step back.

## Implementation in ACT Projects

| Project | Data Types | Sovereignty Measures |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Empathy Ledger** | Personal stories, cultural narratives, photos, audio | Kaitiakitanga License adaptation, community consent flows, story ownership tags |
| **JusticeHub** | Justice system data, personal legal histories | Anonymisation by default, community access controls, no government data-sharing without consent |
| **Goods on Country** | Fleet telemetry, community asset locations | Community org owns their fleet data, exportable, deletable |
| **Black Cockatoo Valley** | Environmental monitoring, land management data | Traditional Owner governance over land data, cultural site data restricted |
| **The Harvest** | Community gathering data, food sovereignty records | Participant control, no commercial use of gathering records |
| **ACT Farm** | Agricultural data, land use patterns | Landowner data sovereignty, open-source methodology |
| **GrantScope** | Public grants data, foundation information | Public data only — no community data collected |

## Governance

- This charter is reviewed annually in consultation with community partners.
- Any material change requires community consultation before implementation.
- Breaches of this charter are treated as critical incidents — immediate response, transparent disclosure, and community-led remediation.
- ACT's board includes community representation to ensure accountability.
