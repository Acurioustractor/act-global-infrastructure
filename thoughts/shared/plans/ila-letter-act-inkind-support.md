**RE: Letter of In-Kind Support — "Voices on Country: Palm Island Elders Knowledge Journeys"**

I write as Co-Founder of A Curious Tractor (ACT) to confirm our in-kind technical partnership supporting the Voices on Country project.

**About A Curious Tractor:**

ACT is a regenerative innovation ecosystem that partners with First Nations communities to build sovereign technology and cultural infrastructure. ACT was co-founded by Benjamin Knight (systems designer) and Nicholas Marchesi OAM (co-founder of Orange Sky Australia, 2016 Young Australian of the Year). ACT partners with — not for — marginalised communities, with a commitment to designing for our own obsolescence so that communities own their narratives, land, and economic futures.

**Existing Relationship with PICC:**

ACT has worked with Palm Island Community Company since 2024. We designed and built PICC's digital storytelling platform and cultural archive system, and supported the documentation of the 2025 Mission Beach Elder heritage journey. This existing partnership means we bring deep understanding of PICC's cultural protocols, community relationships, and technical infrastructure.

**In-Kind Contributions ($30,000 over 3 years):**

ACT commits to providing the following technical services at no cost to the grant:

- **Digital archive architecture** — system design for the sovereign on-Country digital archive, including server configuration, backup strategy, and long-term sustainability planning (estimated value $10,000)
- **Mukurtu platform implementation support** — configuration of cultural protocol-based access controls, tiered permissions (public/community/family/sacred), and community training on the platform designed specifically for Indigenous cultural heritage management (estimated value $5,000)
- **Data sovereignty framework design** — technical implementation of the four-tier Elder-defined access system across all digital materials including photographs, video, audio, and transcripts (estimated value $5,000)
- **Exhibition design support** — digital elements, video integration, and layout for community exhibitions and the major combined touring exhibition (estimated value $10,000)

**Why ACT Supports This Project:**

The Voices on Country project embodies ACT's core mission: communities owning their narratives. The Mukurtu-based digital archive, the four-tier access system, and the on-Country infrastructure ensure that Bwgcolman Elders' knowledge remains under community control. This is not just a cultural project — it is a model for Indigenous data sovereignty that can inform communities across Australia.

ACT provides this support as a mission-aligned partnership. We do not seek any financial return from this project.

This contribution is confirmed pending the project receiving ILA funding and a grant agreement being executed.
