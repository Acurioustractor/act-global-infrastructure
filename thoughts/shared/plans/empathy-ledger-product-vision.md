# Empathy Ledger: The Storytelling Culture Platform

> Every organisation has a story. Most can't tell it. Empathy Ledger is the platform that helps organisations build a storytelling culture — celebrating themselves, their staff, their clients, and their supporters in one place.

## The Shift

Empathy Ledger started as a "story collection platform." That's too small.

The real product is **a storytelling culture.** Not just collecting stories — building the organisational muscle to:
- See what's happened (the record)
- Share it beautifully (the story)
- Remember it all (the knowledge)
- Know where you're going (the intent)

An organisation with a storytelling culture doesn't scramble at annual report time. Doesn't lose knowledge when staff leave. Doesn't struggle to tell funders what they've done. The stories are already there — captured, organised, and ready to be told.

**Empathy Ledger is the platform that makes that possible.**

---

## Who Tells Stories

This is the key reframe. EL isn't just for "storytellers" in the traditional sense (interview subjects, participants). Every organisation has four storytelling voices:

### The Organisation Itself
- "Here's who we are, what we do, and why it matters"
- Annual reports, impact statements, public narrative
- The institutional voice

### Staff and Team
- "Here's what we did, how it went, and what we learned"
- Project journeys, meeting notes, reflections, decisions
- The operational voice

### Clients / Community / Participants
- "Here's my experience, in my words"
- Stories of impact, change, connection
- The sovereign voice (this is EL's origin — and it stays central)

### Supporters / Funders / Partners
- "Here's why we back this, and what we've seen"
- Testimonials, partnership stories, funder perspectives
- The endorsement voice

**One platform. Four voices. All connected.**

## The Platform: Four Modes

What was previously spec'd as four separate products becomes four modes of one platform.

### STORY Mode — "Look What We've Done"

The output engine. Takes everything in the platform and produces beautiful, shareable artefacts.

**What it produces:**
- Annual reports (print + digital scroll storytelling)
- Impact summaries (one-pagers for funders)
- Project spotlights (sharable story pages)
- Photo essays (big photography + narrative)
- Social content (quotes, stats, moments pulled from the platform)

**How it works:**
- AI assembles draft narratives from captured content
- Design templates turn raw material into polished output
- Human review and approval before anything goes public
- Reusable year after year — each year builds on the last

**The magic:** An organisation that's been using EL for 12 months doesn't need to "write" their annual report. They select, refine, and approve. The raw material is already there.

**EL feature this maps to:** New — but builds on existing story display, ALMA analysis summaries, and storyteller profiles. The shift is from "display individual stories" to "compose organisational narratives from many stories."

---

### MEMORY Mode — "Everything We Know"

The knowledge engine. All organisational knowledge, searchable through conversation.

**What it holds:**
- Every story ever captured (already 555+ in EL)
- Every transcript and analysis (251 transcripts with ALMA analysis)
- Uploaded documents (reports, minutes, plans, grant applications)
- Decision records ("We decided X because Y")
- Project histories (what happened, in what order, why)

**How people access it:**
- Chat interface — ask a question in plain language, get a sourced answer
- "What programs have we run for young people?"
- "What did the board decide about X in March?"
- "What has [storyteller name] said about their experience?"
- Source attribution on every answer
- Access controls respect story sovereignty and cultural protocols

**The magic:** New board member gets up to speed in one conversation. Grant writer finds impact evidence in seconds. Key person leaves and the knowledge stays.

**EL feature this maps to:** Extension of existing RAG potential. EL already has the data (stories, transcripts, analysis). This adds a conversational interface and document ingestion beyond stories.

---

### CAPTURE Mode — "Record As It Happens"

The input engine. Simple ways for anyone to add to the organisational record.

**How people capture:**
- Voice note → auto-transcribed → AI-structured as story/meeting note/decision
- Photo + caption → tagged to project, person, or theme
- Quick text → decision record, observation, reflection
- Meeting summary → speak what happened, AI writes it up
- Document upload → ingested into MEMORY automatically

**Organised by journeys:**
- Each project has a visual timeline
- Steps defined by the team (not imposed)
- Content captured at each step builds the project story
- Journey view shows progress and captures gaps

**The magic:** At the end of a project (or year), you don't reconstruct from memory. The record built itself as you worked. Photos, voice notes, decisions — all timestamped, tagged, and ready for STORY mode.

**EL feature this maps to:** Evolution of existing story collection. Currently, stories enter through formal interviews/transcripts. CAPTURE adds informal, lightweight input — voice notes, photos, quick entries. Same sovereignty principles apply. The storyteller (whether staff, client, or partner) still owns their contribution.

---

### DIRECTION Mode — "Where We're Going"

The intent engine. Connects today's work to tomorrow's aspirations.

**How it works:**
- Organisation defines 3-5 aspirations in their own words
- Projects and stories link to aspirations automatically (via ALMA themes)
- Evidence surfaces as it's captured: "12 stories this quarter connected to your aspiration about Elder knowledge"
- Quarterly review: "Are we heading where we want to go?"
- Gap detection: "You have strong evidence for X but nothing captured yet for Y"

**The magic:** Funders ask "what's your theory of change?" and the answer is live on screen — not a document someone wrote two years ago, but a living map populated by real stories, real projects, real evidence.

**EL feature this maps to:** Extension of ALMA's organisational intelligence layer. ALMA already does theme detection and pattern analysis across stories. DIRECTION adds intentionality — "these are the themes we WANT to grow" — and tracks evidence against them.

## How The Four Modes Feed Each Other

```
          DIRECTION defines what matters
                    |
                    v
    CAPTURE records it ──────> MEMORY holds it all
         as it happens            searchable forever
                 \                      /
                  \                    /
                   v                  v
                  STORY tells it beautifully
                        |
                        v
              Shared with the world
                        |
                        v
              Informs next DIRECTION
```

**The virtuous cycle in practice:**

1. Board says "We want to strengthen Elder knowledge transfer" (DIRECTION)
2. Team runs Elders Room project, captures voice notes and photos along the way (CAPTURE)
3. All content is searchable and connected to the aspiration (MEMORY)
4. At year end, AI drafts the Elder knowledge section of the annual report from captured content (STORY)
5. The report shows strong progress — board sets new aspiration: "Youth-Elder knowledge sharing" (DIRECTION)
6. Cycle continues...

## What Already Exists vs What's New

| Capability | Current EL State | What's New |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Story collection | 555 stories, formal interview format | CAPTURE: informal voice/photo/text input |
| Story sovereignty | Consent architecture, access controls | Same — extended to all four voices |
| ALMA analysis | 4-layer pipeline, theme detection | DIRECTION: intentional theme tracking |
| Storyteller profiles | 217 profiles with analysis | Extended to staff, org, supporters (not just clients) |
| Story display | Individual story pages | STORY: composed narratives, annual reports, impact summaries |
| Knowledge search | Not implemented | MEMORY: RAG chatbot across all content |
| Project organisation | Projects exist as categories | CAPTURE: project journey timelines |
| Multi-tenant | Designed for, not fully deployed | Same — each org owns their instance |

**Key insight:** ~60% of this vision already exists in EL's codebase and data model. The shift is:
1. Broadening who tells stories (four voices, not just interview subjects)
2. Adding lightweight capture (voice notes, photos — not just formal transcripts)
3. Adding a knowledge layer (conversational search across everything)
4. Adding composed output (annual reports, impact summaries — not just individual story pages)
5. Adding intentionality (track aspirations, not just collect evidence)

## The Gradual Integration Model

For organisations with low tech literacy (like PICC), EL phases in gently:

| Phase | Mode | What the org does | What they learn |
|-------|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **STORY** | Reviews and approves their annual report | "Our stories are valuable and beautiful" |
| 2 | **MEMORY** | Asks questions, gets answers | "Everything we know is accessible" |
| 3 | **CAPTURE** | Takes photos, records voice notes | "We can build our own record" |
| 4 | **DIRECTION** | Defines aspirations, reviews evidence | "We can see where we're going" |

Phase 1 requires ZERO tech literacy — ACT produces, org approves.
Phase 4 requires only the ability to participate in a facilitated conversation.

**By year 2:** The annual report assembles itself from content the org captured throughout the year. ACT's role shrinks to design refinement and facilitation.

## Why "Empathy Ledger" Is The Right Name

A ledger records transactions. An empathy ledger records **human transactions** — the moments of connection, change, struggle, and celebration that make an organisation real.

Every entry in the ledger is:
- **Owned** by the person who made it (story sovereignty)
- **Connected** to the organisation's broader narrative (ALMA themes)
- **Accessible** to those who need it (with appropriate permissions)
- **Beautiful** when it's time to share (STORY mode)

The name already captures the full vision. The product just needs to match it.

## Open Questions

1. **Architecture:** Does each org get their own EL instance (current multi-tenant design) or do we build a shared platform with org-level isolation?
2. **CAPTURE offline:** Palm Island has patchy internet. How does voice/photo capture work offline and sync later?
3. **STORY production:** How much is AI-composed vs human-designed? Where's the line between "AI drafts" and "designer polishes"?
4. **ALMA as the thread:** ALMA themes connect everything — but are they the right organising principle for non-ACT clients, or too ACT-specific?
5. **Existing 555 stories:** These are the proof that EL works. How do we use them in sales/demos without compromising story sovereignty?
6. **Name/brand:** Is "Empathy Ledger" the client-facing name, or does it need a warmer name for community orgs?
7. **Relationship to Innovation Studio:** Is EL *the product* and Innovation Studio *the service wrapper* (setup, training, annual report production)? That feels right.
