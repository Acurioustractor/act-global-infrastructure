### Holding Wages / Shares for Pty Ltd — Tax Impact

**Current situation:** Ben and Nicholas have NOT drawn wages from the sole trader. This is strategically correct because:

1. **Sole trader income = personal income** — wages would just be drawings, taxed at marginal rate
2. **Under Pty Ltd:** Wages become tax-deductible business expenses AND R&D eligible personnel costs
3. **R&D personnel costs are the BIGGEST eligible expense** — salaries of people doing R&D work
4. **Example impact:**

```
Scenario A: Pay $200K wages as sole trader (FY26)
  - Personal income: $200K (taxed at ~37% marginal)
  - R&D benefit: $0 (sole trader can't claim own wages for R&D)
  - Tax: ~$74K

Scenario B: Pay $200K wages from Pty Ltd (FY27)
  - Company deduction: $200K
  - R&D refund: $200K × 43.5% = $87K CASH BACK from ATO
  - PAYG on wages: ~$74K (paid by employee from wage)
  - Net company position: -$200K wages + $87K R&D refund = -$113K cost
  - vs sole trader: -$200K cost, no refund
  - SAVING: $87K per year
```

**This is the single biggest reason to transition.** R&D personnel costs under a Pty Ltd can generate $87K+ in refundable offsets per year.

## Key Insight: The $87K/Year R&D Leverage

The entire reason to hold wages for the Pty Ltd is this:

**Sole trader:** Cannot claim own time as R&D expenditure. Only materials, contractors, and overhead.

**Pty Ltd:** Employee wages (including directors) spent on R&D activities qualify for the 43.5% refundable offset.

With Ben full-time on systems/AI ($150K+) and Nicholas splitting R&D/ops ($100K+), the personnel R&D claim alone could be:
- **$200K wages × 43.5% = $87K cash refund from ATO per year**

Plus the existing non-personnel R&D spend (~$200K/yr) adds another ~$87K.

**Total potential R&D refund under Pty Ltd: ~$170K/year**

This is not speculative — it's the legislated rate for small entities with <$20M turnover. The key requirement is rigorous documentation (which our automated systems are already generating).
