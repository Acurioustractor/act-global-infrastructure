# Letter of Financial Support — Palm Island Community Company
## For ILA 2026-27: "Voices on Country"

*To be printed on PICC letterhead, signed by Rachel Atkinson*

[PICC Letterhead]

**To the Indigenous Languages and Arts Program**
Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development, Communications and the Arts

**RE: Evidence of Financial Support — "Voices on Country: Palm Island Elders Knowledge Journeys"**

I write as CEO of Palm Island Community Company (PICC) to confirm our financial commitment to the Voices on Country project.

**PICC Cash Co-Contributions ($35,000 over 3 years):**

PICC commits to providing the following cash contributions from our own funds to support the project:

- Travel and accommodation cost top-up for both Elder knowledge journeys, should actual costs exceed grant budget lines (estimated $10,000 per journey = $20,000 total)
- Elder participation payments for project planning meetings, cultural governance sessions, content review, and community consultations throughout the 36-month project — covering activities outside the 10-day journey consultation fees funded by the grant (estimated $5,000 per year = $15,000 total)

**PICC In-Kind Co-Contributions ($69,000+ over 3 years):**

PICC will provide the following in-kind support at no cost to the grant:

- Elders meeting room, office space, and community facilities for project coordination, planning meetings, and exhibition events (estimated value $8,000 per year = $24,000 total)
- Elder group coordination — facilitating weekly Elders meetings, cultural governance oversight, and community engagement that PICC already delivers as part of its ongoing programs (estimated value $15,000 per year = $45,000 total)
- Community networks, Traditional Owner authority, and cultural permissions — relationships and trust built over 19 years of operation that cannot be replicated or purchased
- Existing school language program infrastructure, teachers, and curriculum integration support for the Manbarra language resource

**Organisational Capacity:**

PICC has an annual turnover of $29 million and employs over 200 staff. We maintain audited financial statements, robust governance through our 7-member Indigenous Board of Directors, and established financial management systems including dedicated project cost centres, monthly Board financial reporting, and annual independent audit.

PICC has a proven track record managing complex multi-year funded programs, including a $1.9 million Movember men's mental health partnership (5 years) and multiple federal government service contracts. This project represents approximately 1.3% of our annual turnover and is well within our financial management capacity.

**Total PICC Co-Contribution: $104,000+** (cash $35,000 + in-kind $69,000+)

This contribution is confirmed pending the project receiving ILA funding and a grant agreement being executed.
