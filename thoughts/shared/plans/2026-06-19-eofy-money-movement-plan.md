## 3. The cutover decision (sole trader → ACT Pty)

**Recommended structure (Option A — the repo's strong leaning, *not yet ratified in writing*).** A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd is the operating company, full stop: trades from 1 July 2026, is the R&D claimant, the founder-payroll employer, and runs the Goods commercial arm (trades as "Goods on Country"). The "Pty becomes a pure holdco with a new subsidiary doing the work" hive-down was **rejected** — it forces a second migration of contracts, R&D registration and trading name. **Treat Option A as the working choice; the in-writing lock is today's urgent governance item.**

### The rollover mechanism — and the trap (SL's #1 ruling)

As described (sole trader Nic → Pty owned 50/50 by Knight + Marchesi family trusts), **neither standard CGT rollover cleanly applies:**

- **Subdivision 328-G (Small Business Restructure Rollover)** covers all four asset classes and ACT's turnover is well under $10M — but it requires **no change to ultimate economic ownership (UEO)**, held only by natural persons. A sole trader (Nic, 100% UEO) transferring into a structure 50/50 owned by **two different families' trusts** does not maintain Nic's UEO — it introduces the Knight side as a new ~50% owner. *On its face this fails the no-change-in-UEO condition* (verified — ATO LCR 2016/3 + Webb Martin example). The s328-440 discretionary-trust modified-UEO workaround does **not** rescue it — it needs a single family group, and this spans two (verified).
- **Subdivision 122-A** requires the transferor to own **100% of the company's shares immediately after** — the Pty is 50/50 trusts, not 100% Nic, so 122-A is **not available** (verified). It is also shares-only consideration, so it cannot coexist with taking back a credit loan account.

**The three exits SL will weigh** (inferred — a synthesis of the conditions, not a ruling): (a) restructure the contribution so each individual's UEO is preserved (Nic contributes ~50% value, Ben matching); (b) interpose Nic's own family trust as transferor with aligned family-trust elections; or (c) accept a **taxable disposal sheltered by the 50% CGT discount + Div 152 small business CGT concessions** (15-year exemption / 50% active-asset reduction / $500K retirement exemption). Option (c) is also the route that, via a market-value sale with vendor finance, creates a **credit director-loan account** (§4) — but it is a CGT event, "free" only because Div 152 wipes the gain.

**Three distinct concepts must not be conflated:** (1) cross-entity P&L journals (cutover accounting, *not* the R&D basis); (2) the CGT-asset transfer rollover/sale (328-G vs 122-A vs taxable-with-Div-152); (3) Div 615 (future holdco only). None is SL-confirmed in the source files.

### What transfers vs what stays

- **Transfers:** IP — codebases (~50+ repos), LCAA, Aesthetics of Asymmetry, Empathy Ledger, ALMA, JusticeHub, Goods — via a **written IP assignment deed** (most legally important; after an IP-clause audit of grant/partnership contracts); equipment/hardware (~$30–50K) at market value; contracts (QBE, Minderoo, Goods, EL licensing) by novation/re-sign; SaaS billing entity from 1 Jul.
- **Stays with the sole trader:** cash/bank (~$694K — kept for final BAS + receivables); outstanding receivables (**~$216K per asset table vs ~$507K cited later — the docs don't reconcile; likely different snapshots; flag to SL and confirm the real uncollected figure before drawing founder cash ahead**); ACT-FM (farm) and ACT-BV lines (`reallocate=N`).

### Legal timing

**No legal requirement to complete exactly on 30 Jun 2026** (verified). The Pty can commence mid-FY; the sole trader lodges a final part-year return and the Pty its first return from when it derives income. 30 Jun is *preferred* (clean FY boundary, simplest apportionment) but not mandatory. **Cutover Rule 2 (honest delay) governs.** **GST + PAYGW must be live before any transfer/settlement** for the going-concern exemption to hold.

### Holdco-above-later via Div 615

**Do NOT create a holdco now.** The 50/50 two-trust ownership is exactly what makes **Div 615** (not 122-A) the right interposition tool — Div 615 requires 2+ shareholders (verified). When/if a real sale or raise needs it, interpose a fresh holdco shell *above* the operating Pty: the two trusts swap ACT shares for holdco shares, proportions preserved, **no CGT event**, and the operating company never moves (contracts, R&D registration, Goods name, bank, Xero all stay put). Conditions are strict and literal — non-redeemable shares only, "nothing-else" consideration, exact proportionate interest, holdco owns 100% immediately after completion time, and the **s 615-65 choice lodged within 2 months** of completion time (verified). Watch the future small-business-CGT angle: a holdco layer activates the **90% test** and the **80% active-asset test** on any future share-sale exit (e.g. the Y5 CivicGraph exit) — preserve-able but must be engineered; the ~$1M-saving claim is plausible in concept, **unverified for ACT**.

## 6. R&D ramp & the basis cure

### The problem (verified internal finding, 2026-06-01)

The live `rd_eligible` flag counted raw founder **drawings** as R&D spend. Of **$238,654** flagged FY26 R&D-eligible spend, **~81% was 13 payments to Nic (Jul–Nov 2025), account-880/equity pattern, mostly no receipt** — drawings, not wages. Stripping them re-baselines the as-recorded founder R&D basis from a planned **$317,500 down to ~$55,532** — roughly **$104K of refund component was resting on drawings.** The drawings are excludable on **two independent grounds**: (a) drawings characterisation, and (b) **pre-incorporation** — all 13 payments pre-date the Pty's 24 Apr 2026 registration.

### The cure — exactly what to do before 30 Jun 2026

The associate-payment timing rule (s355-205/s355-480 — **verified**) requires associate amounts (the founders *and* their family trusts — all associates under s318 ITAA 1936, verified) to be **both incurred AND actually paid** in the income year. So:

1. **Re-characterise the genuinely R&D-eligible slice of founder time into actual PAID wages** — run it through Pty payroll with **real PAYG withholding (STP-reported) + SG super**, and **physically pay the net cash from the Pty bank account** by 30 Jun. A journal, a loan-account credit, an offset/set-off, or "pay then immediately lend back" (round-robin) do **NOT** satisfy "paid" — round-robin is high audit risk and **Part IVA now expressly covers R&D offsets** (verified).
2. **Size the wage to the defensible R&D-eligible fraction only** — apportion against the registered core activities, not 100% of effort. A smaller, fully-paid, well-apportioned figure beats a large unpaid one. Founder allocations (Ben 95% / Nic 40%) may need haircutting to ~85% / ~30% to survive audit.
3. **Generate contemporaneous apportionment records now** — timesheets/activity logs/diaries over the FY26 R&D activities (the 2,506 window commits across five repos are timestamped supporting evidence, but need the time-allocation layer).
4. **Confirm market value** (associate market-value cap — over-market amounts excluded) and that total genuinely-paid eligible notional deductions **exceed $20,000** (verified minimum — danger is the base collapsing *below* $20K and there being no claim at all).

**Two grounds, one journal only cures one.** The recharacterisation journal cures the *drawings-characterisation* ground; the **pre-incorporation** ground is cured **only if SL accepts the Path C "sole trader acting on behalf of the Pty" mapping** so AusIndustry registers the Pty for FY26 activities. If SL rejects that mapping, ~$104K of refund component evaporates even with invoices + journal on paper. **The R&D basis is gated OFF until `RD_BASIS_RECORDS_CURED=1`** (invoices 15078-81 on paper + journal booked) — currently neither exists.

**Value-per-dollar tension:** even if a full-year FY26 R&D claim were available, a founder invoice deducted against the sole trader is worth up to ~47c vs 43.5c refundable — so the FY26 R&D window play is **marginal**, and the deduction may be the better lever; the premium flips to the company/R&D side from FY27.
