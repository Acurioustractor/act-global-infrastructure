## 3. The Co-Investment Clause (5.3) — Important

For **3 years** after program completion, SIH retains the right to **co-invest on the same terms** as any other investor/lender when ACT progresses debt or equity fundraising.

**What this means practically:**
- If ACT raises a $500K SEFA loan at 6% over 5 years → SIH can join on identical terms
- If ACT raises equity in ACT Ventures → SIH can participate at the same valuation
- ACT must give SIH **written notice** of any fundraising round
- SIH has **45 days** to respond with intent to participate
- This is a **right**, not an obligation — they may or may not exercise it

**Is this a problem?** No. It's actually helpful:
- Having SIH as a co-investor adds credibility to any raise
- It doesn't change terms for other investors
- It's standard in accelerator/incubator agreements
- 3-year window is reasonable

## 4. How Repayable Finance Works in This Context

The Catalysing Impact grant itself is **NOT repayable** — it's a grant. But the program is designed to make ACT **investment-ready** for repayable finance. Here's the stack:

```
LAYER 1: GRANTS (non-repayable)
├── Stage 1: $10K participation grant ✓ SECURED
├── Stage 2: $150K-$400K matched grant (PURSUING)
└── QBE Local Grants: $50K-$75K (IDENTIFIED in GrantScope)

LAYER 2: CONCESSIONAL DEBT (repayable, below-market terms)
├── SEFA (Social Enterprise Finance Australia) — $100K-$500K
├── Social Traders — enterprise development loans
└── NAB Social Enterprise Fund — impact lending

LAYER 3: IMPACT INVESTMENT (repayable, market-adjacent terms)
├── SIH co-investment (clause 5.3 right)
├── Impact investors via SIH network (PIN)
└── Patient capital from aligned foundations

LAYER 4: COMMERCIAL REVENUE
├── Goods on Country marketplace sales
├── Washing machine service contracts
└── ACT Ventures trading revenue (40% profit-sharing)
```

**The play:** Use the Catalysing Impact program to:
1. Get the diagnostic + advisory to refine the Goods on Country financial model
2. Use the $10K + advisory to prepare investment-ready documentation
3. Secure external capital commitments (SEFA, NAB, others)
4. Apply for Stage 2 with documented external capital → maximise the matched grant
5. Stage 2 grant + external capital = production facility / fleet expansion for Goods

**Repayable finance mechanics for Goods on Country:**
- **Revenue model:** Wash service fees + product sales + government service contracts
- **Loan structure:** SEFA-style 5-7 year term, 4-6% interest, 12-month moratorium on repayments
- **Security:** Equipment (washing machines) + service contracts as collateral
- **Repayment source:** Operating cash flow from Goods on Country operations
- **Risk mitigation:** Orange Sky relationship, existing fleet data (10 machines tracked), demonstrated demand
