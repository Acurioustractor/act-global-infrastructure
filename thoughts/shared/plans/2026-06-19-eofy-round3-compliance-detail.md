## By gap (the 8 lanes)

### Lane 1 — GST + cutover mechanics (going concern, grouping, Div 138, inter-entity)

**Going concern is the spine.** s38-325 has two limbs, both mandatory: (Limb 1) supply for consideration + recipient GST-registered at the day of supply + a **written going-concern agreement signed on/before that day**; (Limb 2) supplier supplies all things necessary to operate the enterprise AND carries it on until the supply day. *GSTR 2002/5* is binding. Consideration need not be monetary or at market value — a **vendor/director loan satisfies "for consideration"** (*GSTR 2001/6*).

**ACT application.** The proposed market-value sale of IP+goodwill+plant into ACT Pty, price left as a vendor loan, **is capable of being GST-free**. Retaining the ~$694K cash, bank and ~$216K (–$507K unreconciled) receivables does **NOT** break Limb 2(a) — cash, bank and trade debtors are run-off assets, not "things necessary to operate." The live risk is Limb 2(b): the sole trader must genuinely operate to the transfer day (Cutover Rule 2 — no premature cessation, no backdating, single dated arrangement).

**Position.** Structure as a GST-free going concern. **Edge-case kills to avoid:** Pty not registered on the day; written agreement made after the day (cannot be retrofitted); the business "winds down" before the supply day; a necessary contract/licence (QBE/Minderoo grant, EL licence) not novated by transfer day; the supply split across multiple days/arrangements.

**Fallback if going concern fails.** Because the parties are **associates**, **Div 72 imposes GST on market value even if the stated price is nominal/deferred** — a "sell for $1 + vendor loan" does NOT dodge GST. So get the clause right; don't rely on a low price.

**GST grouping is UNAVAILABLE.** Div 48 grouping requires a Div 190 **90%-owned group**; *ATO ID 2002/492* confirms **common shareholders/trusts at the top do NOT satisfy it** — the 90% link must run through corporate ownership. ACT Pty (50/50 two trusts) does not own ≥90% of Harvest Pty (external landlord minority). **Do not plan inter-entity charges as GST-free.** Same ownership fact that defeats Subdiv 328-G.

**Div 138 vs supply (corrects a repo mislabel).** Equipment **transferred to the Pty is a SUPPLY** (GST-free inside the going concern), **not** a Div 138 "retention adjustment." Div 138 only bites on assets Nic **keeps personally** for private use when he cancels GST registration (increasing adjustment = 1/11th of market value on the final BAS).

**Inter-entity GST.** ACT↔Harvest = ITC wash (both fully taxable). **ACT↔Butterfly = GST may STICK** — a DGR/PBI charity recovers ITCs only to the extent inputs support taxable/GST-free activity; much of Butterfly's activity is grant-funded/non-enterprise → **partial ITC, non-recoverable GST cost** (up to ~$9K/yr on a $100K management fee). Characterise each flow: genuine grant (no GST) vs fee-for-service (GST + partial ITC). Keep fees arm's-length (PBI + OCAP).

**Residual for pro.** SL/lawyer: draft the going-concern clause; confirm enterprise "operating and capable of operating" at transfer day; vendor-loan = genuine consideration (no sham — Part IVA watch); Pty registration effective ≤ transfer day; map every "necessary thing" against transfer-day readiness; compute Butterfly's ITC recovery rate; confirm Div 72 fallback and valid tax invoice for ITC if taxable.

*Sources: GST Act s38-325, Div 72, Div 48/190, Div 138; GSTR 2002/5; GSTR 2001/6; ATO ID 2002/492; LPLC / Dundas / Hall & Wilcox going-concern notes; ACNC GST guide.* Confidence: **strong** on all six GST questions.

### Lane 2 — QLD transfer duty (the "5.75% on goodwill+plant" correction)

**Mechanics.** Under *Duties Act 2001 (Qld)* ss 9–10, 35–37: business-asset categories include goodwill, business name, licence, supply right, IP and Qld plant. **s37: a transaction SOLELY for IP and/or plant (or solely a supply right / Qld debt) is NOT dutiable** unless aggregated with another Qld business asset (goodwill/business name) or Qld land. Dutiable value = greater of consideration or unencumbered value; rate scale (2025-26): **5.75% only bites ABOVE $1M** (~3.2% effective at $540K, ~2.3% at $300K). Duty is on **GST-inclusive** consideration (QRO DA011.1) — so a GST-free going concern keeps GST out of the base.

**ACT application.** The transfer is overwhelmingly IP (50+ codebases, brands, methodologies) + ~$30–50K plant — **both s37-excluded standing alone.** They become dutiable only by **aggregation once goodwill / the business name is in the sale.** No Qld land (farm stays personal in Nic's name). So the duty hinge is **goodwill + business name, QLD-apportioned for nexus (s36 / DA035)** — a largely interstate/overseas-serving business apportions the goodwill **down**.

**Restructure exemption likely fails.** Now legislated in **Part 1A Ch 10 (ss 413F–413I)**, not just the old ruling. Conditions: Qld small business (turnover ≤$5M), dutiable value ≤$10M, transferee company **must not have traded before**, and **ownership continuity** (full exemption only where ownership mirrors). Nic 100% → company owned 50/50 by **two different families' trusts** introduces a new ~50% owner → **at best partial exemption on Nic's preserved ~50%, full duty on the Knight ~50%**. Same structural failure as federal 328-G. **Binary kill-switch:** if the Pty (registered 24 Apr 2026) has **already traded**, the exemption is dead even on Nic's half. **NOT self-assessable** — lodge Form D2.2 / exemption 58.

**Per-step duty map:** (1) business sale = the **only** dutiable step, ~$3.5K–$17K; (2) corporate-trustee switch (s117) = **NIL** duty + NIL CGT; (3) Harvest lease = **NIL** (lease duty abolished by the *Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2005*) on market rent, no premium, no reversion option; (4) share subscriptions + future Div 615 holdco = **NIL** (no marketable-securities duty in QLD); (5) landholder duty = **NIL** now (no group entity holds ≥$2M Qld land; farm personal); (6) plant alone = **NIL** (s37), enters base only by aggregation.

**Position.** Correct the decision-pack line to: *"transfer duty at the ~3.5% marginal band on QLD-apportioned goodwill + business name (IP and plant duty-free under s37, entering only by aggregation); 5.75% only above $1M; restructure exemption gives at most partial relief on Nic's preserved interest; engage a QLD duties specialist and lodge with QRO."* **Consistency flag:** structuring as IP+plant-only to zero the duty undercuts the vendor-loan/Div 152 goodwill narrative — the goodwill figure must be set **once**, consistently, across the duty return and CGT calc.

**Contract-date lever.** QLD duty rate applies by reference to the **contract date**, which also governs the CGT 50%-discount window — **date the sale agreement within FY26** (the 2026 Budget's removal of the 50% discount from 1 Jul 2027 does not touch an FY26 disposal). Consider a QRO **private ruling** on goodwill apportionment + partial-exemption proportion **only if** the valued duty is five-figure.

**Residual for pro.** QLD duties specialist owns: asset-by-asset dutiable/non-dutiable characterisation; goodwill QLD-nexus apportionment; the "has the Pty traded" kill-switch ruling; partial-exemption proportion; Form D2.2 lodgement; private-ruling decision. SL coordinates the CGT/valuation so goodwill is consistent.

*Sources: Duties Act 2001 (Qld) ss 9-10, 35-37, 117, 147, Ch 3, Part 1A Ch 10; QRO DA011.1, DA000.16.1, DA035, DA167.1.1; QRO duties/restructure/landholder/lease pages; Revenue Legislation Amendment Act 2005.* Confidence: **strong** on mechanics, **lean** on the IP-carve-out re-scoping (source conflict — a couple of broker articles still say "IP is dutiable"; the specialist resolves on actual asset characterisation) and on the SBRR continuity reading (weighted MinterEllison over Grant Thornton, consistent with the 328-G analysis).

### Lane 3 — QLD payroll tax (grouping + crossover)

**Mechanics (FY25-26, verified QRO).** Threshold/deduction $1.3M ($108,333/mo); deduction tapers $1 per $7 from $1.3M to $0 at $10.4M; rate **4.75%** (≤$6.5M) / 4.95% (>$6.5M); mental-health levy +0.25% (>$10M) / +0.75% (>$100M) — all far above ACT's scale. Regional discount 1% (to 3.75%) if principal place of business is regional QLD + 85% regional wages. Grouping (ss 68–74) via related bodies corporate, common employees, >50% common controlling interest, or tracing. **Discretionary-trust trap:** every beneficiary is deemed to hold a controlling interest. **s.74 exclusion is barred (s.74(4)) for related bodies corporate.**

**ACT application.** Taxable wages include salary, **directors' fees, superannuation, grossed-up FBT, and relevant-contract contractor labour.** Founder pay FY27 = 2 × ($120K + $14.4K SG) = **$268,800** vs the **$1.3M group threshold** → ~$1.03M headroom. **Not liable on founder pay alone** — crossover needs ~6–8 more FTE or a substantial Harvest wage bill. ACT Pty + Harvest Pty are **ONE group** (related bodies corporate as parent/subsidiary, and/or common employees like Joey/Joseph Kirmos, and/or >50% common control) sharing **ONE** $1.3M threshold — not one each. The two passive family trusts are members on paper but pay no wages. **Keep Butterfly arm's-length** (separate Indigenous-led board, no shared staff, market-rate written agreements) so it never groups.

**Position.** Treat ACT + Harvest as one group from the moment Harvest pays wages; stand up a group-wages tracker alerting at **~$1.1M annualised** (register within 7 days of the month-end the threshold is crossed). Do not plan around a s.74 exclusion for Harvest (unavailable). Most World Tour / EL / CivicGraph contractors should be exempt (90-day rule; ordinarily-serves-the-public) and only their **labour** component counts.

### Lane 4 — SMSF, asset protection, Division 296

**Farm / Harvest premises in an SMSF.** Mechanically possible under the **business-real-property exception** (farm qualifies even with a ≤2ha dwelling if main use is primary production; premises if used wholly/exclusively in the Harvest business; an LRBA could fund a premises purchase via a bare trust holding a single acquirable asset — **no improvements, no construction with borrowings**). **But NOT viable in FY26:** founders' balances near-certainly below the ASIC ~$200K viability floor; assets lumpy/illiquid (the farm-in-SMSF liquidity trap); the farm is already protected in a family trust; the premises is third-party owned; sole-purpose + market-rent compliance burden. **DEFER both** as long-term-only options. Moving land out of the trust would likely trigger **QLD duty + CGT** with no super concession.

**Asset protection.** Regular, patterned SG + salary-sacrifice is creditor-protected (Bankruptcy Act s116(2)(d)) and **free** — reinforces salary-first / pay-super. **Cannot** be used as a last-minute shield (s128B clawback on out-of-character contributions). SMSF is operationally fragile to a founder's bankruptcy (disqualified trustee, 6-month rollover) — another reason to stay in an **APRA fund** early-stage.

**Division 296 (corrects prior-round assumptions).** **PASSED 10 Mar 2026; commences 1 Jul 2026; first year FY2026-27.** Two material changes from the 2023 design: **unrealised gains are NOT taxed** (dropped — earnings on a realised basis), and thresholds **ARE CPI-indexed** ($3M in $150K increments; $10M in $500K increments). Extra 15% on the TSB share above (indexed) $3M (~30% effective); additional rate above $10M (SL to pin the exact top-tier %). **Assessed + paid personally.** For the founders it is a **non-event** at current trajectory and stays so for years — only live if a CivicGraph windfall or sustained over-contribution lifts a single TSB past indexed $3M; even then it taxes only **realised earnings on the slice above**, not the capital.

**Contribution caps.** FY26: concessional $30K, NCC $120K (bring-forward $360K). FY27: concessional $32,500, NCC $130K. **Carry-forward concessional** (while TSB <$500K) is the key windfall-year lever. **Div 293** (extra 15% over $250K income) does **not** bite on $120K salary but can attach in a one-off CivicGraph-exit/bonus year.

### Lane 8 — Employment classification + WorkCover (QLD)
*(Filled + skeptic-checked in a separate pass after the main run truncated this lane — so it is the one lane that got an adversarial check.)*

**[1] Contractor-vs-employee for R&D personnel (post-2022 + 2024 law).** Two tests run in parallel. (a) **Fair Work:** after *Personnel Contracting* + *Jamsek* [2022] HCA the written contract governed ("primacy of the contract"); from **26 Aug 2024** the Closing Loopholes No.2 **s15AA** reverses that for FW purposes — classification now turns on the "real substance, practical reality and true nature of the relationship" (whole-of-relationship, multifactorial, looks past the paper). The **founders ($120K + 12% SG, controlled, integrated) are plainly employees — do not paper them as contractors.** (b) **Devs:** classify each on the s15AA substance test; a genuinely independent dev (own ABN, multiple clients, real delegation right, fixed deliverables, bears own risk) can be a true contractor. **R&D sharp edge:** a genuine *fixed-fee, at-risk* contractor keeps the spend notionally deductible; an associate or not-at-risk fee can fail R&D eligibility **and** drag in payroll tax + SG. **Action:** founders = employees from 1 Jul; devs = genuine services contracts *operated that way*; run each through the ATO employee/contractor decision tool + the QRO contractor test before engaging. **Confidence:** strong. *(s15AA Closing Loopholes No.2; Personnel Contracting/Jamsek; ATO R&D at-risk.)*

**[2] Sham contracting (Fair Work s357–359) — the aggressive line.** s357/358/359 prohibit dressing up employment as contracting. **From 27 Feb 2024 (Closing Loopholes No.1 — a separate Act/date from the 26 Aug 2024 s15AA change)** the employer defence moved from subjective ("didn't know / wasn't reckless") to an **objective reasonableness** test. Penalties were substantially increased — a "serious contravention" now triggers on knowing **or reckless** conduct (no longer requiring it to be systematic), into **six figures per contravention** (penalty-unit based — lawyer to quantify against the current penalty-unit value). **Paying a founder or an embedded full-time dev as a "contractor" to dodge PAYG/super/payroll tax is exactly the targeted conduct.** **Action:** never contractor-ise the founders or an integrated dev; keep a contemporaneous *reasonable-basis* file per genuine contractor (decision-tool output, services agreement, evidence of other clients / own ABN / delegation). **Confidence:** strong. *(DEWR + Fair Work Ombudsman.)*

**[3] WorkCover Queensland.** A WorkCover Accident Insurance policy is **compulsory** for any QLD employer with workers. A "worker" = an individual who works under a contract and is an employee for PAYG-withholding purposes — **BUT the WCR Act 2003 (Qld) EXCLUDES a person who is also a director of the employing company.** So a **PAYG dev = a compulsory worker who must be covered**, whereas the **founder-directors fall in the director exclusion and are NOT compulsory workers for ACT Pty** — they may elect **optional working-director cover** (not automatic). Premium = % of declared wages; WorkCover Qld **target average net rate $1.343/$100 wages (frozen, 2025-26)** — ACT's actual rate is set by its ANZSIC industry rate + claims experience. **Deemed-worker rule:** labour-only contractors are deemed workers and must be covered + in the wage declaration. **Wage-declaration base = non-director workers' wages; founder-director remuneration only enters if optional cover is elected.** **Action:** open a policy before the first wage; elect working-director cover explicitly if wanted; assess each dev for deemed-worker status. **Confidence:** strong (rate / compulsion / director-optionality) · lean (which devs are deemed). *(WorkSafe Qld; WCR Act 2003.)*

**[4] Harvest casuals.** Rostered venue/cafe/event staff (Harvest's control, premises, equipment, paid hourly) are **casual employees** — no realistic contractor argument (attempting one = sham). They attract the relevant **Modern Award** + casual loading, **12% SG**, and **compulsory WorkCover in Harvest Pty's own policy**. **Action:** Harvest Pty runs its own payroll / super / award / WorkCover — kept entirely distinct from ACT Pty. **Confidence:** strong.

**[5] SG on "deemed employee" contractors (s12(3) SGAA).** Even a genuine common-law contractor is a **deemed employee for SG** if engaged **"wholly or principally for labour"** (s12(3)) — then **12% SG** (from 1 Jul 2025) on the labour component, on top of the fee, capped via the **max contribution base $62,500/qtr** (2025-26). Per TR 2023/4 (replaced SGR 2005/1, 11 Dec 2024) a **genuine delegation right** takes the engagement outside s12(3) — even a *fettered* right qualifies unless it is a sham/empty. **Action:** budget 12% SG on any labour-principal dev unless a genuine delegation right exists; document the s12(3) call per contractor. **Confidence:** strong. *(s12(3) SGAA; TR 2023/4.)*

**[6] The cross-interaction — one call, four+ tests.** A single dev engagement triggers **four independent tests, each with its own definition**, plus R&D at-risk — and a "contractor" can pass one and fail the others: **(a) Fair Work s15AA** (employee vs contractor + sham exposure); **(b) R&D at-risk** (genuine fixed-fee/at-risk = deductible; associate amounts = paid-by-30-Jun, per earlier rounds); **(c) QLD payroll-tax relevant-contract** (deemed employee + payment = "wages" unless the 90-day / 180-day / ordinarily-serves-the-public exemption applies — QRO PTA035 etc.; **SL to confirm the exact PTA ruling numbers**); **(d) SG s12(3)** (deemed employee unless genuine delegation); **(e) WorkCover** (deemed worker = covered + declared). **Danger = partial-pass:** a dev who is a true contractor for Fair Work + R&D can still be a deemed employee for SG, payroll tax and WorkCover. **Action:** one **four-way classification worksheet per dev**, decided *before* signing (it sets the R&D outcome). **Confidence:** strong. *(QRO relevant-contracts + PTA035; ATO R&D at-risk.)*
