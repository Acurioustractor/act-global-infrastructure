## 3. Cluster detail & sources

### C1 — Transfer mechanism + QLD duty
**The crux:** the Pty cap table is *already issued* at Knight FT 50 / Marchesi FT 50, so Nic's one-person sole trader is going into a company half-owned by a second, unrelated family. That single fact breaks 328-G (requires no change in ultimate economic ownership; LCR 2016/3, s328-430; the s328-440 discretionary-trust alternative needs the *same* family group — Sch 2F ITAA36 — and Knight FT ≠ Marchesi FT) and 122-A (requires the transferor to receive 100% of the shares; they're already 50/50). **⚠ This retracts the prior `act-money-thesis-rebuttal.md` S2.3/S3.3 guidance that recommended 328-G and asserted UEO was satisfied — that is wrong on these facts.** The remaining path is a CGT event A1 disposal at market value, deliberately NOT rolled over, then sheltered (50% general discount + Div 152 50% active-asset reduction + retirement exemption to the $500K cap). The 2026 Budget's end of the 50% discount from 1 Jul 2027 does **not** touch Div 152 and does not affect an FY26 disposal. **This whole path is a fork from the documented journal-entry/Path-C model — SL must re-open it.** Sources: ATO LCR 2016/3; s328-440 (austlii); 122-A 100%-shares rule (taxtalks); Div 152 eligibility (ato.gov.au small-business-cgt-concessions). QLD duty: $1,050 + $3.50/$100 over $75K (~3–3.5% below ~$540K; the 5.75% marginal rate only bites above $1M) — but trust shareholders + a new family group may defeat the small-business-restructure exemption **entirely**; not self-assessable, lodge with QRO via a specialist (QRO restructure exemption + rates; CGW/MinterEllison commentary).

### C2 — Founder value via loans + Div 7A
The credit-loan prize works only on the fork (a real sale → the Pty owes the vendor → repayments are tax-free capital, outside Div 7A's company→shareholder direction). But because the founders are associates of the shareholding trusts and the arrangement's principal effect is tax-free extraction, there's **Part IVA exposure → PBR-worthy** (the C1 "strong" Div-7A-direction point and this Part IVA caveat must be read together). The loan is only as big as a *defensible valuation*, and the whole thing rests on the Div 152 shelter holding — **treat that precondition as unconfirmed** (founder-personal-exertion goodwill can be thin; MNAV/active-asset tests unconfirmed). Debit loans = complying s109N at 8.37%, small, last-resort. Never route a loan through a trust/holdco (s109T). Don't double-count the ~81% of the FY26 $238,654 that's already-withdrawn drawings. Sources: ATO Div 7A loans + interposed entities + benchmark rate; taxtalks (122-A); ATO small-business CGT concessions.
