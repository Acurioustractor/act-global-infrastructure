## Understanding — drafted
Katie Kiss is the current Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Social Justice Commissioner at the Australian Human Rights Commission. In this role, she works to advocate for the human rights of Indigenous Australians, aiming to promote equality and justice within the broader Australian society. Her career has been dedicated to social justice issues, focusing on how systemic changes can improve the lives of Indigenous communities.

Katie cares deeply about ensuring that the voices of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples are heard and their rights protected. She is committed to working with various stakeholders, including governments and other organizations, to address inequalities and injustices faced by Indigenous Australians. Her work reflects a strong dedication to human rights advocacy and creating an inclusive society where all members can thrive without discrimination or marginalization.
