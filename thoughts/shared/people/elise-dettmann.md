## Understanding — drafted
Elise Dettmann is a lived-experience researcher at the University of Melbourne, bringing over twenty years of personal experience with severe mental illness to her academic work. Her research focuses on understanding how severe mental illnesses impact physical health and aims to improve healthcare outcomes for individuals suffering from these conditions. Elise has been involved in various studies that highlight the importance of primary care in addressing the unmet physical health needs of people living with severe mental illness.

Elise seems deeply committed to using her personal experiences to advocate for better health care systems that recognize the interconnectedness of mental and physical well-being. Her work underscores a genuine concern for the holistic health of individuals, particularly those who face significant challenges due to their mental health conditions. Through her research, Elise is dedicated to fostering environments where people with severe mental illness can receive comprehensive support and care.
