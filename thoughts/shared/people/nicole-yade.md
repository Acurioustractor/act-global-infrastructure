## Understanding — drafted
Nicole Yade serves as the Chief Executive Officer of the Women’s & Girls’ Emergency Centre (WAGEC), an organization dedicated to supporting women and children who are victims of domestic violence. With over 25 years of experience in her field, Nicole leads an institution that has been active for nearly five decades. WAGEC focuses on providing refuge and support services to those affected by domestic abuse, with a nightly capacity to serve more than 200 individuals. The organization also prioritizes primary prevention strategies, conducts research, and implements economic security programs aimed at empowering survivors.

Nicole's personal journey through lived experience of domestic violence underscores her deep commitment to the cause she champions. This background informs her leadership approach, ensuring that WAGEC’s services are grounded in practical care and support for those who need it most. She cares deeply about creating a safe haven for women and children, emphasizing not just immediate refuge but also long-term solutions such as economic security and preventive measures. Nicole values community engagement and collaboration, inviting others to stand with her organization in its mission to end domestic violence and provide comprehensive support to survivors.
