## Understanding — drafted
The Office of the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Children’s Commissioner (OATSICC) is an independent body led by Ms. Vanessa Turnbull-Roberts, dedicated to advocating for the rights and wellbeing of Indigenous children and young people in Australia. This office conducts inquiries and advises government policies to ensure that these children receive appropriate support and protection. The OATSICC plays a crucial role in promoting the interests of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander youth through its advocacy work and policy recommendations.

The Office clearly cares deeply about ensuring that Indigenous children have access to better outcomes, including health, education, and social protections. They are committed to addressing systemic issues that affect these young people and strive to create a supportive environment where their rights are respected and upheld. The emphasis on independent inquiries and advice highlights the importance of objective assessments in improving the lives of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children.
