## Understanding — drafted
Bradley Clair serves as the Co-Founder and CEO of PowerWells, an innovative social enterprise based in Queensland, Australia, which he established alongside Nicholas Kamols in 2017. The primary mission of PowerWells is to tackle two significant issues—energy poverty and electronic waste—in remote areas by repurposing old batteries for use in affordable solar power systems. Through this approach, they aim to provide sustainable and clean energy solutions that not only illuminate homes but also empower communities with access to reliable electricity.

Bradley Clair appears to be deeply committed to social impact and environmental sustainability. His work with PowerWells reflects a strong dedication to improving the lives of people in underserved regions by offering practical, eco-friendly solutions. Beyond his role at PowerWells, he has been recognized for his philanthropic efforts, winning the QCF Innovation Philanthropist of the Year award in 2022. Bradley’s endeavors suggest that he cares greatly about creating a positive impact on society and the planet through sustainable technological innovation.
