## Understanding — drafted
Aysha Kerr is a prominent advocate for justice reform, specifically in Queensland, Australia. As Co-Chair of the Justice Reinvestment Network Australia, she leads efforts to shift resources away from incarceration towards community support systems through her involvement with the #jailingisfailing campaign. Aysha is also a proud member of the Quandamooka peoples and brings an Indigenous perspective to her work. Her advocacy aims at transforming the justice system by emphasizing rehabilitation over punishment.

Aysha clearly cares deeply about systemic change within the criminal justice system, prioritizing methods that support community well-being and reduce reliance on incarceration. This passion is rooted in her identity as a Ngugi woman of the Quandamooka peoples, which informs her approach to advocating for equitable and culturally sensitive policies. Her work underscores the importance of addressing root causes of crime through social investment rather than punitive measures, reflecting her commitment to justice that heals communities rather than harms them.
