## Understanding — drafted
Brodie Germaine is a gym owner and the founder of Brodie Germaine Fitness, an organization that integrates physical fitness with cultural teachings for Indigenous youth. Through his initiative known as the BAIL Project, he aims to guide young people away from criminal activities by providing them with leadership training and positive role models within their community. His efforts have been recognized and supported financially, receiving a $280k grant to further his youth program. Brodie collaborates with numerous organizations including Youth Justice, Child Protection, and local law enforcement to ensure comprehensive support for the young people he works with.

Brodie cares deeply about fostering leadership and positive community role models among Indigenous youth. He believes in using physical fitness as a tool not only for health but also for personal growth and cultural connection. His mission is driven by his own experiences and past struggles, which he channels into helping others avoid similar pitfalls. Brodie values the support of his community and understands the importance of having strong mentors who can guide young people towards a brighter future. Through his work with the BAIL Project and other collaborations, he aims to empower Indigenous youth and promote positive change within their communities.
