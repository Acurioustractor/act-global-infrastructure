## Understanding — drafted
Juanita Schaffa de Mauri is a researcher and teacher at The University of Western Australia, where she focuses on the reintegration needs of returning prisoners. She brings a unique perspective to her work after having experienced incarceration herself for fraud, which has deeply informed her research and advocacy efforts. Juanita emphasizes the importance of communication and support for individuals reintegrating into society from prison. Her work includes studies such as "Mind the Gap: A study on the reintegration needs of returning prisoners," which highlights the challenges faced by former inmates during their transition back to everyday life.

Juanita cares deeply about supporting people affected by the justice system, particularly those who are returning to society after serving time in prison. She is passionate about addressing systemic issues and improving communication channels between incarcerated individuals and their families and communities. Her experience as a TedX speaker and her publications on women in prison further underscore her commitment to understanding and advocating for the needs of marginalized populations within the criminal justice system. Juanita's work seeks to bridge gaps and foster more compassionate, supportive environments that aid in successful reintegration and reduce recidivism rates.
