## Understanding — drafted
Kate Bjur serves as the Executive Director for Research and Advocacy at PeakCare Queensland, where she plays a pivotal role in advocating for youth support and restorative justice. Her work encompasses raising awareness about the unique needs of young caregivers, particularly those caring for their siblings, and involves conducting inspections of youth detention centers to ensure they meet necessary standards. Kate’s commitment extends beyond these areas; she also actively works on crafting effective policies that benefit youth across Queensland.

Kate seems deeply committed to ensuring that young people have access to supportive environments and resources that help them thrive despite challenging circumstances. Her passion lies in storytelling as a means of empowering youth, highlighting the importance of restorative justice practices that focus on rehabilitation rather than punishment. Additionally, she cares about strengthening family bonds and providing young caregivers with the necessary support they need to balance their responsibilities while ensuring their own well-being.
