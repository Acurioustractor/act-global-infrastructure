## Understanding — drafted
Rebecca Minty is a dedicated professional currently serving as the Inspector of Custodial Services for the ACT Office of the Inspector of Custodial Services. With over fifteen years of experience in human rights law and policy, Rebecca brings extensive knowledge and a deep commitment to her role. Her work focuses on ensuring that correctional facilities uphold high standards of human rights and operate with transparency and accountability. Rebecca began her five-year term as Inspector in 2023, taking over from Neil McAllister.

Rebecca cares deeply about the well-being and fair treatment of individuals within custodial settings. She is committed to promoting oversight and accountability in correctional facilities through a rigorous inspection process that highlights areas for improvement while ensuring compliance with human rights standards. Additionally, her involvement in co-founding a non-government advocacy network demonstrates her broader commitment to enhancing the oversight of closed environments across Australia, working collaboratively with governments and civil society organizations to foster positive change.
