## Understanding — drafted
Robert Tickner serves as the Chairperson of the Board at Justice Reform Initiative, an organization that advocates for evidence-based alternatives to incarceration. His work is deeply rooted in the belief that Australia should move away from punitive justice systems modeled after those in America, which rely heavily on incarceration. Under his leadership, the Justice Reform Initiative focuses on reducing prison populations and addressing social determinants of crime through community-based approaches.

Tickner cares passionately about creating a more just society by challenging the traditional “tough on crime” policies that have proven ineffective. He believes these measures not only fail to address the underlying issues contributing to criminal behavior but also perpetuate cycles of disadvantage, particularly for marginalized communities. His work reflects a commitment to evidence-based practices and the belief that community support and social interventions can lead to more effective and humane outcomes than incarceration alone.
