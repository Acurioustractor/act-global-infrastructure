## Understanding — drafted
John Chambers is a well-known figure in the tech industry, having previously served as the CEO of Cisco. Today, he has taken on roles as a non-executive director at Close the Loop and the CEO of JC2 Ventures. His efforts are focused on fostering entrepreneurship and innovation, particularly within West Virginia where he mentors young CEOs and advocates for startup growth through his involvement with organizations like WVU Magazine and the Chambers College of Business and Economics. Chambers is dedicated to promoting a culture that embraces calculated risk-taking and views failures as opportunities for learning.

John cares deeply about nurturing the next generation of leaders and entrepreneurs. He is passionate about providing mentorship and support, believing in the transformative power of startups not only economically but also socially. His work extends beyond just financial investments; he actively engages with young CEOs to help them navigate challenges and scale their businesses globally. Chambers emphasizes the importance of a supportive environment that encourages innovation and risk-taking, seeing these as critical elements for sustainable growth and development.
