## Understanding — drafted
Shaun Fisher is a Mununjali and Gorenpul man from the Kooka community in Minjerrribah. As the owner-operator of Fisher’s Oysters, he leads an Indigenous-led oyster farming enterprise that aims to revive traditional ecological practices and honor the cultural significance of oysters within his community. Shaun actively works towards fostering a new generation of First Nations aquaculture leaders through programs like the First Nations Aquaculture Pathways Program, emphasizing both environmental stewardship and cultural revitalization.

Shaun cares deeply about reconnecting people with their natural environment and traditional practices. For him, oyster farming is not merely an economic activity but also a way to preserve and celebrate Indigenous knowledge and kinship with nature. He advocates for the recognition of oysters as more than just commodities; they are seen as integral parts of his community’s cultural heritage and ecological systems. His work reflects a broader commitment to youth empowerment, environmental sustainability, and the revitalization of First Nations cultures through practical initiatives in aquaculture.
