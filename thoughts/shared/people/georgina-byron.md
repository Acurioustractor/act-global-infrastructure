## Understanding — drafted
Georgina Byron is a prominent advocate for social change, particularly focusing on issues affecting women and First Australians. As CEO of the Snow Foundation and holding various leadership roles in philanthropy and community development, she emphasizes structured giving and social justice to improve lives. Her efforts span across different organizations where she works collaboratively to advance research and innovative programs that address critical health and societal challenges.

Georgina cares deeply about creating meaningful change through collaboration and circular change. She values the role of philanthropy in funding important initiatives such as tick-borne disease research, which helps communities thrive. Georgina's work is driven by a sense of joy in bringing people together to achieve shared goals for social justice and community development.

In our recent interactions with Georgina, we have focused on supporting her efforts at The Snow Foundation, particularly regarding First Nations Advisory Terms of Reference and the Parliamentary Friends for Ending Rheumatic Heart Disease. We exchanged updates through newsletters and survey feedback, and supported initiatives such as the Deadly Heart Trek aimed at addressing health issues in First Australian communities.
