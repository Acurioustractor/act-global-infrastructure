## Understanding — drafted
Tom Hearn serves as the Marketing and Communications Lead at Wakai Waian Healing Pty Ltd, an organization dedicated to providing psychological and counseling services specifically for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities in remote areas of Australia. His role involves not only enhancing the visibility and reach of Wakai Waian Healing's services but also collaborating on Indigenous media initiatives and health programs that aim to support these communities' social and emotional well-being. Prior to his current position, Tom founded BushTV in 2004 and has worked extensively in the area of documentary filmmaking, holding a Master of Arts in Documentary from his time at the ABC in Melbourne.

Tom clearly cares deeply about storytelling as a means to connect with people and advocate for Indigenous communities. His career choices reflect a dedication to leveraging media and communications to amplify voices that might otherwise go unheard. He has spent over two decades working in areas that support Aboriginal culture, health, and social issues, particularly focusing on remote regions where access to resources can be limited. Tom’s passion lies in using his skills to bridge gaps between Indigenous communities and broader Australian society, ensuring that the unique needs and perspectives of these groups are recognized and addressed effectively.
