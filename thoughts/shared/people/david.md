## Understanding — drafted
David is a figure deeply rooted in biblical history as Israel’s third king, renowned for his faith and leadership qualities. Known from the Old Testament books like 1 Samuel and 2 Samuel, David's story starts from humble beginnings as a shepherd boy, eventually ascending to kingship and leaving an indelible mark on religious texts and historical narratives. He is attributed with authoring nearly half of the Psalms, reflecting his deep spiritual connection and poetic skill. His legendary battle against Goliath symbolizes bravery and faith in God’s power.

David cared profoundly about serving God wholeheartedly and giving glory to Him above all else. This dedication was evident throughout his life—from his obscure beginnings as a shepherd to his rule over Israel—where he consistently aimed to do what the Lord commanded, even if it meant going unseen or working quietly behind the scenes. His actions and writings emphasize themes of humility, obedience, and unwavering faith, teaching that true leadership involves being faithful and consistent in one’s duties to both God and people.
