Zhanae Dodd is a passionate First Nations advocate and business owner dedicated to preserving Indigenous storytelling and cultural knowledge. As the founder of Guyala Yimba, she has been leading initiatives that bring together young people, community leaders, Elders, and government entities to collaboratively shape projects focused on Indigenous advocacy and cultural preservation. Zhanae’s work spans multiple roles: she is not only a business owner and consultant but also serves as a case manager and board member, continually working towards strengthening Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities.

Zhanae cares deeply about the empowerment of her community through education, storytelling, and fostering connections between different generations and sectors. Her efforts are driven by a commitment to honoring Indigenous knowledge and ensuring that it is both preserved and celebrated. She sees herself as a bridge between traditional practices and contemporary opportunities, aiming to create pathways for young people to engage with their cultural heritage while building sustainable futures.
