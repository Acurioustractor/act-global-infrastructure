## Understanding — drafted
The Minjerribah Moorgumpin Elders are a group of respected leaders who focus on preserving and teaching the cultural heritage, local languages, bush plants, and environmental skills of their region. Their work includes delivering guided tours and cultural awareness programs that aim to maintain cultural integrity and promote healing within their community while protecting Indigenous heritage.

The Elders deeply care about the preservation of Mother Earth and the continuity of cultural traditions for future generations. They emphasize the importance of identity, safety, and caring for Country in their teachings. Through cultural talks and educational initiatives, they work tirelessly to support their community’s connection with land and culture, ensuring that these values are passed on and honored.

In our shared work together, we have primarily collaborated on organizing and supporting the 2025 Goomi NAIDOC event, which took place on August 2nd. Much of our recent communication has revolved around coordinating logistics for this event, particularly concerning the video documentation of the day's activities. This collaboration reflects a focus on preserving and sharing cultural events to honor Indigenous heritage and promote community engagement.
