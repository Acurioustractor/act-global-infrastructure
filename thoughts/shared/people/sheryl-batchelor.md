## Understanding — drafted
Sheryl Batchelor is the founder and Chief Executive Officer of Yiliyapinya Indigenous Corporation, an organization dedicated to using neuroplasticity programs to improve brain health and social skills among vulnerable children and adults. As a Kunja woman with over 30 years of experience in merging Aboriginal knowledge with neuroscience, Sheryl has led her corporation in developing holistic approaches that focus on the overall wellbeing of individuals and their communities. Yiliyapinya Indigenous Corporation’s primary goal is to reduce recidivism by addressing brain health through innovative programs designed for at-risk groups, including repeat offenders.

Sheryl Batchelor cares deeply about community empowerment and healing, particularly among those who have experienced adversity. She believes in the power of integrating traditional Aboriginal knowledge with modern neuroscience to create sustainable change. Her work highlights the importance of a holistic approach that considers both mental health and social conditions to foster resilience and positive transformation within individuals and communities. Through Yiliyapinya Indigenous Corporation, she aims to support vulnerable populations by providing them with tools for personal growth and societal reintegration.
