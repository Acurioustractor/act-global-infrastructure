## Understanding — drafted
Sue-Anne Hunter, a Wurundjeri and Ngurai woman, serves as the inaugural National Commissioner for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Children and Young People. In this role, she is dedicated to advocating on behalf of First Nations youth and working towards improving their lives and well-being. Sue-Anne has spent her career in social work and therapy, using her extensive experience to support Indigenous children and families. Her commitment to these issues extends beyond her current position, as she also serves as a Board Member for Safe and Equal, an organization focused on advancing equality.

Sue-Anne Hunter clearly cares deeply about the welfare of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children and young people, drawing from both her professional background and personal heritage to inform her work. Her advocacy is driven by a desire to ensure that these youth have access to better opportunities and support systems, aiming for systemic changes that can address long-standing inequalities faced by Indigenous communities in Australia. Through her roles, she seeks to amplify the voices of First Nations children and families and fight for their rights and well-being on a national scale.
