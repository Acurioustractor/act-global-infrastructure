## Understanding — drafted
Seide Ramadani is the CEO of Nja-marléya, an Aboriginal-led organization representing 14 language groups in Maningrida, Northern Territory. In his role as Training Coordinator at Mala'la Health, he focuses on Indigenous health and community support, with a particular emphasis on primary care and aged care. His work is pivotal in enhancing healthcare access and quality for the communities he represents.

Seide appears to deeply care about fostering sustainable health practices that are rooted in cultural respect and community engagement. He values the empowerment of his community through education and training programs that can lead to better health outcomes, especially for the elderly. The collaborative aspect of his work extends beyond healthcare into broader community development initiatives, reflecting a holistic approach to well-being.

Our shared work with Seide has been centered around community-centered impact reporting and storytelling. This collaboration involves discussing methods and approaches to effectively communicate the impact of Nja-marléya’s and Mala'la Health’s efforts within the communities they serve. Through several email exchanges from July to September 2025, we have explored ways to ensure that these reports and stories are authentic reflections of community experiences and successes.
