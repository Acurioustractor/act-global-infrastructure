## Understanding — drafted
Phillip Orcher is a contemporary Indigenous Australian artist and researcher with deep roots in the Murrawarri and Gumbaynggirr Nations, hailing from Goodooga in Central West NSW. His work as an artist and researcher focuses on cultural heritage and co-design, aiming to bridge gaps between different cultures and promote understanding of Aboriginal traditions. Phillip is actively involved in various projects aimed at preserving Indigenous knowledge systems, such as 'BigaagARri', a cultural protocol project designed by him that serves the Gumbaynggirr community.

Phillip cares deeply about passing on his rich cultural heritage to future generations, particularly his own children whose mother has Filipino heritage. This responsibility drives much of his work in co-creating and preserving Indigenous knowledge systems, ensuring that traditional customs and protocols are not only remembered but also actively practiced and shared. His involvement at the University of Melbourne as a Research Associate underscores his commitment to translating cultural insights into practical applications that can benefit broader communities while honoring Aboriginal traditions.
