How the director loans work

1
Money in, before 30 June. Nick lends the Pty ~$75–100K as working capital, on a written loan note. This funds the ~$59K of window payments plus a buffer for the first month of next year.
2
Repayments out are tax-free. A loan repaid by the company is not income. So the first cash the company pays out later just repays Nick's loan, before anything is called salary or dividend. This is the clean extraction lane.
3
One account per founder. If only Nick seeds the company, only Nick holds that lane, so either Ben seeds too or the imbalance is squared in the Shareholders Agreement. Asset sales next year (the HiLux, tools, gear) also get credited here instead of paid in cash, which gives Ben a lane via the Knight Photography gear.
4
Next year, loans the other way carry a rule (Div 7A). If you draw more than your pay during FY27, it is recorded as a loan from the company to you. Any such loan still owing at the company's tax-return lodgement must be repaid, or put on formal terms (written agreement, benchmark interest, minimum yearly repayments), or the tax office treats it as taxable income. Plan: clear it, or formalise it, before lodgement.
