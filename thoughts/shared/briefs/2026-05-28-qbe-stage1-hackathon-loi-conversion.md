# Hackathon brief: warm pipeline → 3+ signed LOIs by 31 August 2026

## The challenge in one line

**How might we convert a pipeline of warm institutional relationships into 3+ signed Letters of Intent for matched capital by 31 August 2026?**

(Including buyers + philanthropic partners.)

## Why it matters

QBE Catalysing Impact 2026 Stage 2 (Sept 2026 deadline) requires legally-binding evidence of co-funding commitments. Goods on Country has strong warm relationships built over years and a draft LOI template, but nothing signed yet. The 31 August deadline is real and concrete. **A QBE-employee day at the right moment converts the pipeline.**

Without 3+ LOIs, the QBE application is materially weaker. With them, the matching-capital story is the strongest part of the pitch.

## What QBE volunteers bring that we don't have in-house

- B2B sales operations and pipeline cadence
- Contracting + commercial-terms structuring
- Credit / counterparty assessment
- Corporate-relationship management at scale

GOC has the relationships. The hackathon day adds the conversion discipline + commercial pressure-testing.

## The warm pipeline going into the hackathon

The day will work on the live pipeline. We'll share the full contact list privately with the volunteer team in the week before. Headline names:

**Institutional buyers (community-controlled):**
- Centrecorp Foundation — already bought 107 beds. Tranche-2 conversation live.
- NPY Women's Council (Ngaanyatjarra Pitjantjatjara Yankunytjatjara)
- Homeland Schools
- Several ACCHOs (Aboriginal community-controlled health organisations)

**Government + institutional procurement:**
- NT Housing — anchor opportunity ($4B remote-housing pipeline)
- NIAA (National Indigenous Australians Agency)

**Philanthropic / capital partners:**
- Identified prospects from V4 diagnostic + ongoing work (shared privately)

## What we walk in with

- Draft Letter of Intent template (legally reviewable, suitable for both buyer-LOIs and capital-partner letters of support)
- Per-relationship current-state notes (last touch, next step, who owns)
- /impact page with provenance-tagged metrics (verified / modelled / estimated / design / partial)
- Production cost model (refined via the Tailored Advisory engagement happening in parallel)
- Market Demand Research findings (delivered ~2 weeks before the hackathon by the Skilled Volunteering team)
- V4 Diagnostic from Mal + Matt

## What we want out of the day

Two outputs, both usable from day +1:

1. **A lightweight sales-operations playbook** — cadence, follow-up template, LOI delivery process, owner-mapping per deal, what "ready to sign" looks like for each segment (buyer vs philanthropic).

2. **At least one LOI tabletop-completed and ready to send within 5 business days of the hackathon** — picking the warmest deal (most likely Centrecorp tranche-2 or a confirmed philanthropic partner), running the conversation script, surfacing objections, refining the LOI, identifying the named human who will sign and what they need to see.

Stretch: 2 LOIs ready-to-send.
