# Receipt #1 — Mount Magnet, WA

**Title:** Mount Magnet (Wajarri Yamatji Country) has received $104,573 from every funding source we can find. Over five years. Across every entity in the LGA.
**Subtitle:** The community at the very top of A Curious Tractor's Funding Deserts Atlas, in one number.

**Section:** Receipts

There are 412 Local Government Areas (LGAs) in A Curious Tractor's Funding Deserts Atlas right now. The one at the top of the list is Mount Magnet, on Wajarri Yamatji Country in the Mid West of Western Australia.

Three indexed civic entities. SEIFA decile 1, the most disadvantaged classification we have. Very Remote. The Atlas's desert score is 185, the highest of any LGA in Australia.

Total recorded funding from every source we can see: **$104,573.**

That figure includes federal contracts, justice funding, philanthropic donations, foundation grants, ALMA-catalogued interventions, and NDIS flows. All of it. Across multiple years.

That is what a funding desert looks like when you put a single number on it. Not a lot of money is missing. Almost all of the money is missing.

## What is actually there

Mount Magnet Shire has roughly 450 residents, about half identifying as Aboriginal. The town sits between Geraldton and Meekatharra, on a road most Australians have never driven. There is a school. There is a hospital. There is a small museum. There is a community where people live actual lives. There has been ongoing Wajarri Yamatji native title work and cultural continuity for longer than the Australian state has existed.

What there is very little of, on the public record: civic infrastructure money. Three indexed entities means three legal organisations that show up in any of the data systems CivicGraph pulls from. The funding total is what reached them.

## What the number is not

$104,573 is not the total cost of services delivered to Mount Magnet residents over the period. State government provides health and education through other vehicles that do not show up in this dataset. Mining royalties move through structures CivicGraph does not see. Cash and in-kind support from extended family and Country is not in any registry.

The number is what shows up in the public funding stream: the part that gets announced, applied for, reported, audited, written into annual reports. The part where philanthropy and government performance their support of remote and Aboriginal community life. That part, in Mount Magnet, adds up to $104,573.

## The decision worth naming

If your foundation, fund, or agency has a remote-Australia or Aboriginal-self-determination line item, you can look up your own track record against Mount Magnet in the Atlas right now. The methodology is open. The data is public. The names are searchable.

You do not need a five-year strategy to fix this. You need one funded year of community-controlled work in one place, well, and a willingness to publish the result honestly.
