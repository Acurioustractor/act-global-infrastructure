## About page body (paste into Substack)

I'm Benjamin Knight. I co-founded A Curious Tractor (ACT) with Nicholas Marchesi.

This is where I write about the work. Not about me, not about how I feel about the sector, not about leadership lessons. About the work itself, who we're building it with, what we're learning, and what we're getting wrong.

ACT does a lot of different things on the surface. A community hub at Witta on Gubbi Gubbi Country (The Harvest). Place-based circular manufacturing with remote communities (Goods on Country). A national touring exhibition about youth detention (CONTAINED). Long-running partnerships with community-controlled organisations on Palm Island (Bwgcolman), in Mparntwe (Alice Springs), and in Mount Isa (Kalkadoon Country). Civic intelligence infrastructure for the social sector. Art that makes the arguments walkable.

What holds it together is one thesis. The work that needs to happen in civil society is not just better strategy or smarter agentic AI. It is the quiet, unglamorous, reliable work that should happen every time and currently does not. Intake. Consent. Referral. Follow-up. Audit. The kettle on the table. The Elder consulted before the publication. The follow-up email that actually goes out. The reflex layer that lets the human work (care, relationships, art, judgment) actually happen.

The most public expression of that thesis right now is a civic operating system with three layers:

**CivicGraph** sees the field. Where money flows, where it doesn't, who funds whom, where the deserts are, who lobbies and donates and contracts and never gets named together.

**JusticeHub** supports the work. The Australian Living Map of Alternatives (ALMA), the evidence map for community-led justice practice, the operational reflex layer that helps the work happen.

**Empathy Ledger** holds the trust. Consent, audit, AI-use ledger, human review. The accountability layer the other two call when they need to know what's permitted, what's been recorded, what's been touched by AI.

The same posture runs through The Harvest, Goods on Country, the partner-organisation work, and the art. Anywhere ACT puts its name, it's the same posture: build quiet infrastructure that helps community-led work hold, then hand it over.

Field Notes runs in four streams. Subscribe to whichever ones fit:

- **What Communities Already Know.** Long-form essays. Monthly. The argument, the synthesis, what the work is teaching us across communities, places, and the infrastructure that holds it all together.
- **Field Notes from Elsewhere.** Dispatches from the road. Late June through early August 2026 I'm visiting five communities in Africa and Europe that have figured out something Australian philanthropy and government haven't. One post per stop while I'm there, then quiet.
- **State of Civic Money.** Quarterly. Drawn directly from CivicGraph. The funding deserts, the revolving doors, the patterns that get smoothed over in annual reports.
- **Receipts.** Short observations from across the work, the data, the places, the partners, the practice. Weekly when I'm in country. One chart, one number, one story, one decision worth naming.

One promise. Everything ACT publishes here is openly readable. Funders who commission deeper work from us accept up front that we publish the truth either way. That rule filters customers. The ones it filters out were never going to fund the work ACT exists to do.

You can subscribe to any combination of the four streams. Reply to anything that lands. If you're working on something near this and want to talk, I read everything.
