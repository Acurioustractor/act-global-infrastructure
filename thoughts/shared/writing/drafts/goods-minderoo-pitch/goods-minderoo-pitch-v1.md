# Goods on Country

One hand picks. Another writes the order. The path between is not built.

We are building it.

---

## Executive summary

Minderoo has spent a decade building the supply side of First Nations enterprise. Generation One funds the employers. Dream Venture funds the founders. The Indigenous Employment Index measures the parity. Backing Black Business called for an Indigenous-led patient capital fund. First Australians Capital is that fund. QBE put $500,000 into it in April 2024.

The supply side is now funded. The demand side is not.

Goods on Country is that demand-side infrastructure. Australian buyers. First Nations suppliers. A contracted flow of trade between them. The work is measured on the employment parity frame Minderoo commissioned Murawin to build.

The pipeline is not a plan. It is live. $767,000 of trade sits in the register. Seeded from real Xero data. Anchor buyers are named. Suppliers are onboarded. An agent layer runs the reconciliation, the cadence, the invoice drift, the narrative review, nightly.

QBE Foundation named A Curious Tractor in its 2026 Catalysing Impact cohort on 23 March. The Stage 2 grant of up to $400,000 is designed to unlock matched capital. We are inviting Minderoo to be the matched side.

A parallel envelope reached your desk on 1 May. JusticeHub. Community justice infrastructure, same anchor-portfolio method, different market. Two asks, one logic. ACT curates. The funder catalyses. The work carries.

The ask is $900,000 over three years, tapered. The deliverables are an evaluated cohort of anchor buyers, a measured parity outcome, a quarterly acquittal on the Woort Koorliny frame. The full pack follows.

## 1. The problem

The Front Project's 2024 report, commissioned with Minderoo's support, puts the bill at $22.3 billion a year. That is what Australian governments spend on late intervention for children and young people under twenty-four. Child protection. Youth justice. Youth unemployment. Homelessness. Mental health. Family violence. Up from $15 billion five years ago.

The report maps nine domains of late intervention against six wellbeing preconditions that should have prevented the cost. Eight of the nine fail the same precondition. The one the report calls "Participating."

Participating means work. Work and study. The thing the economy was meant to supply.

The Northern Territory costs $2,808 per person per year in late intervention, against $921 in Queensland. The report attributes the difference to remoteness, workforce shortage, and what it calls "levels of Indigeneity." Those are the same three descriptors that identify the communities First Nations enterprise would employ.

The report diagnoses the gap. It does not prescribe the fix.

The Indigenous Procurement Policy operates at Commonwealth level. Mid-market buyers, which is where the trade volume sits, have no equivalent infrastructure. Supply-side capital and capability programs are well funded. Demand-side infrastructure is not.

Goods on Country is the demand-side infrastructure.

## 2. The work

Goods on Country is a CRM, a pipeline, and an agent layer built on live trade data.

The CRM holds the buyers. Twelve stages, from first contact to acquittal. A funder cadence agent nudges any contact dormant longer than fourteen days. A procurement analyst agent surfaces seasonal buying patterns from the Xero history. An invoice drift detector catches any invoice that sits in draft more than three days. A Xero-to-GHL reconciler runs nightly and flags the contact records that have drifted from the financial truth. A narrative gatekeeper reads every outgoing draft for voice and tells us when it sounds like an institution.

The pipeline holds the suppliers. Named, certified where Supply Nation certifies them, vetted where they are not. The register shows which supplier can deliver which order and on what lead time.

The agent layer is not experimental. It runs on PM2 on a daily and weekly schedule. Each agent writes its drafts and cost into a ledger that the team reviews.

The operational question is one line: what does it cost to route a dollar of trade. We track it. It is falling.

Goods on Country does not compete with First Australians Capital or Dream Venture. FAC supplies the capital. Dream Venture supplies the capability. Supply Nation supplies the certification. Murawin supplies the measurement frame. Goods on Country supplies the trade volume that makes all of them pay back.

## 5. The ask

We ask Minderoo for $900,000 across three years.

| Year | Amount | What it funds | Deliverable |
|------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | $400,000 | Scale the pipeline through 30 anchor buyers and 12 First Nations supplier relationships | Closed trade above $2m. Evaluation baseline set with Murawin. |
| 2 | $300,000 | Operate and deepen. Invest in evaluation and systems hardening. | Closed trade above $4m. First parity outcome reported on Woort Koorliny frame. |
| 3 | $200,000 | Sustain and transfer. Prepare the model for other matched funders. | Closed trade above $6m. Published case study and replication playbook. |

The three-year taper is deliberate. The trade volume is the outcome, not the grant. If the pipeline scales as modelled, Minderoo's annual share falls while the work grows. The money leaves the system. The system stays.

Alongside Minderoo's $900,000 sits the QBE Catalysing Impact grant of up to $400,000 and the matched third-party capital the Stage 2 mechanic is designed to unlock. On the 2025 cohort's leverage multiple of 2.7x, the total capital stack is approximately $3.4 million against Minderoo's $900,000.

Reporting is quarterly against the Demand Register signals closed and the trade volume completed. The annual parity outcome is reported on the Woort Koorliny Indigenous Employment Index frame, scored independently.

**The money flow.** Minderoo grants to A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd. The Pty operates the pipeline. The Pty reports quarterly and annually. One grant, one line.

## Appendix D. Data and systems protocol

The Goods stack sits inside the ACT command centre. Three layers.

**Layer 1: the CRM.** Twelve-stage Buyer Pipeline in GoHighLevel. Every opportunity has a named contact, a monetary value, a stage, and a project code. 119 opportunities sit in the pipeline today. Each opportunity links to its Xero invoice on the payment side.

**Layer 2: the Demand Register.** Signals are curated from community requests, asset-end-of-life flags, and procurement analyst reports. 100 signals with a total identified demand of $16,006,450. Each signal carries a community identifier, product type, and unit quantity.

**Layer 3: the agent layer.** Five agents run on a PM2 schedule.

| Agent | Schedule | Role |
|-------|----------|------|
| Xero-to-GHL reconciler | daily 05:00 AEST | Flags contact records that have drifted from Xero financial truth |
| Funder cadence | daily 06:00 AEST | Nudges any dormant buyer contact past fourteen days |
| Procurement analyst | Monday 08:00 AEST | Surfaces seasonal buying patterns and product-fit signals from Xero history |
| Invoice drift detector | Monday 08:30 AEST | Catches any invoice that sits in draft more than three days |
| Narrative gatekeeper | ad-hoc | Reads outgoing drafts for voice |

Every agent call is logged to the `llm_usage` ledger with input tokens, output tokens, estimated cost, and latency. Agents early-exit when they find nothing. In the preceding thirty days, the full stack spent $0.19 across nine calls.

**What Minderoo sees quarterly:** signals closed, trade routed, employment count, cost-per-dollar-routed, agent ledger snapshot. Delivered as a single PDF and data extract.

**What stays inside ACT:** individual buyer contact data, supplier commercial terms, and community-level narratives held under the OCAP governance protocol.
