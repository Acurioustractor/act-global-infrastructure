# A pitch to Minderoo Foundation

Lucy,

The debate is settled. The question is political will.

Minderoo Foundation has named the cost. The Royal Commission named the cost. Every report since has named the cost. The evidence base on what works for First Nations young people in contact with the justice system is, at this point, overwhelming. What is missing is not more research. What is missing is the political courage to act on what the research has been saying for thirty years.

This is what JusticeHub is for. We are not building a new evidence base. We are forcing the existing evidence into rooms where decision-makers can no longer pretend they have not seen it.

## What we are asking for

The ask is $2.9M over 3 years. Year 1 is $780K (per the canonical Three Circles doc, wiki/projects/justicehub/three-circles.md).

The money funds three things. First, the anchor-community hold across the ten communities Minderoo has already invested adjacent to. Second, the evidence engine that every detention reform debate keeps citing but no funder has yet underwritten. Third, the public artefact (The Brave Ones) that puts the human trace on the political-will conversation.

## Closing

A 30-minute call by 14 May 2026 to walk through the staged entry. The work is past the evidence stage. The work is now political.

Best,
Ben Knight
