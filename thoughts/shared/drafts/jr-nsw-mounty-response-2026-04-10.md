## On the date change request — important context

Daniel is asking to date the invoice **1 March 2026** instead of 11 December 2025. Here's the impact:

**On JR NSW side:** moves the expense from Q2 (Oct-Dec) to Q3 (Jan-Mar). They want this so they don't have to redo their previous quarter BAS.

**On ACT side:** moves the GST collected ($2,500) from Q2 FY26 to Q3 FY26.
- ACT's Q2 FY26 BAS is **not yet lodged** (still in the April reconciliation runbook). So we CAN make the change before lodging.
- However: shifting $2,500 GST from Q2 to Q3 reduces ACT's Q2 G1 (sales) by $25,000 and increases Q3 G1 by the same. Q2 BAS will show lower sales, Q3 will show higher.
- ACT's Q2 GST collected drops by $2,500 → Q2 BAS payable is $2,500 less.
- ACT's Q3 GST collected rises by $2,500 → Q3 BAS payable is $2,500 more.
- Net cash effect: nothing (just a 3-month timing shift on when the GST is paid).

**Verdict:** the date change is cost-neutral for ACT and saves JR NSW the rework. **Just do it.** Edit the invoice in Xero before Nic finalises Q2 BAS lodgement.

The only catch: if the invoice gets dated 1 March 2026, Daniel's finance team should not also expect the asset to land on their register as of December 2025. The container was actually delivered/in service from December 2025. There's a small disconnect between invoice date and service date, which is normal but worth noting in passing.
