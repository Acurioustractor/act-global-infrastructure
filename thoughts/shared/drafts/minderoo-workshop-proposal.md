# A proposal for how we pick the ten, together

*For Lucy Stronach · Minderoo Foundation*

---

## The honest answer up front

Most funding applications say *"we have ten anchor communities."* Most are inflated. The honest answer from ACT: **four anchors confirmed today with verbal consent and stories flowing, fifty candidates shortlisted from a queryable data spine, and three or four final anchors selected together with Minderoo.**

The four already in relationship:

- **Oonchiumpa** (Mparntwe, NT) — Kristy Bloomfield + Tanya Turner. 95% diversion, 97.6% less cost than detention. 17 April Judges on Country host (hosted).
- **Palm Island Community Company** (Bwgcolman, QLD) — Rachael Atkinson, CEO. $29M annual turnover. Elder governance.
- **BG Fit** (Mount Isa, QLD) — Brodie Germaine. Tuesday gym + on-Country bush camps + Doomadgee 500km remote quarterly.
- **Mounty Yarns** (Mt Druitt, NSW) — Daniel Daylight, CEO. Youth-led advocacy. 13 storytellers, nine young people at the table.

The remaining four seats are not yet filled because the right way to fill them is not by us alone.

## The shortlist already exists

ACT's data spine — 1,155 verified youth-justice interventions in ALMA, 100,036 entity-resolved organisations in CivicGraph, 71,000 justice-funding rows — is live today and queryable. From it we have shortlisted **50 community-controlled Indigenous organisations** scored against a transparent portfolio model (30% evidence strength, 25% community authority, 15% × 3 for harm risk, implementation capability, option value). Every state and territory is represented. No organisation appears without named cultural authority.

This shortlist is live in the envelope's digital companion at:

**`act-global-infrastructure.vercel.app/minderoo-live-dashboard.html`**

Credentials enclosed separately. Filter, sort, scroll.

## The workshop we propose

**Two ninety-minute sessions.**

### Session 1 — shortlist walk-through

Together we walk through the 50. You flag communities Minderoo already has relationships with. We narrow to roughly 25 based on state coverage + evidence + cultural specificity + known fit with your portfolio.

### In between — our relational work (two weeks)

For each of the 25, ACT reaches out to confirm interest, verify evidence, gather cultural context. Only communities that actively say *"yes, put us forward to Minderoo"* continue to Session 2. Roughly 15–20 survive the consent check.

### Session 2 — community-confirmed selection

Together we choose the final three or four from the 15–20. Each choice is logged with rationale. The envelope's *"ten"* becomes the actual ten, each a real relationship.

## What Minderoo decides

You decide which communities' work resonates most with your 2026–28 priorities. You decide state coverage. You decide the balance between proven and emerging. You decide nothing about whether a community participates — that is the community's call.

## What ACT commits to

We commit to never naming a community in a Minderoo-facing document without that community's explicit consent. We commit to the relational work of outreach. We commit to transparent rationale on every selection. We commit to the live dashboard staying honest about what is confirmed, what is in conversation, and what is in selection.

We commit to this because it is what the method is.

## Why this matters

The pitch's claim is that ACT holds data, voice, and relational work together. The simplest way to prove the claim is to let you see it in motion. The workshop is not a pitch mechanic — it is Year 1 of the program done in front of you, before funding.

If the method is correct, the workshop will be the moment you decide whether to fund. If the method is not correct, the workshop will be where that becomes visible before any community is named publicly.

## Timing
