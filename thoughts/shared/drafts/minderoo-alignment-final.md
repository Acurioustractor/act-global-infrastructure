## The one sentence that writes the whole envelope

**"Over half of children in custody in Victoria have been subject to a child protection order."** (COLI24 p. 3)

This is THE bridge sentence. It says the $10.2B (child protection) and the $3.4B (youth crime) are two points on the same trajectory. They are the same kids, five years apart.

**The Three Circles' anchor communities are exactly the community hold that prevents the transition from child protection to youth detention.**

Every confirmed anchor has direct delivery in that transition zone:
- **PICC**, YOSS (Youth Offender Support Service) grown 3× since 2021, serves exactly the post-protection pre-justice cohort
- **Oonchiumpa**, 95% diversion rate; young people who would otherwise appear in 5 years in the Victoria stat never get to that sentence
- **BG Fit**, Tuesday gyms + bush camps for ages 10–17, the pre-detention age band
- **Mounty Yarns**, Shayle McKellar completed HSC in custody, now a lived-experience consultant. Literally walks kids back from that pipeline
- **MMEIC**, Elders-in-Council cultural governance holding children before the system touches them

**We are upstream of $10.2B AND upstream of $3.4B, because they're the same kids.**

## The vertical fiscal imbalance, Minderoo's own wedge

COLI24 p. 11 says it directly:

> *"Incentives to invest in early intervention are reduced when benefits do not accrue to the same portfolios or levels of government... States and territories overwhelmingly bear the cost of late intervention in the short-term. Direct Commonwealth expenditure is generally limited to welfare expenditure and aspects of healthcare... Commonwealth benefits in the long run from early intervention through increased tax revenue and reduced reliance on benefits."*

**States pay $19.24B (87%). Commonwealth pays $3.05B (13%). Commonwealth benefits long-term. Neither is incentivised.**

This is philanthropy's job. Specifically Minderoo's. The envelope explicitly names:

*"The Three Circles is exactly the upstream work that the vertical fiscal imbalance prevents either level of government from funding at scale. Minderoo bridges the gap. Three years of sustained partnership infrastructure catalyses what neither state nor Commonwealth will fund alone."*

## The NT-specific hook for Oonchiumpa

NT spends $2,808 per capita on late intervention, nearly 3× SA's $1,011 (COLI24 p. 20). 33% of NT's population is 0–24 (highest youth share in the country · COLI24 p. 22). Youth detention cost is high; youth offender rate *rose* marginally in NT (the only state-level rise alongside ACT) (COLI24 p. 19).

Oonchiumpa operates inside this specific number. Every dollar spent on Oonchiumpa's on-Country practice is:
- Direct substitution for the $2,808 NT per-capita spend
- Pre-protection intervention that prevents the Victoria child-protection → youth-detention pipeline
- 97.6% cheaper than detention (Oonchiumpa's own figure) against Victoria's $7,775/day benchmark

**We don't need to argue ROI. Minderoo's own numbers make the ROI case for us.**

## Scale the ratio, ROI projection

Per COLI24 p. 5: early intervention delivers **$2 for every $1 invested** across multiple programs (PWC 2019, SVA 2019, Accenture 2022). The ratio is conservative, published, Minderoo-cited.

Apply to The Three Circles:
- **$2.9M invested → minimum $5.8M forward cost saving** (per published ROI)
- **$66.9B of 3-year COLI → we only need to shift 0.009% of the trajectory** to break even
- 5–8 anchor communities, each holding ~20–50 young people per year per anchor, = ~500–1,200 young people over 3 years
- Cost per young person at Victoria detention benchmark ($7,775/day × 365 = $2.8M/year per child), **we prevent ONE Victoria-detention year across the cohort and we already return the entire $2.9M ask**

This math is all COLI24's. We cite, we don't claim.
