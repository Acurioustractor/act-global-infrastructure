## Oonchiumpa (Mparntwe)

### Kristy Bloomfield — Elder, Traditional Owner (Bloomfield/Liddle)
> "That's what happens when you lead with culture instead of compliance."

*Supporting context:*
> "Last month we took eight young people out to Atnarpa for three days on Country. Three of those eight have gone back to school. Two got jobs. All of them are off the police radar."

### Henry Bloomfield — Elder (Ross River)
> "I grew up between here and Ross River as a kid. It's good to be back home. To grow my kids up here."

### Tanya Turner — Eastern/Central Australian Aboriginal, traditional owner
> "Our young people need to know they are valued. Not by the system, but by their own mob."

### Braydon Dema — young person, Ross River
> "We're here with 55 judges, showing them how we live. Pops country."

### Nigel Alice — young person, Alice Springs
> "When I'm talking to the judge, I feel like I'm panicking."

*Supporting context:*
> "Going away from my family and stuff. Kids like fighting every day there."

### Jackquann — young person, Alice Springs
> "Detention. That's not my home."

*Supporting context on what works:*
> "Not locking kids up. Programs. Bush tucker. Do some art."

### Laquisha — young person, 16, Alice Springs
> "It would've been better hanging around with family instead of sitting in your cell by yourself. Twelve-minute calls. Then two hours waiting for the next one."

*One-word answer when asked what causes offending:*
> "Oppression."
