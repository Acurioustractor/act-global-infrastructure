# Judges on Country — 17 April 2026 field record

> Fifty-five judges and magistrates walked onto Eastern Arrernte Country on Friday 17 April 2026 (trip completed). The trip must do three jobs at once: honour the community's hosting, produce the envelope's strongest artefacts, and avoid any extractive behaviour that would break Kristy and Tanya's trust. This doc is the field reference for the day.

---

## The three non-negotiables

1. **Kristy and Tanya lead the day.** Capture crew follows their direction, not a pre-set shot list. If they ask the camera to stop, it stops. No exceptions.
2. **Every young person featured is asked in the moment, even though org-level consent is already given.** Org approval is not the same as one particular kid saying *"yes, film me."* Ask each time.
3. **No filming of sacred material, ceremony, or anything an elder signals should not be captured.** If unsure, don't record. The content in the envelope cannot include anything culturally sensitive that didn't get explicit go-ahead.

## Part 1 — The 90-second film

**What it does emotionally:** refuses to explain. Shows the day. Lets the silence sit. Ends on Tanya's line about young people being collateral. 30 seconds of that is worth 10 pages of proposal prose.

### Rough edit structure (cut Wed 22 – Fri 24 April, before envelope print)

| Seconds | Content | Capture requirement |
|---|---|---|
| 0–8 | Country wide shot. Atnarpa landscape. No voiceover. One natural sound — wind, fire, birds. | Morning or late-afternoon shot for the warm light. Drone if available, handheld if not. |
| 8–20 | Kristy on Country with the judges in shot. Welcome to Country from Aunty Bev or Uncle Terry if they give permission. Can be silent B-roll over Country audio. | Always frame the judges as LEARNERS in the shot, not central. Kristy's positioning matters. |
| 20–38 | A young person on Country (one of the 14–16-year-old Oonchiumpa cohort if any attend, or cutaway to Atnarpa stills from prior trips). Natural activity — yarning round the fire, bush walk, working with an elder. | Fresh verbal consent in the moment. Face visible if young person OK'd; otherwise frame from behind, hands, or silhouette. |
| 38–55 | A judge asking a real question OR a storyteller responding. Capture several on the day — best one goes in the cut. | Ask the bench before you start filming if any are happy being on camera. Those who decline, never point the lens at. |
| 55–75 | The data moment — one of the numbers made visible. Kristy or Tanya naming *"95% diversion, 97.6% less than detention"* in their own words on Country, not as a slide. | One clean take of either of them delivering this. Prep Kristy + Tanya the night before that the line is coming up. |
| 75–90 | End card: Tanya's recorded quote from the long-form piece: *"Our young people are just collateral in a bigger issue. The issue doesn't sit with them. It sits on a much broader level and with adults, not with children."* Text on Country imagery, held silence. | If Tanya delivers this live on the day, use the live take. Otherwise pull from the already-consented EL v2 recording. |

## Part 5 — Cultural safety + judges handling

**Do:**
- Let Kristy and Tanya run the sequencing
- Let elders speak first and last where they choose
- Frame everything as learning, not delivering
- Ask before EVERY new filming context, not just once at the start of the day
- Leave capture gear in the car during any explicitly ceremonial moment

**Don't:**
- Film sacred sites — if unsure about a location, ask
- Film any judge or young person who hasn't given explicit same-day consent
- Direct Kristy or Tanya's positioning in the frame — follow their lead, don't pose them
- Talk over elders when they're speaking, even between filming
- Import a city-media aesthetic to Country — the film should feel like the place, not a promo

**Judges specifically:**
- Brief crew that judges may NOT want to be filmed even if their institution is happy. Default: ask first, assume no until a specific judge agrees on camera.
- At least one High Court judge is attending. If media presence is sensitive, Kristy or Tanya will flag on arrival — check with them before ANY filming of the bench.
