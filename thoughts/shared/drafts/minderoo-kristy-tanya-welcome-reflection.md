# What this welcome is, and what the envelope does with it

The welcome is not a prelude to the program. **It IS the program.** Tanya's 20 minutes to the 55 judges says more about The Three Circles, in Oonchiumpa's own voice, than 14 months of ACT drafting has managed.

Below: the nine sentences that rewrite the envelope, the structural lesson the welcome teaches, and concrete changes to the canonical pitch.

---

## The nine sentences that now carry the envelope

### 1. The foundation sentence

> *"In Trumper Service Response is not an add-on to the justice system. It's a cultural foundation that justice should stand on in this region."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This is the thesis of the whole pitch in one sentence.** The Three Circles is not asking Minderoo to fund a new program. It is asking Minderoo to resource the cultural foundation that already exists, that the justice system has been standing on without paying for, for thirty thousand years.

### 2. The devastating truth about the system

> *"You hear people say the system is broken. From where we stand, the truth is even harder. The system is working exactly as it was built. It just wasn't built for us."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This reframes the COLI24 $22.3B argument entirely.** Minderoo's 2025 report says the late-intervention spend is rising because the system is failing. Tanya says the system is *succeeding at what it was designed to do* — which is why community-led alternatives are not optional reforms, they are structural replacements.

### 3. The ASR — Alternative Service Response, Oonchiumpa's four pillars

> *"Our alternative service response... has four pillars: cultural reconnection. Intensive family support. Realistic flexible case management. Bridging two worlds."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This is COLI24's "wrap-around models" recommendation, named from the source.** COLI24 (p. ii) says *invest in wrap-around models*. Tanya names what they are. Four pillars, sourced from cultural logic not service-design theory.

### 4. What the pillars mean in practice

> *"Our team doesn't sit behind a desk... We are country in the community in the late night spaces where young people actually move. We respond to crises early and prevent escalation — because if you meet a young person at a time that works for them, you prevent half the things that bring them to court in the first place."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This is the operating practice translated.** "Late-night spaces, where young people actually move" is the single best one-sentence description of what The Three Circles funds that isn't funded anywhere else.

### 5. The culture-as-backbone argument

> *"Culture isn't an optional extra for Aboriginal young people. Culture is the backbone that teaches responsibility, respect, behavior, identity, and belonging. Where culture is strong, kids do well. Where culture is eroded, everything else becomes fragile."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This is why the child-protection → youth-detention pipeline exists.** Culture erosion is the upstream variable. The COLI24 $10.2B + $3.4B are downstream measures of the same broken upstream. The anchors hold the upstream.

### 6. The offence reframed

> *"When we talk about offending, we are often talking about kids who are simply surviving."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This one line dissolves the entire "youth crime" framing.** COLI24 has youth crime as the #2 line item ($3.4B). Tanya says the kids on that ledger line are, statistically, survivors. Not a policy argument — a reality correction.

### 7. The ask to the judges (and to Minderoo)

> *"When a judge believes in a young person, even quietly, they feel it."*
> — Tanya Turner

**Translate to Minderoo:** when a funder believes in an anchor community, even quietly, they feel it. Endurance and accountability, Hartman's phrase, rendered as relational truth. Three years of Minderoo belief is three years of the anchor communities feeling it. That feeling is the asset.

### 8. The origin of justice in this place

> *"No Aboriginal person in this region is born dangerous or hopeless or broken. They're born into layers of history that most Australians never have to face."*
> — Tanya Turner

**This is the sentence for the cover letter.** It is the one-line rebuttal to every "youth crime" headline that will run in the next three years. The envelope carries this sentence to Minderoo as a gift.

### 9. The operating philosophy

> *"Two cultures, one world working together."*
> — Tanya Turner / Oonchiumpa

**This is more precise than Minderoo's "partner-led."** Partnership implies two entities. "Two cultures, one world" implies there is no "other" — only one continent, two ways of knowing, working together. ACT has no better tagline than this.

## The structural lesson the welcome teaches

The welcome has a pattern the envelope should learn:

1. **Open with country** — Kristy names the 1999 Federal Court native title determination at Ilkerrmpa. Names the caterpillar dreaming. Names the language. Before any argument about policy.

2. **Invoke lineage** — Nana Bessie Little's butterfly dance passed to the five nieces. Authority travels through generations. Kristy's grandfather stolen; Kristy's nieces dancing the inheritance.

3. **Name the welcome as protocol, not politeness** — "Welcome ceremonies are a cultural safeguard, grounded in respect, relationship and responsibility." The welcome is a *spiritual-safety protocol*. Permission to walk.

4. **Name the two truths side-by-side** — Central Australia was colonised 100 years after the East coast. Language, kinship, ceremony remain incredibly strong. Simultaneously, education and infrastructure arrived late. **Both truths sit side by side.** This is the ACCO reality. The envelope should echo this "both truths side by side" rhetorical move.

5. **Move from past to practice** — the ASR's four pillars are then grounded in *exactly* the reality the two truths describe. The solution is not a theoretical framework; it is cultural logic applied to lived reality.

6. **Ask specific things of the listener** — patience, flexibility, stability, belief. Small verbs. Each with a practical meaning. The envelope should ask Minderoo for the *specific* small verbs, not grand commitments.

7. **End by bringing it back to country and relationship** — "Justice in Central Australia cannot be delivered from afar. It can only be shaped by those who choose to understand this place."

**This is the shape of every effective Three Circles artefact, period.** Country → lineage → protocol → two truths → practice → ask → return to country.

## Concrete changes to the canonical pitch

### Page 1 — replace the opening with the welcome

Move from COLI24 opening to Tanya's welcome as the opening. COLI24 framing moves to Page 3 (it remains load-bearing, but the pitch enters through Country, not through dollars).

Proposed new Page 1:

> ***"When you stand here in Mparntwe, you are not just standing in a town. You're standing in one of the oldest cultural landscapes in the world, and the Arrernte word for this place isn't just a name — it's a story, it's a responsibility."***
> — Tanya Turner, Eastern Arrernte woman, welcoming 55 judges and magistrates to Mparntwe, 20 April 2026.
>
> This envelope begins where the program begins: on Country, with permission, under cultural authority.

### Page 2 — the Alternative Service Response (ASR) as the method

Replace the current "Oonchiumpa" page with the four-pillars framing, in Tanya's exact words. Call the page: **"The Alternative Service Response — cultural foundation that justice stands on."** Frame it as: *this already exists; Minderoo funds its endurance.*

### Page 3 — the two truths side-by-side

A new page. Left column: *"Language, kinship systems, ceremony, and cultural laws remain incredibly strong."* Right column: *"Education and infrastructure arrived much later and often abruptly."* Bottom: *"Both truths sit side by side and shape the challenges and strengths of Aboriginal people in Central Australia today."* This is the honest frame COLI24 doesn't have the standing to say. The anchors do.

### Page 4 — the system sentence

A dedicated full-page spread of *"The system is working exactly as it was built. It just wasn't built for us."* Nothing else on the page but that sentence and attribution. Page turn. The reader absorbs it.

Then COLI24 follows on Page 5 as evidence of what the system was built to produce.

### Page 10 — the bow, now anchored in Tanya's close

Replace current bow framing with:

> ***"Carry the truth of this place back with you into your courtrooms, your decisions, your leadership, and your hearts."***
> — Tanya Turner

Recontextualise for Minderoo: *carry the truth of this place back into your portfolio, your board paper, your distribution strategy, and your heart.*

### Throughout — replace "partnership" with "two cultures, one world working together"

Wherever the pitch says "partner-led" or "partnership infrastructure", add alongside: *two cultures, one world working together (Oonchiumpa).* Let Minderoo's vocabulary and Oonchiumpa's run alongside each other.
