## 5 · The theory of work

- We work in a loop: **Listen → Curiosity → Action → Art**, and Art returns to Listen. It is the founders' lived practice externalised, not an imported framework.
- Every dollar gets two tags: project and LCAA phase. The ratios are drift signals. Listen under 20%: we stopped sitting in rooms before building. Art under 10%: the work has gone quiet. Action over 50%: we are scaling, not seeding.
- Every dollar lands in one of four lanes: **To Us · To Down · To Grow · To Others**. To Us is method, not tax: custodianship only works if the custodians can keep showing up.
- The lanes are where money lands. Where the founders' time lands is the 2026-04 founder-lanes decision (section 3). Never collapse the two.
- ACT earns two ways and refuses to pretend it is one. Engine one: philanthropic partnership funds the build. Engine two: our own commercial revenue (Voice, Flow, Ground). The pile mix is the dial; the next five years move it until the platforms earn. Live sources: Grant Tranches DB + GHL opportunities (grants) · Minderoo and QBE one-liners with dates (philanthropy in flight) · the Harvest Monday sweep takings line (corporate revenue).
- The filter on engine two: every layer publishes ungated public-good outputs. We sell the Practice, never the Atlas.
- The community is not an audience. Two lanes, never one funnel: the supporter **orbit** (warmth rings, a hand-picked core, a ladder earned through realised gives) and the community **constellation** (sovereign contributors, measured by what ACT owes back, never laddered, never dripped, worked by hand). Elder authority is a veto, not a preference.
- Tend the field, and leave the field to be replenished. You cannot tend from an empty well.
- We build with community, transfer capability, and get unhitched. The handover test, all five: can they run it without us, modify it, export their data, own the decisions, and is there a handover plan. If we cannot hand it over, we are still in Curiosity.
- Applied to money: standalone P&Ls, books readable by others, founders never the bottleneck. Built to be handed back to the work, not inherited.
- SOPs accrete where the work happens: Denis drafts at the Harvest hub, the weekly 1:1 harvests them, stable ones graduate to wiki runbooks. That is the literal answer to "what's our SOP".

## 6 · The horizon arc

Lock policy: the 5-year line and the 30-year sentence are locked; 10 and 20 are proposed and corrected quarterly. Each weekly move is tagged to one of these lines.

**~5 years (2031).** The engines pay for themselves. The five-year model runs FY27 $2.6M toward FY31 $5.5M+: Empathy Ledger licensing real (SMART Recovery the first licence, ~15 customers in the model), the first JusticeHub node self-funding on commissioned Practice revenue (proposed), Harvest holding itself in its own Pty, Goods fully The Butterfly Movement's, founder pay boring at $120K each plus a year-end settle decided on actuals. CivicGraph is commercially active and not for sale (the 2026-05-25 ADR governs; Division 152 positioning quietly preserved). What must be true: FY26 and FY27 R&D claimed; one platform passes all five handover questions. **The founders stop:** rescue ops, unpaid bespoke support, funder reports they do not believe.

**~10 years (2036).** The network runs on its own revenue. JusticeHub supports nodes across Australia that earn their own keep; the node structure (federated community-owned entities vs one platform with local partners) is an open joint decision. Empathy Ledger is the default storytelling-consent infrastructure in Australia: open source, forkable, consent vocabulary set per community. Goods is a community-owned facility network run by Butterfly. What must be true: every node and licensee passes the handover test; ACT has shifted from operator to steward. **The founders stop:** running platform delivery.

**~20 years (2046).** The model is no longer ours. Empathy Ledger operates at world scale through forks and licences ACT does not control; the ACT model is replicated by people who never asked permission, which is the point. PICC is the precedent already in the books. ACT is small again: a studio, not a holding company. What must be true: most of today's map handed over or sunset. **The founders stop:** holding decision rights.

**~30 years (2056).** "ACT? We don't need them anymore," and it is simply true everywhere, JusticeHub's national nodes included. What remains: the records, the stories with consent trails intact, and two founders freed for the experiences they want through art and storytelling. The picture came before the platform; the gesture came before the van. The standing art test, checked quarterly: Art holds at or above 10% of spend without forcing (today it is ~$227 of tagged spend, the drift light is on), and at least one piece a year exists that ACT could not invoice anyone for. **The founders stop:** everything except listen, stay curious, make, hand it over.
