# Minderoo pitch — narrative arc (draft 1)

Generated 2026-04-22. Evidence: `data/pitch/*.json` + `transcript-map.txt`.
Voice rules: Curtis method, no em dashes, no AI tells, abstract nouns load late.
Length target: four beats. Each beat is the claim, the body, the room, the stop.

The pitch is an argument, not a parade. Minderoo already sees parades.
What they have not seen is four communities, five hundred storytellers, one
consented corpus, producing the same four-note spine without coordination.
That is the argument. Everything below exists to make that argument
unfalsifiable.

---

## Beat 1 — Identity (the room: who is already doing the work)

Kristy Bloomfield took eight young people to Atnarpa for three days on
Country. Not a program. A month ago. The Bloomfield and Liddle families have
been doing this on Love's Creek station since before the words youth
diversion entered the funding vocabulary. The evidence sits in the Empathy
Ledger now because Kristy agreed it should, not because a consultant
extracted it.

The pattern repeats. At Palm Island, Uncle Frank Daniel Landers, Aunty Ethel
Robertson, the Elders Group — seventeen transcripts between them, one
shared claim: we know what works here, we have always known, and the
knowledge is intergenerational before it is anything else.

**Name the body.** Kristy. Uncle Frank. Aunty Ethel.
**Name the room.** Country, not clinic.
**Load the noun.** Identity is not a theme; it is the prior condition.
**Stop the line.**

## Beat 2 — Community (the proof that this is not anecdote)

Across Oonchiumpa, Palm Island Community Company, BG Fit, Orange Sky and
Young Guns, identity is named by ten different storytellers in ten
different rooms. Community, by eight. Resilience, by seven. Healing, by
seven. This is five organisations, four nations, three thousand kilometres
of separation, one consented corpus, no shared playbook.

The four-note spine did not emerge because someone wrote it into a grant.
It emerged because the storytellers named it themselves, independently,
and the AI analysis could not avoid finding it. We did not load the question.
The corpus answered the question we were not asking.

**Name the body.** Five hundred storytellers. Five organisations.
**Name the room.** Mparntwe, Bwgcolman, Mount Isa, the van, the prison.
**Load the noun.** Community is not a virtue; it is a measurement.
**Stop the line.**

## Beat 3 — Resilience (the counter-evidence the pitch needs to name)

Brodie Germaine at BG Fit trains young people in Mount Isa and Doomadgee
who the system has already written off. The transcripts say so. So do the
re-offending rates the system records against its own interventions. Henry
Bloomfield, sixty-seven, watched his family homestead restored and calls it
coming home. The same weekend a Palm Island Elder named the three decades of
government control she had already survived.

Resilience is the word funders reach for when they do not want to name
what it is resilience against. We will name it. Section headings will say
over-policing, forced removals, NT intervention, Queensland child
protection. The bodies in the corpus have already named them; pretending
otherwise breaks the consent.

**Name the body.** Brodie. Henry. An Elder whose name Ben confirms before
this sentence leaves the draft.
**Name the room.** The station. The island. The youth detention wing.
**Load the noun.** Resilience is accounting, not poetry.
**Stop the line.**

## Beat 4 — Healing (what the Minderoo envelope is actually funding)

The fund is not buying stories. The stories are evidence of something the
fund has not had evidence of before: that community-led infrastructure,
under OCAP governance, produces measurable change at population scale
without extraction. Every quote in this pitch was consented. Every image was
elder-approved where elder approval was required. Every theme surfaced from
the transcripts, not the cover letter.

What we are asking Minderoo to fund is the next turn of the wheel. More
storytellers on the ledger, more rooms, more nations, more voices in the
argument we are building with them, not about them. Healing is the word the
storytellers keep returning to. We will trust them on that.

**Name the body.** The storyteller who has not yet been recorded.
**Name the room.** The one that does not exist yet.
**Load the noun.** Healing is what happens when the infrastructure is theirs.
**Stop the line.**
