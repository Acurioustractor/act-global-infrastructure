## §2. Executive summary

Minderoo built the supply side. Generation One. Dream Venture. Backing Black Business. First Australians Capital. The basket is full. The hand that fills it is First Nations. The buyer who carries it home is not.

Indigenous Procurement Policy reaches Commonwealth agencies. Mid-market buyers, the businesses that move the bulk of Australian trade, have no equivalent layer. The supply side waits for a demand side that has not arrived.

Goods on Country is that layer.

We curate anchor buyers with verified spend. We vet and onboard First Nations suppliers. We facilitate contracts. We measure parity outcomes against the Indigenous Employment Index. We acquit, monthly, against Xero.

In FY25-26 Goods has invoiced **$1.35 million** through real buyer contracts. **$621,000 has settled.** The open pipeline across 128 opportunities sits at **$16.3 million.** The operational layer that runs underneath this, the Buyer Pipeline, Demand Register, Procurement Analyst, Funder Cadence agent, Invoice Drift Detector, Reconciler, is in production now, not on a roadmap.

QBE Australia announced ACT in the 2026 Catalysing Impact cohort on 23 March. Stage 2 grants, AU$150,000 to AU$400,000, are designed to draw matched investment. QBE writes that the 2025 cohort drew 2.7x.

We are inviting Minderoo to be the matched side. The dollar figure sets the stack.

## §3. The problem, anchored to CoLI24

The Cost of Late Intervention 2024 report, which Minderoo supported through the Front Project, costs the failure of participation at $22.3 billion nationally. The "Participating" precondition fails across eight of nine domains. NEET rates have climbed from 12.6 per cent to 13.4 per cent. The Northern Territory cost per head sits at $2,808, driven by remoteness and what the report names plainly as "levels of Indigeneity."

The report diagnoses the failure of participation. It does not prescribe the demand-side intervention.

That gap is where Goods sits. Indigenous Procurement Policy is one instrument, narrow to Commonwealth procurement. Outside Commonwealth, mid-market buyers have intent and no path. Supply Nation certifies. First Australians Capital invests. Dream Venture trains. Goods routes the demand into the certified, financed, capable enterprises these programs already produce.

When the demand-side infrastructure works, the participation precondition resolves through trade, not transfer. Forrest's frame, not welfare's.

## §4. The work

Goods runs a 12-stage Buyer Pipeline. Stages move from cold prospect through verification, contract drafting, onboarding, first order, repeat order, and sustained supply.

The Demand Register catalogues every buyer interaction with line of sight to dollar value and Indigenous spend percentage. It is queryable by region, sector, and product class.

Supplier vetting is in two parts: Supply Nation certification check, and an internal capability profile that maps each supplier to their delivery ceiling. We do not place a buyer with a supplier who cannot fulfil. We place buyers with suppliers we have already watched deliver.

Acquittal runs through Xero, line-item per contract, and reconciles weekly into the agent layer. The Invoice Drift Detector watches our wiki claims against live Xero state. The Procurement Analyst surfaces the top three weekly buyer touches. The Funder Cadence agent watches funder silence and drafts a progress note when the silence stretches. None of this is novel. All of it is operational.

## Voice + style audit (before sending)

Run through:
- [ ] No em-dashes. Use commas and full stops.
- [ ] First Nations, not Indigenous (except in Indigenous Procurement Policy and Indigenous Employment Index, which are proper nouns).
- [ ] Employment parity, catalytic, systems-level, financial inclusion, self-determination.
- [ ] Never: welfare, closing the gap, Voice (unloaded), empowerment, reconciliation as primary frame.
- [ ] Curtis method on every weighted sentence: name the room, name the body, load the abstract noun, stop the line.
- [ ] No "delve, crucial, pivotal, tapestry, leveraging, fostering, in the heart of."
- [ ] No "not just X but Y." No rule-of-three adjectives. No "-ing" significance tails.
- [ ] Australian spelling.
- [ ] Sentence-case headings.
- [ ] Plainness test: a fourteen-year-old in Doomadgee can follow it.
