## Why this journal exists

This is not a deck and not a report. It is a **physical artefact** Lucy can read in one sitting and hand to a colleague. Three jobs, in order:

1. **Refuse the deck format itself.** A field journal signals that we work the way we are asking Minderoo to fund — slow, returning, hand-made. The medium is the first argument.
2. **Make the architecture legible.** The proposal has many moving parts (centre, ten communities, international tour, four-form artefact). The journal sequences them so each builds on the last.
3. **Make the methodology visible, not just the outputs.** The seven diagrams below carry the *theory of change* without ever saying "theory of change". They are the spine of the visual argument.

## The seven diagrams — what each one means and why it is here

All seven were regenerated 2026-04-08 with **zero text in the image** (handwriting was rendered fake by the model). All real text now lives in the actual font on the facing page. Old fake-text versions archived to `wiki/output/_archive/`.

### 1. Three Circles — `three-circles-journal-spread.png` (3:2 full-bleed)
**What it shows visually:** A brain at centre (Circle 1, the data spine) → ringed by ten distinct hand-drawn community marks (Circle 2, the ten anchors) → ringed by an outer band of institutions plugging in from outside (Circle 3, frameworks/JRI/magistrates/international peers).

**Why it exists:** This is *the* model of the program. Circle One is the centre that learns. Circle Two is the ground truth that feeds it. Circle Three is the public surface where the work becomes leverage. They are not parallel workstreams — they feed each other. Without this diagram the proposal reads as three separate things; with it, it reads as one organism.

**Where it sits in the journal:** Right after the "Three circles. One program." text spread — so the reader meets the words and the picture in the same opening.

### 2. ALMA Loop — `alma-loop-journal.png` (1:1 single page)
**What it shows visually:** A four-phase clockwise loop. Speaking mouth → signal wave → shifting horizon → an eye over a far horizon with a small seed. Connected by curving arrows.

**Why it exists:** ALMA is **how the centre learns**. It is the answer to "how does a story become national evidence without being extracted?" The four beats — STORY → SIGNAL → SHIFT → SCOPE — describe a loop where consent travels with the story, patterns become visible to magistrates and journalists, and the next round of work knows what worked. It is the *epistemic engine* underneath Circle One.

**Where it sits:** Single-page diagram on the left, full text walkthrough on the right. After "What is already built" so the reader has the data context first.

---

### 3. Three Ripples — `three-ripples-journal-spread.png` (3:2 full-bleed) **[NEW]**
**What it shows visually:** A top-down pond with a single point of impact in the centre. Three concentric ripples spread outward, each progressively wider.

**Why it exists:** This is the **theory of how change actually travels** in long-arc, relationship-based work. The first ripple is *the work itself* (one young person, one workshop). The second is *the people in the room next door* (family, community, the local magistrate). The third is *the wider field* (peers in other states, international cohort, public discourse). The journal needs this diagram because Minderoo will reasonably ask "how does ten communities become national change?" — Three Ripples is the answer that does not require the word "scale".

**Where it sits:** First in the methodology cluster (after ALMA, before the other ACT methods). It establishes the geometry of impact before we show the worksheets that operationalise it.

---

### 4. Intentionality Canvas — `intentionality-canvas-journal-spread.png` (3:2 full-bleed) **[NEW]**
**What it shows visually:** A loose hand-drawn 3×3 grid of cells. Each cell holds a single small ink sketch — heart, open hand, horizon, ear, seed, bridge, campfire, two figures shaking hands, brushstroke. No labels.

**Why it exists:** This is **the worksheet ACT carries into every community**. Before any program, partnership, or build, we sit with the partner and walk the nine cells. Each cell is a question — *what are we caring about, what are we listening for, what are we willing to risk, what are we leaving behind, what does art look like here, who is in the bridge, what is the campfire we'll gather around?* It is the artefact form of the LCAA loop. Lucy needs to see it because it is what makes the program **non-extractive on day one** — the questions get asked before the contract.

**Where it sits:** Second in the methodology cluster. After the geometry (ripples), we show the instrument we use in-room.

---

### 5. Field Diagnostic — `field-diagnostic-journal-spread.png` (3:2 full-bleed) **[NEW]**
**What it shows visually:** A central hand-drawn figure (community worker with notebook) standing in a sparse landscape. Dotted sightlines radiate outward to small ink sketches of things being observed — a house with smoke, a tree, a circle of seated figures, a road, a river bend, a fence, a child's bicycle, a campfire.

**Why it exists:** The Field Diagnostic is **what ACT looks for when we visit a community for the first time**. It is not a checklist — it is a way of seeing. We are watching for the things that funded programs miss: who is feeding the kids that are not in any program? Whose Tuesday afternoon is the actual governance? Where does the Wi-Fi reach and where does it not? The diagnostic exists because the proposal commits to "assertive in-community visits" as the only verification that works. This diagram shows what that *means* in practice.

**Where it sits:** Third in the methodology cluster. After the geometry and the worksheet, we show what we do with our eyes once we are in the room.

---

### 6. LCAA Loop — `lcaa-loop-journal.png` (1:1 single page) **[NEW to this journal — was always an ACT method]**
**What it shows visually:** Four-phase clockwise loop. Ear with soundwaves → eye with a curl of thought → reaching hand → calligraphic ochre brushstroke. Curving arrows connecting them.

**Why it exists:** LCAA — **Listen → Curiosity → Action → Art** — is the ACT operating system. It has been our four-beat loop since day one. Listen *before* anything is offered. Curiosity asks the questions a consultant would not. Action only after listening (small, returnable, useful). Art brings the work back as something — a film, a portrait, a journal, a brushstroke — because art is how the work becomes shareable, citable, real. **The reason it is in this journal:** Lucy needs to understand that the program is not invented for her grant. The four-beat loop runs underneath every line item and is the same loop ACT runs on every project, in every community, regardless of funder. It is the methodology Minderoo is buying access to.

**Where it sits:** Fourth and last in the methodology cluster. The other models describe what we do; LCAA describes *how we hold it together*. The right page is the only text-paired page in the cluster because LCAA is the meta-method and needs to be named.

---

### 7. Fire Crescendo — `fire-crescendo-journal.png` (3:2 full-bleed)
**What it shows visually:** Six fire stages building left to right across the spread — single ember → tinder catching → kindling → small campfire → roaring fire → great bonfire lifting sparks.

**Why it exists:** This is the **emotional and temporal arc** of the three-year program. Year 1 is the ember and the tinder. Year 2 is the kindling and the campfire. Year 3 is the bonfire — the artefact, the international cohort, the moment the work becomes visible at scale. It is also the answer to "why three years and not one?" — fire does not arrive at bonfire stage. You build it, slowly, with attention.

**Where it sits:** Right before the costed ask. It primes the reader emotionally for the three-year shape of the budget. The crescendo of the picture matches the crescendo of the ask.
