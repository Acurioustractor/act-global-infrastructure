## Slide 3 — The move

**Every other youth justice program leaves. Staying is the move.**

For 3 years, a small ACT team rotates through 7 communities — one in each Australian state — accompanying the work that is *already* preventing detention, photographing it, journaling it, and producing a bound book per young person at the end.

In Year 2, every community sends 2 people (1 young person + 1 support) on an exchange visit to **one** of three European hosts: **Diagrama** (Spain), **Halt** (Holland), or **Children's Hearings** (Scotland). They come home with the experience.

In Year 3, the work goes home — seven community-led launches, one published book.

The methodology is the name. **Staying.**

## Slide 6 — Why Year 2 in Europe

Year 2 puts a young person and a community Elder on a plane from each of the seven communities to one European country. **Those conversations have never happened in this country's history.**

- **Spain — Diagrama.** Thirty years of proof that community-based youth justice scales. Door already open.
- **Holland — Halt.** Restorative + diversion-first, the closest thing Europe has to an integrated alternative.
- **Scotland — Children's Hearings.** Whole System Approach + the political courage to legislate a higher minimum age of criminal responsibility.

**The trips run in both directions.** European teams travel to Australia in the same year and walk Country with the communities they only ever read about. Local knowledge meets local knowledge — neither side is the "expert".

The masterstroke is not Spain. It is *the conversation between an Aunty from Mparntwe and a Spanish educator from Murcia*, then bringing the Spanish educator to the Town Camp.

## Slide 7 — The journal — the centre artefact

> *If the program produces nothing else, the journals are the proof.*

- **Bound book per young person** — A5, hand-stitched, ~80–120 pages
- **Three years of accumulation, one delivery in Year 3** — portraits in the Brave Ones discipline, the young person's own handwriting, voice transcripts from Empathy Ledger, letters from Aunties and uncles, ledger entries of who showed up and when, drawings, marks, place — and the European exchange visit captured as its own chapter
- **Owned by the young person.** Right of withdrawal at any point. OCAP / CARE protocols at the data layer.
- **One archival copy** held by ACT for the program's evidence base.

The visual register is the **ledger page**, not the diagram. Hand-made, lived-in, accumulated over time. Christian Boltanski / Sophie Calle / Edmund Clark / Theaster Gates / Judy Watson / Daniel Boyd / Vernon Ah Kee.

## Slide 9 — Why Minderoo, and what changed

You told us the last shape was too big. We listened.

The previous proposal was ~$8M over three years. This one is **less than half that** at the Recommended tier — $3.43M — and we have a Foundation tier at $1.8M sitting underneath it if even that is too much. Nothing was diluted to get here. We changed the architecture, not the ambition: smaller team (1 FTE + 1 support, not 3), shorter cadence (4 months on, not 6), distributed European exchange (14 travellers visiting one country each, not a year-long team in residence), one Year-3 journal delivery (not three chapter deliveries), and the national gallery tour deferred to a Year-4 stretch goal raised separately.

Minderoo is the only Australian foundation with the *combination* of:

1. **Walk Free + Generation One alignment** — a settled view that the system needs replacing, not optimising
2. **Capacity for 3-year catalytic capital** — most philanthropy can't write a multi-year cheque this size
3. **International ambition** — Year 2 in Europe is legible to Minderoo in a way it isn't to a domestic-only funder
4. **A live conversation** — Lucy Stronach already asked us to come back smaller. This is us coming back smaller.

The legacy positioning still holds: *"The Minderoo Australian Justice Cohort sent fourteen people to Europe, and brought Europe back."*

For the cost of detaining a single child for three years, Minderoo funds the largest community-led youth justice evidence base ever assembled in this country, plus an international cohort experience, plus a published book that lands in every electorate office.

## Speaker notes — for the room

- **Open with the $3.9M number, then silence.** Don't fill it. Let the room sit with it.
- **The Brisbane three-way pick is a feature, not a bug** — it shows we have multiple existing relationships and that the program can absorb a community choice without re-architecting.
- **Don't over-explain Europe.** "An Aunty and a young person from each community, sent to one European country, and Europe sent back to them." That's the whole sentence. The room will get there.
- **Three scales is a permission structure.** It tells the funder *we have already done the work of scoping a smaller version*, which removes the negotiation friction that killed the prior $8M shape.
- **The journal is the close.** When the room asks "what do we get?", answer: "Fifty bound books, one per young person, owned by them. One archival copy each held by ACT. A published volume of the seven journals together, distributed to every electorate office. Three years of relationships you can stand inside."
- **Do not promise the gallery tour at Recommended.** It is deferred to a Year 4 stretch goal raised separately. Promise it only if Lucy moves us to the Full ambition tier.
