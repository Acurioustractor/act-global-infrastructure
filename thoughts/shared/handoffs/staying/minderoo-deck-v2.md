## Slide 4 — The proposition in one paragraph

Minderoo funds a three-year program that does three things simultaneously:

1. **builds the JusticeHub + CivicGraph data spine** so every existing model, framework and dataset on youth justice in Australia lives in one queryable, community-owned place
2. **backs 10 anchor communities** with deep, low-reporting, untied support to do longitudinal storytelling, model documentation and cross-community exchange
3. **convenes them — and international peers — into a national framework for community-led oversight** that replaces the current detention-default with evidence the public, magistrates and Treasury can't ignore

This is not 10 grants and a website. It is **one program with three concentric circles** that feed each other.

## Slide 5 — The Three Circles (visual)

**◉ Circle One — The Living Brain**
A queryable, locally-hosted intelligence system holding every program, evaluation, framework, grant, Hansard mention, organisation and consented story on Australian youth justice.

**◉ Circle Two — The Ten Anchor Communities**
Ten community-led organisations supported with untied funding and minimised reporting. The embedded storytelling practice that runs across them is called **Staying** (Country & Council methodology) and produces a hand-stitched journal per young person — the intimate counterpart to the country-scale artefact.

**◉ Circle Three — The Outer Ring**
Frameworks plug in. JRI plugs in. Philanthropy plugs in. International peers plug in. Magistrates plug in. Journalists plug in. *Coordination by infrastructure, not by meetings.*

## Slide 8 — The artefact: *The Country We're Building*

Every three-year program needs something the people inside it are working *toward*.

**One artefact, four forms:**

1. **A documentary** (90 minutes) — feature-length, co-directed with each community, premiered at the Year 3 convening
2. **A book** — long-form, photography-rich, structured around the ten communities + their international counterparts; published Year 3 with a major Australian publisher; community organisations retain rights and royalty share
3. **A living platform** — Circle One opened to the public, every framework, story, evaluation and dollar searchable, citable, permanent
4. **The Journals** *(new in this refresh)* — one bound, hand-stitched A5 book per young person in deep relationship with the program. Their portraits, their handwriting, voice transcripts from Empathy Ledger, letters from Aunties and uncles, ledger entries of who showed up and when, drawings, marks, place. **Owned by the young person.** Right of withdrawal at any point. The intimate counterpart to the country-scale forms above.

The visual register is the **ledger page**, not the diagram. Hand-made, lived-in, system as marginalia. References: Christian Boltanski, Sophie Calle, Edmund Clark, Theaster Gates, Judy Watson, Daniel Boyd, Vernon Ah Kee.

## Slide 9 — Why Minderoo, and what changed

**You told us the previous shape was too big. We listened.**

The previous proposal was on its way to ~$8M+. This one is **$2.9M total over three years** ($780K · $960K · $1.16M annual flow). Nothing was diluted to get here — we tightened the architecture: ten anchor communities held with depth (not twelve held thinly), one program with three feeding circles (not three parallel workstreams), one artefact in four forms (not three discrete deliverables). The principles are yours: *"twelve organisations, one per month, one per state, minimised reporting, maximised relationship."*

**Why Minderoo specifically:**

1. **Walk Free + Generation One alignment** — a settled view that the system needs replacing, not optimising
2. **Capacity for 3-year catalytic capital** — most philanthropy can't write a multi-year cheque this size
3. **International ambition** — the Year 1 Africa + Europe tour (Diagrama, Halt, Norwegian restorative justice, Portuguese diversion, South African community courts, Canadian First Nations sentencing circles) is legible to Minderoo in a way it isn't to a domestic-only funder
4. **A live conversation** — Lucy Stronach already asked us to come back smaller. This is us coming back smaller.

**International precedent we lean on most heavily:**
**Diagrama (Spain): 13.6% recidivism, 5.64:1 cost-benefit ratio over 30 years.** €31K/year per young person against $1.33M/year detention in Australia.

**The legacy positioning:** *"The Minderoo Australian Justice Cohort sent ten communities into deep relationship and brought Europe back."*

For less than the cost of detaining a single child for one year, Minderoo funds the largest community-led youth justice evidence base ever assembled in this country, plus an international cohort experience, plus a published book that lands in every electorate office, plus a documentary, plus the living platform.

### Three options for Minderoo

We want this to be the easiest possible "yes" internally:

1. **Anchor funder, full program** — $2.9M over three years. Minderoo's name on the artefact, the convening, the framework launch.
2. **Lead Year 1 with right of first refusal on Years 2–3** — $780K now, watching traction before committing further. PRF, Snow, Dusseldorp, Ritchie co-fund subsequent years against demonstrated progress.
3. **Fund a single circle** — most likely **the Centre** ($520K over three years), as the durable infrastructure piece. We assemble a coalition for the community and storytelling layers separately.

**Our preference is option 1**, because the three circles only work as one program. But we will make any of these work.

1. Indication of fit — does the $2.9M anchor option sit inside your conversation, or does another shape work better?

## Speaker notes — for the room

- **Open with the $1.33M number, then silence.** Don't fill it. Let the room sit with it. The Closing-the-Gap projection (24.6/10,000 by 2030–31) lands second.
- **Slide 3 — Lucy's quote — is the trust move.** It says *we listened to you specifically.* Don't paraphrase it; read it verbatim from the slide and let her recognise her own words.
- **Slide 6 (verified numbers) is the de-risking move.** Lead with the live MCP server and the Hansard scraper — these are the things that prove the platform isn't vapourware. Don't just rattle the numbers; pause on "live, deployed" three times so the room hears the tense.
- **Don't over-explain Europe.** "Ben and three community leaders go to Spain, Holland, Scotland and three more places, document what works, bring it back into the centre. Year 3 they come to us." That's the whole thing.
- **The journal is the intimate close.** When the room asks "what do we get?", answer: "A documentary that runs in cinemas, a book in every electorate office, a public platform, and — for every young person in deep relationship with the program — a hand-stitched journal that's theirs to keep. Not Minderoo's. Not ACT's. Theirs."
- **Three options on Slide 10 is a permission structure.** Option 1 is the ask; options 2 and 3 are the de-escalation paths if the room is hesitant. Don't lead with options 2 or 3.
- **The "Minderoo Australian Justice Cohort" naming line is the legacy hook.** Use it once, on Slide 9, then don't repeat it. It works because it's restrained.
- **Frameworks Institute** comes up as a Year 1 deliverable inside Circle One — *not* a Y0 precondition. If Lucy presses: "Andrea Davidson's interest becomes a real test case during Year 1 dialogue with the ten anchor communities, not a pre-built artefact."
- **Oonchiumpa 95% diversion** — cited from Oonchiumpa's own programmatic reporting. If Lucy presses for the document: confirm we'll send the specific reference.
