# What I found

The JusticeHub wiki holds a **fully developed prior Minderoo pitch** — much more developed than the brief in the original handoff suggested. Key facts:

- **Prior ask:** $10.5M BASE / $5.9M LEAN / $17.6M UPPER over 3 years (the "$8M that was too much" turns out to have been $10.5M BASE)
- **Architecture:** A **digital intelligence platform** for the youth justice sector — *not* a field accompaniment program
- **Core mechanism:** **CORE / NETWORK community model** — paid CORE communities at $180K/year each, free NETWORK communities at $40–50K of value each
- **Scale:** 5 → 7 → 9 CORE communities + 20 NETWORK communities = **29 communities reached by Year 3**
- **Team:** 16 Indigenous staff by Year 3 (this is the headline) — *not* a small ACT-led rotating team
- **Onboarding:** 4–5 month sequence from first meeting to Year 1 launch (Deep Dive → Community Consultation → Co-Design Workshop → Due Diligence → Formal Agreement)
- **Already-named CORE communities:**
  - Year 1: Mparntwe (Oonchiumpa, NT) · Minjerribah (MMEIC, QLD) · Bourke (Barranbinya, NSW) · Moree (Yaama, NSW) · Palm Island (PICC, QLD)
  - Year 2: Darug Country (Mounty Yarns) · Kalkadoon (BG Fit, QLD)
- **Existing platform proof points:** 521 programs catalogued · 38+ stories · 450+ orgs mapped · $303K value created · platform already operational in beta
- **Problem-framing stat:** 17× overrepresentation in detention; 30–40% of community time lost to unsuccessful grants
- **Voice triad to preserve verbatim:** *"Not creating dependency. Building sovereignty. Not extracting knowledge. Valuing it. Not another project. Permanent infrastructure."*
- **Headline framing:** *"Built under Indigenous leadership. Owned by communities by Year 5."*

## The real decision: what *is* Staying?

There are three honest framings, and Ben needs to pick:

### Framing A — Staying *replaces* the prior JusticeHub pitch

The prior pitch was too big and platform-heavy for a foundation room. Staying is the new shape: tighter, embodied, story-first, with Europe and the journal. We *retire* the prior pitch internally and present Staying as the answer to "make it less."

**Risk:** loses the platform proof (521 programs, $303K value). Loses the 16-Indigenous-staff scale. Lucy may have liked aspects of the prior pitch.

### Framing B — Staying *is the field arm* of JusticeHub, and the pitch is for both at once

JusticeHub is the world model; Staying is the field arm that populates it with embodied work. Together they are one program with two layers. Lucy funds the integrated thing. The CORE/NETWORK model holds. The Indigenous-led leadership holds. The European exchange and the journal are the new layers Staying adds.

**Risk:** scope creep. The combined ask might bounce back to $5–6M and we're back where we started.

### Framing C — Staying is a *standalone smaller program* that lives inside JusticeHub's CORE community list

We propose Staying as a discrete $3.43M program for the 7 CORE communities, with the explicit understanding that JusticeHub continues to operate as the platform layer (separately funded or self-sustaining). Lucy gets a discrete deliverable. JusticeHub's prior $10.5M ask is parked.

**Risk:** Lucy may want the integrated story, not the discrete one.
