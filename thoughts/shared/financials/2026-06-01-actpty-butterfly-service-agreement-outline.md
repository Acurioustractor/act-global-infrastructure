# Service agreement outline — ACT Pty ↔ The Butterfly Movement Ltd

> The Goods structure splits across two entities: **A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd** (commercial/operational arm) and **The Butterfly Movement Ltd** (charity / grants / DGR home). Ben and Nic sit on or near both sides. That makes every dollar moving between them a **related-party transaction** the ACNC and the ATO will look at. This agreement is the firewall: it lets ACT Pty provide real services to the charity at a defensible price, keeps trading income out of the charity, and documents the conflict so the charity's directors can stand behind it. This is an outline for SL's lawyer to draft, not the deed itself.

## 1. Parties
- **Provider:** A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd (ACN 697 347 676, ABN 36 697 347 676)
- **Recipient:** The Butterfly Movement Ltd (ACNC charity, DGR application pending)

## 2. Services ACT Pty provides to the charity
The "product engine + back office" behind Goods on Country:
- Product design + technical development
- Manufacturing systems, tooling, quality control
- Procurement, logistics, warranty + service systems
- Storytelling, sales, buyer relationships (the commercial channel)
- Back office: finance, IT, evidence/impact systems, governance support
- Training + capability transfer to community production entities (delivered on the charity's behalf where grant-funded)

## 3. The money boundary (the whole point)
- **Commercial product sales + procurement contracts → ACT Pty.** Buyers (PICC, Centrecorp, community orgs) pay ACT Pty for goods. This trading income does **not** flow through the charity.
- **Grants + philanthropy + donations → the charity.** Tax-deductible once DGR endorsed; auspiced in the interim.
- **The charity may pay ACT Pty for services at market rate**, and may **grant/subsidise** community production readiness + access. The charity must **not** overpay ACT Pty (that would be a disguised private benefit and an ACNC breach) and ACT Pty must **not** systematically undercharge in a way that drains the charity or props up the company artificially.

## 4. Pricing — arm's length
- **Basis:** cost-recovery plus a defensible margin (cost-plus), or independently benchmarked market rate, whichever SL recommends. Documented methodology, reviewed annually.
- **Defensibility:** must withstand ACNC "private benefit" scrutiny and ATO related-party/transfer-pricing review. Keep the workings.
- **GST:** ACT Pty's services to the charity are **taxable supplies** (GST applies); the charity claims input-tax credits if registered.

## 5. Related-party + conflict-of-interest safeguards
- Ben and Nic are conflicted (interests on both sides). They **do not vote** on this agreement; the charity's **independent / Indigenous-majority directors** approve it.
- Maintain a **conflict-of-interest register**; disclose the transaction in the charity's ACNC reporting as a related-party transaction.
- Charity board reviews the arrangement at least annually and can terminate.

## 6. IP (flag for legal — currently open)
- **Who owns the Goods on Country brand + IP** — the charity, ACT Pty, or co-owned? Decide before signing.
- If ACT Pty uses the Goods brand commercially, include a **licence** (royalty-free or nominal, documented).
- Product designs / methodologies developed by ACT Pty: ownership + any licence-back to the charity for community-production use.

## 7. Term, review, termination
- Initial term (e.g. 12 months), auto-review annually, either party may terminate on notice. Survival clauses for IP + confidentiality.

## 8. Open items for SL's lawyer
- Pricing basis (cost-plus vs benchmarked market) + the margin.
- Goods brand/IP ownership + licence structure.
- Whether the charity's DGR endorsement (once granted) constrains payments to a related commercial party.
- Treatment during the **auspicing interim** (before DGR + before the agreement is signed) — how money received for Goods by the charity is handled and on-paid.
- Interaction with R&D: ACT Pty incurs + claims Goods R&D (the charity can't); ensure the service pricing doesn't have grants effectively reimbursing ACT Pty's R&D (keeps R&D "at risk").
