# Thinking like Standard Ledger

## The headline reframe

**FY26 R&D is very likely largely stranded — and that's fine, because the real refund engine is FY27.**

The same rule that forfeited FY24-25 (an R&D entity must be a *company* — s355-35) applies to most of FY26: the company only existed from **24 April 2026**, and a company can only claim R&D expenditure **it incurred** (s355-205). The "treat the sole trader as operating on behalf of the Pty" journals move the P&L for *accounting*, but they don't make pre-incorporation sole-trader spend *company-incurred* for the R&D claim — and pushing that invites Part IVA.

So the ~$200K we'd been modelling as a **FY26** refund is not lost. It **moves to FY27** (the first full year of the Pty operating with founders on payroll) and then **recurs every year**. The work now is to make FY27 a maximal, clean R&D year — not to chase a FY26 claim that won't survive audit.

## R&D refund — the realistic scenarios

| Scenario | What it assumes | FY26 refund | FY27 refund (recurring) |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| **Downside** | Do nothing special; Pty incurred ~no R&D spend before 30 Jun | **~$0** | ~$200-260K |
| **Base (recommended)** | Accrue founder R&D remuneration for the **24 Apr – 30 Jun** post-incorporation window + register those activities | **~$15-25K** | ~$200-260K |
| **Upside** | SL finds a defensible basis for more FY26 (unlikely on plain law) | partial | ~$200-260K |

**Why base, not upside:** on plain statute (s355-35, s355-205, s6-5) the Pty's FY26 claim is limited to what *it* incurred in its ~10-week existence. The only legitimate FY26 lever is to **accrue genuine founder remuneration to the Pty for R&D work done 24 Apr – 30 Jun** (the "incurred" test can be met by a definite, committed liability even if paid later) and register those activities with AusIndustry. That's a small but real claim, not the headline number.

**FY27 maths (the engine):** founder personnel (Ben ~95% × ~$200K ≈ $190K + Nic ~40% × ~$200K ≈ $80K) + R&D infra (the ACT-IN-type build) + SaaS + Goods manufacturing R&D → ~$500-600K eligible × 43.5% refundable ≈ **$200-260K cash**, refundable in full because the Pty's turnover is under $20M. This recurs annually.

## How SL would likely land each open question

| Mechanic | Likely SL position | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **FY26 R&D via "on behalf of" journals** | No for R&D — limit to Pty-incurred post-24-Apr | Minimal, defensible FY26 claim only; no aggressive journals |
| **Subdiv 328-G rollover** (asset CGT) | Yes, with conditions | Get valid **family trust elections for both trusts**; confirm <$10M turnover + genuine restructure |
| **GST going-concern** (s38-325) | Yes, with conditions | Written "supply of a going concern" agreement; both registered; enterprise carried to day of supply |
| **Div 7A director loans** | Yes | Complying written loan agreement **before lodgement day**, benchmark rate, ≤7yr |
| **Goods charity ↔ ACT Pty** | Arm's-length or bust | Written **service agreement** (ACT Pty bills the charity for back-office at market rate); keep trading income *out* of the charity; document related-party + conflict-of-interest; lodge DGR application now; interim auspicing must fit the charity's objects |
| **Founder FY26 pay catch-up** | Weak R&D rationale now | Pay founders properly **from 1 July (FY27)** where it IS R&D-eligible; treat any FY26 founder pay as cashflow/fairness/super-cap, not an R&D play |

## What to set up NOW — no-regrets, whatever SL says

1. **Founders onto Pty payroll from 1 July** ($10K/mo + super + director loan), with the **95% (Ben) / 40% (Nic) R&D split documented weekly**. This is what makes FY27 the maximal R&D year.
2. **Register R&D activities with AusIndustry for the Pty** (FY27 window; and the small 24 Apr–30 Jun FY26 slice if claiming base).
3. **Contemporaneous R&D records from 1 July** — git, calendar, the activity register. The evidence is the claim.
4. **Keep R&D "at risk"** — fund it from ACT Pty commercial revenue; let grants fund non-R&D community/access work via the charity. (Grant-reimbursed R&D gets clawed back.)
5. **Proceed with the clean mechanics** — 328-G, GST going-concern, Div 7A. Get the two family trust elections sorted (gates 328-G).
6. **Goods:** stand up the arm's-length ACT Pty ↔ Butterfly service agreement; lodge the DGR application; keep the books split (commercial → ACT Pty, grants → charity).
7. **FY26 claim:** accrue founder R&D remuneration for 24 Apr–30 Jun, register it, claim the ~$15-25K. Don't over-engineer.

## What actually changes in the plan

- **Re-baseline the refund:** FY26 ≈ small ($0-25K); **FY27 ≈ $200-260K, recurring.** Update any cashflow that banked $200K of R&D into FY26.
- **The cutover's "journal FY26 into the Pty" premise stays valid for accounting/P&L** (and may still matter for income-tax positioning) **but not as the R&D claim basis.**
- **Founder-pay urgency shifts** from "pay in FY26 to catch R&D" to "start clean FY27 payroll." Less pressure to backdate.
- **None of this blocks the cutover.** It changes the *expectation*, not the *setup*. Everything in the no-regrets list can proceed now.
