# Question for Standard Ledger — FY26 R&D eligibility across the cutover

Hi Vanessa,

Before we lock the FY26 R&D claim assumptions into the cutover, we want one thing confirmed **in writing** because the whole refund estimate (~$200K) rides on it. We've done an independent read against the legislation and found a tension we can't resolve ourselves. We'd rather surface it now than discover it at lodgement.

## The situation

- **A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd** (ACN 697 347 676, ABN 36 697 347 676) was **registered 24 April 2026** and its ABN/GST issued ~1 June 2026.
- For **most of FY26** (1 July 2025 → 24 April 2026) the R&D work and the spend sat in **Nic's sole trader** (ABN 21 591 780 066).
- Our cutover strategy plans to claim FY26 R&D **via the Pty**, using the "treat the sole trader as conducting business on behalf of ACT Pty Ltd" journal approach you described on 5 May.
- Our own locked R&D position ("Path C") already **forfeited FY24-25 because the sole-trader period is ineligible** — and we now can't see why FY26's sole-trader period would be different.

## The tension we need resolved

On a plain reading of the legislation:

1. **s355-35 ITAA97** — an R&D entity must be a **company** (the sole trader / individual is not an eligible R&D entity).
2. **s355-205** — a company can only notionally deduct R&D expenditure **it incurs**.
3. **s6-5** — income is assessable to the entity that **derived** it.

A company that **did not exist before 24 April 2026** cannot have *incurred* R&D expenditure (or *derived* income) before that date. Year-end cross-entity journals record where value *ends up*, but they don't appear to change **who legally incurred the expenditure**. If that's right, the Pty's FY26 R&D claim may be limited to expenditure **the Pty itself incurred between ~24 April and 30 June 2026** — a small slice of the year — not the full-year ~$200K we've been modelling.

## What we need confirmed (in writing)

1. **Eligible window.** Can the Pty validly claim FY26 R&D expenditure incurred during the **sole-trader period (1 Jul 2025 – 24 Apr 2026)**? If yes, on **which specific provision**? If no, please confirm the claim is limited to **post-incorporation company-incurred** spend.
2. **The "on behalf of" journals.** Do the cross-entity reallocation journals give the Pty a valid basis to treat sole-trader R&D expenditure as **incurred by the Pty** for Div 355 purposes? Does this require a **documented agency / bare-trust arrangement**, and can such an arrangement be valid for a period when the Pty did not yet exist?
3. **Re-sized estimate + strategy.** If the claim is limited to post-incorporation, what is the **realistic FY26 eligible amount**, and does that change what we should do **before 30 June** — e.g. deliberately incurring more eligible R&D spend *through the Pty* (founder payroll, contractors, SaaS) in the 24 Apr – 30 Jun window?
4. **Founder personnel cost.** For founder time (Ben ~95% R&D, Nic ~40%) to count in the Pty's FY26 claim, must it be **paid by the Pty** (payroll or contractor invoice) **within the income year**? This is our largest lever, so we want the mechanism exact.
5. **Goods R&D going forward.** We've confirmed the Goods structure: **The Butterfly Movement Ltd** (charity) holds grants/DGR; **A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd** runs the commercial/operational arm. Please confirm **ACT Pty is the correct R&D claimant** for Goods manufacturing R&D (the charity being income-tax-exempt cannot claim), and how to keep that R&D **"at risk"** so the offset isn't clawed back where Goods R&D is **grant-supported** (feedstock / clawback / associate-funding rules).

## Lower-risk items — please just confirm (our read says these hold with conditions)

6. **Subdivision 328-G restructure rollover** for the asset transfer (equipment + any goodwill): confirm aggregated turnover < $10M, "genuine restructure", and the **ultimate-economic-ownership** test is satisfied given the transferee is owned by **two different family trusts** — including whether **valid family trust elections** are needed for both (s328-440).
7. **GST going-concern** (s38-325): confirm the transfer qualifies as a GST-free supply (written agreement, enterprise carried on to day of supply, both registered).
8. **Division 7A**: confirm the director's-loan top-up model needs a **complying written loan agreement before lodgement day** at the benchmark rate (≤7-yr unsecured) to avoid a deemed dividend.

## Why this matters now

We're 4 weeks from cutover and want the FY26 R&D claim assumptions **defensible at audit** before we book any journals or set the founder-pay structure. Items 1–4 are the ones that change the dollar figure; the rest are confirmations. A short written response on 1–5 is what we need most.
