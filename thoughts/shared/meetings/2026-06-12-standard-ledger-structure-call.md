### Founder Pay — This Financial Year

* Target: up to ~$200K each ($300K total across both founders) before end of financial year
* $100K already moved from sole trader to the partnership business in September; needs to be invoiced
* Mechanism: invoice sole trader from personal entity or partnership — income lands at personal level and is declared on personal tax returns
* Tax implication: ATO will treat ~$300K as income and expect close to half in tax; payment plans can be arranged with the ATO if needed
* ATO may require quarterly PAYG installments going forward if similar income is expected; switching to payroll through the company can be used to opt out of that regime

### Founder Pay — Next Financial Year

* Plan: $120K each per year ($10K/month) via payroll from the company, handling tax along the way
* Any additional cash draws during the year should be recorded as director's loans
* Div 7A warning: if a director's loan remains outstanding at year-end, the ATO treats it as income; must either repay it or formalise it as a genuine loan with interest
* Extra lump-sum draws could alternatively be called director's fees, shareholder distributions via the trust, or contractor invoices — but payroll requires grossing up for super
* Expectation is to mostly reinvest in the business next year, with six-monthly reviews for any top-ups

### Proposed Corporate Structure

* Recommendation: current Pty Ltd becomes a holding company with separate subsidiaries for each major business activity
* ACT Projects (subsidiary): for R&D, contracts, and project work — closest to what the sole trader is currently doing; Nick would seed this entity with capital
* Harvest (subsidiary): venue/centre for workshops, restaurant/café, events — keeping it self-contained allows future carve-off, sale, or capital raise
* DGR Charity — Butterfly Program (subsidiary): being gifted over the coming months; a lawyer is managing the transfer; will have an Indigenous board; intended primarily as a philanthropic vehicle to raise capital for on-country production facilities, not for drawing founder wages
* Key open question: whether the holding company owning a DGR subsidiary creates any compliance issues — to be verified with the DGR lawyer before August

### Farm vs. Harvest

* The farm (Witta, Sunshine Coast hinterland) is separate from the Harvest centre and will not be a core business entity for now
* Farm income/accommodation would operate in Nick's personal name
* Artists-in-residence programs may link the farm to Harvest in future
* Farm accounting noted as a distinct area to keep separate

### Upcoming Projects & Revenue

* Justice project: expected several hundred thousand dollars in contract revenue
* Storytelling project: similar projected income
* Snow Foundation / Goods Project: recent grant received; ongoing R&D and technology work
* Revenue is primarily contract/project-based; paying through the ACT Projects subsidiary makes most structural sense
* R&D work being done continuously — getting it into the right entity is key to being able to claim R&D tax incentives

### Working Relationship & Financial Reporting

* Standard Ledger team is currently handling bookkeeping
* Payroll setup to follow once company structure is operational
* CFO-style services (financial forecasting, regular reporting) available if needed as the business scales
* Currently has a QBE mentor helping with financial modelling for the Goods Project
* Next phase goal: move away from grant-dependency toward commercial revenue, particularly through Harvest and outside investors
* More financial rigour (monthly reporting, forecasting) noted as a priority over the next 6–12 months, especially once Harvest begins generating income
