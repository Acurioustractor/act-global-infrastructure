## Part B: QBE Foundation and the $400K matched mechanic

### B1. Two public mechanics exist, only one fits $400K

**Mechanic 1 — QBE Workplace Giving (Qgiving)**: dollar-for-dollar matched personal fundraising, **up to $5,000 per employee per year**. Rules out $400K. https://www.qbe.com/sustainability/qbe-foundation/governance-and-initiatives

**Mechanic 2 — QBE Foundation "Catalysing Impact" (delivered by Social Impact Hub)**:
- Stage 1 seed and participation grants from $10,000 AUD.
- **Stage 2 catalytic matched grants from AU$150,000 to AU$400,000.**

Sources: https://www.qbe.com/newsroom/news/qbe-foundation-catalysing-impact-initiative, https://www.socialimpacthub.org/catalysing-impact

**This fits. And ACT is already in the 2026 cohort.**

### B2. Catalysing Impact mechanic

Per QBE and Social Impact Hub, Stage 2 grants are *"designed to be catalytic, helping unlock additional investment and build a larger, more sustainable capital stack"*. Mechanic:

1. Cohort selection (ACT through this gate for 2026).
2. Stage 1 participation grant of roughly $10,000 plus capability-building and QBE skilled-volunteer access.
3. Stage 2 catalytic grant of AU$150K to AU$400K, **conditional on matching third-party investment or funding**.
4. 2025 inaugural cohort: 10 enterprises, QBE's AU$1.02m leveraged a total capital stack of AU$3.77m = **2.7x external co-funding on top of QBE grant.**

Source: https://econews.com.au/catalysing-impact-unlocks-3-77m-for-climate-inclusion-enterprises/

**Read carefully**: Catalysing Impact *"is not designed for charities seeking philanthropic funding or donations. While charities may apply, all participating enterprises must be seeking investment or repayable debt to support a social enterprise operation."* The Minderoo ask therefore needs to be framed either as a programmatic grant to ACT Foundation (charitable, DGR) that enables ACT Ventures or the Goods trading entity to access QBE's repayable-debt or investment stack, **or** as matched grant funding that sits alongside QBE's Stage 2 grant.

### B3. QBE Foundation + Indigenous signals

- **April 2024**: QBE Foundation's **$500,000 seed capital grant to First Australians Capital** — first seed capital grant in its history, explicitly to support Indigenous business financial inclusion. https://www.qbe.com/au/media-centre/press-releases/qbe-foundation-provides-seed-capital-grant-to-first-australians-capital
- **2023 QBE Local Grants**: Arise Foundation $40,000 to *"support Indigenous and Torres Strait Islander women to undertake economic recovery programs"*.
- **Premiums4Good** funds Indigenous-adjacent infrastructure offshore (Métis Crossing Solar, Burchill Wind, Canada). No comparable Australian Indigenous Premiums4Good line item located.

## Part D: Rhetorical framing — which Minderoo words to use

### Terms Minderoo uses publicly

- **"Employment parity with and for Indigenous Australians"** — load-bearing Generation One phrase. Use verbatim.
- **"First Nations"** — current preferred 2024-2025 term. Older materials (2021-22) used "Indigenous". **Lead with "First Nations".**
- **"Uplift communities"** — Focus Area label. Use verbatim if positioning Goods as community-scale.
- **"Catalytic"** — used by both Minderoo ("catalyse change") and QBE. Safe loaded word in this room.
- **"Systems-level change" and "systems outcomes"** — 2030 Strategy language.
- **"Financial inclusion" and "financial exclusion"** — Backing Black Business framing.
- **"Indigenous business sector"**, **"$8.8 billion economic contribution"** — direct Backing Black Business quote. Using this number tells Minderoo you read their report.
- **"Indigenous Procurement Policy"** — referenced in Backing Black Business as the government lever. Goods positions as demand-side infrastructure operationalising the IPP for **mid-market buyers**, not just Commonwealth agencies.
- **"Self-determination"** — standard Minderoo usage in First Nations contexts.

### Loaded terms — use with care

- **"Welfare"** — Andrew Forrest has loaded this negatively ("mining welfare"). **Do not frame Goods as welfare-adjacent or wealth-transfer.** Frame as building trade, demand, and enterprise.
- **"Closing the gap"** — federal government frame, not Minderoo's voice. Minderoo uses "parity" instead. Keep sparing.
- **"Voice"** — post-referendum political weight. Avoid unless directly relevant.
- **"Empowerment"** — corporate cliche. Minderoo uses "self-determination" and "agency" instead.

### Terms Minderoo avoids (inferred)

- **"Reconciliation"** as primary frame — present but not load-bearing in 2030 Strategy. Minderoo leads with economic parity.
- **"Decolonisation"** — absent from public copy.
- Generic philanthropy language ("giving back", "helping those in need") — Minderoo writes in systems-change and outcomes cadence.

> "Goods on Country is the demand-side infrastructure for First Nations employment parity: procurement demand from Australian buyers, routed into Indigenous-owned supply chains, with the trade volume that makes capital (FAC) and capability (Dream Venture) pay back."

That sentence uses "employment parity" and "First Nations" verbatim, references Backing Black Business, names two Minderoo-world grantees, and ducks "welfare", "closing the gap", and "Voice".
