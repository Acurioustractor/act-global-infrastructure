## The frame, in one line

**"QBE has pre-committed $400K catalytic capital to Goods on Country in the 2026 Catalysing Impact cohort. That grant is explicitly designed to unlock matched investment. We are inviting Minderoo to be the matched side, at a dollar figure that sets the whole stack."**

This is not a flat grant ask. It is a leverage pitch. Minderoo's money compounds QBE's.

## Why this is a Minderoo pitch, not a Goods 101 pitch

Minderoo already owns the First Nations employment parity frame. They fund the supply side (Generation One, Dream Venture, Blak Angels, the Indigenous Employment Index, Backing Black Business). They endorsed the First Australians Capital thesis in 2021; QBE funded FAC $500K in April 2024. The two foundations have already converged on the capital side of First Nations enterprise. **What is missing from the system is the demand side.** Goods is the demand-side infrastructure that makes every supply-side dollar FAC and Dream Venture deploy actually pay back.

That is the insight to build the pack around. Not "Goods is great," but "the system Minderoo has built is missing its demand-side — Goods is that piece, and QBE has already named it."

## The through-line to JusticeHub

Lucy reads two ACT envelopes in three weeks. They must not feel scattered. The connective tissue:

> Both envelopes propose anchor portfolios — JusticeHub assembles community-led justice infrastructure, Goods assembles First Nations supply chains. In both cases ACT curates a small number of vetted anchors, the funder catalyses, we operationalise. Same logic, different market.

One paragraph acknowledging this in the Goods pack. Not more. Let the pattern register.

## Pitch pack structure (pending Q1–3 answers)

### 1. Cover (one line)
*"Goods on Country: the demand-side infrastructure for First Nations employment parity."*

No subhead. One sentence, black Pretext type, ACT mark.

### 2. Executive summary (1 page, ~300 words)
- Minderoo's thesis: employment parity, systems-level change, First Nations as cross-cutting lens.
- The unsolved piece: supply-side capital and capability assumes demand-side volume. That volume does not exist at the scale required.
- Goods's proposition: curated anchor buyers, vetted Indigenous supply chains, contracted volume, measured parity outcomes.
- The ask: match QBE's $400K Catalysing Impact grant over [term]. Stack unlocks AU$[X]m in the first [Y] months.
- The evidence: $767K already seeded from Xero pipeline, [N] anchor buyers at stage [S], [M] First Nations suppliers onboarded.

### 3. The problem, anchored to CoLI24 (1 page)
- Borrow Minderoo's own report language: $22.3B late-intervention spend, "Participating" precondition failing across 8 of 9 domains, NEET rising from 12.6% to 13.4%, NT cost per head $2,808 driven by remoteness and "levels of Indigeneity."
- Position: the report diagnoses the failure of *participation*, but does not prescribe the demand-side intervention. Goods is that intervention.
- Specificity: Indigenous Procurement Policy exists for Commonwealth agencies; mid-market buyers have no equivalent infrastructure. That gap is where Goods sits.

### 4. The work (1 page)
- What Goods actually does: 12-stage Buyer Pipeline, Demand Register, supplier vetting, contract facilitation, payment rails, acquittal.
- The system-level move: demand-side infrastructure that routes procurement into Indigenous supply chains at mid-market scale. Not a broker, not a marketplace, an *infrastructure layer.*
- The team: Ben, Nic, [First Nations leadership to be named], [advisors].

### 5. The evidence (1 page)
- $767K pipeline seeded from Xero real trade data (not projections).
- Named anchor buyers, named suppliers, where current at what stage.
- Agent layer running in production: Funder Cadence, Procurement Analyst, Invoice Drift Detector, Reconciler. This signals operational maturity.
- JusticeHub as proof-of-pattern: the anchor-portfolio method works (reference the envelope Lucy has just read).

### 6. The ecosystem fit (half page)
- Names adjacents Minderoo will recognise: First Australians Capital (capital side), Dream Venture (capability side), Supply Nation (certification), Murawin Consulting (evaluation), Blak Angels (deal flow).
- Says plainly: Goods does not compete with any of these. Goods routes demand *to* the enterprises these programs graduate.
- One sentence on Indigenous Employment Index as the measurement frame we will use for parity reporting.

### 7. The ask (1 page, structured)
- Dollar figure (to be set with Nic, probably matched at $400K or stepped across 2-3 years).
- Term.
- Deliverables (quarterly reporting, annual parity outcomes on IEI frame, named cohort of anchor buyers and suppliers).
- Matched-side mechanic: how Minderoo's money unlocks the full QBE Stage 2 stack and what Ministerial-grade reporting looks like.
- Governance: who reports to Minderoo quarterly, what independent evaluation looks like.

### 8. Appendices
- Full budget.
- ACT governance (Foundation / Ventures structure, board, First Nations advisors).
- Evaluation frame mapped to Minderoo's IEI categories.
- Data protocol (GHL, Xero, the agent layer) — not as showpiece but as evidence of how acquittal will happen.

## Voice constraints (not stylistic, load-bearing)

From `references/writing-voice.md` (ACT brand alignment skill):
- Curtis method on every weighted sentence: **name the room, name the body, load the abstract noun, stop the line before the explanation.**
- Reject AI tells: delve, crucial, pivotal, tapestry, underscore, "not just X but Y," rule-of-three adjective padding, "-ing" significance tails, "challenges and future prospects."
- **No em-dashes in ACT copy.** Use colons or commas.
- Australian spelling.

From Minderoo-QBE briefing:
- Lead with "First Nations," not "Indigenous" (Minderoo 2024-25 preference).
- Use "employment parity" verbatim (Generation One signature phrase).
- Use "catalytic," "systems-level," "financial inclusion," "self-determination."
- **Never** use "welfare," "closing the gap," or "Voice" unloaded.
- Avoid "empowerment," "reconciliation" as primary frame.
