# Harvest + Farm entity structure research

> Date: 2026-04-24
> Trigger: Ben said "we will make a new entity for The Harvest and need to think about how to run the farm as well and might need a further entity" — flagged for Standard Ledger advice.
> Status: pre-Standard-Ledger brief. Not legal advice. Standard Ledger to confirm all tax positions.

## Summary

The January 2026 entity plan has Harvest and Farm running as activities **inside** A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, with the Pty as the tenant on both leases. This research concludes **that plan still holds for now** and recommends keeping Harvest and Farm within the Pty through Year 1, with defined milestones that would trigger a later spin-out. Keeping everything inside one entity consolidates R&D claims, simplifies admin, and avoids double ASIC fees. Separate entities make sense later when specific triggers fire, not pre-emptively.

## Key findings

1. **Harvest's primary structural concern is food-safety / public liability,** not income tax. The $20M Public Liability already required by the lease addresses the main risk without a separate entity.
2. **Farm's primary structural concern is where R&D is claimed and whether land gets owned later.** While Farm land is leased from Nic personally, R&D stays cleanest consolidated in the Pty.
3. **Australian Pty Ltds commonly run multi-vertical operations inside one company,** then spin out a vertical into a subsidiary only when a specific trigger (external investment, scale liability, separate management) justifies the extra ASIC fees and administrative complexity.
4. **Inter-entity transactions between related companies attract Division 7A and arm's-length-pricing scrutiny from the ATO.** Splitting Harvest or Farm into separate entities adds this compliance burden.
5. **R&D tax incentive claims must be made by the R&D entity.** Split R&D across multiple associated entities requires separate AusIndustry registrations and increases the documentation cost.
6. **Food production liability in Australia is typically managed through insurance + correct Food Safety Supervisor compliance + state food-authority licensing, not primarily through entity separation.** A separate entity adds a corporate veil but does not substitute for correct food safety operations.

## Recommended approach

**Option 6: Start flat, spin out on defined triggers.**

Year 1 keeps both Harvest and Farm inside A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd. The Pty holds the Harvest lease ($20M PL coverage), holds the Farm lease (Nic as landlord, arm's-length rate), consolidates R&D, and files one tax return.

Spin-out triggers to revisit with Standard Ledger at Year 1 review or when any fires:

- **Harvest food production scales beyond cafe scale** (e.g. commercial kitchen, wholesale to third parties, branded product). Food product liability separation becomes more defensible.
- **External investor wants Harvest-only equity.** Investors prefer a single-vertical entity to invest in; spinning Harvest into a Sub-Pty is the standard structure.
- **Harvest revenue passes $500K annually** and is consistent. Above that threshold, the food-safety liability exposure is usually large enough that a separate entity's corporate veil justifies the overhead.
- **Farm buys land.** Land-holding is almost always separated from operating activity; a trust or separate Pty becomes necessary. This is a "when" question, not "if."
- **Farm introduces a co-farmer or community grower arrangement** that requires distinct tax/revenue allocation.
- **R&D claim exceeds the activity base of a single entity** (unlikely Year 1).

## Structural details for Year 1 flat operation

### Harvest inside the Pty

- Lease from philanthropist in Pty's name (already planned)
- $20M Public Liability policy in Pty's name (required by lease Clause 9)
- Public Liability product cover (separate line item) if Harvest sells food or physical product
- Food Safety Supervisor appointed and certified (QLD Food Act)
- Local council food business licence for any food service at Harvest
- Workers Compensation for any staff on site
- Xero department/tracking tag "Harvest" so Harvest P&L is separately reportable within the Pty

### Farm inside the Pty

- Lease from Nic Marchesi personally in Pty's name
- Lease rate at arm's length (market rate; Standard Ledger to confirm); this creates deductible rent for Pty and assessable rental income for Nic
- Public Liability covers Farm activities
- Xero tracking tag "Farm" for separate P&L reporting
- R&D activities happen here; R&D registration under A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd

### Shared infrastructure

- One Pty Xero file with tracking categories for Harvest, Farm, Goods, JusticeHub, Studio
- One ASIC filing
- One company tax return
- One R&D registration
- One insurance broker handling multi-activity policy bundling

## Questions for Standard Ledger

1. Is the Pty's corporate rate (25% base rate entity vs 30% non-base) correct given expected revenue mix? Base-rate status depends on passive income thresholds.
2. Nic leasing Farm land to the Pty: what is the arm's-length rate defensible to the ATO, and what documentation is needed (valuation, lease agreement, evidence of comparable rates)?
3. R&D tax incentive: confirm the R&D registration transfers cleanly from Nic's sole trader (currently grandfathered) to the Pty at 30 June. Any activities that need to be re-registered?
4. Food-safety insurance vs entity separation: is there any ATO or ASIC-side benefit to separating Harvest that is independent of the liability argument?
5. If a Harvest spin-out becomes relevant later, what's the cleanest mechanism (asset sale to new Pty vs shares-for-assets rollover vs ring-fenced division)?
6. GST grouping: should the Pty and any future Sub-Pty be GST-grouped to simplify inter-entity invoicing?
7. Trust involvement: the two family trusts own the Pty's shares. For Harvest or Farm spin-outs, would the trusts own the new entity directly, or would it sit below the Pty? The first option optimises for trust distributions; the second simplifies group structure.

## Caveats

- This is not legal or tax advice. Standard Ledger confirms all positions before any structural commitment.
- Food-safety regulation is state-level (QLD Food Act for Brisbane operations); specific licence requirements for Harvest depend on the food offering and Standard Ledger will refer to the council/QLD Health.
- R&D tax incentive rules change; 2025-26 thresholds and eligibility should be checked against current AusIndustry guidance at the time of registration transfer.
- Public Liability coverage for $20M is the lease-driven minimum. A broker should size coverage to actual exposure across all operations (Harvest + Farm + Goods + Studio).
- If Ben or Nic's family trusts hold non-trivial assets that could be at risk from operational liability, an additional asset-protection layer (e.g. a bare trust or testamentary structure) could be considered; out of scope here.
