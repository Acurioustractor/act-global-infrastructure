## Context recap

ACT Pty Ltd is a new Australian proprietary limited entity operating a deliberately mixed risk profile: a leased physical venue with food service (Harvest), a leased R&D and prototyping site (Farm), a product marketplace routing physical goods from Indigenous suppliers to buyers (Goods on Country), two digital platforms (JusticeHub, Empathy Ledger) and a consulting arm (Innovation Studio). One lease clause already sets a hard floor of $20M public liability. Two directors, growing to contractors then a small PAYG team within 12 months.

The important structural fact for a broker conversation: this is one legal entity carrying six very different risk surfaces. That combination is unusual and most off-the-shelf online products will not fit it. A broker is the right starting point, not a direct online quote engine.

## 1. Insurance types needed

### Public Liability (PL)
Covers third-party personal injury, death, and property damage caused by ACT's business operations. A customer slipping at Harvest, a workshop attendee cutting themselves on equipment, a delivery causing property damage at a buyer's site. Not legally mandatory federally, but the Harvest lease makes it contractually mandatory at a $20M sum insured, and most Queensland landlords now require $20M as standard for commercial tenancies.

### Product Liability
Covers claims arising from physical harm or damage caused by goods ACT supplies. Critical for Goods on Country because Australian Consumer Law treats an Australian importer, re-brander, or repackager as the "manufacturer" for liability purposes even when the actual maker is offshore or upstream. If a fridge overheats, a mattress catches fire, a bed collapses, ACT can be the one named in proceedings. Usually sold as a combined "public and products liability" policy.

### Professional Indemnity (PI)
Covers claims arising from the advice, services, or designs ACT provides. Relevant to Innovation Studio (consulting), JusticeHub (platform outputs that communities rely on), and Empathy Ledger (data handling decisions that could cause loss if wrong). PI responds to economic loss claims and allegations of negligent advice, whereas PL responds to physical injury or property damage. Most consulting contracts, and many government or philanthropic funders, require a minimum $2M or $5M PI as a contracting precondition.

### Workers Compensation (Queensland WorkCover)
Statutory, non-negotiable from the first worker employed in Queensland under the Workers' Compensation and Rehabilitation Act 2003. Covers income, medical, and rehabilitation costs for work-related injury or illness. Also covers sub-contractors you engage and some labour-hire and commission arrangements. Ben and Nic as directors receiving PAYG wages count as workers; directors paid only via dividends typically do not.

### Directors and Officers (D&O)
Covers the personal legal exposure of Ben and Nic for decisions made in their capacity as directors: alleged breaches of duty, insolvent trading allegations, regulator investigations, employee claims against directors personally. Not legally required for Pty Ltds, but creditors, philanthropic funders making large commitments, and future co-investors often ask about it. For a private Pty at ACT's scale, D&O is usually bundled inside a Management Liability policy rather than purchased standalone.

### Cyber Liability
Covers the cost of data breach response, notification obligations under the Privacy Act, ransomware payments, business interruption from cyber events, and third-party claims. Empathy Ledger holds community storytelling data, much of it identifying First Nations storytellers, which is exactly the kind of sensitive personal data that raises a premium. JusticeHub touches justice-sector community data. The Australian Signals Directorate put average self-reported small-business cybercrime cost at over $56,600 per incident for 2024-25.

### Contents, Equipment, and Business Interruption
Covers physical assets at Harvest and the Farm: fit-out, fridges, coffee equipment, kitchen plant, workshop tools, prototyping equipment, retail stock, IT. Business interruption cover pays for lost earnings if a covered event forces temporary closure. Most brokers will package this with PL into a "business pack" at a discount to buying components separately.

### Management Liability (ML)
A bundle product built for private companies that folds together D&O, company legal liability, crime/fidelity, statutory liability, and often employment practices liability. For a Pty under roughly $100M revenue, ML is usually the sensible choice rather than a standalone D&O.

### Food-specific cover for Harvest
Covers food contamination, product recall, and food spoilage caused by power failure or equipment breakdown. Product contamination cover is usually a sub-limit inside the products liability section or bolted on as an endorsement. Broker will ask about cooking method (deep frying and wok cooking push premiums sharply), seating capacity, alcohol, and whether workshops involve food preparation.

## 3. Broker vs direct insurer

**Use a broker.** Not a close call for ACT.

- Activity mix is non-standard. Online direct-to-consumer engines are optimised for single-activity SMEs.
- Vero's SME survey found broker-placed businesses were ~2x more likely to have claims paid in full vs direct.
- Broker fee is usually embedded in the premium via insurer commission, not additional. Brokers often negotiate down from rack rates.
- Specialist NFP/social-enterprise underwriters (Community Underwriting, Ansvar) are broker-only channels. Going direct cuts ACT out of the specialist market.
- Lease compliance certificates and renewal management are standard broker deliverables.

## 5. Key questions to ask the broker on first call

**Fit:**
1. How often do you place for social enterprises structured as Pty Ltds rather than ACNC charities? Does that change insurer appetite or pricing?
2. For a single entity carrying six activities (food venue, R&D site, product marketplace, digital platforms, consulting), single combined policy suite or split per activity? Trade-offs?
3. Can you place with Community Underwriting or Ansvar, and will you volunteer that comparison or do I need to ask?

**Specific coverage:**
4. For the Harvest $20M PL requirement, what's the excess, and are there sub-limits for food service, alcohol, or workshop activities?
5. What exclusions apply for remote or Indigenous community delivery of product? Does the policy territory cover NT and WA communities?
6. For Goods on Country, does product liability treat ACT as manufacturer for ACL purposes, and what's the recall sub-limit?
7. On PI: claims-made or occurrence-based? Retroactive date recommended? Cover if ACT winds down a product line?
8. On ML: does the D&O component include retroactive cover for director acts before the Pty was incorporated (the sole-trader period)?
9. On Cyber: ransomware sub-limit, data-breach notification response included, and how is First Nations storytelling data handled?
10. Renewal date alignment strategy across PL, PI, ML, Cyber, business pack. Consolidate to a single anniversary?

**Structural and claims:**
11. Per-occurrence vs aggregate limits on each policy. Defence costs inside or outside the aggregate?
12. Claims handling workflow. Dedicated claims advocate?
13. How do you price Nic personally leasing the Farm to the Pty? Related-party insurance gotchas?
14. Run-off cover period recommended when Nic's sole trader winds down? (Australian practice: 7 years to align with director statutory limitation periods.)
15. If ACT wins a government or philanthropic contract requiring specific endorsements (waiver of subrogation, certificate to a specific party), how quickly can you issue?

**Before the Harvest lease is signed:**

## 7. Pitfalls and underwriter gotchas

### Coverage gaps at activity boundaries
PL covers physical injury; PI covers economic loss; Products Liability covers harm from goods. A workshop attendee suffering economic loss from bad advice at the workshop can fall through gaps. Brief the broker explicitly on the mixed Harvest nature.

### Sum-insured mistakes
Underinsuring contents and fit-out is common. Ask broker to reference indemnity vs replacement-cost bases.

### Per-occurrence vs aggregate limits
Liability policies often carry per-claim limit plus annual aggregate. Confirm both for PL, Products, PI. Defence costs inside/outside aggregate matters.

### Claims-made vs occurrence-based
PI, D&O, and Cyber are typically claims-made. Policy that responds is the one in force when claim is made, not when act occurred. Retroactive dates matter. Run-off cover when policies lapse matters.

### Run-off cover when Nic's sole-trader winds down
Claims-made policies held by sole trader should convert to run-off cover OR have retroactive date ported into the new Pty's PI policy. Australian practice: 7 years minimum run-off, aligned with statutory limitation periods.

### Retroactive cover for director acts before Pty existed
Pty registered April 2026. Decisions Ben and Nic made in 2025 or earlier that relate to activities the Pty is now carrying forward (supplier conversations, MOUs, product testing) may give rise to future claims. Ask broker to extend D&O/ML retroactive date appropriately.

### Landlord noted as interested party
Match exact contractual wording from Harvest lease.

### Nic personally leasing Farm to the Pty
Related-party arrangement. Disclose openly. Ask how policy treats Farm liability incidents: Nic as third party for PL purposes, Pty as occupier, what PL excludes if Nic is also an insured director.

### Food-service sub-limits
Deep frying, wok cooking, BYO alcohol, live-fire cooking all push premiums and sub-limits sharply.

### Remote and Indigenous community delivery
Some commercial liability policies exclude or sub-limit coverage in remote areas or require specific endorsements. Goods on Country crosses state lines to remote destinations. Confirm territorial scope.

### Policy renewals not aligned
Align renewal dates at first renewal for leverage at renegotiation.

### Turnover disclosure accuracy
Insurers price on disclosed turnover. Undisclosure at inception can void cover. Disclose conservatively high, update broker if revenue exceeds.
