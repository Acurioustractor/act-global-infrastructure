# PICC Photo Kiosk

> 2,491 photos documented. Not by outside photographers — by community, for community, stored on community terms.

**Status:** Active | **Canonical code:** `ACT-PI` | **Tier:** Studio
**Parent project:** [[picc|PICC (Palm Island Community Company)]]

## What It Is

The PICC Photo Kiosk is a community photography storage and sharing infrastructure project — part of ACT's sovereign storytelling work on [[../communities/palm-island|Palm Island]]. It provides accessible, community-controlled photo capture and storage, connected to the broader [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] deployment and the On Country Photo Studio (ACT-PS).

The kiosk sits alongside the Palm Island Photo Studio as part of ACT's infrastructure for ensuring that images of community are captured, stored, and shared on community terms — not uploaded to commercial platforms, not held by visiting photographers, not archived in government databases that communities cannot access.

## The Numbers

As of April 2026, PICC has documented **2,491 photos** and captured **74+ community stories**. The Photo Kiosk is part of how this archive stays alive and accessible to community members.

## The Sovereign Storytelling Argument

Photography on Palm Island has a complicated history. For over a century, images of Aboriginal communities were taken by outsiders — government officials, researchers, journalists, welfare workers — and used in contexts community members had no say over. The archive of those images lives in institutions, not in families.

The Photo Kiosk inverts this: community members can access their own images, add images, and control what happens to them. The connection to Empathy Ledger means consent architecture is built in — [[../concepts/ocap-principles|OCAP principles]] applied to visual media as well as narrative.

The "kiosk" framing reflects accessibility: not a sophisticated media production suite requiring technical training, but a simple interface that anyone can use to capture a moment, access a photo of a family member, or print an image for a wall.

## Connection to On Country Photo Studio

The Photo Kiosk is distinct from but connected to the **On Country Photo Studio (ACT-PS)**: a mobile photography studio for community-controlled storytelling on Palm Island. Where the Studio handles more formal portrait and documentary photography, the Kiosk handles everyday community photo storage and sharing — the snapshot archive that families actually use.

Together they form a two-tier visual infrastructure: professional media production (Studio) and everyday photo access (Kiosk), both under community control.

## LCAA Phase

**Action + Art** — The kiosk is operational infrastructure (Action), and the archive it builds is a material expression of community life over time (Art). PICC's own [[../concepts/lcaa-method|LCAA]] framing includes: *"Some projects span all four phases. PICC work: listen to Elders → curiosity about what works → action through services → art through Photo Kiosk."* The Photo Kiosk is explicitly named as PICC's Art phase expression.
