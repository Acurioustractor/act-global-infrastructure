# PICC Elders Hull River Trip

> Knowledge lives in Elders. When you bring Elders back to country, you bring the whole community's past into its present.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-ER | **Tier:** Studio
**Parent project:** [[picc|PICC (Palm Island Community Company)]]

## What It Is

The PICC Elders Hull River Trip is a cultural programming initiative connecting Elders from [[../communities/palm-island|Palm Island (Bwgcolman)]] with the Hull River area — a site of deep historical and cultural significance for the communities that were displaced and relocated to Palm Island under the Queensland government's Protection Era policies.

It sits within PICC's broader Elders Group Activations: Elder-led cultural programming that brings intergenerational knowledge into active circulation rather than archive storage.

## Historical Context

Palm Island was established as a penal settlement where Aboriginal people from across Queensland were forcibly relocated. Many community members on Palm Island today carry connections — family, language, country knowledge — to places they were removed from, including communities along the Hull River and surrounding regions.

The Hull River trip is, among other things, a return: Elders visiting country that carries their family's history. This is not recreation. It is cultural work with direct consequences for how young people on Palm Island understand who they are and where they come from.

## Why This Matters for PICC's Work

PICC's "Homely" Safe House model rests on a foundational argument: that children taken off country lose contact with the four pillars of identity — family, culture, language, and country. The Safe House keeps children on Palm Island rather than sending them to mainland institutions. The Elders Hull River Trip extends this logic across generations: if Elders have been separated from country, reconnecting them transmits knowledge that the Safe House model depends on.

You cannot preserve children's connection to country if Elders' connection to country has been severed and not restored.

[[../people/richard-cassidy|Richard Cassidy]] (Manbarra Operations) articulates the underlying principle: country is not background. *"Country helps me be, and helps me be me, and helps me understand who I am."* The Elders trip makes this concrete — a supported journey back to country that returns something to the community's cultural store.

## LCAA Phase

**Listen + Art** — The trip is an extended listening exercise: Elders' stories, protocols, and knowledge about Hull River country surface through presence on country rather than through interview. The documentation that follows (photographs, stories captured in [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]], oral history recordings) is the Art phase: bearing witness to transformation, making the invisible visible.
