# SMART Recovery

> Evidence-based addiction recovery support — science-grounded, secular, and self-empowerment focused. ACT's partnership brings storytelling, technology, and community design to peer support infrastructure.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-SM | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

SMART Recovery is an evidence-based addiction recovery program operating globally, offering an alternative to 12-step models. It is science-grounded, secular, and self-empowerment focused rather than reliant on spiritual or disease-based frameworks.

ACT's involvement in SMART Recovery is not running the recovery program itself. ACT contributes what it knows: storytelling infrastructure, digital platform design, and community connection systems. The partnership applies ACT's ecosystem approach to a context where peer support, clinical integration, and stigma reduction intersect.

## ACT's Contribution

ACT works across three areas within the SMART Recovery ecosystem:

### GP Integration (SMART Recovery GP Kits)
Practical toolkits for general practitioners to introduce SMART Recovery in primary care settings. GPs are often the first professional contact for people experiencing addiction. The kits turn routine consultations into potential entry points for recovery. See [[smart-recovery-gp-kits]].

### Digital Platform (SMART Connect)
A digital connection platform integrating peer support, digital access, and community connection. Closes the gap between people who need peer support and the networks that can provide it, while addressing digital access barriers. See [[smart-connect]].

### Health Professional Uplift (SMART HCP GP Uplift)
Training and capability building for health care professionals to understand and refer to SMART Recovery pathways. See [[smart-hcp-gp-uplift]].

## Connection to ACT Ecosystem

SMART Recovery demonstrates ACT's satellite model: bringing ecosystem capabilities (storytelling via [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]], platform design, community connection) to an established organisation that has deep domain expertise but needs support in technology, narrative, and community design. ACT does not deliver the recovery program. ACT builds the infrastructure that makes the program more accessible and visible.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], the SMART Recovery partnership sits in the **Action** phase — ACT contributes storytelling infrastructure, digital platform design, and community connection systems to an evidence-based addiction recovery program with deep domain expertise. ACT builds the infrastructure; SMART Recovery delivers the recovery work.

LCAA arc for the SMART Recovery partnership:
- **Listen** — to facilitators in their own voice (Erin / Sally / April at SMART Recovery Australia); cultural safety gate for Aboriginal facilitator content; what the 20 draft stories and 24 transcripts already in the editorial review say about peer-support practice
- **Curiosity** — what does ACT's ecosystem approach (storytelling + platform + community connection) add to peer support, clinical integration, and stigma reduction? What makes a GP comfortable referring to SMART Recovery?
- **Action** — three concrete delivery streams: GP integration kits ([[smart-recovery-gp-kits|SMART Recovery GP Kits]]), the SMART Connect digital platform ([[smart-connect|SMART Connect]]), and health professional uplift training ([[smart-hcp-gp-uplift|SMART HCP GP Uplift]])
- **Art** — facilitator portraits and voice clips for SMART Connect onboarding; the annual impact summary; the editorial register that lets facilitators speak in their own voice without translation

SMART Recovery is one of the four Y1 bespoke customers for the [[empathy-ledger/annual-field-service|Empathy Ledger Annual Field Service]] (~$40–60K/year, scope overlapping with the SMART Connect build).
