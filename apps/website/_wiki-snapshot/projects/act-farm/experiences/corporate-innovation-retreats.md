# Corporate Innovation Retreats at BCV

> We host leadership teams at Black Cockatoo Valley for multi-day innovation retreats that don't look anything like a hotel offsite. Country is the facilitator. We hold the room.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-corporate-innovation-retreats | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

We run corporate and partner innovation retreats at [[black-cockatoo-valley|Black Cockatoo Valley]]. Teams come here when they're trying to do work that won't survive another conference room — strategy resets, product rethinks, honest conversations about what the business is actually for. We design and facilitate the retreat, host it on the land, and feed people from the garden where season permits.

These are not team-building weekends. We're selective about which teams we take. The retreat works when a leadership group genuinely wants to slow down enough for a harder conversation; it fails when it's used as a reward trip with a strategy veneer.

## Who it's for

- Leadership teams at organisations that want to change how they work, not just where they work
- Strategic partners of ACT who are paying into the ecosystem and want retreat infrastructure to match
- Mid-size teams (8–20) where the whole group can sit in one circle
- Boards or executive teams doing a strategy cycle that needs real attention

We're not for: incentive trips, large off-sites where half the group is on their phones, or companies whose core business is at odds with conservation-first land practice.

## What happens

A typical retreat runs 2–4 days. The shape we hold:

- Arrival, welcome to Country, a walk before any talk
- A first evening meal together with no agenda
- Facilitated sessions broken by land time — no screens indoors, no indoor-only days
- [[the-harvest|Harvest]] pairings: paired meals, kitchen sessions, or a day in Witta
- Closing circle focused on decisions, not themes

We design the sessions with the client. The facilitation craft is ours; the content is theirs. ACT holds the frame; the team does the work.

## How to inquire

Send us a brief about the team, the problem you're trying to work on, and rough dates. We reply within a few working days and always run a discovery call before confirming. We turn down retreats we don't think will produce change. Inquiries go through `/experiences/corporate-innovation-retreats` on the ACT Farm site.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], a Corporate Innovation Retreat at BCV uses Listen → Curiosity → Action → Art as session architecture — not as branding. Here's how the four phases show up across a typical 2–4 day retreat:

- **Listen** — welcome to Country, a walk before any talk; first evening meal with no agenda; the team genuinely arriving before working
- **Curiosity** — facilitated sessions broken by land time; the harder question the team came here to face surfacing in the slowness
- **Action** — closing circle focused on decisions, not themes; the work the team carries back as a real shift, not a deck
- **Art** — the artefact of the retreat itself: a leadership group that returns with a different relationship to the work, or a different relationship to each other

ACT designs the sessions with the client. The facilitation craft is ours; the content is theirs. ACT holds the frame; the team does the work.
