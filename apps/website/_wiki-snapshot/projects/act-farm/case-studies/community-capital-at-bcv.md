# Community Capital at BCV — a year in the valley

> Community Capital paid for a year of accommodation at Black Cockatoo Valley. The Caravan came here. The retreat came home. This is the case study for what a convening program looks like when it's no longer a single event in a rented venue — it's a year on Country.

## What happened

[[community-capital|Community Capital]] began as a convening model: a 10x10 leadership retreat bringing community leaders and social impact capital into the same room. The Phase 1 retreat happened in Bowral, 23–25 February 2025, at a "super fancy wedding venue" in the Southern Highlands. That convening produced what matters most in this work — relationships — and it produced [[the-caravan|the Caravan]] as a studio object.

After Bowral, Community Capital made a structural decision: instead of renting a new venue for each convening, fund a year of accommodation at Black Cockatoo Valley. The program would have a home on Country. The Caravan would live there too. ACT would hold the space year-round. Convenings, retreats, and smaller sessions would run out of the same ground, with the same land practice underneath them.

That's the case study: a convening program that stopped being an event and became a place.

## What we did

- **We gave Community Capital a year of BCV accommodation**, paid for in advance, across our cabins and convening infrastructure. The Caravan came with it — now parked and hosted here for the same 12-month stretch.
- **We held the space in the ACT way.** Conservation-first, unhurried, on Jinibara Country. Community Capital cohorts arrive at a working land-practice, not a conference facility. The environment signals what the work is worth.
- **We separated what ACT holds from what Community Capital holds.** ACT runs the place and the facilitation craft. Community Capital holds the invitation list, the capital conversations, and the relational commitments. The room is ours to shape; the conversations in it are theirs to lead.
- **We kept the retreat travelable.** Phase 1 was Bowral. Future convenings can return there, come to BCV, or land elsewhere. Having a home doesn't mean the program can't move.

## What the work produced

**A physical home for a convening program.** Before this engagement, Community Capital existed as an event. After it, Community Capital has a place — accommodation, caravan, land, hosts — and a 12-month runway to use it.

**Phase 2 follow-through: Catalysing Impact.** Signed 17 March 2026. Up to $400K drawn from a $1M pool funded by QBE via the Social Impact Hub. This is the programmatic extension of the relational work that started at Bowral and continues at BCV.

**The Caravan as resident object.** The mobile dignity infrastructure built for Phase 1 now lives at BCV for 12 months at a notional $550/month (unbilled). It's both a studio piece and a working part of future convenings. See [[case-studies/the-caravan-at-bcv|The Caravan at BCV]] for the standalone case study.

**A repeatable method.** "Make candles while talking about money." The Phase 1 retreat used the candle-making kits (line-itemed on INV-0289) as the activity that loosened the capital conversation. It worked because shared making created the relational ground the capital talk needed. That method now has a home at BCV — the workshop infrastructure is here, not rented.

**Verbatim, to preserve:**

> "ACT does not hold the capital in Community Capital. It holds the room."

> "The gap is not a knowledge gap. It's a relationship gap."

## What we learned

- **Relational infrastructure benefits from place.** Rented venues work for single events. Programs that are trying to change how capital and community meet each other need more continuity than a venue calendar allows.
- **The same room can host very different conversations.** A space that held a 10x10 leadership retreat can host a cohort convening, a funder learning day, a quiet two-person conversation, a Caravan sit-down. That range is the point of BCV, not a compromise.
- **Funding a year of accommodation is cheaper than funding a year of venues.** The Community Capital → BCV arrangement is more cost-efficient than the per-event venue pattern, and it returns the spend to the ecosystem rather than to extractive venue operators.
