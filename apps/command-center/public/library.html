The artefact · how the seven’s work travels · 2028

The Stay Series.
The seven communities’ work, made into a library that travels.

The artefact of the Three Circles is not a report. It is a seven-volume library, one book per anchor community, produced with them over three years. The artefact is the receipt for what the seven have already done. It is also the form in which the next seven communities pick it up. This page shows what it looks like, how it works, and who holds it.

The slipcase holds seven volumes. The Stay Series is organised by method (Three Circles, Living Map Loop, Three Ripples, I Have I Am I Can, Field Canvas, Listen · Curiosity · Action · Art / LCAA, Fire Crescendo). Seven volumes

Seven methods. One library. Co-authored across the seven anchors.

The Stay Series is organised by method, not by place. Each of the seven volumes carries one framework, illustrated by every anchor community’s practice of it. Methods are chosen by the field as the load-bearing frameworks emerging from a decade of community-led work. They are also fluid: each volume is a living text, refreshed as the practice deepens, with new chapters added at every release. Today’s seven, in their current order:

How it works

The library is not documentation about the communities. It is produced with them.

Year 1 goes to listening. Year 2 goes to co-authoring. Year 3 goes to editing, binding and release. Every page the communities don’t want in is not in. Every page they do want in is. OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession) and CARE (Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics) Indigenous data-sovereignty principles at the data layer. Right of withdrawal at every layer.

1

Listen · FY2026

A two-person embedded storytelling team (one North, one South) visits each anchor monthly. No extraction. Recording only with consent, in rooms the community chooses, on devices they can see. Oral history, on Country walks, yarning circles. The result is a corpus of transcripts held in Empathy Ledger v2, revocable within 24 hours.

2

Co-author · FY2027

The team grows to three FTE. Editorial partners are named. Each anchor drafts its volume with the team. Not as consultation. As co-authorship. Elder-approved where elder approval is required. Community sign-off at each draft.

3

Bind and release · FY2028

Documentary post-production. Book editorial. Journal binding for every young person who wants one. A national release event (The Doing Convening, workshop-led, no keynotes). International return visit, two or three peers from Spain, Africa and Europe visit anchor sites.

4

Lives well beyond the funding

The library goes into state libraries, university libraries, community centres. The documentary streams. The platform opens to journalists, magistrates, researchers and funders. The journals stay with the young people who own them. Minderoo’s name sits on the artefact, the convening, and the framework launch.

The journals

One per young person. Hand bound. Theirs.

The journals are separate from the library. Not indexed. Not searchable. Not Minderoo’s. Not ACT’s. One bound book per young person who wants one, owned by them, right of withdrawal at any point. One archival copy is held by ACT for the program’s evidence base, with explicit consent only.

And a travel diary

A ninth thing. A travel diary, for the international ring.

The Outer Ring of the Three Circles includes an international tour: Diagrama in Spain, peers in Africa and Europe. A separate bound volume holds what those visits surface. What Australia learns from Murcia. What Murcia learns from Palm Island. Two cultures, one world working together.
