Three options

All three options back the seven. Option 1 backs them properly.

The three circles only work as one program because the seven are the load-bearing element. We will make any of these work. Option 1 is the recommendation because it is what the communities already voted for by showing up.

Option 1 · recommended FY2026–FY2028
$2.9M over three years

Back all seven. Plus the spine that makes their work visible. Plus the artefact that lets it travel.

Seven community partnerships at $35K–$50K per year, untied. Two to three storytelling FTE working alongside them. Continuing JusticeHub and CivicGraph build so what these seven do becomes evidence for the next seven. The Stay Series, the documentary, the touring exhibition, the journals. Minderoo’s name on the artefact, the convening and the framework launch in 2028.

Option 2 · stepped FY2026 only, with right of first refusal on FY27–FY28
$780K Year 1 only

Lead Year 1 with right of first refusal on Years 2–3.

PRF, Snow, Dusseldorp and Ritchie co-fund subsequent years against demonstrated progress. Minderoo retains the option to renew at the end of FY26 with full visibility of the Year 1 results.

Option 3 · single circle FY2026–FY2028, Centre only
~$520K over 3 years

Fund the data spine only.

Circle One. Continuing JusticeHub and CivicGraph build, plus the six AI agent tools, so what works in the sector becomes visible to the next funder, the next minister, the next magistrate. We assemble a separate coalition to back the seven communities and produce the artefact.

Context

Three years of program costs less per year than detaining one child.

One year of detention for a single young person costs $1.33M (ROGS 2024–25 national average). Australia’s late-intervention bill is $22.3 billion a year and climbing at 7.7% annually, more than double inflation. Child Protection alone consumes $10.2B. The number of young people in detention is falling while the cost per person per day is rising 50%.

This three-year program costs less per year than detaining one child. It is designed to make the case for not detaining the next thousand. Source: Cost of Late Intervention 2024, The Front Project, supported by Minderoo Foundation. thefrontproject.org.au
