# He trusts us. We earned that trust.

> Fred Campbell, Oonchiumpa youth case worker

## Context

**Place:** Mparntwe (Alice Springs) / Atnarpa (Loves Creek Station)
**Voice Owner:** Fred Campbell
**Consent Status:** Public

## Story

Fred Campbell is a youth case worker at [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]]. His job description would say case planning, care team meetings, school transport. What he actually does is show up. Every day. When it is hard and when it is boring and when nothing seems to be changing.

Xavier came to Oonchiumpa carrying formal reports that said he had "limited ability to understand." The system had written its version of him. Oonchiumpa bet on his.

Xavier built a Stretch Bed. From recycled plastic. By hand. The bed is used for ceremonies and bush trips out at Atnarpa. It is a real, functional thing that people rely on.

> "He just was so proud showing them that he can build it. He's quite capable of building that on his own and sharing that onto other kids."

A young person who was told he could not understand built something the whole community uses. That is not an outcome metric. That is a life corrected from a lie.

Fred's work is not dramatic. It is rides to school at seven in the morning. It is sitting in care team meetings where the language is not designed for the families in the room. It is being the person who answers the phone. Trust is not a program component. It is what happens when you earn it, slowly, by not leaving.

> "He trusts us. We earned that trust."

That sentence is the method. There is no manual for it. You stay. You stay when the funding is uncertain and the system is slow and the young person tests whether you are going to disappear like everyone else did. You stay, and eventually something shifts.
