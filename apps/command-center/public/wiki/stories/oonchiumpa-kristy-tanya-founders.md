# Our young people are just collateral in a bigger issue

> Kristy Bloomfield & Tanya Turner, Oonchiumpa founders

## Context

**Place:** Mparntwe (Alice Springs)
**Voice Owners:** Kristy Bloomfield, Tanya Turner
**Consent Status:** Public

## Story

Kristy Bloomfield is Central Arrernte, Eastern Arrernte and Alyawarra Traditional Owner of Mparntwe. Her grandfather was Stolen Generations, taken to the Bungalow as a child. That history did not stay in the past. It moved through families, through policy, through the justice system that now cycles her community's children through detention at a rate the rest of the country would never accept.

Tanya Turner is Eastern Arrernte. She worked in the Supreme Court of Victoria, watching the system from inside its own machinery. Then she came home.

Together they built [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]]. Not from a grant. Not from a government invitation. From cultural authority. From knowing what was needed and refusing to wait for permission.

> "Our young people are just collateral in a bigger issue. It sits on a much broader level and with adults, not with children."

That sentence holds the whole thing. Australia spends $1.3 million a year to lock up one child. More than 60% of those children are Indigenous. The system-wide reoffending rate from detention is above 80%. The money flows. The outcomes don't.

Oonchiumpa runs a 95% diversion rate. 72% school re-engagement. At 97.6% less cost than detention. Those are not projections. They are what happens when community leads.

Kristy and Tanya did not ask the system to change. They built the thing that works and let the numbers speak.
