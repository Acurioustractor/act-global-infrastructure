# Programs. Go to school every day.

> Jackquann, 14 & Nigel, 14

## Context

**Place:** Mparntwe (Alice Springs)
**Voice Owners:** Jackquann (J), Nigel (N)
**Consent Status:** Public

## Story

Ask a young person in Mparntwe what Oonchiumpa means and you don't get a policy statement. You get something smaller and more honest.

"Programs."

"Go to school every day."

Jackquann is 14, from Upper Camp. He raps. He is working out who he is, what belonging sounds like when you put it in your own words. [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]] gives him the space to do that, not by running a music program but by being present enough that a young person can bring what matters to them.

Nigel is 14, from Double Camp. He wants to play AFL. He understands the connection, that consistent school attendance is the path to football at the level he dreams about. What he needs is not a lecture about showing up. He needs someone to walk it with him.

That is the gap Oonchiumpa fills. Not services. Not interventions. Presence. Someone who knows your name, knows your camp, knows when you haven't been at school and comes to find out why.

For Jackquann, it looks like identity taking shape through music. For Nigel, it looks like footy training and a ride to school in the morning. Neither of those things appears in a funding acquittal. Both of them change a life.
