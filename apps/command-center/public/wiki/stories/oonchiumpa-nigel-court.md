# When I'm talking to the judge, I feel like I'm panicking

> Nigel, 14

## Context

**Place:** Mparntwe (Alice Springs)
**Voice Owner:** Nigel (N), 14, Double Camp
**Consent Status:** Public

## Story

Nigel is 14 and from Double Camp. He wants to play AFL. He knows what it takes. Go to school. Go consistently. Build the discipline that lets talent become something real.

But between him and that future there is a courtroom.

> "When I'm talking to the judge, I feel like I'm panicking."

A courtroom is designed for adults who understand its language. It is not designed for a 14-year-old from Double Camp who is trying to explain his life in a room full of strangers with authority over his body. The panic is not weakness. It is the correct response to an environment that was never built for him.

Nigel does not need a better lawyer. He needs someone to walk the path with him. Someone who knows that footy training and school attendance and court appearances are not separate problems but the same life, lived by the same kid, in the same week.

That is what [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]] does. Not legal representation. Relationship. Fred and the team sit with him in the hard rooms and drive him to the good ones. They hold the whole picture because Nigel's life is not a series of disconnected service referrals. It is one life.

He can see the path to where he wants to go. He just needs someone beside him on it.
