# Aunty Bev and Uncle Tony's Story

> Aunty Barb and Uncle Tony

## Summary

Aunty Bev and Uncle Terry share deep memories of Alice Springs from the 1960s onwards, describing a community that was once harmonious with Aboriginal people returning to their communities regularly. They express profound concerns about cultural erosion among younger generations who are disconnected from traditional knowledge through excessive screen time. The conversation highlights traditional protocols around entering country, their father's role as a speaker of multiple languages and custodian of the region, and ongoing struggles to protect land and heritage from development. The couple demonstrates resilience, community advocacy, and hope through initiatives like the boxing program and mattress project for community members. They share stories of large family networks, rodeo culture, and the importance of maintaining connection to land and culture.
