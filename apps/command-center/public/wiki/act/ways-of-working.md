# Ways of Working

> Our way of working is part of the output. How we show up is the first signal of trust.

## Overview

ACT's internal working practices aren't administrative procedures — they're expressions of values. The process is the product. If the way we work isn't respectful, the outcomes won't be either. This article documents the daily rhythms, meeting practices, media governance, and decision-making principles that keep the team aligned.

## Daily Practice

| Practice | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Listen first** | If in doubt, pause and return to community voice |
| **Use LCAA** | Structure planning and delivery through the phases |
| **Use ALMA** | Check consent, authority, provenance, review, and action readiness |
| **Ground language** | Keep it non-extractive, honest, no savior framing |
| **Mark nuance** | Tag high-nuance partner details as `[INTERNAL ONLY]` |

## ACT Voice in Daily Work

When writing and communicating, hold these qualities:

| Quality | What It Means |
|---------|---------------|
| **Warm** | Approachable, human, inviting |
| **Grounded** | Based in practice, not theory |
| **Humble** | We don't have all the answers |
| **Visionary** | We see what's possible |
| **Professional yet rebellious** | Serious about the work, questioning the system |

**Do:** Use regenerative metaphors with restraint, ground claims in lived practice, acknowledge uncertainty, center community voice.

**Don't:** Corporate jargon, savior framing, overclaiming, speaking for communities.

See [[voice-guide|ACT Voice Guide]] for the full language system.

## Recurring Calls to Action

Small, repeatable actions that keep the model real:

1. Apply the [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] framework to every project plan and update
2. Route listening-to-action decisions through Empathy Ledger and [[alma|ALMA]]
3. Keep enterprise pathways practical and community-led (The Harvest, Goods on Country)
4. Protect consent and cultural protocols at every stage
5. Review claims before external sharing

> If a call to action cannot be done this week, it is not a call to action.

## Meeting Practice

### Before
- Clear purpose documented
- Right people invited
- Materials shared in advance

### During
- Listen before speaking
- Note who holds authority
- Capture next actions clearly

### After
- Actions distributed within 24 hours
- Consent confirmed where needed
- LCAA phase noted

## Knowledge Capture

Weekly rhythms:
- Update the agent system with project slugs, consent status, authority, provenance, and review fields
- Capture learnings in the appropriate space (Telegram or Discord)
- Review shareability before any external communication
- Run the [[social-soil-canvas|Social Soil Canvas]] when the field feels noisy — especially before adding work, after a hard week, or before making a portfolio cut
- Keep the public website aligned to upstream truth using the [[living-website-operating-system|Living Website Operating System]] — wiki first for durable framing, EL for live story/media, website for composition

## Field Diagnostics

When projects multiply faster than proof, stop planning and diagnose the field.

The [[social-soil-canvas|Social Soil Canvas]] is the founder-facing diagnostic for this. It looks at what is visible above ground (projects, people, rhythms, product packaging) and what sits beneath it (friction, learning, root-system health). The point is not introspection for its own sake. The point is to produce one decision, one pattern, and one proof line that improve the field this week.

## Media Governance

Empathy Ledger is the source of truth for stories, photos, video, and consent records.

Before using any media:
1. Check consent status
2. Verify current (not expired)
3. Confirm appropriate scope
4. Attribute properly

## Data Hygiene

Every record should have:
- Clear ownership
- Version tracking
- Consent flags
- Review date

## Infrastructure Philosophy

> "If a system is loud, it is not doing its job. Quiet systems create room for people to show up."

- Fewer tools, clearer pathways
- Faster handover
- Quiet reliability
- Infrastructure serves the work, not the other way around

See [[act-architecture|ACT Technical Architecture]] for how this applies to systems.

## Conflict Navigation

When principles seem to conflict, prioritize in this order:

1. **Country sets the pace** — Land and community readiness come first
2. **Community authority comes first** — Their voice, their decision
3. **Share with care** — If uncertain, keep internal

## Process Is Product

How we work reflects what we believe. Every interaction is an expression of values. There is no "once we scale, we'll do it properly" — the way we work now is the standard.
