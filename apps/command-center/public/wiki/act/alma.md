# ALMA — Authentic Learning for Meaningful Accountability

> We do not measure people. We measure whether the system is learning.

## Overview

ALMA is ACT's shared impact-and-learning model. It reads signals at a system level, not an individual level, and feeds learning back into [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] practice. ALMA is a way of paying attention, not scoring. It protects against extractive data practices while enabling genuine learning from community experience.

ALMA is distinct from a reporting framework — its purpose is to keep ACT accountable to learning, not to generate proof for external audiences.

## How ALMA Works

```
Community Stories (Empathy Ledger)
         ↓
    ALMA Signals (system-level patterns)
         ↓
    Learning & Reflection
         ↓
    LCAA Practice Changes
         ↓
    Better Outcomes
```

Community stories live in Empathy Ledger with consent controls. ALMA reads signals from consented stories only. Individual identities are protected throughout — system-level patterns inform decisions, not individual profiles.

## The Six Signal Families

These are the lenses used to read system-level learning:

| Signal | What It Means | Scale |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| **Evidence Strength** | How reliable is the learning? What's the source? | 1 (anecdotal) → 5 (robust, triangulated) |
| **Community Authority** | Who holds authority here? Is consent in place? | 1 (external) → 5 (community-owned) |
| **Harm Risk** (inverted) | What could go wrong? Who might be harmed? | 1 (high risk) → 5 (no foreseeable harm) |
| **Implementation Capability** | Can this be done well? Is capacity ready? | 1 (beyond capacity) → 5 (ready) |
| **Option Value** | Does this open or close future possibilities? | 1 (limiting) → 5 (significant new options) |
| **Community Value Return** | Does value flow back to community? | 1 (extracted) → 5 (community-first) |

## ALMA in Reporting

Every piece of evidence should include four elements:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Story** | A consented narrative or quote from Empathy Ledger |
| **Signal** | ALMA lens notes at system level (not individual) |
| **Shift** | What we changed or will change in practice (LCAA step) |
| **Scope** | What is shareable now vs internal-only |

## Capture Prompts

When gathering evidence, ask:

1. What did we hear, and who holds authority?
2. What system-level shift is emerging?
3. What harm risk or consent boundary must be held?
4. What practical change do we commit to next?
5. What should remain internal for now?

## ALMA Data Anchors

Every ALMA record should link to:
- Story record (Empathy Ledger ID)
- Consent scope and cultural sensitivity level
- Project slugs and place/Country
- ALMA signal tags and LCAA stage

## What ALMA Is Not

| ALMA Is | ALMA Is Not |
|---------|------------|
| System-level learning | Individual profiling |
| Patterns across stories | Scoring or ranking people |
| Community-controlled | Externally imposed |
| Consent-first | Surveillance |
| Practice-changing | Report-generating |

## Field Notes

> "If a story cannot be told without harm, we do not tell it. The signal stays internal until it is safe."

> "Evidence serves relationships, not reports. We count what we can, but we listen to what we cannot."

> "ALMA is not about proving impact. It's about staying accountable to learning."
