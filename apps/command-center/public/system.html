All eight sectors live in one Supabase Postgres. The join keys are abn, postcode, lga_code and storyteller_id. Querying the overlay is a single agent call via the CivicGraph MCP server. The questions in the right-hand column are not hypothetical. Each is answerable today.

Three roles, one system

Each layer has a distinct job. Each one fails alone.

CivicGraph without JusticeHub is a power-mapping tool with no theory of what works. JusticeHub without CivicGraph is a research database with no idea who pays for it. Either of them without Empathy Ledger is data extraction. Together they are infrastructure.

CivicGraph · civicgraph.app

Tracks the money. Engages the system.

  • Resolves identity across 30+ public sources by ABN
  • Maps political donations, contracts, board overlap, geography
  • Surfaces funding deserts, revolving doors, crossover alerts
  • Engages government with ROGS-grade evidence in their language
  • Coordinates philanthropy by making funder-to-funder visibility default
  • Six MCP tools live for agent-driven queries
JusticeHub · justicehub.com.au

Supports the orgs. Showcases the models.

  • Hosts the Australian Living Map of Alternatives (1,155 interventions)
  • Gives every community organisation a discoverable presence with consent flow
  • Surfaces models from history and the ones being supported right now
  • Carries the campaign and advocacy work outward (CONTAINED, Brave Ones)
  • Funder-facing landscape and portfolio analytics
  • Three-tier consent ledger: Public Knowledge Commons / Community Controlled / Strictly Private
Empathy Ledger v2 · empathyledger.com

Holds the voice. Gates the consent.

  • Storyteller corpus under OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access and Possession) and CARE (Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics) Indigenous data-sovereignty protocols
  • Twelve-gate consent chain, 24-hour right of withdrawal
  • Selective consent envelopes per story, per use, per audience
  • Audit trail for every quote, image and theme
  • The lens the other two layers are accountable to
  • Bridges to JusticeHub via storyteller_id and to CivicGraph via organisation_id
Where this overlaps Minderoo’s strategy

The infrastructure compounds across Minderoo’s 2026 portfolio.

The Three Circles is youth-justice-shaped because that is where Lucy and ACT have the deepest community relationships and the strongest evidence base. The infrastructure underneath it is not. Same spine, same agent tools, same consent layer answer questions across most of Minderoo’s 2026 portfolio.

Minderoo’s 2030 strategy: three pillars (Communities · Gender Equality · Natural Ecosystems) and four Impact Missions (Plastics + Health · Crisis Response · Artificial Intelligence · Walk Free). Three cross-cutting lenses applied to every program: First Nations · gender equality · climate. The Three Circles maps below.

Communities pillarThrive by Five · Penny Dakin ED
The same prevention thesis at a different age band. Thrive by Five funds 0–5; The Three Circles backs the seven communities reaching the same children at 5–25. Child protection is 43% of CoLI24 ($10.2B). The Living Brain bridges ROGS spending to ACNC providers and SEIFA postcodes so “where is child-protection spend highest and prevention funding lowest?” returns a place-level answer in one query.
Gender Equality pillarEmbedding Equality · Freedom From Violence · Walk Free
Every confirmed anchor is run by women, elders, or both: Tanya Turner, Kristy Bloomfield, Aunty Ethel Robertson, Rachael Atkinson. The voices in the artefact carry their own gender frame, named by the storytellers themselves. CivicGraph holds the AusTender contract layer; JusticeHub holds family-violence-adjacent interventions in ALMA; Empathy Ledger holds the consented voice. The same infrastructure the Three Circles uses for youth justice answers gender-equity questions across the same data.
First Nations cross-cutting lensApplied to every program
Every confirmed anchor is First Nations or First-Nations-led (Oonchiumpa, PICC, MMEIC, BG Fit). Story sovereignty is enforced at the data layer through OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession) and CARE (Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics) protocols. CivicGraph resolves 7,500 ORIC Indigenous corporations and the social-enterprise registers (Supply Nation, Social Traders). The same lens Minderoo applies to all its work is the lens this proposal applies to youth justice.
AI Impact MissionMitigating harms + tooling
Six AI agent tools live today, hosted on infrastructure ACT controls (not third-party APIs), so a community in Tennant Creek can query their own context without their data leaving their control. Plain-language questions in, plain-language answers out, with citations. The mission’s anti-harm posture (algorithmic bias, disinformation, employment impacts) is built into how these tools were designed, not bolted on.
Cost of Late InterventionMinderoo + Front Project supported
CoLI24 is the policy doorway. Minderoo named the cost ($22.3B/year, $10.2B child protection, $3.4B youth crime, 7.7% growth). The Three Circles funds the response. Every dollar the spine ingests refreshes the evidence base CoLI relies on.
Generation OneIndigenous economic participation
CivicGraph resolves 7,500 ORIC Indigenous corporations and the social-enterprise registers. JusticeHub’s Australian Living Map of Alternatives (ALMA) scoring is Indigenous-first by design. Empathy Ledger v2 enforces OCAP at the data layer. Generation One’s economic and social work runs on the same rails. When the seven young people walk into work instead of remand, this is where they walk into.
Disability inclusionNDIS layer
CivicGraph holds NDIS active providers, registered providers, and participant data by service-district and disability group. Cross-reference with SEIFA and remoteness to surface where supply does not meet need. Same spine, different field. Justice-system-involved young people are over-represented in NDIS need.
Government engagementAcross all priorities
ROGS Productivity Commission spending by state. AEC donations 1998–2024. Hansard ingest live for QLD. Revolving-door tracking. The Living Brain is the argument-quality data layer for engaging Treasury, AGs, DSS and state governments with the same evidence base they cite.
Philanthropy coordinationPRF · Snow · Dusseldorp · Ritchie · Minderoo
10,779 foundations indexed with giving-pattern enrichment. Funder-to-funder visibility by default. PRF, Snow, Dusseldorp, Ritchie and Minderoo can see what each other is funding without needing to ask. Coordination by infrastructure, not by meetings.
The arithmetic line. Minderoo has helped Australia name the cost of acting too late. The Three Circles is a chance to fund what Australia should be investing in instead, on infrastructure that the rest of Minderoo’s portfolio can also stand on.
The showcase function

JusticeHub always has a way to surface what works.

Every intervention in ALMA gets a public profile. Every funded program gets a tracked outcome. Every model from history (back to the early 2000s policy archive) is queryable alongside what is being funded right now. Magistrates filter by postcode and offence type. Funders filter by evidence level and cultural authority. Communities query their own context on a local agent. The same spine answers all three.

The advocacy work (CONTAINED, The Brave Ones, the Three Circles documentary and library) is built on the same data spine. Every campaign argument has a live citation. Every portrait has a consented quote. Every quote has an audit trail. The system is set up so the work is harder to dismiss the more of it lands.
