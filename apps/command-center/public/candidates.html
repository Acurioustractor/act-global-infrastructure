Circle Two · how we identify the seven

Identified, not chosen.
The seven anchors are co-selected with Minderoo from a transparent pool.

The method

Four steps. All queryable today.

Identification is not a survey or an open call. It is a query against an evidence base we already maintain, narrowed by linkage strength and verified on a per-record basis. Outreach is a separate step that only happens after Minderoo has seen the shortlist.

1

Map

Start from CivicGraph: 100,036 entities resolved, 4,729 of them flagged as Aboriginal Community-Controlled or community-led. Filter on operating status, ABN currency and ACNC registration.

Source: gs_entities + ACNC + ABR · refreshed nightly.

2

Filter through ALMA review

Bridge each candidate to ALMA-governed JusticeHub evidence records (1,155 verified youth-justice interventions). Review by portfolio fit: youth-justice intervention count, cultural authority, evidence type. Only candidates with at least one ALMA-linked program survive.

Source: alma_interventions joined to gs_entities via alma_intervention_id.

3

Verify per-record

For each survivor: linkage strength (strong / medium / weak), verification score, justice_funding history, recent state grants where data is available. The result is a per-state top 20, ranked. 138 candidates total across the eight jurisdictions.

Source: justice_funding (FY24–26 QLD) + verification_score · cleanup notes in the JSON file.

4

Reach out only after Minderoo has seen the shortlist

This is the line we will not cross. No community is contacted as a potential anchor before the workshop with Minderoo confirms they are on the shortlist. Outreach uses verified email and phone where present in the record. Where contact data is missing, the verification team retrieves it before the conversation.

Outreach status tracked in minderoo-selection-tracker.json · status values: candidate, shortlisted_s1, outreach_sent, interested, confirmed_anchor.

What happens next

Two workshops with Minderoo. Then the seven are public.

Sessions co-selected, decisions documented, communities consenting. The method that runs in the program runs in front of you, before funding lands.

Session 1 · 90 minutes

Narrow the 138 to ~20.

Minderoo and ACT walk through the verified set state by state. We surface fit signals: ALMA portfolio score, youth-justice intervention count, linkage strength, recent funding, geography. We narrow to roughly 20 candidates that we both agree are worth a community conversation.

Outreach window · 4–6 weeks

Approach the ~20. Listen. Decline gracefully where the fit is wrong.

ACT’s storytelling team approaches each candidate community using the verified contact channels. Conversation is exploratory, not transactional. Communities have full right of refusal. Some will decline. Some will not be the right fit on second look. Minderoo sees the outreach status tracker live throughout.

Session 2 · 90 minutes

Confirm the final seven.

Minderoo and ACT confirm the final seven anchor communities, one per state and territory wherever possible, after each community’s explicit consent has been recorded in Empathy Ledger v2. From this point on, the seven are publicly named in the program.
