# ACT Art Domain

> Art is not a side portfolio inside ACT. It is one of the core fields through which ACT thinks, tests, persuades, earns, and shifts power.

## What This Domain Holds

The `wiki/art/` domain is where ACT keeps the durable curatorial and strategic understanding of the Studio line.

It does **not** replace the canonical project/work pages in `wiki/projects/`. Those pages can still hold the load-bearing article for a work when that work needs a project code, a website route, or operational sync. This domain does a different job:

- explains what the art field is for
- names how philosophy moves through artworks
- shows how the Studio creates innovation, proof, and strategy
- defines how art earns, sustains itself, and funds the commons

## Folder Model

### `art-projects.md`

The curatorial index of the Studio line:

- which works belong to the line
- how they relate to one another
- why they matter in ACT's ecosystem

### `philosophy/`

Where ACT explains **why art matters** at all.

This is where we say clearly:

- art is not decoration
- art is not post-rationalized advocacy garnish
- art is the place where systems become felt
- ACT's philosophy often becomes legible through the work before it becomes legible through policy language

### `innovation/`

Where ACT explains how the Studio creates innovation and strategic movement.

This is where we map:

- how works emerge from LCAA
- how the Studio prototypes public arguments
- how art, data, story, and product strategy feed each other
- how the website should surface works as part of ACT's operating system, not as a detached gallery

### `business/`

Where ACT explains how the Studio survives and succeeds economically without flattening art into product marketing.

This is where we document:

- commissions
- touring / licensing
- fellowship and residency pathways
- artwork sales and editions
- documentation and story/media value
- how Studio revenue feeds the commons and strategic ecosystem work

## Canonical Homes

The rule is:

- **one durable article per work**
- **one live media/story layer in [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]]**
- **one public composition layer on the websites**

This domain exists to help those layers stay coherent.

## How Art Flows Across The System

The art field should move through the ACT stack in a very specific way:

- **Tractorpedia / wiki** holds the durable meaning of a work: what it is, why it exists, what field it belongs to, and how it links to the rest of ACT.
- **Empathy Ledger** holds the live layer: photos, video, story fragments, storyteller portraits, gallery order, and hero selections.
- **Supabase** holds the operational mirror: canonical codes, sync state, public routes, historical rows, and the joins needed by the websites and admin tools.
- **Websites** compose the public surface: works pages, flagship cards, media-led storytelling, enquiry paths, and links back into the wider ACT ecosystem.

That means the wiki stays the anchor. A work should not need to be re-explained differently in each system. The other systems should attach to the same canonical meaning and keep the live/public layers moving around it.

## What Success Looks Like

The art field is succeeding when:

- ACT can explain its philosophy through works as clearly as through strategy documents
- works generate real public attention, invitations, and revenue
- the websites can showcase a coherent Studio line, not isolated art pages
- stories, photos, and video flow from EL into work pages cleanly
- the Studio clearly strengthens the rest of the ecosystem rather than competing with it
