# Y1 Art Release Program — 2026/27

> Eight art projects across the first year, following the Daft Punk release cadence: each project is a "release" — a finished body of work that drops, gets one campaign push, then silence until the next. No daily content. No thought leadership. The work IS the marketing.

Total estimated budget: ~$200K. Funded from: existing grant runway + a Y1 Art Program application (Ian Potter / Creative Australia / Copyright Agency Cultural Fund) + customer co-funding where appropriate.

## The releases

### Release 1 — Brave Ones Phase 1 (April–May 2026)

Five studio-quality B&W portraits of Indigenous and system-impacted young people. The visual counterpoint to [[contained|CONTAINED]]. Brian Duffy / *Station to Station* register: high contrast, soft halation, film grain, deep blacks, silvery mid-tones.

- **Budget**: ~$15K (sittings, studio hire, printing, consent process)
- **Output**: 5 portraits + consent envelopes in [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] + gallery-ready prints
- **Trigger**: launches alongside CONTAINED Mount Druitt (April 20, 2026)
- **Revenue model**: co-funder naming rights ("The [Funder Name] Brave Ones Portrait Series"). Print sales + licensing income flows to subjects and nominated community organisations on transparent published split.
- **Reference**: `wiki/projects/justicehub/the-brave-ones.md`

### Release 2 — Extraordinarily Ordinary (JCF) (May–August 2026)

10–15 portraits of "ordinary people who change lives in simple ways" — the June Canavan Foundation's philosophical centre. Anne Gripper's mother. Ben's grandmother. JCF grantees from Sunshine Coast + East Africa.

- **Budget**: ~$15–30K (lower production value than Brave Ones; environmental portraits not studio, warmth over drama)
- **Output**: portrait series + interview excerpts in EL + a printed broadsheet or zine for JCF's board
- **Tone**: warm, soft, exploratory. "Acknowledgement is free."
- **Funded by**: JCF relationship (no direct ask; production costs absorbed or offset by other customer revenue)

### Release 3 — World Tour Travelogue (June–October 2026)

Ben's photographs and recordings from the Empathy Ledger Tour: Kenya safari → Uganda working week → Lesotho (STAY leg 1) → Amsterdam YOPE. Personal, observational, not promotional.

- **Budget**: ~$20K (travel already funded; this is production — editing, sequencing, printing)
- **Output**: photo book (self-published, 80–120 pages) OR short film (15–20 min) OR exhibition of 30 prints
- **Tone**: travelogue, intimate, no institutional voice. Ben as photographer, not as founder.
- **Decision**: book vs film vs exhibition — decide after the trip based on what the material wants to be

### Release 4 — Resonance Field Installation (August–October 2026)

A gallery show where visitors walk into a room and the Resonance Field is projected on walls — 738 faces drifting, cursor interaction, ambient drone. The brand made physical.

- **Budget**: ~$20–50K (venue, projection, AV hire, opening night, documentation)
- **Output**: 1–2 night exhibition in Brisbane or Sydney. Video documentation for future touring.
- **Venue targets**: IMA Brisbane, Carriageworks Sydney, or a shipping container (CONTAINED crossover)
- **Technical**: the observatory canvas rendered to high-res video (skia-canvas server path from the Resonance Field Phase 3 proposal) + a live interactive station

### Release 5 — STAY Journal Chapter 01 (September–December 2026)

The first bound journal artefact from the [[staying|STAY methodology]]. One community (Oonchiumpa or PICC). A5, hand-stitched, 80–120 pages. Contains: young person's own handwriting, voice transcripts from Empathy Ledger, portraits in Brave Ones register, letters from Aunties/Uncles, drawings, marks, place.

- **Budget**: ~$20–40K (photography, transcription, design, printing, cultural authority sign-off)
- **Output**: 50–100 copies of a physical journal. Owned by the young person. Right of withdrawal at any point. OCAP/CARE at the data layer.
- **Funded by**: Minderoo STAY if funded (EL studio budget $60K Y1); otherwise self-funded from EL customer revenue

### Release 6 — The Buttery: Fifty Years of Coming Home (October–February 2027)

200-page hardcover anthology documenting The Buttery's 50-year history — interviews, archival photos, Paul Kelly connection, timeline, program data, staff/alumni voices.

- **Budget**: ~$50–80K (design, photography, printing, editing, distribution)
- **Output**: printed book (500–1000 copies) for capital campaign distribution to funders, staff, alumni, community
- **Funded by**: The Buttery engagement budget + potential co-publication with a small press
- **Note**: the cinematic documentary (already in build at port 3031) launches alongside or slightly before

### Release 7 — The Buttery Cinematic Documentary (November 2026)

Short documentary (~15–20 minutes) using the cinematic/scrapbook design direction already locked. Supersonic-style, Paul Kelly soundtrack permission pending.

- **Budget**: ~$30K (editing, sound design, music licensing, colour grade)
- **Output**: screened at The Buttery's capital campaign launch event + embedded on the documentary site
- **Status**: already in build (design direction locked 2026-04-02, site at port 3031)

### Release 8 — Resonance Field Public Embed Campaign (ongoing)

Partner with 5 orgs to embed their Resonance Field on their public websites. An ACT-branded "Participating Voices" directory at act.place links all the fields together.

- **Budget**: ~$0 extra production cost (infrastructure already shipped)
- **Output**: 5 live Resonance Fields on external sites + a directory page on act.place
- **Targets**: JCF, PICC, The Buttery, SMART Recovery, Orange Sky
- **Value**: every embed is proof-of-brand and a reference for future customers

## What makes this "art" and not "marketing"

The test: would this work survive the removal of every ACT logo and URL? If yes, it's art. If no, it's marketing pretending to be art.

Every release in this program passes that test. Brave Ones is a portrait series that stands on its own. The Buttery book is a history that exists for the community, not for ACT. The Resonance Field installation is a room you walk into and feel something. The travelogue is Ben's personal photographic record.

The fact that these also serve ACT's brand is a consequence of the [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] principle: art is the first form of revolution. Not the other way around.
