# ACT Art Projects

> Art is the fourth letter of [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] — the Art that follows Listening, Curiosity, and Action. It's not decoration on top of the work. It is the work, made into a thing you can stand in front of.

## The Studio Line

ACT's Studio cluster is **nine pieces** that take an ACT argument and put it into a physical, sensory, walkable form. Some are installations. Some are objects. Some are phones, sheds, vehicles, or rooms. All of them exist because some things are easier to feel than to read.

The order matters less than the lineage: each piece teaches the next.

## Why nine, why these

Each piece answers a question the policy work alone can't reach:

- **Uncle Allan Art** answers *whose stories belong on whose walls*
- **CONTAINED** answers *what does youth detention actually do to a body*
- **The Confessional** answers *what does it cost to be honest in an institutional setting*
- **Gold.Phone** answers *what gets lost when communication becomes a screen*
- **Treacher** asks *where does sound carry the memory of a place that moved*
- **Redtape** answers *what does compliance feel like in your hands*
- **The Regional Arts Fellowship** answers *what happens when an artist lives at a research station for a season*
- **The Vagina** is still asking its question
- **The Caravan** answers *what if leadership development came to you*

## The Pattern

Read together, these pieces are a **slow argument that the policy fights are about bodies, not numbers**. Every Studio work translates a system into a sense. That's why the Studio cluster exists alongside the [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] / [[civicgraph|CivicGraph]] / [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] data infrastructure — they are the same case made twice, once in spreadsheet form and once in a room you walk into.

This is [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] in its most literal form. Listen → Curiosity → Action → **Art**. The Art isn't the publicity. It's the conclusion.

## Who makes the work

The Studio is not a single artist. It's a rolling cast of collaborators around [[benjamin-knight|Benjamin Knight]]'s practice, often partnering with the communities the policy work is about: [[picc|Palm Island Community Company]] for Uncle Allan's work, [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]] for desert pieces, the [[community-capital|Community Capital]] cohort for The Caravan. The works belong to those collaborations, not to ACT in any clean ownership sense.

## Where to find the photos

Most of the Studio pieces have media in [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]]. The viewer (`tools/act-wikipedia.html`) pulls thumbnails into each piece's article via the `projectOrgMap` — open any of the pieces above and you should see photos load from EL.

If a piece's photos look thin, it's because the consent layer in EL hasn't been told these media belong to that org yet. The fix is in the Empathy Ledger admin, not in the wiki.
