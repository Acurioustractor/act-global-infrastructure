# Founder Lanes and Top Two Bets — April 2026

> Decision: Benjamin focuses on product, infrastructure, positioning, and paid pilots. Nicholas focuses on relationships, field practice, art, and place-based pull. ACT narrows its active drive lane to two flagship bets: [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] and [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] + [[civicgraph|CivicGraph]] treated as one stack.

## Why This Decision Exists

The field diagnostic is showing the same pattern in multiple places:

- too many active projects
- low closure relative to insight volume
- blurred co-founder boundaries
- Benjamin carrying too much bespoke support and technical ambiguity
- public proof lagging behind the actual depth of the infrastructure

The underlying problem is not lack of good work. It is a mismatch between what ACT has built and where founder attention is still going.

The strongest structural asset in the ecosystem is the integrated stack:

- story sovereignty
- evidence
- civic legibility
- campaign surface

That stack is already described publicly in the platform-stack reveal:

> *We are not building it. We have built it.*

This decision narrows attention so the field can convert infrastructure into proof, revenue, and handover.

## The Decision

### 1. Founder Lanes

#### Benjamin owns

- product definition
- technical architecture
- knowledge systems and Tractorpedia
- packaging and positioning
- paid pilots and productised offers
- the bridge between evidence, story, and revenue

#### Nicholas owns

- field relationships
- community pull
- art and experiential expression
- place-based activation
- founder-level relationship stewardship
- the emotional and cultural signal of the work

#### Joint only on

- portfolio cuts
- capital allocation
- new major partnerships
- handover decisions
- public claims that reshape ACT's center of gravity

### 2. Top Two Bets

#### Bet One — Empathy Ledger

This is the closest thing to repeatable product leverage in the current portfolio.

Why it stays in drive:

- strongest link to sovereign story infrastructure
- clearest path to packaging and billing
- high mission alignment
- highest potential to turn listening into a reusable surface

#### Bet Two — JusticeHub + CivicGraph

These should be treated as one stack, not as separate distractions.

Why they stay in drive:

- together they form ACT's most differentiated public infrastructure
- they make the structural argument legible
- they connect story, alternatives, and power
- they give funders and government a reason to take the whole ecosystem seriously

### 3. CONTAINED Is the Front Door, Not the Building

[[contained|CONTAINED]] remains strategically important, but as a campaign surface for the stack rather than a third independent flagship.

Its role is to:

- create the entry moment
- make the infrastructure feel real
- move attention from affect to action

If it starts demanding its own standalone operating logic, it is drifting.

### 4. What Moves Out of Benjamin's Lane

Benjamin should not remain the default support engine for:

- general founder rescue work
- operational smoothing for place-based projects
- Harvest / Farm / Goods day-to-day activation
- custom one-off support that does not strengthen the two flagship bets

These projects still matter. They are simply not the center of Benjamin's scarce attention.

### 5. PTO Discipline

From the Field Canvas:

| Lane | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Drive** | Powered now. Maximum three live implements. |
| **Field** | Planted, held, or maturing. Not powered yet. |
| **Shed** | Park, sunset, or hand over. |

Current interpretation:

- **Drive**: Empathy Ledger; JusticeHub + CivicGraph; CONTAINED as campaign surface only
- **Field**: Farm / Black Cockatoo Valley; Harvest; Goods operational growth
- **Shed or handover test**: bespoke support work that does not compound

## What This Changes Practically

For the next cycle:

1. No new custom work unless it strengthens one of the two flagship bets.
2. Productised offers replace undefined helping.
3. The co-founder operating agreement becomes explicit.
4. Benjamin's proof lines are product proof, positioning proof, and revenue proof.

## Proof Required

This decision is only real if it produces visible proof.

Minimum proof for the next 90 days:

- Empathy Ledger billing or equivalent revenue milestone shipped
- JusticeHub / CivicGraph beta or platform-legibility milestone shipped
- co-founder operating split documented and agreed
- one public “we built the stack” argument deployed cleanly

## Review Trigger

Review this decision if any of the following happen:

- a new major funder requires a third flagship to enter drive
- the two flagship bets fail to convert into proof
- founder energy continues to collapse despite the narrower scope
- community ownership logic is being weakened by the cut
