# The Work

Art is the destination. Everything else is how we get there.

We are not a technology company that makes art on the side. We are artists and storytellers who build tools because the tools make the art sustainable. If a better tool existed tomorrow, we would use it. If the tools stopped mattering, we would still make the work. The platforms, the data, the dashboards, the revenue. All of it exists in service of one thing: art that shifts who holds power and who gets to speak.

Story is the thread that runs through everything we do. Not story as "content". Not story as "impact narrative for a funder deck". Story as the oldest technology for redistributing power.

One voice in a room changing what the room believes is possible. Every tool we build exists to protect that voice and carry it further than a single room can reach.

---

**Listen.** We start in silence. Before building anything, before proposing anything, before spending anything we sit with the people who have been talked about but not listened to. Empathy Ledger exists because listening needs infrastructure. Consent needs architecture. A grandmother's voice recorded in a kitchen on Palm Island needs the same sovereignty protection as a government database. We built that. We will keep building it. But the building is not the point. The listening is the point.

**Curiosity.** We lean into what we don't understand. CivicGraph maps $107 billion in Australian public money not because we love data, but because we wanted to know: who funds what, and whose stories are underneath the numbers? JusticeHub indexes 1,155 community-led alternatives to child detention because we wanted to know: does the evidence exist, and if it does, why is nothing changing? Curiosity builds the instruments. The instruments make the invisible visible. But instruments are not the end they are the means to ask better questions about power.

**Action.** We make things happen in the real world. CONTAINED puts policymakers inside a shipping container and asks them to feel what detention feels like. The Brave Ones puts a camera in front of young people the system has written off and asks Australia to look. The World Tour takes the Empathy Ledger to East Africa, to Amsterdam, to Lesotho, and asks: what does listening look like in a place that is not ours? Action is not campaigning. Campaigning tries to win. Action tries to shift the ground so that winning becomes unnecessary.

**Art.** Art is the first form of revolution. Not the illustration of the revolution, the form. A portrait of a young person, printed at gallery scale, hung in a room full of magistrates, changes what those magistrates believe is possible. A field of 738 real human faces drifting in pigment sampled from their own portraits, projected onto a gallery wall, changes what a visitor believes a "data platform" can feel like. A hand-stitched journal owned by one young person, containing their handwriting and their Aunty's voice and a photograph of the country they were taken from, changes what that young person believes about their own worth.

The art is not a by-product. The art is the point. Everything else...the revenue, the SaaS, the residencies, the farm, the campaign infrastructure exists to make more art possible, in more places, with more people, for longer.

---

We are not locked into these tools. We are locked into the work.

The work is this: communities coming together to fuck with power dynamics, to shift systems (justice, child protection, housing, health, land) toward more community power, more sovereignty, more agency. We do this through art and expression and philosophical thinking about who gets to decide. The tools are how we sustain it. Story is how we carry it. Art is where it arrives.

The five-year plan builds a financial base. The ten-year arc funds the tours, the books, the films, the installations, the journals. But the plan is not the work. The plan is the tractor. The work is the field.
