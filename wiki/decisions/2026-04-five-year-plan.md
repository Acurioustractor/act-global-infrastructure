# Five-Year Plan — Voice, Flow, Ground

> Decision: ACT builds a revenue-generating machine over five years across three layers — Voice ([[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] bespoke storytelling), Flow ([[civicgraph|CivicGraph]] as the commercial trading arm), and Ground ([[act-farm|ACT Farm]] + [[the-harvest|The Harvest]] residency and eco-tourism revenue). Both founders invoice retroactively for FY26 R&D work. CivicGraph is positioned as the exit lever for Years 6–10 art and activism. Founder sustainability is treated as a structural requirement, not a nice-to-have.

This plan extends [[2026-04-founder-lanes-and-top-two-bets|Founder Lanes and Top Two Bets]] with a financial arc and operational rhythm. The Founder Lanes decision narrowed what we do. This decision says how long, for how much, and toward what personal and institutional outcomes.

## Why This Exists

The Founder Lanes decision diagnosed the problem: too many projects, low closure, blurred boundaries. This plan answers the question that decision left open: *what does success look like in five years, and how do we fund the art and activism phase that follows?*

Three structural truths drive the plan:

1. **ACT's FY26 revenue is $1.89M invoiced through March 2026.** The studio is already bigger than the documented projections ($350K–$1.2M mature in `visual-strategy.md`). The plan can be built from actual numbers, not aspirations.
2. **Both founders have under-invoiced.** Benjamin has $0 FY26 invoices despite 2,151 commits at 95% R&D allocation. Every $100K of founder invoicing generates ~$43.5K of ATO R&D refund to ACT. This is free money sitting in the tax code.
3. **Story must be the primary use of founder time.** Everything else — admin, invoicing, reconciliation, customer support, bookkeeping — either gets automated via AI agents or owned by an operations hire. The plan protects founder creative energy as a structural priority, not a luxury.

## The Three Layers

Instead of Soul / Wallet / Place, ACT's products map to three layers named in ACT's own vocabulary:

| Layer | What it means | Products | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Voice** | The listening substrate | [[empathy-ledger\|Empathy Ledger]], [[the-brave-ones\|Brave Ones]], STAY journals | Consent-first storytelling. Never sold, always operated. Revenue from bespoke annual engagements. |
| **Flow** | Where capital, evidence, and attention move | [[civicgraph\|CivicGraph]], [[justicehub\|JusticeHub]], [[goods-on-country\|Goods on Country]], [[contained\|CONTAINED]] | Commercial engine. CivicGraph SaaS + Goods retail + JusticeHub grants + CONTAINED tour stops. |
| **Ground** | Where it lands in place | [[act-farm\|ACT Farm]], [[the-harvest\|The Harvest]], STAY anchor communities | Residencies, workshops, eco-tourism, CSA subscriptions. Revenue from land-based experience. |

[[lcaa-method|LCAA]] weaves through all three: Listen is how we enter Voice. Curiosity is how we move through Flow. Action is how we land on Ground. Art is how we make all of it visible.

## Three Commitments

### 1. Both founders invoice FY26 retroactively

$100K each, 95%/40% R&D allocation, through the family trust structures being set up. Combined: ~$87K refundable R&D tax credit back to ACT annually. Compounding: ~$400K recycled cash over 5 years. This is the single highest-leverage financial move available and requires zero product work.

**Deadline:** Invoices issued by end of April 2026. CPA booked within 2 weeks for RDTI claim strategy.

### 2. CivicGraph is the commercial trading arm

[[civicgraph|CivicGraph]] keeps its Stripe tiers ($79–$1999/mo), grows its data moat (100K entities, 672K contracts, 312K donations), and is explicitly positioned as the product that funds the other products' community handover.

The [[beautiful-obsolescence|Beautiful Obsolescence]] language stays on [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]], [[justicehub|JusticeHub]], [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]], [[the-harvest|The Harvest]]. It is never applied to CivicGraph publicly. CivicGraph exists to be commercially valuable and, in Y5, potentially sold to a strategic acquirer ($4–10M EV range at 3–6x ARR) to fund the Years 6–10 art and activism phase.

### 3. Empathy Ledger stays bespoke in Y1

Four existing customers — [[picc|PICC]], JCF, [[the-buttery|The Buttery]], [[smart-connect|SMART Recovery]] — at roughly $50K each annual bespoke engagement. That is $200K recurring from work already in flight, formalised into scoped annual commitments and invoiced on a schedule.

We do not productise into a three-tier SaaS yet. We learn from the actual bespoke work first. Productised version designed in Y2 once we know what the customers actually buy.

## The Daft Punk Communication Rule

ACT's marketing is the work itself. The studio follows the Daft Punk discipline:

- Never explain when you can show. The Resonance Field, Brave Ones portraits, STAY journals, the World Tour documentary — these ARE the marketing. Nothing else.
- No daily posting, no founder-thought-leadership content, no personal brand hustle.
- Release cadence over content cadence. Major bodies of work 2–3 times a year. Each release gets one campaign push, then silence until the next.
- When press asks who runs ACT, the answer is "the studio". When a conference asks for a keynote, we send a film instead.
- Portfolio as album art. act.place looks like an artist's portfolio, not a consultancy website.
- Mystery is a feature. Under-describe. Let curiosity do the work.

This protects the founders from becoming the brand. The work is the brand.

## Six-Months-Ahead Operating Rhythm

The studio works **six months ahead**, always. Every quarter, half the effort delivers work prepped 6 months ago. The other half prepares deliverables for 6 months from now. Nothing gets done in the week it is due.

| Quarter | Delivering | Preparing |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 (Apr–Jun) | Minderoo response, CONTAINED, Brave Ones Phase 1, World Tour | Y1 Annual Atlases for JCF/PICC/Buttery/SMART |
| Q2 (Jul–Sep) | Tour documentation, annual atlases, Brave Ones gallery | R&D tax filing, Y1 customer renewals, Brave Ones Phase 2 |
| Q3 (Oct–Dec) | Customer renewals, R&D filing, Phase 2 outputs | New customers, Field Service productisation v1 draft |
| Q4 (Jan–Mar) | Productisation, new customer onboarding | Minderoo Year 2, Brave Ones Phase 3, farm residency season |

For CivicGraph exit specifically: if Y5 (FY31) is the target window, Y4 Q3 (October 2030) is when informal acquirer conversations start. Six months of prep before the deal opens.

## Ground Revenue — Farm + Harvest + Eco-Tourism Ideation

The residency, workshop, eco-tourism and subscription revenue streams that build toward $1.2M/yr by Y5:

- **Philanthropy-sponsored residencies** — foundations pay $15–30K per residency spot × 20–25/yr = $300–750K
- **Corporate innovation retreats** — 3-day immersives at $15–25K per group × 10–14/yr = $150–350K
- **Innovation workshops** — 1–2 day facilitated workshops at $5–10K × 18–24/yr = $100–240K
- **Eco-tourism stays** — premium $300–500/night × 120 nights/yr = $40–60K
- **Harvest CSA + dining events** — subscriptions $600/season × 200 members = $120K + seasonal events $120K
- **June's Patch healthcare wellness** — hospital-funded staff programs, scaling to $120–200K/yr

The 40% community profit share applies to net, after costs. Founders draw salary as a cost, not a profit distribution.

## What This Changes Practically

1. Both founders invoice for the work they have already done. The R&D refund compounds.
2. CivicGraph engineering time increases. Every hour widening the entity graph is an hour toward exit value.
3. [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] stays bespoke until the customers teach us what to productise.
4. Act-Farm and Harvest shift from "40% flows back / beautiful obsolescence" messaging to **"regenerative capital engine — every dollar generates more residencies, more accommodation, more places for artists and communities."** The values of community stewardship stay. The growth-limiting framing goes.
5. The co-founder operating agreement from [[2026-04-founder-lanes-and-top-two-bets|Founder Lanes]] is ratified with financial specifics.
6. Marketing becomes release-driven, not content-driven.
7. Admin automation becomes a structural priority equal to product work.
