# Strategic Decisions Log

> The load-bearing decisions that shape ACT's strategy. Distilled from 58 decision records in `project_knowledge`, filtered down to the dozen that matter most.

## Why a Curated Log

Of the 58 decisions stored in Supabase, most are routine student-team meeting choices from 2023–2024 development sprints (`ACT-MISC` code). The decisions below are the genuinely strategic ones — the moments where ACT chose a direction, refused a path, or framed a problem in a way that changed downstream work.

## 2026

### PRF Power Analysis: 61/22/17 Split (2026-03-26, ACT-HQ)

Of ~$90M in traceable Paul Ramsay Foundation grants:
- **61%** → universities + large intermediaries
- **22%** → community organisations
- **17%** → ACCOs directly (only 1 of top 10 grants — Anindilyakwa $3.45M)
- **ACF** intermediates $17.6M+
- PRF claims 50%+ First Nations support, but that's strategy-aligned only — actual $220M Indigenous work = 19% of $1.15B total
- Revenue source: Ramsay Health Care dividends (PRF holds 17.58% stake)

**Why it matters:** The Kristy Muir Power letter (Feb 2026) was a genuine signal of intent, but structural inertia in PRF's grant-making means most dollars still flow upstream. This analysis is the basis for ACT's 2026 funder engagement strategy: don't pitch PRF as a transactional funder; pitch them on the structural problem their own data reveals.

### PLACE as JusticeHub Entry Point (2026-03-26, ACT-HQ)

PLACE Network (Dr Luke Craven, CEO) connects every target funder ACT cares about — PRF, Dusseldorp, Minderoo, Ian Potter, Bryan, AusGov — and has no data platform of its own. Ian Trust bridges PLACE Community Council and PRF FNAC. Tennant Creek (Oonchiumpa basecamp) is in the SPSP network.

**Decision:** Position [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] as the evidence platform PLACE communities need, not as a competing initiative. Entry through Luke Craven (systems-change practitioner who will immediately understand the value proposition) rather than through traditional foundation-relations channels.

## 2025

### Spain Trip — Diagrama Engagement (2025-01-16, ACT-DG)

**Decision:** Move forward with planning the Spain trip.
**Rationale:** Engage high-profile decision-makers in understanding [[diagrama|Diagrama]]'s approach to youth justice.

This is the decision that produced the 2025 Spain delegation and the foundation for ACT's [[global-precedents|global precedents]] research thread. Without this trip, the Diagrama partnership stays theoretical.

## 2024

### Reject the Loan (2024-11-07, ACT-DG)

**Decision:** Do not pursue a loan.
**Rationale:** Preference for support from the UK instead.

A small decision with implications: ACT chose relational capital over debt, even when a loan was available. This pattern (prefer aligned capital over neutral capital) recurs across 2025–2026 funding decisions.

### YJ Legislative Push (2024-05-22, ACT-DG)

**Decision:** Pursue legislative changes for youth justice.
**Rationale:** Improve the system based on experiences from Spain.

The genesis decision for ACT's [[youth-justice-reform|youth justice reform]] advocacy posture — shifting from program delivery to systems change.

## 2023

### Central Tech Platform (2023-05-29, ACT-JH)

**Decision:** Develop a central technology platform to support individual agency and data control.
**Rationale:** Enable better integration and user experience in the human services system.
**Source:** TYSON Bank workshop.

**This is the genesis decision for what became [[justicehub|JusticeHub]].** Everything downstream — ALMA, the intervention portfolio, the funding landscape work — traces to this single sentence.

### ACT Supports Confit Pathways (2023-03-29, ACT-EL)

**Decision:** A Curious Tractor will support the pitch and build of the Confit Pathways application.
**Rationale:** Address pain points in scaling the program and enhance community support.

The first formal commitment to the [[confit-pathways|Confit Pathways]] partnership — a pattern ACT would repeat: take responsibility for the technology layer of someone else's mission rather than running parallel programs.
