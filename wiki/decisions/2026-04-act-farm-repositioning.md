# Act-Farm Repositioning — From Obsolescence to Regenerative Capital Engine

> Decision: Act-Farm's public messaging shifts from "40% of profits flow to community ownership / designed for beautiful obsolescence" to **"every dollar Act-Farm generates builds more residencies, more accommodation, more places for artists, researchers and communities to do transformative work."** The values of community stewardship stay. The growth-limiting framing goes.

## Why this change

The current brand language for [[act-farm|ACT Farm]] and [[the-harvest|The Harvest]] emphasises handover and sunset:

> *"40% of profits flow directly to community ownership. Every tool comes with a sunset clause."*

> *"We design for the day this land is held by the community it serves."*

This language is philosophically consistent with [[beautiful-obsolescence|Beautiful Obsolescence]] and it was right for the studio's early identity — it signalled that ACT is not extractive.

But it is now growth-limiting for the specific products where ACT needs capital to compound:

1. **Act-Farm needs investment to build more accommodation.** The 150-acre property on Jinibara Country has capacity for eco-cottages, artist studios, and workshop spaces that don't yet exist. Capital investors and philanthropy partners won't fund infrastructure they're told will be handed away.
2. **The Harvest needs a revenue-generating model, not a cost centre.** At $550–900K capex to build the physical site, it needs to demonstrate returning capital, not promising obsolescence.
3. **Residency networks scale.** A single 150-acre site has a revenue ceiling (~$600–900K gross). A portfolio of 3–5 sites operated or co-managed by ACT breaks that ceiling. That requires reinvestment, not distribution.

## What changes

### Language removed (from Act-Farm and Harvest brand)

- ❌ "40% of profits flow to community ownership"
- ❌ "Beautiful obsolescence" as a descriptor for the residency/property businesses
- ❌ "Designed for the day this land is held by the community it serves" as a primary positioning statement
- ❌ Any framing that suggests the farm is temporary infrastructure

### Language added

- ✅ **"Act-Farm is a regenerative capital engine.** Every stay, workshop, and residency we host funds the next one. By 2030 we want to operate or partner with 10 residencies like this — each grounded in its own place, co-governed with Traditional Owners, and generating the capital that builds the next."
- ✅ **"The Harvest grows the ecosystem."** Revenue from CSA memberships, dining events, and farm-to-table operations reinvests into community programming, therapeutic horticulture, and the next Harvest site.
- ✅ **"Jinibara Country stewardship is permanent, not transitional."** First Nations cultural authority over this land is non-negotiable — the governance stays community-led. What changes is the business model underneath: from charity pass-through to revenue engine.

### Language preserved (across the rest of ACT)

The [[beautiful-obsolescence|Beautiful Obsolescence]] principle remains **fully intact** for:
- [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] — designed to be community-forkable and operated without ACT
- [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] — open-source models and data
- [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]] — production assets and revenue-generating capacity left with communities
- Every software tool ACT builds — "all APIs standardised, all code open-sourceable"

The distinction is structural: **tools are designed for handover. Land-based businesses are designed for compounding.**

### Capital raising enabled

With a "regenerative capital engine" framing (not a charity sunset clause), Act-Farm can credibly approach:
- **Impact investors** (SEFA, IBA up to $5M) for accommodation infrastructure
- **Government programs** (Building Better Regions Fund $500–800K, SEDI $120K) for site development
- **Philanthropic partners** for named residency sponsorships (e.g. "The [Foundation] Artist Studio at Black Cockatoo Valley")
- **R&D tax incentive** — residency hosting of researchers/technologists prototyping new methods counts as eligible R&D activity (43.5% refundable offset on qualifying spend)

### Portfolio pathway

| Year | Sites | Revenue ceiling (gross) |
|---|---|---|
| Y1 | 1 (BCV/Jinibara only) | $200K |
| Y3 | 1 fully operational + 1 partnership-model site | $800K |
| Y5 | 2 operational + 1–2 partnership sites | $1.5M |
| Y7–10 | 3–5 sites (mix of owned/operated and partnership) | $2–4M |

Each new site follows the same model: ACT brings operations + brand + booking infrastructure, partner brings land + local relationships. Revenue share, not purchase. This is how the network compounds without ACT buying property.

## Governance

The governance concern is real: if we stop saying "40% flows to community", what replaces it as a structural commitment?

**Proposed replacement structure** (for CPA + lawyer to validate):

1. **ACT Pty Ltd** operates the residencies. Founders draw market-rate salary as a cost.
2. **Net profit** (after costs) is allocated:
   - Community programming reinvestment (therapeutic horticulture, First Nations heritage work, volunteer programs): **minimum 30%**
   - Site expansion and accommodation capital: **minimum 30%**
   - Founder distributions (through trusts): **up to 40%**
3. **Traditional Owner advisory** — Jinibara cultural authority retained in governance for all land-based decisions. Not a token committee; an actual veto on activity types and cultural programming.
4. **Annual transparency report** — published, showing where money flowed. Generated from the Resonance Field data (same as EL Annual Atlas but for Act-Farm).

This replaces "40% to community ownership" with a three-way split that protects community programming, enables site expansion, AND sustains the founders. More honest than a percentage that sounds generous but doesn't scale.

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Brand perception: "ACT is no longer community-first" | The change applies ONLY to Act-Farm/Harvest. EL/JH/Goods keep obsolescence language. The Annual Transparency Report makes the split public. |
| Nicholas may disagree (Act-Farm is in his lane per [[2026-04-founder-lanes-and-top-two-bets\|Founder Lanes]]) | This decision is flagged as requiring Nic agreement. See one-pager conversation. |
| Investors may expect eventual full commercial control | Mission-lock the Pty Ltd structure (B Corp certification or constitutional objects clause). Legal to confirm. |
| Traditional Owners may see the reframe as reduced commitment | Engage Jinibara cultural authority directly and co-design the governance language. Their veto on activity types is non-negotiable. |

## Review trigger

Review this decision if:
- Jinibara cultural authority expresses concern about the reframe
- Act-Farm fails to reach $200K gross in Y1 (the model may not work)
- A community partner publicly perceives the change as extractive
- The Pty Ltd mission-lock structure is not legally achievable
