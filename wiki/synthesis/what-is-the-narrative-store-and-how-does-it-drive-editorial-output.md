# What is the narrative store and how does it drive editorial output?

> Synthesised from the wiki on 2026-04-13. This article was generated by a query and saved back into Tractorpedia so the next question can build on top of it.

## Answer

## The Narrative Store

The narrative store at \`wiki/narrative/\` is a structured claims database that tracks what ACT has said publicly, where, with what evidence, and how it landed. It turns editorial from a blank-page problem into an assembly problem.

### Structure

\`\`\`

  justicehub/
    INDEX.md                    — all claims, channels, evidence scores
    STRATEGY-REVIEW.md          — what is landing, what is not, where to push

### What a Claim File Contains

Each claim file documents:
- **The claim itself** — a specific argument or stat (e.g. "it costs $1,500/night to detain a young person vs $150/night for community-based support")
- **Evidence** — which wiki articles, EL stories, data sources back it up, with strength rating
- **Channel history** — where this claim has been published (LinkedIn, grant application, pitch deck, op-ed)
- **Deployment dates** — when it was used, preventing over-repetition
- **Resonance notes** — what response it got, from whom

### How It Drives Editorial

**Without the narrative store:** Ben opens LinkedIn, stares at blank page, tries to remember what to write about.

**With the narrative store:**
1. \`/wiki narrative status justicehub\` — see all 7 claims, which channels they have been deployed to, evidence strength
2. \`/wiki narrative draft justicehub --frame moral --channel linkedin\` — AI assembles a draft from the strongest unused claims for that channel
3. Ben edits for voice, publishes
4. \`/wiki narrative log "tier-pricing beats detention costs" linkedin\` — claim is marked as deployed on that channel

**For grant applications:**
1. \`/wiki narrative funder minderoo\` — pulls funder profile, cross-references against ACT's claims
2. Assembles a pitch brief showing which claims match funder priorities
3. Evidence links point back to wiki articles and EL stories for the full backing

**For strategy review:**
1. \`/wiki narrative review justicehub\` — weekly editorial retro
2. Which claims are landing? Which channels are working?
3. Where are the gaps? What should we say next?

### Ingestion Pipeline

New claims enter through:
- \`scripts/narrative-ingest.mjs <path>\` — extracts claims from essays, pitches, posts
- \`scripts/narrative-refresh.mjs\` — regenerates INDEX files from claim files
- \`scripts/narrative-strategy-review.mjs\` — generates STRATEGY-REVIEW from deployment history

### The Compounding Effect

Over time, the narrative store becomes a strategic asset:
- Every claim is tested and scored across channels
- Funder profiles accumulate resonance data
- The AI gets better at assembling drafts because the claims library is richer
- Grant applications take hours instead of days because the evidence is pre-linked
- No claim is ever made without traceable backing — critical for credibility
