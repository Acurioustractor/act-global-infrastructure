# What are the specific opportunities to deploy the Third Reality in the next 12 months?

> Synthesised from the wiki on 2026-04-13. This article was generated by a query and saved back into Tractorpedia so the next question can build on top of it.

## Answer

## Twelve-Month Opportunity Map

The Third Reality framework is built. The platforms exist. The question is: where does it meet the world in the next 12 months?

### Tier 1 — Already in motion (convert within 3 months)

#### 1. Judges on Country → National Judicial Education
**Status:** 55 judges just went through it. Postcards designed. QR to JusticeHub working.
**Next move:** Formal proposal to the National Judicial College of Australia (NJCA) to integrate the card deck + experience into their standard professional development. Also approach state judicial commissions (NSW, VIC, QLD first — largest bench populations).
**Revenue:** \$50-100K per engagement. R&D offset on development.
**Third Reality in action:** Postcard (Voice) + JusticeHub search (Evidence) + CivicGraph gap data (Data).

#### 2. Empathy Ledger Y1 Bespoke Customers
**Status:** PICC, JCF, The Buttery, SMART Recovery — four orgs, \$50K each, \$200K total.
**Next move:** Formalise annual engagement agreements. Invoice on schedule. Document the bespoke work as R&D evidence.
**Revenue:** \$200K recurring from Y1. Foundation for productised EL in Y2.
**Third Reality in action:** Each customer deploys all three layers in their context. PICC uses EL (Voice) + community data (Evidence) + government reporting (Data) to produce annual reports that funders can't ignore.

#### 3. R&D Tax Offset — FY26 Claim
**Status:** \$251K eligible spend tracked. \$109K potential offset. Both founders need to invoice retroactively (\$100K each).
**Next move:** CPA appointment within 2 weeks. Founder invoices by end of April. AusIndustry registration.
**Revenue:** \~\$87K refund (combined founder R&D allocation). Recurring annually.
**Third Reality in action:** The spending intelligence system tags every dollar to a project code, which maps to R&D eligibility. The system IS the evidence.

### Tier 2 — Ready to pitch (convert within 6 months)

#### 4. Minderoo Foundation — Three Circles / STAY
**Status:** Pitch package exists (wiki/projects/justicehub/three-circles.md). \$2.9M / 3 years. 10 anchor communities.
**Next move:** Warm introduction via someone who's been through CONTAINED or Judges on Country. The experience is the pitch — not the deck.
**Revenue:** \$850K H2 Y1, \$1.14M Y2-3.
**Third Reality in action:** The entire Three Circles program IS the Third Reality deployed at national scale. CivicGraph (data spine) + Living Map (evidence) + Empathy Ledger (stories) + STAY journals (art) = the library that ends the program.

#### 5. CivicGraph SaaS — First Commercial Customers
**Status:** \$79-\$1999/mo Stripe tiers. 587K entities. 21 data sources.
**Next move:** Target justice sector intermediaries: law firms doing pro bono, philanthropic advisors, state government policy teams. The pitch: "You're making decisions with partial information. We have the full picture."
**Revenue:** \$400K MRR target Y1.
**Third Reality in action:** CivicGraph IS the systemic data layer. Customers pay for the data. Communities benefit from what the data reveals.

#### 6. Australia Council — Brave Ones Portraits
**Status:** Phase 0 (placeholder). Experience design complete. Pilot budget \~\$15K.
**Next move:** Apply for Creative Australia project funding. Position as "the counter-mugshot archive" — a national photographic project that redefines how Indigenous young people are seen.
**Revenue:** \$80K Phase 2 (25 portraits + first gallery show).
**Third Reality in action:** Each portrait is simultaneously art (Voice), evidence (a young person's documented journey via EL), and data (a new ALMA node in the justice evidence base).

### Tier 3 — Build toward (convert within 12 months)

#### 7. State Justice Department Commissioning Intelligence
**Status:** CivicGraph has the gap data. JusticeHub has the program evidence. No state government has been pitched.
**Next move:** Produce a sample gap analysis for one state (QLD — best data coverage). Show: "Here's where you have no funded alternatives to detention. Here's what exists in neighbouring regions. Here's what it would cost vs detention."
**Revenue:** Government consulting at \$150-300K per engagement.
**Third Reality in action:** Gap analysis (Data) + program evidence (Evidence) + community stories from those regions (Voice) = a commissioning recommendation no bureaucrat could produce alone.

#### 8. International — UK or NZ Pilot
**Status:** CivicGraph UK market entry research exists (wiki/research/civicgraph-uk-market-entry.md). Diagrama partnership in Spain. The pattern is not Australia-specific.
**Next move:** UK has comparable public data infrastructure (Companies House, Charity Commission, government contracts). Build a proof-of-concept UK CivicGraph instance. Approach UK justice reinvestment orgs.
**Revenue:** International SaaS + consulting.
**Third Reality in action:** Same three layers, different jurisdiction. Proves the framework travels.

#### 9. University Research Partnership
**Status:** No formal partnership. ANU law students already work with Oonchiumpa (Kristy's bridge program).
**Next move:** Approach ANU, UNSW, or Melbourne with: "We have 587K linked civic entities, 226 consent-governed storytellers, and 1,775 verified community models. No university has access to this. Let's write papers together."
**Revenue:** ARC funding, PhD candidates (free labour), credibility.
**Third Reality in action:** Academic rigour validates the framework. Publications make it citable. The university gets data access. ACT gets legitimacy.

#### 10. Media Feature — Tractorpedia as Story
**Status:** 206 articles, 17 synthesis pieces, clean health. The wiki is extraordinary for an org this size.
**Next move:** Pitch to The Saturday Paper, Guardian Australia, or ABC: "A two-person organisation in the Sunshine Coast hinterland built the most comprehensive civic intelligence platform in Australia. Here's how."
**Revenue:** None directly. But media coverage converts to funder attention, partner interest, and storyteller trust.
**Third Reality in action:** The story of ACT IS a Third Reality story — systemic data about the justice system + sovereign narratives from communities + evidence that alternatives work, all held in one ecosystem.

### The filter for every opportunity

Before pursuing any of these, ask:

1. **Does it produce Third Reality output?** (Data + Evidence + Voice)
2. **Does it create an experience people will tell others about?**
3. **Does it compound?** (Each round makes the next one better)
4. **Is the R&D eligible?** (43.5% back on development)
5. **Does it move toward Beautiful Obsolescence?** (Can communities run it without ACT eventually?)

If all five: pursue aggressively.
If four: pursue.
If three or fewer: probably admin dressed up as opportunity.
