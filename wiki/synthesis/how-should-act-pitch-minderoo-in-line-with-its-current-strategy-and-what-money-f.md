# How should ACT pitch Minderoo in line with its current strategy, and what money framing fits best?

> Synthesised from the wiki on 2026-04-21. This article was generated by a query and saved back into Tractorpedia so the next question can build on top of it.

## Answer

## Short answer

The strongest Minderoo pitch is **not** "fund JusticeHub" and it is **not** "fund the library" and it is definitely not "fund an AI platform."

The strongest pitch is: **fund a place-based, ACCO-led early-intervention infrastructure that bends a late-intervention cost curve Minderoo already publicly owns.**

As of **21 April 2026**, this is the closest fit to Minderoo's current public strategy based on their official [Communities page](https://www.minderoo.org/communities/), [2030 Strategy PDF](https://cdn.minderoo.org/assets/new/documents/Minderoo-Foundation-Strategy.pdf), [Cost of Late Intervention media release from 27 August 2025](https://www.minderoo.org/media/australia-s-spiraling-cost-of-missing-early-warning-signs-in-our-children/), [Nicola Forrest's statement from 27 August 2025](https://www.minderoo.org/media/every-child-no-matter-their-postcode-deserves-the-best-start-in-life/), and the [2025 Impact Report](https://report.minderoo.org/).

## What Minderoo is actually telling us it wants

Minderoo's current Communities focus area is explicit about five things:

- all children reaching their full potential in thriving communities
- local knowledge and lived experience
- place-based engagement
- stronger child and family support ecosystems
- vibrant, connected communities

Its 2025 impact language is also explicit about the four roles it plays: **Partner, Develop, Advocate, Generate Evidence**.

That means ACT should pitch The Three Circles as a program that lets Minderoo do all four roles at once:

- **Partner** with 5 confirmed ACCO anchors and 2-3 more co-selected with community consent
- **Develop** a transferable place-based method across those anchors, the Judges on Country loop, and the Brave Ones practice
- **Generate evidence** through JusticeHub, CivicGraph, Empathy Ledger, and the live anchor dataset
- **Advocate** through the national artefact, magistrate-facing tools, and policy visibility that follows from them

This is a much cleaner fit than leading with product, design, or art.

## The most meaningful oar

The most meaningful oar is **not Circle One** and it is **not Circle Three**.

It is **Circle Two: the relational hold inside the anchor communities.**

Why:

1. It maps directly to Minderoo's **Place-based Engagement** body of work.
2. It is where the Communities strategy is strongest: local knowledge, lived experience, community power, vibrant connected places.
3. It lets ACT translate youth justice into an **early-intervention** story instead of a detention-response story.
4. It makes the rest of the stack make sense. JusticeHub becomes the evidence layer underneath the anchor work. The Brave Ones becomes the qualitative trace layer and human proof. The library becomes the advocacy artefact at the end, not the thing being bought at the start.

If ACT is asked what the program really is in one sentence, the cleanest answer is:

**A three-year partnership to hold community-led alternatives long enough for them to bend the child-protection-to-youth-justice trajectory, prove what works, and make it visible nationally.**

That is much closer to Minderoo's official language than "a national justice platform" or "an art-and-data library."

## The decisive wedge: child protection to youth justice

The strongest strategic wedge is not detention by itself. It is the transition from **child protection into youth justice**.

Minderoo's COLI framing makes this powerful because their own public material says:

- late intervention costs **$22.3B annually**
- that figure is up **47% since 2019**
- child protection is the largest line item
- youth crime is the second-largest line item
- acting earlier is about "spending smarter," not merely spending more

So ACT should make one move very clearly:

**We are upstream of the point where child protection becomes youth justice.**

That turns the pitch from a justice special case into a Communities portfolio bridge:

- early-years logic
- wrap-around supports
- family and community environment
- later youth-justice consequences

That bridge is what makes Lucy's internal case easier.

## What to lead with, in order

### 1. Start with Communities, not JusticeHub

Open with the five ACCO anchors and the place-based method. JusticeHub is not the opening noun. It is the evidence infrastructure under the method.

### 2. Use Minderoo's own portfolio grammar

The cleanest order is:

- **Place-based engagement**: five confirmed anchors, two to three more co-selected
- **Improving child and family supports**: wrap-around community hold, not fragmented services
- **Generate evidence**: the live map, service legibility, anchor data, storyteller corpus
- **Advocate**: judges, magistrates, national artefact, public proof

### 3. Use The Brave Ones as proof, not as the main ask

The Brave Ones matters because it carries the human trace layer that makes the system memorable and honest. But it should not be the top-line reason for the grant.

### 4. Make the library the destination, not the starting price tag

The library is a strong conceptual hook. It is not the first budget category Lucy should have to defend internally.

## The best money framing

### Recommended primary ask

**Lead with the canonical `Three Circles` ask: $2.9M over 3 years.**

Why this is the best fit:

- it is already the canonical ask in [[three-circles|The Three Circles]]
- it is sized as a real **multi-year partnership**, which fits Minderoo's 2025 "Pay What It Takes" posture and three-year budget language
- it is big enough to fund the actual place-based hold, evidence layer, and national translation
- it does not overweight the artefact and publishing layer the way the older $3.43M Crescendo package does
- it is easier to defend as a Communities investment than a larger, more library-forward number

### Recommended entry point

If Minderoo needs a staged internal decision, the best on-ramp is:

**$780K for Year 1, with right-of-first-refusal or indicative hold on Years 2-3.**

This is already the clean internal gate in the campaign brief and the canonical page. It gives Minderoo a credible Year 1 commitment without forcing ACT into a one-year-only posture.

### Recommended external budget story

Narrate the $2.9M in Minderoo's role language, not ACT's internal production language:

- **Partner / place-based hold:** $1.4M
  This is the anchor communities, domestic exchanges, and relational infrastructure.
- **Generate evidence:** $0.8M
  This is the living map, JusticeHub, Empathy Ledger, CivicGraph integration, and the measure/learn layer.
- **Develop + Advocate:** $0.7M
  This is Judges on Country, national artefacts, documentary/book/platform release, and policy translation.

This is cleaner for Lucy than opening with line items like journals, printing, or media production.

## What not to do with the money story

### Do not lead with $3.43M

The older `minderoo-pitch-package` framing defaults to **Crescendo = $3.43M / 3 years**. That is still useful as a stretch or expanded version, but it is not the best opening number if the goal is clean strategy fit.

The problem is not that $3.43M is impossible. The problem is that it foregrounds the library and production envelope too early, which makes the ask feel more like a large cultural product build than a place-based Communities investment.

### Do not lead with the single-circle infrastructure ask

The `three-circles` page includes a single-circle option. That is useful as a rescue path. It is a weak opening move because it strips out the exact place-based relational work Minderoo is most clearly saying it wants.

### Do not frame the grant as software build

Minderoo's public role language leaves room for evidence and tools, but the Communities strategy is not asking for a speculative platform build. The tech only works in the pitch when it is visibly subordinate to the community hold.

## The practical recommendation

If ACT wants the cleanest, most strategy-native sentence to carry into the next Lucy conversation, it is this:

**We are asking Minderoo to make a three-year Communities investment in 5 confirmed ACCO anchors and 2-3 more co-selected with community consent, with JusticeHub as the evidence layer and The Brave Ones as the human trace layer, to bend the child-protection-to-youth-justice trajectory that Minderoo's own $22.3B late-intervention work has already made undeniable.**

And if Lucy asks the number:

- **Primary answer:** `$2.9M over 3 years`
- **If staged:** `$780K for Year 1, with pathway to the full three-year partnership`
- **Only if invited upward:** `$3.43M` as the expanded Crescendo expression

That is the strongest strategic fit currently available in the repo.
