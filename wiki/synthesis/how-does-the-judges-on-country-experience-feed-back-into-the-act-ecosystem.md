# How does the Judges on Country experience feed back into the ACT ecosystem?

> Synthesised from the wiki on 2026-04-13. This article was generated by a query and saved back into Tractorpedia so the next question can build on top of it.

## Answer

## The Judges on Country Feedback Loop

The 30-minute experience in Mparntwe is not a one-off event. It is the beginning of a feedback loop that makes every part of the ACT ecosystem smarter.

### What flows out of the room

When 55 judges leave that room, several things have happened that feed back into the system:

**1. New stories for Empathy Ledger**

The judges themselves become storytellers. Their reactions — the moment of realisation, the pause after the reveal, what they say to each other afterward — are a new category of EL content. Not the young people's stories retold, but the decision-makers encountering those stories for the first time.

With consent, post-experience reflections from judges become:
- New EL articles at /stories/empathy-ledger/
- Evidence for the narrative claim store (what landed, what shifted)
- Content for the next round of postcards (a judge's voice alongside a young person's voice)

**2. Signal data for JusticeHub**

Every QR scan on the back of a postcard is a signal:
- Which regions are judges searching for programs?
- Which needs appear most (diversion, bail support, cultural connection, transport)?
- Where do they hit "no local option found"?

This data feeds directly into JusticeHub's gap analysis. If 15 judges scan for diversion programs in regional NSW and find nothing — that is a policy signal worth surfacing to funders and government.

**3. New narrative claims**

The experience generates fresh claim material:
- Judge quotes (with consent): "I realised I was deciding without knowing who this child trusts"
- Outcome stories: what happened when a judge changed their referral pattern after the experience
- Institutional signals: which judicial education bodies are interested in rolling it out

Each becomes a new claim file in wiki/narrative/justicehub/ — ready for the next LinkedIn post, grant application, or media pitch.

**4. Evidence for R&D**

The entire experience design — the card deck, the QR-to-JusticeHub flow, the consent-layered storytelling, the data capture — is genuinely novel. No judicial education program in Australia does this. That makes it:
- Core R&D under ACT-JH (JusticeHub) and ACT-EL (Empathy Ledger)
- Eligible for the 43.5% R&D tax offset on all development costs
- Tagged automatically through the spending intelligence system

**5. Repeatable infrastructure**

The card deck and JusticeHub QR pattern mean any magistrate's court in Australia could run a local version:
- Replace Story Cards with local young people (via EL consent flow)
- Replace Program/Place Cards with local JusticeHub data
- Keep the Decision Point and Pathway cards universal

That makes this a product, not just an event. Scalable through community partnerships, not through centralised delivery.

### How it connects to each part of the ecosystem

| System | What it receives | What it gives back |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Empathy Ledger** | Judge reflections as new stories | Consent-governed Story Cards for the deck |
| **JusticeHub** | QR scan data showing demand + gaps | Program search filtered by region and need |
| **Tractorpedia** | Synthesis articles from the experience | Context for future grant applications |
| **Narrative store** | New claims from judge reactions | Draft material for posts and pitches |
| **Spending system** | Tagged R&D spend on development | $109K+ offset on eligible costs |
| **The Brave Ones** | Real use case for Phase 1 portraits | Visual identity for the Story Cards |
| **CONTAINED** | Companion experience (container + postcards tour together) | Physical space for the portraits to live |
| **CivicGraph** | Judicial entity relationships | Program availability data by region |
| **STAY journals** | A chapter on the judges experience | Editorial voice and visual discipline |

### The weekly loop after the event

1. **Monday** — spending cron tags any event costs to ACT-JH (auto)
2. **Tuesday** — ingest judge reflections: /wiki ingest <notes>
3. **Wednesday** — review QR scan data, identify program gaps, update JusticeHub
4. **Thursday** — /wiki narrative draft justicehub — fresh post using new material
5. **Friday** — push to main, wiki rebuilds, all surfaces updated

### The compounding effect

Each round of Judges on Country makes the next round better:
- More stories in EL = richer Story Cards
- More QR scans = better gap data = better JusticeHub
- More judge reflections = stronger narrative claims = easier to attract the next 55 judges
- More program data = more referral options = more diversion = fewer children detained

The system is not built to run once. It is built to compound.

### What makes this different from every other judicial education program

Most judicial education is:
- Delivered by institutions (not communities)
- Based on information (not felt experience)
- Measured by attendance (not by changed decisions)
- Disconnected from live data (no JusticeHub equivalent)
- One-off (no compounding feedback loop)

This is:
- Led by Traditional Owners on their Country
- Designed to create moral uncertainty, not deliver answers
- Connected to a live national service map
- Built on consent-governed real stories
- Self-improving through every round

The postcards are not a communication tool. They are the entry point to an infrastructure that gets smarter every time someone picks one up.
