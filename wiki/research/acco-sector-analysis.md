# ACCO Sector Analysis

> The trajectory of Aboriginal Community Controlled Organizations represents a fundamental shift from state-mandated paternalism to institutionalized Indigenous self-determination.

## Scale of the Sector

- **800+** Aboriginal Community Controlled Organizations nationally
- **148** NACCHO member organizations operating **550+** clinics
- **3.6 million** episodes of care annually to **410,000+** clients
- **80+** ACCO peak bodies in the Coalition of Peaks (formed 2018)
- Coordinated by national peak bodies sitting as equal partners with government

## Historical Timeline

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 1967 | Constitutional Referendum | Commonwealth empowered to legislate for Indigenous affairs |
| 1970 | Aboriginal Legal Service (Redfern) | First community-controlled legal org |
| 1971 | Aboriginal Medical Service (Redfern) | Launched community-controlled health model |
| 1972 | Aboriginal Tent Embassy | Asserted sovereignty, pressured for land rights |
| 1975 | NAIHO Formed | National umbrella for health services (became NACCHO) |
| 1990 | ATSIC Established | Integrated policy under Indigenous-led national commission |
| 1991 | Royal Commission into Deaths in Custody | Highlighted necessity of community-controlled services |
| 2005 | ATSIC Abolished | Shift to "mainstreaming", closure of representative body |
| 2018 | Coalition of Peaks Formed | 50+ (now 80+) ACCO peaks to negotiate Closing the Gap |
| 2020 | National Agreement on Closing the Gap | "Building the Community-Controlled Sector" as core priority |
| 2021 | PICC Shareholder Transition | Palm Island Community Company → 100% community control |

## Sector Taxonomy

### Health (NACCHO)
- 148 members, 550+ clinics
- State peaks: AH&MRC (NSW, 51 members), AMSANT (NT, 1.35M sq km), VAHS (VIC)
- Comprehensive primary healthcare (CPHC) model addressing social determinants

### Legal (NATSILS)
- VALS (VIC): 24-hour criminal law, 7 sites
- NAAJA (NT): Criminal/civil law, "Adult Throughcare" reintegration
- ALRM (SA): Detention assistance, UN rights monitoring

### Child, Family, Social (SNAICC)
- ~300 member organizations
- VACCA (VIC): Therapeutic services and cultural strengthening
- AbSec (NSW): Early intervention to prevent child removals
- The Healing Foundation: Intergenerational Stolen Generations trauma

### Education, Housing, Land
- Education: VAEAI (VIC), AECG (NSW), Tauondi College (SA, since 1973)
- Housing: AHV (VIC), NATSIHA (national)
- Land: NNTC (native title), NLC (NT, 200K sq km), NTIBN (business network)

## ACCO vs. Mainstream: Comparative Evidence

| Metric | ACCO | Mainstream |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Trust/Access | 23% more effective at attracting/retaining Indigenous clients | Avoided due to institutional racism or fear |
| Clinical Efficiency | 13 contacts/year; better chronic disease management | Lower continuity, episodic and crisis-driven |
| Economic Impact | $1.19 health benefit per $1 spent; all revenue reinvested | Profit-taking or high admin overhead |
| Workforce | 80%+ Indigenous employment; cultural safety embedded | Low Indigenous representation; high turnover |
| Scope | CPHC addressing social determinants | Narrow biomedical focus; siloed responses |

Primary success mechanism: **cultural safety** — beyond awareness to a state where the recipient feels respected and safe. For communities with transgenerational trauma, knowing the service is "ours" is a prerequisite for engagement.

## Structural Challenges

1. **Fragmented, short-term funding** — most contracts 12 months, even for long-term services
2. **No baseline operational funding** — forced to allocate "admin levies" across insufficient service grants
3. **High reporting burden** — overlapping state/federal requirements
4. **Lack of data sovereignty** — forced to report on government metrics that don't reflect community priorities
5. **Lateral violence** — organizations "caught in the middle" between community expectations and government policy

## Future Direction (2025-2030)

### Priority Reform 2: Building the Sector
- Sector Strengthening Plans: Early Childhood, Health, Housing, Disability
- Aboriginal Expenditure Reviews: SA found only 22.4% of targeted spend goes to ACCOs
- Transition frameworks: programs like "Connected Beginnings" transitioning to ACCO backbone

### Federal Investment Commitments
- **RJED:** $290M for 6,000 new jobs by 2030
- **NT Housing:** $4B over 10 years for remote overcrowding
- **Economic Empowerment:** $75M for PBCs in clean energy and economic decision-making

### Indigenous Leaders Accord (QLD)
Signed by leaders including Lockhart River Mayor Wayne Butcher and Palm Island Mayor Alf Lacey — guarantees First Nations councils a seat at the policy table for liveability, economic future, and prosperity.
