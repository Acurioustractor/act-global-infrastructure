## BK and Joe: The Confit Pathways Partnership (March 2023)

The most substantive early Empathy Ledger meeting in the database is the second meeting between BK and Joe — founder of Confit Pathways, a post-incarceration support program for young men.

Confit Pathways is built around a specific theory of change: *"a brand that is cool for young people getting out of prison and also for the older ones."* Joe's insight is that compliance-based reintegration programs fail because they don't offer belonging or aspiration. He wanted something more like a streetwear brand than a social service — a community with its own identity, where being part of it meant something you'd want to tell people about.

From Meeting 2 (29 March 2023), the durable knowledge:

**What Joe was building:** A national digital program built on face-to-face recruitment and support, with an app as the connector, motivator, and communication tool for maintenance of reintegration. Community of rappers, artists, pro boxers, and well-known people as advocates. A partnership with Geed Up Clothing (Black Out line) — brand alignment where the aesthetic does the cultural work. Prison is 94% male but Joe saw room for women.

**What ACT committed to:** Supporting the Snow Foundation pitch and building the Confit Pathways application. A brainstorm session organised for April 12th with Joe's squad — "a wide variety of people with him all with lived experience." Draft pitch page built at confit-app.webflow.io. Snow Foundation application target: ready for board meeting first week of May 2023.

**Why this matters for Empathy Ledger:** Confit Pathways was one of the first external organisations ACT built with using the Empathy Ledger approach — centring lived experience as core capability, not as background colour. The structure ACT proposed (video reflections, lived experience co-design, story-as-pitch) is an early iteration of what became the platform's methodology. Joe's model also appears in [[justicehub|JusticeHub]]'s proof points — 60% reduction in recidivism — which means this 2023 partnership contributed directly to the evidence base that JusticeHub cites three years later.

## The Student Team Phase (Semester 2, 2023)

Through Semester 2 of 2023, ACT ran an Empathy Ledger student team in parallel with the JusticeHub student team. Meeting records from August through September 2023 are largely routine (task tracking, iteration notes) but establish that EL was under active development with university cohort support. The "First Yarn" from May 2023 was likely the earliest stakeholder conversation in this cohort, though the record is sparse.

The student team meetings capture an important phase: EL being built before it had a committed funder. The platform's underlying methodology — the four-layer voice-preserving pipeline, the consent architecture, the story-as-sovereignty framework — was being tested with students and community contacts before the platform itself existed.

## BK and Max: ACT Government Channel (December 2024)

By December 2024, EL was mature enough to be presented to government as a deployable methodology. Max holds the ACT (Australian Capital Territory) Government's community services portfolio with a specific mandate for disrupting stagnant structural processes.

Three opportunities emerged from this meeting:

**TOMNET Storytelling Initiative** — TOMNET is a community services network with 20+ years of impact and a successful peer support model. Max wanted to capture that legacy as an evidence base for community-led solutions. This is precisely the Empathy Ledger use case: narrative sovereignty + institutional memory.

**Department Annual Gathering Workshop** — Max wanted ACT to facilitate a workshop sharing innovative approaches to community support, philanthropic landscapes, and sustainable engagement models. Positioning ACT as a sector-convener, not just a platform provider.

**Community Pathways Research Project** — Mapping human journeys through community support systems, documenting natural helping relationships, service discovery and navigation patterns, informal and formal support networks. The "human journey" framing maps directly to the citizen-sovereign model from the 2023 TYSON Bank workshop — the same insight applied to community services rather than youth justice.

BK positioned Empathy Ledger as the methodology for all three. The ACT Government partnership opened a channel that sits distinct from ACT's Queensland-heavy engagement portfolio.

## What the EL Meeting Arc Reveals

**EL's origins are relational, not technical.** The first significant EL-coded meeting is BK sitting with Joe talking about what makes a program feel worth belonging to. The consent architecture, the multi-tenant platform, the 207 Supabase tables — all of that came later. The methodology was built from conversations, not from product specs.

**Government is a natural partner, not a natural funder.** The Max meeting opened a new channel but the framing was facilitation and methodology sharing — not procurement. ACT's relationship with government is consistently "we'll show you what works" rather than "we'll deliver your program."

**The student team phase was formative.** Two years of university-cohort development before dedicated funding is visible in the EL record. This pattern (sustained build before funder commitment) appears across JusticeHub and SMART Connect too — it is an ACT trait, not an anomaly.

**By 2026, EL is infrastructure.** The March 2026 operational synthesis shows EL as the default methodology for storytelling across Goods, Oonchiumpa, and the FRRR conference. It has become background infrastructure — the way ACT does narrative — rather than a named product being sold.
