# Global Precedents

> International models that validate and inform The Third Reality, CivicGraph, and ACT's approach to community-led civic intelligence.

## Spain & Holland: Data Commons and Digital Democracy

### Barcelona — DECODE Project & Decidim
- **DECODE:** Massive decentralized data infrastructure — citizens possess absolute data sovereignty, choosing what data they "donate" to improve public services
- **Decidim** ("we decide" in Catalan): Digital democracy platform for direct citizen participation in allocating millions in municipal budgeting
- **Lesson for ACT:** Successfully branded the "Data Commons" concept. CivicGraph and JusticeHub can be positioned as a public digital commons, not private software

### Amsterdam — Commons Network
- Civil society think-tank promoting "the commons" — communities collaboratively stewarding resources, separate from both market and state
- Works directly with policymakers to build networks and defend the category
- **Lesson for ACT:** "The Third Reality" should be treated like the Commons Network treats "the commons" — a philosophical standard, not just a product feature

## Sweden: Indigenous Data Sovereignty (Sapmi Region)

### Sami Parliament & GIDA-Sapmi Network
- Sami people's traditional territory spans northern Sweden, Norway, Finland
- Through GIDA, pushing institutions to adopt CARE Principles as mandatory global standard
- Sami Parliament actively formalizing data rights into national policy
- **Lesson for ACT:** Sovereign storytelling and data control can be institutionalized in law. Use CARE principles as the foundational certification for Third Reality assessments

## Kenya & Tanzania: Catalytic Capital

### Miamba Yetu Program (Kenya-Tanzania Coast)
- "Blended finance" model deploying capital directly into local, environmentally positive businesses while delivering verified community benefits
- Bypasses slow bureaucratic philanthropy

### African Transition Acceleration Fund (ATAF)
- "Catalytic capital" for early-stage infrastructure that traditional grant-makers deem too risky
- **Lesson for ACT:** Reframe JusticeHub's Impact-Weighted Quadratic Funding as "catalytic capital" — high-leverage investment that unlocks massive downstream savings for the state

### East Africa M-Pesa Precedent
- Leapfrogged traditional banking infrastructure entirely
- Proves communities can bypass slow-moving bureaucracies to route capital directly
- **Lesson for ACT:** CivicGraph can be the M-Pesa of social impact — leapfrogging grant bureaucracy

## Holland & Europe: Rewriting Narrative

### Commons Network (Netherlands)
- Realized Europe needed a new story to orient its future
- Aggressively branded "the commons" — collaborative, democratic resource stewardship
- Works with policy makers to build networks and defend the new category
- **Lesson for ACT:** Category design requires narrative infrastructure, not just technology
