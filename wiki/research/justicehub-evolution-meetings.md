## The Genesis (February–May 2023)

JusticeHub did not begin with a product brief. It began with a conversation.

### The QCOSS Introduction (February 2023)

The first recorded JusticeHub-adjacent meeting is an email exchange with Aimee from QCOSS (Queensland Council of Social Service) dated 20 February 2023. Aimee and her colleague Pip had just met with BK and NM and saw immediate alignment with QCOSS's "Raise the Age" campaign. BK's response named ACT's upcoming meetings with Queensland Corrective Services, the Women's Justice Network, and noted a photojournalist project concept inspired by juvenile-in-justice.com. This is the moment ACT entered the formal QLD youth justice advocacy space — through relationship, not infrastructure.

### The TYSON Bank Workshop (May 2023)

Three months later came the pivotal workshop. TYSON stands for "Tell Your Story Once Now" — a design thinking approach to service reform. The workshop brought together BK, NM, Paul (a youth homelessness charity founder who had survived government audit harassment after criticising robo-debt), and Sam (who had built NAB's payment system).

The conceptual anchor was a thought experiment: imagine a 7-year-old Aboriginal girl who has just had a child. She is simultaneously:
- The "largest employer of the feminised workforce" — with healthcare workers, housing officers, social workers, justice system workers, educators, and disability planners all attached to her case
- Repeating her story across multiple platforms and agencies, each time from scratch
- Watched as every dollar spent on her system contact generates economic benefit for the system, not for her

The question the workshop asked: what if she were at the centre of her own data, her own plan, and her own service selection?

From this thought experiment came the earliest articulable form of JusticeHub:
- A citizen-sovereign data model (the "Tyson identity token")
- Smart contracts that transfer value when outcomes are achieved — not when inputs are delivered
- A digital wallet architecture modelled on Up Bank but purpose-built for the care economy
- An "avoided cost model" that demonstrates $7M saved when 10 kids are diverted from a $700K/year system

Paul introduced the justice reinvestment argument. Sam named the MVP question: what is the leanest integration that creates demand pull? BK pushed on the story and lived experience layer: what does it feel and look like in city, regional, and remote contexts?

This workshop is JusticeHub's founding document. The platform built three years later — the evidence layer, the cost calculator, the ALMA map, the capital routing vision — all trace back to this room.

## The Student Team Phase (Semester 2, 2023 — Semester 1, 2024)

Following the TYSON workshop, JusticeHub entered a student-team-driven development phase across two university semesters. Meeting records from 1 August 2023 through February 2024 show the platform being iterated by student cohorts working on the evidence base, the directory architecture, and the user experience.

These records are mostly task-tracking: light on durable decisions. But they demonstrate something important — JusticeHub was being built with genuine student energy and university partnership before ACT had secured dedicated funding. The platform had momentum because the idea had pull.

## The Strategic Consolidation (Early 2026)

### Catch Up with George (January 2026)

By January 2026 JusticeHub had evolved far beyond its workshop origins. A strategic conversation with George (identity not fully specified in records) sharpened the funding thesis.

George's core argument: Queensland spends $1.5B annually on community safety. If JusticeHub can demonstrate that 10 children have been diverted from the system — at $700,000 per child per year — that's $7 million in avoided costs from a single cohort. The justice reinvestment argument isn't aspirational; it's arithmetic. Every dollar diverted to community-led alternatives produces compounding savings because those children don't cycle back through a system that costs more each time.

George framed the funding strategy as "justice as a portfolio" — multiple funders sharing risk, no single institution carrying the bet alone. He named Minderoo Foundation as the most likely entry point: they have the mandate, the scale, and the existing positioning.

The hardest constraint George named: institutional resistance is fierce. The bureaucratic incentive is toward incumbent systems that already have line items. JusticeHub's task is not just to prove the case — it's to create enough public pressure that the political cost of ignoring the evidence exceeds the political cost of change.

### Standard Ledger Meeting (February 2026)

Standard Ledger (ACT's financial advisory firm) met with ACT in late February 2026 to work through JusticeHub's commercial and legal structure. The $500K funding ask needed a clear entity model and revenue stream logic before it could be presented credibly to institutional funders.

This meeting marks the transition from advocacy project to commercially structured platform — the moment JusticeHub became fundable at scale.

## What the Meeting Record Reveals

Reading the JusticeHub meeting arc from 2023 to 2026 reveals three distinct phases:

**Phase 1 — The Idea (2023):** A workshop about systemic failure generated the founding concept. The TYSON workshop's citizen-sovereign data model is still visible in JusticeHub's 2026 architecture — the ALMA evidence layer, the avoided-cost framing, the community control principle.

**Phase 2 — The Build (2023–2024):** University student teams iterated on the platform. This phase is lightly documented but clearly sustained — the platform had continuous development without dedicated ACT resources.

**Phase 3 — The Consolidation (2025–2026):** Strategic conversations with George, Standard Ledger, and funders converted the platform into a commercially viable proposition. The $94.6B funding tracked, 1,000+ evidence models, and $500K funding ask all emerge from this phase.

The through-line: the economic case was present from Day 1 in the TYSON workshop (Paul's avoided-cost model, Sam's MVP question) and was refined — not invented — by the 2026 conversations.
