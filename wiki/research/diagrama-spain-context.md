## The Olga Meeting: A Sector Elder's Assessment (January 2025)

The longest and most analytically dense meeting in the entire ACT-DG record is a three-way conversation between Olga, Nicholas Marchesi, and Benjamin Knight on 16 January 2025.

Olga grew up in the Northern Territory — boarding school in Alice Springs, then Townsville and Charters Towers. She came back to Darwin just before Cyclone Tracy and stayed. Her working life has been a mix of private sector, government, NGO, and a spell with Foreign Affairs in Canberra (*"really wasn't my cup of tea. I don't think they're my kind of people"*). She has worked most of her life in community-based organisations. By the time of this meeting she is semi-retired but still connected to the **Justice Reform Initiative** — one of the key national bodies advocating for evidence-based youth justice reform.

This meeting is ACT's deepest recorded conversation about the Australian context for a Diagrama-style implementation. Key threads:

### What Changes in Legislation Would Actually Be Required

Olga's contribution here is important: she did not just validate the model, she worked through what it would take for the model to survive the Australian political environment. The Diagrama model operates within Spain's *Ley Orgánica de Responsabilidad Penal de los Menores* (LORPM, 2000), which frames youth justice as fundamentally educational and rehabilitative. Australia has no equivalent legislative framework at a national level. Any implementation would require sustained legislative advocacy, not just a pilot program.

### The Political and Institutional Barrier Map

Olga's assessment was sober and experienced. The barriers are not primarily about evidence — the evidence for therapeutic detention is strong and she understands it well. The barriers are structural:
- Bureaucratic incentive toward incumbent systems that already have line items
- Political risk aversion — "tough on crime" remains electorally palatable even when it is empirically wrong
- Federation complexity — youth justice sits at state/territory level, making national reform slow
- The "repair mindset" — consultants and policymakers focused on fixing broken systems, not redesigning them (this framing appears directly in the 2023 TYSON Bank workshop notes and seems to be a shared ACT/Olga diagnosis)

### The NT Context

Olga's lens is predominantly NT — Alice Springs, Darwin, the remote communities she has spent decades working in. The Northern Territory's youth justice crisis is the most acute in Australia. The existing [[diagrama|Diagrama]] article notes the 2020 *Blueprint for Change NT* produced by Danila Dilba Health Service and Diagrama — Olga's presence as a NT sector elder makes this meeting a direct bridge between that document and ACT's implementation strategy.

### Relationships Formed

Olga is explicitly positioned in ACT's network as a key figure for NT strategy. Her connections to the Justice Reform Initiative and her decades of government-facing work make her a credibility anchor for conversations with NT bureaucrats and policymakers. This meeting established that relationship — not just as a one-time consultation but as an ongoing strategic channel.

## May Second Meeting: Political Strategy and Media (May 2024)

Fourteen months after the Olga conversation, the second substantive ACT-DG meeting shows the Diagrama strategy in a more operational phase.

Key developments reported:
- **Two more Australians going to Spain** — one through a Churchill Fellowship; Mindy also wants to go. The Spain visit program is building momentum independent of ACT's direct facilitation.
- **David meeting the Shadow Minister** — David (CEO of Diagrama Foundation UK / Australia) has secured a meeting with the Shadow Minister for youth justice. Political engagement is active.
- **Brett McKlopp, Nine Network** — a journalist and presenter at the Nine Network has requested another interview with David about the Diagrama model. Media coverage accelerating.
- **Cairns centre "a year away"** — an actual Diagrama-modelled facility is in planning for Cairns. This is the most concrete progress marker in any ACT-DG record.
- **Legislative changes required** — the meeting identified specific legislative changes needed for the model to operate in Australia. David met with Kate (presumably Shadow Minister or her representative) who "had a lot of questions about the system."
- **LNP as an opportunity** — *"LNP will get in and they are looking for things that are different."* This is a strategic read: the incoming Queensland LNP government in late 2024 might be more receptive to a non-incumbent model precisely because it represents change from the previous government's approach.
- **Domestic violence data** — two programs dealing with domestic violence, which comprises ~30% of youth offences. An increase in DV-related youth justice contacts noted.

### The "Woodford Opportunity"

A brief but significant note: *"Could we run it out of somewhere we are already building."* "Woodford" is close to The Harvest's Witta location in the Sunshine Coast hinterland. This is the first explicit mention of ACT's place-based infrastructure (the farm, The Harvest) as a potential physical home for Diagrama-model programming in Australia. The convergence of ACT's land strategy and its justice reform strategy in a single sentence.

### The Comparative Frame

The meeting discussed developing a comparative narrative: the journey of a young person through the youth justice system in Australia vs. Spain, told from the young person's perspective. How the police engage, how the judge is involved throughout (not just at sentencing), how the staff relate. The power of this framing is that it makes the systemic difference legible at a human scale — not a data comparison but an experiential one. This is the seed of what became Room 2 of CONTAINED.

## What the ACT-DG Record Doesn't Capture

The Diagrama story as documented in the [[diagrama|Diagrama]] article describes a five-day immersion visit to Diagrama centres in Spain, guided by David. That visit — and the photographs on Room 2's walls — is not recorded in the project_knowledge database as a meeting. The Spain visit is captured in published writing (see the Diagrama article's published pieces list) but not in the meeting record.

What the meeting record *does* capture is the flanking work: the political groundwork (Olga's legislative map, David's Shadow Minister meetings), the media strategy (Nine Network interviews), the institutional relationship-building (Churchill Fellowships, the Blueprint for Change NT), and the early convergence of ACT's place-based infrastructure with the reform agenda.

## The ACT-DG Meeting Arc in Context

The four ACT-DG meetings span January 2025 to May 2024 (note: the Olga meeting is later than the May meeting in calendar terms — the ACT-DG record is not in strict sequence). Reading them together:

1. **Olga conversation (Jan 2025):** Deep NT context, legislative barriers, relational groundwork with a sector elder
2. **May second meeting (May 2024):** Operational progress — more Australians going to Spain, political engagement, Cairns pilot in view, LNP strategy, Woodford opportunity

The arc shows ACT's Diagrama work shifting from learning and understanding (the Spain visit, the Olga conversation) to active Australian implementation strategy (legislation, pilots, political relationships, media).

The Cairns centre being "a year away" as of May 2024 means it would have been expected to open around mid-2025. Whether that timeline held is not captured in the meeting record.
