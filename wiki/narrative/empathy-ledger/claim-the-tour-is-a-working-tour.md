# Claim: This isn't a documentary. It's a working tour.

**The argument:** Six months on the road is not a travelogue. It is the founder of a multi-project ecosystem doing his actual job in public — recording stories with the Empathy Ledger app, logging contacts into ACT Global Infra, posting field notes to Substack, collecting prison stories that JusticeHub then learns from. **The trip IS the product launch.** The polished travelogue gets watched. The working tour gets funded.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as a **funder pitch line** — *"We are not asking you to fund a trip. We are asking you to fund six months of field research for a working ecosystem."*
- Never used to **disqualify the documentary frame** publicly — every nonprofit travelogue gets dismissed as content; this needs to actively reject that category
- Never paired with the **"you can watch it happen live"** angle — Substack + Empathy Ledger + posts + contacts logged in real time = transparent working
- Never said as: *"If the answer to 'can ACT run from a backpack?' is yes, that itself is the product."*
- Never used to **separate sponsorship from charity** — the tour budget is fieldwork capital, not patronage
- Never said in the **"every stop has a buyer"** structural form — see `claim-every-stop-has-a-buyer`
