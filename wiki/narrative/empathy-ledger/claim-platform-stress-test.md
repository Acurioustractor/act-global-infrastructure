# Claim: The trip is the platform's stress test. That's a real R&D claim.

**The argument:** The world tour is not just content production. Every story collected at every stop is a real test of whether the Empathy Ledger consent + ownership model holds in a different language, a different legal jurisdiction, a different cultural protocol, a different power dynamic. **The trip stress-tests the product across 12+ contexts.** That is a defensible R&D claim — including for the R&D Tax Incentive — and it makes the trip a recognised research activity, not a marketing exercise.

## What's actually being tested at each stop

| Test category | What "passing" means |
|---|---|
| Consent UI in non-English | Can a Warumungu elder, a Rwandan coffee farmer, a Sami storyteller, and a Spanish bertsolari all give meaningful consent without translation friction? |
| Cultural protocol enforcement | Does the platform honour Mukurtu-style TK Labels across jurisdictions where copyright differs? |
| Multi-tenant scope per partner org | Does Shyaka's Uganda crew get the same per-org branded experience PICC has? |
| Sovereignty against legal pressure | If a Spanish court asks for a Diagrama story, does the consent metadata hold? |
| Offline / low-bandwidth capture | Does the field journal sync when bandwidth is intermittent? |
| Storyteller revocation across borders | Can a Tanzanian storyteller revoke a story published in Australia, and does it disappear cleanly? |

Each of these is a measurable engineering test. Each is also a credible R&D activity.

## What we haven't said yet

- **Never said as an R&D Tax Incentive claim.** This is potentially a 43.5% refundable tax credit on engineering + travel costs related to platform testing. Needs verification with the Xero / Tax R&D pipeline before claiming.
- Never said in a **funder pitch** — *"You are not funding a trip. You are funding 12 sovereignty stress tests of a product that has no peer."*
- Never paired with the **engineering changelog** — every test that fails generates a real ticket, every fix becomes a published patch note
- Never positioned as a **research output** — the synthesis is correct that this is structurally R&D, but the narrative has been treating it as content production
- Never connected to **Indigenous Data Sovereignty** as the formal academic frame (CARE principles, OCAP)
