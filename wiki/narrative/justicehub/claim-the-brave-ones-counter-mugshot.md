# Claim: A portrait is stood in front of. The Brave Ones is the move.

**The argument:** The dominant media calls these young people "at-risk youth", "offenders", "system-impacted." The Brave Ones inverts that in three words: *they are not the problem. They are the proof. And they are braver than anyone in the system that failed them.* The visual register is the deliberate counter-mugshot — Brian Duffy / Station-to-Station-era Bowie B&W, devotional close framing, single key light, the dignity register policing and media have refused to give Indigenous and system-impacted young people for two centuries. A diagram is read once and filed. A portrait is stood in front of. The CONTAINED tour proved that the move that breaks a funder, a judge, or a minister is not a stat — it is a body in a room. The Brave Ones is that move at scale and on repeat. **It is also self-replicating: every new portrait is a new ALMA node, a new Empathy Ledger story, a new Tractorpedia article. The system gets smarter every time someone stands in front of the lens.**

## Cultural protocol (non-negotiable)

The first consent conversation is more important than the first photograph. Aunty Bev, Uncle Terry, Tanya Turner, Kristy Bloomfield at Oonchiumpa are consulted and the protocol is co-designed before any sitting. Right of withdrawal at any point, including after exhibition. Subjects under 18 require guardian and cultural authority sign-off. Income flows on a transparent published split.

## What we haven't said yet

- **Never publicly launched.** The brave-ones project is at Phase 0 (placeholder portrait). The first real sitting has not happened.
- Never said as **the visual architecture of JusticeHub** in a public funder pitch — the brave-ones project is the human face of the data layer and the EL/JusticeHub crossover this whole stack has been missing
- Never positioned as the **photographic answer to mugshots** — *"For two centuries, the only portraits Australia has made of these young people are the ones the police took. The Brave Ones is the other archive."*
- Never said as a **lead-funder naming opportunity** publicly — the brave-ones project literally has *"The [Funder Name] Brave Ones Portrait Series"* as the legacy positioning, and no funder has been pitched on it yet
- Never paired with the **R&D Tax Incentive frame** — photography, consent-tech, and platform work are all eligible under ACT's existing R&D claim
- Never connected to the **STAY Series journals** explicitly — the brave-ones visual register IS the journal visual register; same discipline, different format
