# Claim: The people sentencing these children have never met one outside the dock.

**The argument:** 55 judges came to Mparntwe. Traditional Owners put a photo and a story in their hands and said: decide. Then revealed what they didn't know. The child hasn't been home in three weeks. There is no transport to the nearest program. This is his third breach, not third offence. Would you make the same decision now? The system asks judges to decide quickly, without knowing what matters. And young people live with those decisions for years. 80%+ reoffend from detention. $1.3M per year per child to produce that failure rate. The alternative exists — Oonchiumpa runs at 95% diversion and 97.6% less cost. Operation Luna took 20 of 21 high-risk youth off the list. But a judge can only refer to a program they know exists. A card deck with a QR code that opens JusticeHub filtered to that region turns a moment of moral uncertainty into the beginning of a different decision. The portraits the judges hold are from The Brave Ones. The consent layer is Empathy Ledger. The platform is JusticeHub. The methodology is Staying. The question is not whether the alternative works — the evidence is already in. The question is whether the people making the decisions have ever been in a room where they had to feel what their decisions produce.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as **a direct challenge to judicial education institutions** — *"The National Judicial College of Australia has never put a judge in a room where they had to sit with what their decision produces. We did it in 30 minutes."*
- Never paired with **the specific count of judicial education hours on Indigenous context** vs hours on sentencing procedure — the ratio is damning and should be sourced
- Never used as **a media line** — *"55 judges came to Alice Springs. Traditional Owners put a photo in their hands and said: decide. Then told them what they didn't know."* This is a headline, not a report.
- Never positioned as **repeatable infrastructure** — the card deck and JusticeHub QR codes mean any magistrate's court in Australia could run a version of this experience with local community leadership and local Program/Place Cards
- Never said in **the judge's own voice** — post-experience reflections from participating judges would be the most powerful variant of this claim, and none have been captured yet
- Never connected to the **STAY Series journals** — a judge's experience at Mparntwe could be documented as a journal chapter alongside the community stories, same visual discipline, same editorial voice
- Never paired with **the cost-per-judge arithmetic** — if the experience shifts even one judge's pattern on diversion referrals, the ROI against $1.3M/year/child is immediate and measurable
