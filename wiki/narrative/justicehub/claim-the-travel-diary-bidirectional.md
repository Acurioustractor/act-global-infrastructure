# Claim: The traffic flows both ways. The proof is on the desk.

**The argument:** Every philanthropic conversation about Indigenous justice gets stuck in centre-and-periphery framing — Australia sends people overseas to learn, foreigners come to study. The Travel Diary collapses this. *The same notebook a young person from Mparntwe takes to Murcia. The same notebook an Olga from Diagrama takes to Mount Isa.* Outbound (AU → Europe): a young person + support person from each of the ten anchor communities, going to Diagrama (Spain), Halt (Holland), or Children's Hearings (Scotland). Inbound (Europe → AU): small delegations from each European host, hosted on Country. ~13 Travel Diaries per cycle, each one a chapter in the relevant young-person Journal AND a standalone publication that joins the library. The diary makes reciprocity legible in a way a report cannot — two notebooks, sitting next to each other on the library shelf, one Australian and one Spanish, both filled out by the same kind of hand in the same template, IS the entire argument made physical.

## The format

| | What's in it |
|---|---|
| Cover | Aged kraft, hand-stitched, with a small linocut symbol of the journey direction |
| First spread | Who is travelling, who is hosting, the dates, the Country at each end |
| Daily entries | Hand-written. Photographs taped in. Receipts. Tickets. Pressed leaves. |
| The interview | One long conversation with the host community. EL voice transcript alongside the handwritten version. |
| The model encounter | Which of the seven STAY Series methods showed up here? In what form? |
| What I'm taking back | The traveller's own words. |
| What I'm leaving here | A page the traveller writes for the host. Stays in the host's diary. |

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as **the answer to "Australia is too expensive to be a youth justice destination"** — Diagrama has been receiving international visitors for thirty years; the Travel Diary makes the same case for Mparntwe, Mount Isa, Halls Creek
- Never paired with **the visa / logistics / cultural-safety budget line** — funders need to see this is real fieldwork, not a press tour
- Never said in **a young person's own voice** — by definition, it can't be until the first one happens; but the prompt is ready
- Never used to position **Australia as the world model** for a specific funder type — the European funders who already fund Diagrama may fund the Travel Diary as a co-investment
- Never said: *"The Travel Diary is the smallest object in the entire program. It is also the most expensive — because the cost of the diary is the cost of the trip."*
