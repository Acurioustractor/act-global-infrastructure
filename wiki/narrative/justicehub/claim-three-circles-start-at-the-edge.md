# Claim: Start at the edge. Work outward.

**The argument:** Most programs start at the centre — the policy room, the foundation board, the academic study — and try to move outward to the people the system has failed. The Three Circles methodology runs the opposite direction: **The Edge** (the people the system has failed hardest, first, always) → **The Next** (the kin, workers, advocates immediately around them, strengthened by what comes from the centre) → **The Rest** (funders, government, sector, last, changed by what travels outward). Authority sits with the people closest to the consequence. Tyson Yunkaporta has been documenting this on this continent for 65,000 years. Elinor Ostrom proved it across five continents. Fundación Diagrama has scaled it nationally in Spain for 30 years. Most programs do the opposite. Most programs fail.

## The three circles

| # | Circle | Who | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Edge | The people the system has failed hardest | First. Always. |
| 2 | The Next | The kin, workers, advocates immediately around them | Second. Strengthened by what comes from the centre. |
| 3 | The Rest | Funders, government, sector | Last. Changed by what travels outward. |

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as a **public structural critique** of how Australian funders typically operate — *"Most foundations fund Circle 3 first and wonder why nothing changes."*
- Never named as the **operating principle of every other ACT project** explicitly — Goods on Country starts at the edge, CONTAINED Room 1 was designed by survivors first, Empathy Ledger captures Edge voices first
- Never paired with the **failure rate of centre-out approaches** — there should be a count of how many "evidence-based" centre-out programs have failed in Australia in the last decade
- Never used in a **funder pitch as the explicit ask** — *"Fund us to start at the edge. We will tell you when the work is ready to travel outward. We will not fund Circle 3 first."*
- Never said as: *"The Three Circles is the only methodology in Australian youth justice that has been proven for 65,000 years AND verified by a Nobel Prize."*
