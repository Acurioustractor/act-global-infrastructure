# Claim: This is not a stack of grants. It is 7–8 long-term relationships.

**The argument:** Most "place-based" Australian programs still behave like competitive grant rounds in disguise — communities apply, criteria are weighted, an evaluation panel picks, and a contractual relationship begins. The current Three Circles shape runs the opposite way. **The filter is relationship, not process.** ACT already has 5 confirmed ACCO anchors and is asking Minderoo to co-select 2–3 more through Sessions 1 and 2, with community consent as the final gate. There is no abstract funnel. We start with the people we already know and trust, then radiate outward through trust, recommendation, openness, and in-community verification. Cultural protocol is non-negotiable: no community is approached without prior consultation with existing ACT relationships in that region; Elder and Council sign-off before any photographs, recordings or journals; subjects under 18 require guardian and cultural authority sign-off; income from book/exhibition sales flows on a published transparent split. The right unit is not ten grants. It is a small number of long-term relationships that can actually hold weight.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as a **direct rebuke of competitive grant rounds** — the entire competitive grant infrastructure produces inverse selection: the communities best at proposal-writing get funded, not the communities best at the work
- Never paired with **the cultural protocol as the technical baseline** — *"OCAP / CARE is not aspirational. It is the data layer."* This is uncommonly explicit for a program of this size.
- Never positioned as **proof against scale arguments** — *"Yes, 7–8 communities. We could go to 50. We will not, because 50 means parachute."*
- Never said in the **specific named-person form** publicly — Aunty Bev, Tanya Turner, Kristy Bloomfield, Richard Cassidy, Brodie Germaine — these are the relationships, and they should be visible in the funder pitch
- Never used to **disqualify "scaling" as the funder demand** — *"Scaling kills relationship. The methodology refuses to scale until the relationship can take the weight."*
