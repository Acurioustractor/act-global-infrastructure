# Claim: Witness · Document · Verify · Publish — the loop that grows the map

**The argument:** The Australian Living Map of Alternatives currently holds 1,775 community-led models, of which 128 are verified at the top three tiers (Indigenous-led, Effective, Proven). The rest are the work. The loop that grows the map has four moves: **Witness** (someone notices a working model the formal system has never recognised) → **Document** (the model gets captured properly in the witness's own voice) → **Verify** (community sovereignty over what counts as proof — Elder, kin, place-led validation, NOT academic peer review, NOT government audit) → **Publish** (the entry joins the map and becomes searchable, citable, fundable). The library grows by recognition, not by extraction.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never named as a **counter-method to peer review** — *"Community sovereignty over what counts as proof is not lower-rigour. It is different rigour. Elder review is rigour."*
- Never paired with the **"who validates whom"** politics — academic peer review excludes most of the people doing the work
- Never positioned as a **funder shortcut** — *"Stop commissioning 6-month landscape reviews. Use the map. We did the review. It's free."*
