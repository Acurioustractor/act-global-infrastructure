# Claim: Bourke. Maranguka. KPMG. The proof point that survives every scrutiny.

**The argument:** When a funder says *"show me the Australian evidence at scale"*, the answer is Maranguka. The Bourke Tribal Council's justice reinvestment program has been independently evaluated by KPMG and produced numbers that no other Australian community-led intervention can match: **23% drop in violent crime, 38% drop in major offences, 42% fewer custody days, $3.10 returned for every $1 invested.** Town-scale. Community-led. Indigenous governance. KPMG-audited. Hard data, not stories. Maranguka is the proof point that every single funder objection collapses against.

## Why Maranguka beats every other Australian proof point

1. **It has been independently evaluated by KPMG** — most community-led programs are not. The KPMG name disqualifies the "but where's the rigorous evaluation?" funder objection in one sentence.
2. **The numbers are not soft.** Recidivism reduction or "improved wellbeing" can be argued. -23% violent crime cannot. The number is on the police data.
3. **The ROI calculation is on the table.** $3.10 returned per $1 invested is the kind of figure a foundation board can defend in a meeting they did not attend.
4. **It is town-scale, not pilot-scale.** Bourke is a real town. Maranguka covers the whole community, not a selected cohort. There is no creaming bias to argue about.
5. **It is Indigenous-led with formal cultural authority.** The Bourke Tribal Council holds the governance. This is not "Indigenous community engagement." This is Indigenous governance.

## What we haven't said yet

- **Never used as the lead of a public post.** The Maranguka numbers are the strongest single set of figures in the entire ACT corpus and they have not yet been deployed in a single public-facing post.
- Never paired with the **Oonchiumpa numbers** as a two-site evidence base — Maranguka (Bourke, NSW) and Oonchiumpa (Mparntwe, NT) together cover both the urban-fringe and the desert contexts
- Never used to **disqualify "we need a pilot"** — Maranguka IS the pilot, evaluated, published, replicable
- Never said as a **funder challenge** — *"You have funded landscape reviews of community-led justice for ten years. KPMG evaluated Maranguka in 2018. The number is $3.10:$1. What is the next landscape review going to add?"*
- Never used to position the **STAY 10-anchor-community proposition** as "a Maranguka cohort, not a pilot" — the methodology already has KPMG-grade evidence behind it
- Never paired with the **Diagrama 30-year track record** as the international anchor — Maranguka is the Australian anchor, Diagrama is the international anchor, both KPMG-grade
- Never used in a **CONTAINED Room 3 post** — Maranguka belongs there with Oonchiumpa, PICC, BG Fit, Mounty Yarns

The Minderoo STAY pitch I drafted on 2026-04-09 cites Maranguka in passing (in the lineage table) but does not lead with the numbers. **The Maranguka KPMG figures should be the anchor evidence for the STAY ask** because they answer the funder's strongest objection ("show me Australian evidence that has been independently evaluated") with a clean, defensible, KPMG-audited dataset. The current Minderoo draft buries this. The next revision should lead with it.
