# Claim: The library is the proof — and the destination

**The argument:** STAY is a three-year program that leaves a library. Seven method books in The STAY Series, ten community volumes from ten anchor communities on Country, around fifty hand-stitched young-person Journals, and a national edition that goes to every electorate office. The library is not the report on the program. The library *is* the program. It is the proof and it is the destination — the thing the rest of the world will fly in to read.

## What's on the shelf (per the-full-idea §7)

- 7 STAY Series method books (Three Circles · Living Map Loop · Three Ripples · I Have I Am I Can · Field Canvas · LCAA · Fire Crescendo)
- ~50 hand-stitched young-person Journals (one per young person across the 10 communities)
- 10 community volumes (one per anchor community)
- 1 national edition (sent to every electorate office)
- ~13 Travel Diaries per cycle (bidirectional — see `claim-the-travel-diary-bidirectional`)

## What we haven't said yet

- Never publicly framed as "the move from framework to action" — the library is what makes a 3-year program's outputs walkable, holdable, sellable
- Never paired with the **young people as publishers, not subjects** angle — the strongest line in §7.4 is *"the library makes them publishers and hosts, not subjects"*
- Never positioned as a **bookshop / library / school distribution play** — the books ARE goods that ship to bookshops and schools, with royalties flowing back to communities
- Never said as **"we are not asking you to fund a report, we are asking you to commission a library"** in a funder pitch
- Never said in the **forkable** form — *"Other communities can write their own books. The seven methods are forkable. The journal template is forkable. A community in Birmingham, in Lima, in Murcia can pick up the series and add their own volumes."*
- Never visualised as the **shelf hero image** publicly — three variants generating per §7 open questions
