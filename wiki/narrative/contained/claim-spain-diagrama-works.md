# Claim: A place in Spain proves another way is not theory

**The argument:** Diagrama Foundation has run youth detention centres in Spain since 1991 with 13.6% reoffending. Curtains, gardens, social educators who stay for 15 years, family contact every week. €5.64 returned per €1 invested (University of Valencia). The cure is not hypothetical — it is operating right now.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never the lead of a confrontational post — *"Spain figured this out in 1991. Australia is still arguing about it in 2026."*
- Never used to make the **age question** — Spain detains children very young but does it humanely; Australia debates the age of criminal responsibility instead of the conditions
- Never paired with the **staff retention** angle as a labour story — Carmen 15 years vs Australian youth detention staff turnover (a stat we don't yet have but could pull)
- "They looked like children" — never used as the **only** sentence in a post with one image. This is the strongest line in the entire campaign and we have buried it.
- Never used to ask Australian DCJ ministers a single question: *"What would have to be true for you to visit Diagrama before the next budget?"*
- Never connected to **Diagrama UK** existing — meaning the model has already crossed a common-law jurisdiction. Currently we frame Spain as exotic; we could frame it as already-translated.
