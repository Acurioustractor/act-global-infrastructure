# Claim: Two people. Built it with our hands.

**The argument:** CONTAINED is not the output of a foundation, an agency, or a strategy deck. Nicholas Marchesi physically constructed the majority of the rooms, wired the electronics, embedded the technology. Benjamin Knight followed the paper trails from funding records to a kid in a cell. The campaign is artisanal — the form is the politics. *"Revolution doesn't happen in boardrooms. It happens when builders, dreamers, and survivors converge."*

## What we haven't said yet

- **Never used as the lead of a launch-week post.** This is the most under-deployed claim in the entire campaign. The launch posts open with the cost. They could open with the hands.
- Never said with a **photo of Nick in the workshop** as a single-image post
- Never said in Nick's own voice — Ben writes the essays, Nick builds the thing, and the public never hears Nick
- Never paired with the **what it would have cost an agency** comparison — *"A consulting firm would have charged six figures for a strategy. We bought a container."*
- Never said: *"The container was built before the funding came. We started anyway because the kids inside cannot wait for us to be perfect."* (The Lucy Stronach email contains this thought; it has never been a post.)
- Never used to argue against **professionalisation as deferral** — the way "we are still scoping" becomes "we are still not doing"
- Never connected to **Goods on Country** as the same kind of claim — Nick builds beds, Nick builds rooms, the through-line is hands
