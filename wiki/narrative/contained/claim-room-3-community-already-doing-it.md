# Claim: The cure already exists

**The argument:** Australia is not waiting for an answer. The answer is already running in Alice Springs (Oonchiumpa, 95% diversion), Palm Island (PICC, 78% diversion), Mount Druitt (Mounty Yarns), Mount Isa (CAMPFIRE), Sydney (ConFit, 60% recidivism reduction). 800-981 verified programs on ALMA. They are underfunded, underreported, and outperforming detention on every metric. They do not need permission. They need resources.

## Stat watch

- Program count drift: 150+ (Oct 2025) → 800 → 981. Each is true at a different moment but the drift is unflagged. **Pick a date-stamped number for launch.**

## What we haven't said yet

- Never named the funders **failing** these programs. Always celebrates the program, never names the gap. The post that says *"Oonchiumpa is doing 95% diversion on a fraction of one detention bed's budget. Here is the list of foundations that have not funded them."* has never been written.
- Never quantified the **collective** Room 3 number — *"If you funded every Room 3 organisation we have ever featured at the level they have asked for, the total ask is $X. That is %Y of one year of QLD detention spend."*
- Never told as a **map** post — one image, every Room 3 org pinned, "this is what the Australia we already have looks like"
- Never said as a **one-line proof** — *"The cure already exists. Its name is Kristy Bloomfield."*
- Never used to invert the discovery frame — *"Funders pay consultants $50K to find programs we have on a free public website."*
- Never used the staff-to-recidivism ratio: *"Oonchiumpa has fewer staff than one wing of one detention centre."*
