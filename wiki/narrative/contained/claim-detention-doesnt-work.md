# Claim: Detention is the most expensive way to make a child more dangerous

**The argument:** 84-85% of children released from Australian youth detention reoffend. The system does not reduce harm — it manufactures repeat contact. The dollar figure makes it absurd; the recidivism rate makes it indefensible.

## Stat watch

- **Inconsistent time window:** "within 12 months" (Building Revolution) vs "within two years" (some launch material) vs no window. Pick one, use it everywhere.
- **84% vs 85%** — both used in the same week. Pick one.
- Source: campaign bible says Queensland Youth Justice Strategy 2023. Other places say "Productivity Commission". Confirm primary source.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said *"this is a manufacturing line for repeat contact"* — strong industrial framing untouched
- Never weaponised against **"tough on crime"** politicians by their own logic — *if your goal is community safety, your method has an 84% failure rate*
- Never compared to **medical malpractice standard** — *if 84% of a hospital's patients came back sicker, the hospital would be shut down*
- Never paired with a named child's second sentencing
- Never used to ask: *"Who in this country defends 15.5%?"* — direct rhetorical confrontation with no current owner
- Never said in the voice of a **conservative**, even though the argument lands hardest from the right ("we are wasting money to make crime worse")
