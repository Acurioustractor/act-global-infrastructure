# Claim: It is not waiting for permission. It is happening.

**The argument:** The cure already exists. It is running in Alice Springs, Palm Island, Mount Druitt, Mount Isa. It does not need approval, evaluation, or another inquiry. It needs the rest of the country to catch up — and CONTAINED is built so the catching-up has somewhere to start.

## Variants used

| Variant | Where |
|---|---|
| "It is not waiting for permission. It is happening. The only question is whether the rest of us will catch up." | Cure Already Exists essay closing — the strongest sentence in the entire campaign |
| "We do not have all the funding locked in yet. But we are going anyway because the young people in this system cannot wait for us to be perfect." | Lucy Stronach personal email |
| "They don't need permission. They need resources." | Launch Day 4 |

## What we haven't said yet

- **Never used as the opening of a post.** Always a closing. The essay buries the strongest line on page 12.
- Never said as a **single-line single-image post** — the closing line, one image of Kristy or Isaiah, nothing else
- Never used to invite the **reader into the same posture** — *"You don't need permission either."*
- Never weaponised against **the gatekeepers** — peer review, ethics committees, funder strategy days, government working groups
- Never connected to the **founding story** — Nick built CONTAINED before the funding came; the campaign embodies its own claim
- Never said at the **start of a tour stop** — *"This stop is happening because we did not wait."*
- Never paired with **Just Reinvest** as a coalition claim — *"Nicole Mekler has been making this case for years. We are showing up to the same fight with hands and a container."*
