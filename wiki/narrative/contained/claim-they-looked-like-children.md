# Claim: They looked like children

**The argument:** In Diagrama in Spain, the young women showed Ben their bedrooms. Teddy bears. Family photos. Embroidered pillows. They looked like children. The sentence is shocking because in Australia we have stopped seeing detained kids as children at all. The whole campaign is, in one sense, an attempt to restore that recognition.

## What we haven't said yet

- **This is the strongest single sentence in the entire campaign and it has been deployed twice, both inside long essays.** Most readers will never reach it.
- Never said as a **single-image, single-line post**: one Diagrama photo, the sentence, nothing else
- Never said as a **question** — *"When was the last time the public discourse about youth justice in Australia treated the people inside as children?"*
- Never paired with the **opposite image** from an Australian centre (what the public sees: orange jumpsuits, mugshots, masked faces in news footage)
- Never used to argue against the **age-of-criminal-responsibility** rollback by simply asking: *"What age does a child stop looking like a child? Spain knows. Australia is still arguing."*
- Never said in **Carmen's voice** — the staff member who has been there 15 years and could say it with more authority than Ben
- Never connected to **mugshot media policy** — the Australian convention of publishing children's faces is itself part of how we stop seeing them as children
