# Claim: Same money. 16x the kids.

**The argument:** Australia spends $1.55M / year / child to detain a kid in a system where 84% reoffend. The same money funds 16 kids in a community program at 88% success. These are not our numbers — they are the Productivity Commission's.

## Stat watch

This claim has the most internal inconsistency in the campaign. See `STAT-CONFLICTS.md`.

- Cost: $1.1M (Oct 2025) → $1.3M (Mar 2026 essay) → $1.55M (campaign bible / launch posts). Source attributed to ROGS each time. **Reconciliation needed before launch.**
- Recidivism: 84% / 84.5% / 85% / "above 80" — same source, drifting wording
- Community cost: $58K/year (Building Revolution) vs $75/day (= $27K/year) (campaign bible). Different units, different numbers — not the same claim wearing two hats.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never compared to a **specific named line item** in any state budget. ("Queensland will spend $X on detention this year. The same money would fund every CAMPFIRE in the state for Y years.")
- Never personalised — never *"this is the price of one Isaiah"*, never *"the cost of locking up Brodie's cousin"*
- Never said as a question to a specific minister by name
- Never paired with the climate-of-loss frame: *"Same money. Same kids. We chose."*
- Never inverted: *"Imagine being a treasurer and approving the more expensive option that fails 84% of the time."*
- Never said with the *budget cycle* as the timing hook (state budgets land May–June 2026 — this stat lands hardest in budget week)
