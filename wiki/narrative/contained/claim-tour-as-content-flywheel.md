# Claim: The tour is the campaign

**The argument:** Each tour stop generates content that fuels the next. Local young people design Room 1. Local organisations fill Room 3. Local decision-makers walk through. The reactions, photos, quotes, and stories from one stop become the recruitment material for the next. The tour is not promoting the campaign — the tour *is* the campaign.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as a **public ask for tour hosts** — *"If your city is not on the tour list, ask us to come. The next stop is whoever raises a hand."*
- Never said with the **map view** — one image, five pins, dotted lines, the country
- Never said in **post-stop format** until a stop has happened (chicken-and-egg). The launch is the first stop's first moment.
- Never used to argue for **decentralisation** — *"There is no national tour committee. There is a container, a calendar, and the people who said yes."*
- Never connected to the **content compounding** logic publicly — only in the internal comms plan
- Never paired with the **demand map** numbers — 29 inbound requests, 136 commenters, demand from 8 states. The fact that the tour is being pulled (not pushed) is itself the argument.
