# Claim: This is not foreign. It is just unchosen.

**The argument:** Australian audiences hear "Spain" and think "different country, different culture, doesn't apply here." That dismissal is exactly the problem. Diagrama already operates in the UK — a common-law jurisdiction with similar institutional inheritance to Australia. The model has already been translated. The only remaining barrier is the choice not to translate it again.

## What we haven't said yet

- **The strongest angle in this entire claim has never been said publicly.** The fact that Diagrama runs in the UK — meaning the model has already crossed from civil law to common law — destroys the most common dismissal. We have a personal email to the UK director and have never used the existence of the UK operation in any post.
- Never said: *"If you think Spain is too different, look at the UK. Diagrama runs there too. What's stopping us is not culture. It is choice."*
- Never said as a **map** — Spain and UK pinned. Two countries. Same model. Different legal traditions.
- Never used the **age-of-the-model** as an argument — *"Diagrama has been running since 1991. That is older than most Australian youth detention centres."*
- Never pre-empted the **"Australia is special"** dodge — every politician's go-to defence, never directly punctured
