# Claim: Same money. 16 kids instead of one.

**The argument:** For the cost of detaining one child for one year, you could fund 16 children in a community program with an 88% success rate. This is not a hypothetical — it is the math of every state budget. The detention dollar is not just expensive. It is the *most expensive* way to fail.

## Stat watch

- The "16x" derives from $1.55M ÷ ($75/day × 365). Math: $75 × 365 = $27,375. $1,550,000 ÷ $27,375 ≈ 56. So the actual multiplier is **~56x, not 16x** — or the daily cost figure is wrong, or the annualisation is wrong. **Critical reconciliation needed before launch — see `STAT-CONFLICTS.md`.**

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said in **per-state** form — *"Queensland could move 1,200 kids out of detention into community programs for the same money it spends now."*
- Never said with a **specific kid** — *"For what it costs to detain Isaiah for one year, we could fund Mounty Yarns for 8 years."*
- Never paired with the **labour creation** angle — community programs employ Aboriginal youth workers; detention employs guards. Same money, different employment outcome.
- Never said in **opportunity-cost** language — *"Australia chooses, every year, to help one child instead of 16. We do this on purpose."*
- Never used to invite **funder co-investment** publicly — *"For $25K (one tour stop), one foundation can put 200 decision-makers inside the math."*
