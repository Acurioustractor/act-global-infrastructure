# Claim: This system falls with devastating precision on First Nations children

**The argument:** Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander young people are 21–23 times more likely to be detained, and make up over 60% of the detained population despite being a fraction of the youth population. The 1991 Royal Commission into Aboriginal Deaths in Custody made 339 recommendations; most remain unimplemented. The youth detention system is the contemporary site of an old failure.

## Stat watch

- **21x vs 23x** — same claim, two numbers. Confirm primary source. ROGS or AIHW.
- "60% of the detained population" — needs date stamp; the figure has shifted year to year.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never deployed as a **standalone post**. Always nested inside a longer essay. There is no single image, single number, single line that just says: *"21x. That is the multiplier. That is the system."*
- Never paired with **Royal Commission count** as a generational frame — *"339 recommendations in 1991. 35 years later. The kids being locked up today were not born when the report was written."*
- Never used to ask the **age of criminal responsibility** question directly — most of the children inside are under 14
- Never connected to **specific living families** — the statistic erases the people; the Cure essay tells stories of Kristy and Tanya, but never of the kids on Operation Luna by name
- Never said by the **Indigenous co-leads in their own voice**. Currently this claim is in Ben's voice, in Ben's essays. It is a claim that should not have to come from a non-Indigenous founder.
- Never paired with **Closing the Gap** failure data — the formal national metric this directly violates
