# Claim: The debate is settled. The question is political will.

**The argument:** Every inquiry has said this. Every report confirms it. The Royal Commission, the Productivity Commission, ROGS, the Human Rights Commission, every coroner's report from Banksia Hill to Don Dale to Cleveland. The question is no longer *what works.* The question is who is willing to act, and what is stopping them.

## What we haven't said yet

- This is the most **under-deployed** claim in the campaign. Twice. Both inside long essays. Never as a standalone post.
- Never used to **name a specific minister** with a specific report on their desk
- Never used as a **list post** — *"Reports that have already said this: 1) Royal Commission Aboriginal Deaths in Custody (1991). 2) Don Dale Royal Commission (2017). 3) Banksia Hill Inquiry. 4) Cleveland inquest. 5)... We are not waiting for the next one."*
- Never weaponised against the **"need more research"** dodge that politicians use
- Never used to **pre-empt** the next inquiry — *"If you are about to commission another inquiry, here is what it will find. Save the money. Spend it on what works."*
- Never paired with a **clock graphic** — every year since 1991, what we kept doing
- Never said as: *"There is nothing left to learn. There is only the choice not to act."*
- Never used at **budget timing** — state budgets land May–June. This claim hits hardest when the failure is being re-funded.
