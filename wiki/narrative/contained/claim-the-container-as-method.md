# Claim: One container. Three rooms. Thirty minutes.

**The argument:** A shipping container is the right form because the metaphor is the message — kids are literally contained. Three rooms compress the entire argument into thirty minutes you walk through with your body. It is not a lecture, not a report, not a petition. It is a designed environment that makes the cost and the alternative felt before they are intellectualised. Then it asks one question: *who else needs to experience this?*

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said the **why containers** in a single image post. The metaphor is the most powerful thing about the campaign and we have never explained it in 50 words.
- Never inverted: *"The same shape that holds children in cells is the shape we are using to free people from their assumptions."*
- Never used the **shipping** metaphor — containers move things across oceans; this one is moving truth across Australia (we touched this on Day 6 but never as a lead post)
- Never said: *"We could have written a report. We built a room. Here is why that matters."*
- Never said: *"Thirty minutes is the longest amount of attention you can ask of a politician. So we built thirty minutes."*
- Never paired with the **physical labour** claim — Nick built it with his hands (see `claim-built-by-hand`). The fact that the campaign is artisanal is itself the argument.
