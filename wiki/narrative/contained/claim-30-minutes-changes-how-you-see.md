# Claim: Thirty minutes changes the way you see this issue

**The argument:** No one is going to read another report. No one is going to attend another panel. But thirty minutes inside a shipping container — three rooms, designed to be felt before they are intellectualised — that, almost no one walks out of unchanged. The whole campaign is built on the bet that experience moves people that argument cannot.

## What we haven't said yet

- Never used the **time-cost trade** as the lead — *"Thirty minutes is the longest amount of attention you can ask of a politician. So we built thirty minutes."*
- Never said with the **before/after face** image — visitor walking in vs walking out, no caption
- Never used to make **the politician dare** — *"Name a minister who is willing to spend 30 minutes inside the system they are funding."*
- Never compared to the **time politicians have already spent on the wrong answer** — *"The Queensland youth justice strategy was 18 months in development. CONTAINED takes 30 minutes."*
- Never said in the **ritual** voice — *"Thirty minutes. Three rooms. One question. That's it. That's the whole thing."*
