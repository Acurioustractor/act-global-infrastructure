# Claim: Not charity. Infrastructure.

**The argument:** CONTAINED is not asking for sympathy. JusticeHub is not asking for donations to a cause. Goods on Country is not asking for handouts. These are infrastructure for community-led change — public goods built so that the people closest to the problem can own the solution. The right question is not *"who deserves help?"* It is *"what does the country need to build?"*

## What we haven't said yet

- Never said as a **standalone post**. Lives in identity docs and essay closings.
- Never used to **rename the funder relationship** — *"We are not asking you to fund us. We are asking you to co-build public infrastructure."*
- Never paired with the **public-good comparison** — roads, libraries, hospitals; nobody calls a library *charity*
- Never positioned as a **Closing the Gap implementation tool** — government already has a treaty obligation to fund this; we are giving them the means to meet it
- Never said: *"The most insulting thing you can do to a community-led organisation is treat it as a charity case. The most respectful thing is to treat it as infrastructure that needs maintenance funding."*
- Never used to argue **against pity** as a political force — pity defunds you when the news cycle moves; infrastructure does not
