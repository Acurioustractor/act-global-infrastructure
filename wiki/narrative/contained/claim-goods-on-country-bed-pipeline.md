# Claim: From a bed to a courtroom is a line you can draw with your finger

**The argument:** One in three children in remote communities has scabies at any given time. Scabies leads to Rheumatic Heart Disease. 59% of remote homes lack washing machines. Sick kids don't go to school. Out-of-school kids end up in the system. The bed Goods on Country builds is a youth justice intervention. The pipeline is direct, and it starts at the ground.

## What we haven't said yet

- **Never said as a CONTAINED post.** This argument lives entirely in the long essay. It is the most original claim in the campaign and almost no one has seen it.
- Never said as a **diagram** — bed → school → not in detention. One image. Five icons.
- Never said with the **dollar comparison** — *"A bed costs $X. One day in detention costs $4,250. The bed lasts a decade."*
- Never used to **expand the funder ask** — most funders want to fund Room 3 programs, but the upstream prevention case (Goods, housing, washing machines) is a much larger and less crowded category
- Never said in the **public health voice** — RHD is the leading cause of preventable cardiovascular death in Indigenous Australians and almost no one outside health knows the youth justice connection
- Never used to invert **the discovery** — most youth justice campaigners do not know about scabies. Most public health campaigners do not know about detention costs. We are one of the only places that hold both.
- Never said as a **Nick claim** in his own voice — Goods is his project; he never speaks the connection out loud
- Never paired with **Xavier as the closing image** — a young person proud of something he made with his own hands
