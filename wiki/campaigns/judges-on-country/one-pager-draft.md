## What this is

On Tuesday 21 April 2026, 55 judges and magistrates, including at least one High Court judge and Indigenous magistrates, came onto Eastern Arrernte Country at Atnarpa homestead, Loves Creek Station, 80km east of Mparntwe (Alice Springs). They were hosted by Oonchiumpa, under the authority of Aunty Bev and Uncle Terry, and led by co-directors Kristy Bloomfield and Tanya Turner.

This is what the day was for, what the bench saw, and what we are asking them to do next.

## The numbers, where the numbers help

- **95% diversion** from the justice system. [WORKSHOP — confirm current figure, source, denominator.]
- **97.6% less cost** than detaining one child for one year. Australia spends over $1.3M per child per year in detention with reoffending above 80%.
- **Operation Luna:** 20 of 21 high-risk young people in Alice Springs came off the list within a year of Oonchiumpa working alongside NT Police.
- **7 language groups** served. **150km** homeland radius. **21** active young people right now.

## What Oonchiumpa is, in one paragraph

Oonchiumpa is a 100% Aboriginal-led youth civic agency in Mparntwe, grounding young people in culture, Country, and identity across seven Central Australian language groups. Cultural authority is held by Aunty Bev and Uncle Terry. The central site is Atnarpa homestead on Loves Creek Station, the Bloomfield family's reclaimed ancestral Country. The program stays when other services leave. That is the method.

## What we are asking the judiciary to do next

1. **Before sentencing an Indigenous young person,** ask what community-led programs exist within their kinship and Country. Oonchiumpa can help identify them.
2. **Open a public conversation** about community-led alternatives. One judge, one magistrate, within 60 days.
3. **Shape the tool.** JusticeHub is building a magistrate-facing service-directory prototype. The bench's questions from Tuesday define what it has to answer.
