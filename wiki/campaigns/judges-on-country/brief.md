# Judges on Country — campaign brief

## What

Fifty-five judges and magistrates including at least one High Court judge and Indigenous magistrates visit Mparntwe (Alice Springs) with Oonchiumpa on Tuesday 21 April 2026. Ben films, captures storytellers (with consent via Empathy Ledger v2), and uses JusticeHub to demonstrate how a community-led service directory could change sentencing.

## Why

This is the live version of Example 2 in the Minderoo pitch. A magistrate, before sentencing, queries a tool that shows available community-led programs within 200km, their evidence ratings, cost per young person per year, cultural authority, capacity today. The JoC visit makes the demonstration real in front of the bench. Oonchiumpa's hub-and-spoke model on traditional owner country is the anchor example.

## Who (audience)

- Primary: the 55 judges and magistrates on the trip
- Secondary: Oonchiumpa community (Mparntwe), Indigenous magistrates' ongoing practice, the broader judiciary
- Tertiary: Minderoo (the pitch evidence), Aesthetics campaign (Episode 02 material), sector media

## When

- **Tue 21 April 2026:** on-country day with Oonchiumpa in Mparntwe. Ben films, captures with consent.
- **Wed 22–Fri 24 April:** rush editing, one-pager drafted (with Kristy + Tanya), 3 Brave Ones portraits selected for Minderoo envelope.
- **Mon 28 April:** Brave Ones portrait prints ordered.
- **Fri 1 May:** Minderoo envelope posted with JoC one-pager + Brave Ones + Artefact 001.

## Success metric

**Three specific outcomes inside six weeks:**
1. A one-pager authored with Oonchiumpa (not about them) in the Minderoo envelope by 1 May — Kristy + Tanya sign-off before dispatch.
2. At least one judge or magistrate who attended opens a public conversation about community-led alternatives within 60 days.
3. JusticeHub magistrate-facing prototype wireframe drafted from what the bench actually asked for during the day.
