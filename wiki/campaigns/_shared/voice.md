## Quick gate (before ship)

Every draft passes these checks or it does not ship:

1. **Opens specific.** First sentence is a named person, a dollar figure, a dated event, or a contradiction. Never a generalisation, never a question, never a reference to a previous post.
2. **No em-dashes for punch.** Commas, full stops, colons. Parenthetical em-dashes are fine if the parenthetical is real.
3. **No rule of three.** No adjective-adjective-adjective. No phrase-phrase-and-phrase. Break the pattern.
4. **No rhetorical-question series.** One rhetorical question per piece, maximum.
5. **No stage-direction analysis.** No "-ing" phrase at sentence end telling the reader how to feel (*fostering*, *highlighting*, *reflecting*, *emphasising*).
6. **No vague attributions.** "Observers note", "experts say", "some argue" — banned. Name the source or drop the claim.
7. **Force-a-trap if the target is a claim with a gap.** Claim plainly → gap → fork A → fork B → flat request.
8. **Plain imperative close.** Not a rhetorical flourish. Not "who's in?" Not "let's do this." A direct action or a direct request.
9. **Every dollar, date, and person traceable.** If you can't name the source, don't make the claim.
10. **Under the word count** for the platform (LinkedIn 350, carousel 9 slides × 40 words, artefact opener 1500).

## When to run AutoReason

- Primary campaign posts (episode launches, pitch openers, ministerial traps)
- Artefact openers (page 1 of document.md)
- Brand voice revisions

## When not to run AutoReason

- Short factual updates
- Reply-in-thread comments
- Internal memos
- Anything under 100 words
