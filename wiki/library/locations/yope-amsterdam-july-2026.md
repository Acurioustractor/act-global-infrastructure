## The Travel Diary books

Per [`claim-the-travel-diary-bidirectional`](../../narrative/justicehub/claim-the-travel-diary-bidirectional.md) and Chapter 13 of the master book, every young person in the cohort gets a physical Travel Diary book — kraft hand-stitched, with the same template used in both directions. The books contain:

| Section | What goes in it |
|---|---|
| **Cover** | Aged kraft, hand-stitched. Linocut symbol of the journey direction. Young person's name. |
| **First spread** | Who is travelling, who is hosting, the dates, the Country at each end (Mparntwe / Mount Druitt / Amsterdam). |
| **Daily entries** | Hand-written by the traveller. Photographs taped in. Receipts. Tickets. Pressed leaves. Notes on what was unexpected. Names of the people who held the room. |
| **The interview** | One long conversation with the host community in their own words. Voice transcript via Empathy Ledger sits alongside the handwritten version. |
| **The model encounter** | Which of the seven STAY Series methods showed up here? (Three Circles? Living Map Loop? Three Ripples? I Have I Am I Can?) In what form? What did it look like in this place that it doesn't look like at home? |
| **What I'm taking back** | The traveller's own words on what they will carry home. |
| **What I'm leaving here** | A page the traveller writes for the host. Stays in the host's diary. |

**Number of books needed:** ideally one per young person travelling, plus one per young person at YOPE who participates in the exchange. If the cohort is 4 Australians + 4 YOPE young people, that is 8 books.

## Cultural protocol — non-negotiable

1. **Aunty Bev, Uncle Terry, Tanya Turner, Kristy Bloomfield are consulted** before any Oonchiumpa young person is approached about this trip. The protocol comes from Country first.
2. **Shayle and Leah at Mounty Yarns** are consulted before any Mt Druitt young person is approached.
3. **Subjects under 18 require guardian sign-off** AND cultural authority sign-off — both, not either.
4. **Empathy Ledger consent flow is the legal and technical baseline** — not aspirational.
5. **Right of withdrawal at any point.** A young person who decides not to be in the diary after the trip can have their entries removed within 7 days of request.
6. **No photographs of any young person** without explicit consent for the specific use (book? exhibition? website? all three are different consents).
7. **No exhibition opens without at least one young person from the cohort present** — this is their book, not ours.
8. **Income from any commercial use** of the resulting artefact (book sales, exhibition royalties, documentary licensing) flows on a published transparent split to subjects and their nominated community organisations.

The first consent conversation is more important than the first photograph. If we get the protocol wrong, the trip should not happen.
