# The Community Volumes

> *Ten anchor communities. Ten books. Each one bound at the end of Year 3, alongside the community-led launch in that place. The community holds the editorial pen. ACT holds the printer.*

These are the ten community books that sit at the heart of the STAY library. They are *not* a chapter each in a single national book. They are ten standalone bound volumes, one per community, written largely by the people in the community across the three years of the program. The national edition (the volume that goes to every electorate office) gathers them with the community's consent — but each community volume can stand alone, be sold from the local shop, sit in the school library, and be carried home.

## What's in each volume

The structure is the same across all ten so the books read as a series, but the *content* of every section is the community's. The structure:

| Section | What it holds |
|---|---|
| **The Country** | The Country the community lives on, in language and in English. Acknowledgement written by the community. |
| **The People** | Who is in this book. Named, with consent. Photographs. Voices. The Aunties, the Elders, the workers, the young people. |
| **The Work** | What the community does — the program, the model, the practice. In the community's own words, not consultancy summaries. |
| **The Year** | A year-by-year account across the three years of STAY — what happened, what changed, what shifted, who showed up. |
| **The Voices** | Verbatim quotes from the people in this book. Drawn from Empathy Ledger with consent. The voices in the community's own words. |
| **The Photographs** | The visual register. Drawn from the studio's photo pipeline. Brave Ones-style portraits where the subject has consented to that register. Documentary photographs of the work happening. |
| **The Travel Diary chapter** | If a young person from this community travelled to Diagrama (Spain) or Halt (Holland) or the Children's Hearings (Scotland) in Year 2, the Travel Diary chapter is included here. The same notebook the European visitor brought back is also kept here. |
| **What we ask of you next** | The community's own ask of the reader — what they want next, who to contact, how to help, how not to help. |

## Cultural protocol — non-negotiable

Per the [`../../projects/justicehub/the-full-idea.md`](../../projects/justicehub/the-full-idea.md) §6:

1. **No community is approached without prior consultation with existing ACT relationships in that region.**
2. **Elder and Council sign-off before any photographs, recordings, or journals begin.**
3. **OCAP and CARE protocols at the data layer via Empathy Ledger** — the legal and technical baseline, not aspirational.
4. **The community owns the volume.** Not ACT. Not the funder. The community holds the editorial pen, the right of withdrawal at any point, and the income from sales.
5. **Subjects under 18 require guardian and cultural authority sign-off.**
6. **Cultural review fees are budgeted as line items, not absorbed as goodwill labour.**
7. **No community volume is launched without the community present at the launch.** This is their book, not ours.
8. **Income from book / exhibition sales flows on a published transparent split** to subjects and their nominated community organisations.

If we get the protocol wrong, the volume should not exist.

## How a community volume gets built

The build process is intentionally slow — Fire Crescendo applies. You do not arrive at Bonfire stage in Year 1.

| Year | Stage | What happens in this folder |
|---|---|---|
| **Year 1 (Listen)** | Ember + Tinder | One Aunty, one yarn, one set of permissions. The folder is mostly blank. The relationship is the work. |
| **Year 2 (Compare)** | Kindling + Campfire | First photographs (with consent), first voices captured (via Empathy Ledger), first written sections drafted by community members. The Travel Diary chapter starts after the European visit. |
| **Year 3 (Tell)** | Roaring fire + Bonfire | The volume is bound. The community-led launch happens in the community itself, on Country. The book then travels to bookshops, libraries, schools, and the community-owned entity collects the income from sales. |

## Connection to the studio

Every photograph, every voice transcript, every handwritten page that ends up in a community volume passes through the studio at [`../studio/`](../studio/) first. The studio is the production layer. The locations folder is where the produced material is bound into the form the community owns.

This means the community volumes do not need to be edited in isolation — they assemble from the same shared production infrastructure that feeds the master book chapters and the series method books.
