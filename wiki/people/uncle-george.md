# Uncle George

> Kalkadoon Elder in Mount Isa whose method is simple and difficult: show up, know the kids, and turn presence into re-engagement.

## Overview

Uncle George is a Kalkadoon Elder based in Mount Isa, currently surfaced in ACT's knowledge base through [[mount-isa|Mount Isa (Kalkadoon Country)]], [[bg-fit|BG Fit]], and the Empathy Ledger candidate queue. The candidate report identifies him as an Elder with one public story record, Kalkadoon cultural background, and Mount Isa as his location. (Source: `wiki/output/el-people-candidates-latest.json`)

The current verified wiki holds a sparse but meaningful picture of his work: he is one of the Elders getting children re-engaged in school through presence, conversation, and steady relationship rather than punitive response.

## Presence Over Surveillance

In the [[mount-isa|Mount Isa]] article, Uncle George is named as "another Elder working in schools" whose approach mirrors the logic behind Brodie Germaine's youth work. His method is quoted directly:

> "Pull 'em up and have a chat... We've got some kids back there that haven't been to school for years and now they're enjoying it... because the thing is I get 'em together."

That line makes him important beyond a single mention. It names the operating logic behind a whole Mount Isa ecosystem: relationship first, visibility without policing, authority without institutional distance.

## Relationship to BG Fit

The Empathy Ledger candidate report currently links Uncle George to [[bg-fit|BG Fit]]. The fit is conceptual as much as operational: his work in schools and Brodie's work in the gym express the same community pattern. In both cases, young people respond to people who are present, known, and culturally grounded.

## Why He Matters in ACT

Uncle George is a good example of why the people layer should stay curated. The wiki does not need every storyteller, but it does need the people whose specific way of working reveals how a whole community system functions. Uncle George helps make the Mount Isa thesis legible.
