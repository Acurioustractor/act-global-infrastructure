## The Shed

In [[green-harvest-witta|Green Harvest Witta]], Barry is described as the most important figure in understanding the site: eighty years old, on the land since 1972, with a shed full of machines that built the Sunshine Coast hinterland. The point is not nostalgia. His presence turns the site into a memory-bearing place.

The shed holds an AB184 log truck from 1963, ex-army Blitz trucks, a Valpadana tractor, and a 1957 Land Rover Barry hopes to restore "if I live long enough." He starts the machines sometimes just to hear them run. His line about rust being "like cancer in humans" carries the whole sensibility: this is someone watching a world decay and still trying to keep it alive.

## Why He Matters in ACT

Barry Rodgerig matters because The Harvest is not meant to be a lifestyle project dropped onto blank land. ACT's own founding narrative treats Barry as archaeology, continuity, and local truth. He is the reason the Witta site reads as a place with inheritance, not just potential.
