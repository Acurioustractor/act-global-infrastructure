# Benjamin Knight

> Co-founder of A Curious Tractor — photographer, technologist, and community practitioner building infrastructure for justice reinvestment and sovereign storytelling.

## Overview

Benjamin Knight is co-founder of [[../concepts/act-identity|A Curious Tractor (ACT)]] alongside Nicholas Marchesi OAM. He is the architect of the ACT technology ecosystem and the primary field practitioner behind [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] and [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]].

Ben grew up in Muswellbrook, NSW. His practice spans photography, community engagement, and technology — shaped by a long apprenticeship in empathy, innovation, and resilience learned "by standing close to people's lives and letting their stories teach me what change actually takes."

## Formative Experiences

### Orange Sky (3 years)
Three years as photographer with Orange Sky Australia, sitting with people experiencing homelessness across Australia and New Zealand. This work taught the discipline that carries into justice work: "Earn trust slowly; don't take stories — receive them."

### South America
An encounter inside a prison in Bolivia, listening to men talk about hope. "That encounter didn't flatter me with the feeling of helping; it humbled me with the responsibility to keep learning."

### Mount Isa — Ground Truthing
NAIDOC week in Mount Isa crystallised the case for JusticeHub. Walking alongside families, Elders, and young people — meeting [[brodie-germaine|Brodie Germaine]] and witnessing the web of relationships, gyms, camping trips, and cultural practices that already change trajectories. The insight: "The answers live in community already. Our job is not to arrive as saviours but to remove friction, fund the intelligence that's there, and help it circulate."

### Minjerribah / North Stradbroke Island
The Minjerribah Moorgumpin Elders-in-Council modelled governance that holds culture at the centre, not the edge. "When Elders lead, young people feel the ground under their feet."

### Palm Island
[[../communities/palm-island|PICC]] demonstrated community control at scale — 200+ workforce spanning health, family services, and more. Evidence that "when you invest in capacity where people live, the system gets smarter and trust deepens."

### Spain — Diagrama
Visiting [[diagrama|Diagrama]]'s youth justice centres to learn what parts of that ecosystem might translate to Australia — and what must be adapted by, with, and for First Nations communities.

## Core Beliefs

- The solutions to youth justice are **community from community**
- Our role is to **resource without prescribing** — pay for intelligence that already exists and help it flow
- The work isn't to build new monuments; it's to **unblock the paths communities are already walking**
- **Evidence travels fastest as a human story** — funders and policymakers change their minds when they can see and feel what a young person experiences
- "Presence over surveillance" works — relationship-based approaches outperform compliance-based ones

## Why JusticeHub

> "JusticeHub sits at the intersection of everything I've learned: that stories move resources; that community wisdom gets lost without lightweight infrastructure; that innovation is social before it's technical; that change sticks when it's owned locally."
