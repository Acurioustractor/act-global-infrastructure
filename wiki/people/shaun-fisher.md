# Shaun Fisher

> Quandamooka food practitioner whose oysters make ACT's regenerative loop tangible: harvest, gather, return, remake.

## Overview

Shaun Fisher appears in ACT's wiki through [[fishers-oysters|Fishers Oysters]] and the founding material for [[the-harvest|The Harvest]]. The Empathy Ledger candidate queue identifies him as a public storyteller with three public story records, First Nations Australian cultural background, and a canonical link to Fishers Oysters. (Source: `wiki/output/el-people-candidates-latest.json`)

The current verified source base is enough to say something specific: Shaun is one of the people who makes ACT's ecological and material practice concrete.

## Fishers Oysters

[[fishers-oysters|Fishers Oysters]] describes a cultural-first aquaculture enterprise restoring Moreton Bay's oyster industry through Indigenous knowledge, community ownership, and regenerative practice. Shaun Fisher is the person most directly attached to that thread in ACT's founding story of The Harvest.

In [[harvest-property-arc|The Harvest: Property Arc and Founding Vision]], Nicholas Marchesi's Compendium names Shaun as a Goenpul man, one of the three clans of the Quandamooka People, and describes the oyster-shell cycle at The Harvest as "LCAA made physical": oysters sold direct to community, shells collected, then worked into benchtops and surfaces.

## Why He Matters in ACT

Shaun Fisher matters because he ties together enterprise, ecology, material culture, and method. He is a good example of ACT's best pattern: not an abstract sustainability claim, but a specific person carrying a return loop through food, country, and making.
