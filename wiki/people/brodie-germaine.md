# Brodie Germaine

> Founder of BG Fit, CAMPFIRE, and BAIL — transforming youth empowerment in Queensland through fitness, cultural connection, and mentorship on Kalkadoon Country.

## Overview

Brodie Germaine is a community leader and youth mentor based in Mount Isa on Kalkadoon Country. He founded BG Fit, a fitness and mentoring program, and leads two major youth initiatives: the CAMPFIRE Project and BAIL (Be an Indigenous Leader). His work sits at the intersection of physical wellness, cultural identity, and community-led justice alternatives.

Brodie's own story arcs from youth crime to community service. He is open about this trajectory, using his lived experience as the foundation for authentic mentorship: "Unfortunately I was part of youth crime growing up and I'm now in a position giving back."

## Programs

### BG Fit
A community gym and mentoring space in [[mount-isa|Mount Isa]] that serves as the hub for Brodie's youth work. Young people describe it simply: "He keeps us away from trouble." The gym functions as much as a relational space as a fitness facility.

### CAMPFIRE Project
A holistic, community-led program prioritising cultural connection, ancestral wisdom, and personal growth. Rooted in Elder guidance, it provides young people in the child protection system with tools for resilience and identity-building through:
- On-Country camping trips
- Yarning circles with Elders
- Fitness activities and mentorship
- Trauma-informed cultural immersion

### BAIL (Be an Indigenous Leader)
Combines fitness with cultural teachings for youth aged 12-17. Activities include touch football, fishing, yarning circles, and on-Country experiences. Target: 100+ young people per year. Goal: reduce reoffending by providing constructive alternatives and strengthening cultural connection.

## Community Recognition

Uncle Warren King, Kalkadoon Elder, on Brodie: "He comes from good stock... and he'll continue." The pride in Uncle Warren's endorsement carries the weight of cultural approval — success understood not as individual triumph but as continuation.

Nigel Tain (Men's Rugby Union): "We collaborated with Brodie last year to do a bit on country stuff... to see the growth in the kids... Brodie does an absolute amazing job for the community."

Young people: "I'd rather Brodie here 'cause it's face to face" — the irreplaceable value of presence over distance-based service delivery.

## Approach: Presence Over Surveillance

Brodie's methodology embodies a principle shared across ACT's ecosystem partners: that relationship-based intervention works where compliance-based intervention fails. He invites police officers on camping trips — transforming them from enforcers into human beings in the eyes of young people.

His mother Danielle Germaine articulates the intergenerational philosophy: "We just got to keep going, be resilient and not to give up. Even though at times where we think we can't do enough for these kids, we just gotta keep trying so they know that they've got someone there to listen out and look out for."

## Vision

Brodie envisions a comprehensive wellness centre integrating fitness, cultural education, and community support — a safe, inclusive space for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people to strengthen physical, mental, and emotional health.
