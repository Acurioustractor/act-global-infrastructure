# Rachel Atkinson

> CEO of PICC and one of the clearest contemporary leaders in ACT's Palm Island field, holding community control at organisational scale.

## Overview

Rachel Atkinson is the CEO of [[picc|Palm Island Community Company (PICC)]], and is also named in the wiki as Chairperson of QATSICPP. The current Empathy Ledger candidate queue identifies her as a public storyteller with eight public story records, Yorta Yorta cultural background, and Palm Island, Queensland as her location. (Source: `wiki/output/el-people-candidates-latest.json`)

In the current verified wiki, Rachel appears as a leadership figure wherever PICC moves from principle into operational reality: workforce scale, reporting burden, and infrastructure decisions.

## Leadership at PICC

[[picc|PICC]] names Rachel Atkinson as CEO of one of the strongest community-controlled proof points in the ACT ecosystem: a Palm Island organisation with a large Aboriginal workforce, integrated services, and a twenty-year arc toward full community control.

In [[picc-annual-report|PICC Annual Report]], Rachel is used to frame the accountability burden community organisations carry: overlapping funder requirements, short contracts for long-term services, and reporting loads that consume time that should go to service delivery. The annual report project is partly a response to that burden.

The same leadership logic appears in [[picc-centre-precinct|PICC Centre Precinct]], where PICC leadership is explicit that the organisation cannot credibly lead health and family services if its physical home is not fit for purpose.

## Why She Matters in ACT

Rachel Atkinson matters because she makes ACT's Palm Island thesis legible at institutional scale. Richard Cassidy explains the cultural philosophy; Rachel shows what it looks like when that philosophy is carried through staffing, infrastructure, reporting, and executive responsibility.
