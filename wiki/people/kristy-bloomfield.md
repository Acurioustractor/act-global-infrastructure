# Kristy Bloomfield

> Central Arrernte, Eastern Arrernte, and Alyawarra woman; Traditional Owner of Mparntwe; co-director of Oonchiumpa and one of the clearest relationship anchors in ACT's justice work.

## Overview

Kristy Bloomfield is a co-director of [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]], the Aboriginal-led youth civic agency in Mparntwe (Alice Springs). The current Empathy Ledger candidate queue identifies her as a public storyteller with strong mention signal, Aboriginal Australian - Traditional Owner cultural background, and Alice Springs, Northern Territory as her location. The verified wiki already contains a substantial account of who Kristy is and why she matters.

## Oonchiumpa

In [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]], Kristy is named as a Central Arrernte, Eastern Arrernte, and Alyawarra woman, a Traditional Owner of Mparntwe, and one of the two core directors of the organisation. The page makes clear that her authority is not procedural. It is cultural, familial, and place-based.

The same article explains that she grew up in Alice Springs and carries stories of sacred sites and burial grounds that only her family would know. Her grandfather was a Stolen Generations man taken to the Bungalow, and that history remains active in her leadership rather than sitting as distant biography.

## Why She Matters in ACT

Kristy Bloomfield matters because Oonchiumpa is one of ACT's strongest evidence cases for community-led justice alternatives, and Kristy is one of the people who makes that evidence real. She appears repeatedly across the JusticeHub and Oonchiumpa narrative field because she anchors the work in cultural authority, family connection, and practical care.
