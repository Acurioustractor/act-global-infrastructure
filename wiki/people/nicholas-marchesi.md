# Nicholas Marchesi OAM

> Co-founder of A Curious Tractor — social innovator, community builder, and the architect of Orange Sky Laundry, who learned at 20 that clean clothes are a pretext and belonging is the point.

## Overview

Nicholas Marchesi OAM is co-founder of [[../concepts/act-identity|A Curious Tractor (ACT)]] alongside Benjamin Knight. Before ACT, he co-founded Orange Sky Laundry — Australia's first free mobile laundry service for people experiencing homelessness — and received the Medal of the Order of Australia (OAM) for his service to the community.

Nic holds the Witta Farm property (on Jinibara Country, Sunshine Coast hinterland, QLD) in a family trust, leased to ACT — making him the landowner-in-partnership behind [[the-harvest|The Harvest]]. His practice spans social enterprise design, regenerative land practice, and the kind of deep community listening that shaped Orange Sky and now shapes everything ACT builds.

## Orange Sky: The Origin

### The Founding (2014)

At age 20, Nicholas Marchesi and Lucas Patchett borrowed a washing machine, mounted it in a van, and drove to a park in Brisbane where people experiencing homelessness gathered. The hypothesis was simple: free laundry for people who couldn't access it. What they discovered was something larger — that the five minutes of conversation while the wash cycle ran were as valuable as the clean clothes.

Orange Sky became Australia's first free mobile laundry service for people experiencing homelessness. What began with one van grew into a national service operating across Australia and New Zealand, reaching tens of thousands of people.

The insight at the core of Orange Sky — that the laundry is a pretext for dignity and connection — became foundational to how ACT thinks about all service design. When you remove the transaction, you create the conditions for relationship.

### OAM

Nic received the **Medal of the Order of Australia (OAM)** for his service to the community through Orange Sky. The recognition came not just for scale but for the way the model honoured people's dignity rather than their deficits.

### What Orange Sky Taught ACT

Every element of ACT's practice carries Orange Sky's fingerprints:
- Services designed around people, not around measurable outputs
- The physical act of showing up repeatedly in the same place
- Relationship as infrastructure, not outcome
- The laundry (or the cafe, or the garden) as the pretext; community as the point

## The Harvest: Witta Property

### The Compendium (February 2026)

The richest document in ACT's founding record for The Harvest isn't a business plan — it's a brainstorm NM wrote in February 2026, structured in nine movements, which captured the full vision before the lease was signed. It is archived at [[../research/harvest-property-arc|Harvest Property Arc]].

The Compendium opens with "The Silence" — Witta has no gathering place. Two thousand cars pass through every weekend. The hinterland is hollowing out. This is not a market gap analysis; it is a listening exercise made visible.

Key movements:
- **The Shed** — [[barry-rodgerig|Barry Rodgerig]], 80 years old, whose shed full of working machines is archaeology of what the hinterland was
- **The Seed** — four beliefs, not services: *Eat. Gather. Make. Grow.*
- **The Cycle** — [[shaun-fisher|Shaun Fisher]]'s oyster shells, which become benchtops at The Harvest in a cycle NM explicitly names as "LCAA made physical"
- **The Handover** — "Maybe it's ACT in Year 4. Maybe it's not." Beautiful Obsolescence written into the founding document, eight days before the lease was signed

> "You're not coming to something finished — you're coming to something you can be part of."

## As Co-Founder of ACT

Nic and Benjamin Knight co-founded A Curious Tractor as a dual-entity structure: ACT Foundation (CLG, charitable) and ACT Ventures (mission-locked trading). Where Ben is primarily the technology and field practitioner, Nic brings the social enterprise design sensibility, community hospitality, and the lived understanding of what it means to build something for handover.

Their practice together follows the [[../concepts/lcaa-method|LCAA method]]: they met through a shared commitment to listening before building. The Harvest is the most visible expression of Nic's contribution to ACT's physical infrastructure.

## Global Laundry Alliance

Orange Sky's model has influenced a growing international network: the [[global-laundry-alliance|Global Laundry Alliance]], which connects community laundry hubs worldwide and advocates for policy recognition of hygiene as a dimension of human dignity. Nic's founding work at Orange Sky is the seed from which the Alliance grows.

That same mobile-dignity logic also carries into [[feel-good-project|Feel Good Project]], where ACT helped build the caravan infrastructure for mobile hair and beauty care rather than expecting women in crisis to come to a fixed service.

## Working Style

Nic brings a particular quality of hospitality to ACT's work — the kind that comes from years of setting up a van in a park and waiting for trust to form. His Compendium shows a writer who listens first and describes what he finds before proposing what to build. The Harvest is the result: a hub designed around four verbs (*eat, gather, make, grow*), not around four services.
