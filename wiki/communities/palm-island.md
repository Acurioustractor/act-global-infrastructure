# Palm Island (Bwgcolman)

> From penal settlement to self-determined community — a story of survival, sovereignty, and innovation.

## Overview

Palm Island is a remote Aboriginal community in the Hinchinbrook Shire, North Queensland. Home to the Bwgcolman and Manbarra peoples, with a population of approximately 2,500.

## History

Palm Island was established as a **penal settlement** and site of intense departmental control under the "Protection Era." Aboriginal people from across Queensland were forcibly relocated here. The community survived:
- Segregation and restricted movement
- Forced removal of children
- Intense government control over daily life
- Transgenerational trauma from the Aborigines Protection Act

Despite this history, Palm Island has become a **beacon of community self-determination**.

## The Sovereignty Journey

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2007 | PICC established (tri-partisan: QLD Government + Shire Council + community) |
| 2007-2021 | Hybrid model — state/council shareholders, capacity building phase |
| 2021 | **Full community control** — all shares transferred to community members |
| 2027 | 20th anniversary of PICC |

## Cultural Significance

Palm Island's four pillars of identity:
1. **Family** — kinship connections maintained through local workforce and Safe House model
2. **Culture** — cultural authority embedded in service delivery
3. **Language** — Manbarra language preservation
4. **Country** — children remain on country, not sent to mainland institutions
