# The Buttery

> The Buttery is a drug and alcohol rehabilitation and treatment service based in Bangalow, northern New South Wales. Founded in 1976, it is one of Australia's longest-running therapeutic communities.

**Relationship to ACT:** The Buttery is one of four Y1 bespoke customers for [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]]'s Annual Field Service (alongside JCF, [[picc|PICC]], and [[smart-recovery|SMART Recovery]]), at roughly $50K annual engagement.

## What The Buttery Does

The Buttery operates residential rehabilitation programs for people experiencing drug and alcohol addiction, using the therapeutic community (TC) model. Residents live together in a structured, supportive environment where recovery happens through shared responsibility, mutual aid, and peer accountability rather than purely clinical intervention. Programs typically run 16 to 26 weeks.

The organisation also delivers non-residential services including day programs, family support, aftercare, and community outreach across the Northern Rivers region. It is a member of the Australasian Therapeutic Communities Association (ATCA).

## History

Founded in 1976 by a group of community members in the Byron Bay hinterland, The Buttery is one of Australia's oldest therapeutic communities. The name comes from the original property, a former dairy in Bangalow. For nearly fifty years it has operated continuously through shifting government policy, funding cycles, and public attitudes toward addiction, maintaining its founding commitment to community-based recovery.

The Buttery's longevity is itself evidence: a community-controlled model that has survived five decades of institutional pressure to centralise, medicalise, or defund residential rehabilitation.

## Why It Matters to ACT

The Buttery is a natural partner for [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] because recovery narratives are among the most powerful and most exploited stories in the social sector. People in residential rehab share deeply personal experiences of addiction, trauma, family breakdown, and transformation. Those stories are routinely extracted by funders, media, and government for reporting purposes, with little regard for the storyteller's ongoing consent or dignity.

Empathy Ledger's consent-governed storytelling infrastructure gives The Buttery's residents and alumni sovereign control over their recovery narratives. Stories can be shared, restricted, or withdrawn at any time. This is not a technology overlay on existing practice; it is the missing layer that makes ethical storytelling possible in a context where vulnerability is the baseline.
