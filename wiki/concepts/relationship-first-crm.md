# Relationship-First CRM — belonging before money

> A normal CRM sorts people by what they pay. This one sorts people by energy. Money is plumbing that runs underneath — never the thing that defines the relationship.

## The inversion

Most CRMs exist to move a crowd toward a transaction and rank people by dollars contributed. ACT's does the opposite. What Ben and Nic want to hold is the **orbit** — the general people around A Curious Tractor who bring *doing, energy, and life*. **They do not have to be philanthropic. They do not have to give money.** The qualifier for belonging is energy, not a cheque.

This is the same refusal that runs through everything ACT builds: a [[ghl-crm-strategy-map|belonging ladder, not a funnel]]. Here it goes one step further — belonging is decoupled from money entirely.

## Belonging is decoupled from money

The relationship rings (curious → connected → member → active → steward) measure **relationship depth and energy**, full stop. Someone can sit dead-centre of the inner circle having given ACT zero dollars, because what put them there is that they *do things* and *bring life*. The old rung "Member = gave money" is retired; money no longer earns a rung.

This is exactly why the inner circle — the **Getting Shit Done Alliance** (`circle:gsd-alliance`) — is **hand-curated by Ben**, not computed. Energy can't be inferred from a payment or an email count. It's a relationship judgement, and only a human makes it.

## Three layers

The model has three layers on two different axes. The relationship core is **identity** (who someone is to ACT); the pipelines are **activity** (what's happening with them right now). A person can be in all three at once.

| Layer | What it is | Money? |
|---|---|---|
| **Relationship core** | the orbit + the Alliance — the human CRM | money-agnostic |
| **Money pipelines** | philanthropic (process dollars) + per-project commercial (Harvest, Goods) | money lives *here*, as overlay |
| **Sovereign showcase** | Empathy Ledger — value-back + case-study evidence + the community OCAP line | not for sale |

## Money is plumbing — two kinds of pipe, neither one is identity

- **Philanthropic pipeline** — funders and grants, to *process dollars* cleanly: the ask, the tranche, the acquittal. A pipe someone passes through; not who they are. (See [[ecosystem-value-exchange]] for the funder give/get.)
- **Commercial pipelines** — Harvest and Goods: customers, buyers, commercial partners. This is the pipe that **pays Ben and Nic** and keeps each project *healthy on its own legs*. It is how ACT steps out of the transactional philanthropic world and shows that **innovation and art make a viable venture**, not a grant line. Each project owns its own commercial pipeline (Goods buyers, Harvest customers); the relationship core sits *above* them, cross-project.

The whole point of separating these: money becomes visible *as plumbing*, so the relationship is never quietly reduced to it. A doer who gives $0 and a funder who writes a cheque but brings no energy are read correctly — one is inner-circle, one is in a pipe. This is [[four-lanes|The Four Lanes]] applied to people: don't let Grants become the identity when Flow and Ground (commercial) are how the founders get paid.

## The value exchange runs on showcase, not reward

The "get" for the people in ACT's orbit is not a transactional reward. It is **sovereign showcase**: the people who hang out with ACT get *extreme value* because they get to **show who they really are, really well — and they own it**. That ownership is [[consent-as-infrastructure|consent as infrastructure]] and [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]], where data sovereignty is architectural, not a feature.

And those same stories do a second job: they are the **case studies that prove the model**. Art + innovation → a successful existence, evidenced in the words of the people who lived it. One engine, two outputs — value back to the person, and proof for ACT.

## Artists, not heroes

The frame underneath all of it: showcase Ben and Nic as **artists running real ventures** — not benefactors. The storytelling layer must point the camera at *the people and the projects' success*, never at ACT's generosity. The moment a case study reads "look what we did for them," it has fallen into the **hero trap of philanthropy** — the same line ACT already holds by partnering *with* communities and never positioning as the savior (see [[act-identity]]). Sovereign showcase is the antidote: the subject is the storyteller's success and the venture's health, with ACT as the artist alongside, not the rescuer above.

## What this changes in the CRM (the teeth)

- `tier:` = **relationship depth (energy/doing), never money.** The "Member = gave money" rung is retired.
- `circle:gsd-alliance` = the **hand-curated** inner ring; orthogonal to `tier:`.
- Money lives in **pipelines** — philanthropic + per-project commercial — as overlay stages/tags, never as a belonging rung.
- **Per-project boards (Harvest, Goods) are commercial pipelines** (customers/buyers → founder pay + project health), distinct from the relationship core.
- The **community lane is unchanged** — OCAP constellation, co-owners, never laddered, never in a pipe (see [[ecosystem-value-exchange]] § the community lane).
