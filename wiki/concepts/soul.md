# Soul

> Two humans, one tractor. This is why we do it.

## Frame

This is the upstream file. Everything else in the wiki, identity, method, projects, money, is downstream of two specific people choosing to do this together. If ACT disappeared tomorrow, the drives in this file would still exist. They predate the org. They are older than the vehicle.

Two voices. First person each. We harmonise. We are not identical.

This is a draft. Edit freely. Voice has to be ours before it goes anywhere else.

---

## 1. What each of us did, alone, before ACT existed, that already pointed at this

**Ben.** I grew up in Muswellbrook. Then I spent three years sitting with people on the street as a photographer for Orange Sky. Then I went to a prison in Bolivia and listened to men talk about hope. Then NAIDOC week in Mount Isa, where I met Brodie and watched a community already running its own answers. The pattern was always the same. Stand close to a life. Receive what is offered. Resist the urge to arrive with a fix. Every job I have ever done has been an apprenticeship in not interrupting.

**Nic.** I was twenty. Lucas and I bolted a washing machine into a van and drove to a park in Brisbane. We went to do laundry. We discovered we were really there to do conversation. The five minutes while the cycle ran was the actual product. From the first morning, I was building the same thing I am building now. A room where the official transaction is a pretext, and the real thing happening is people remembering they belong to each other.

---

## 2. What we would still do if funding, structure, and audience disappeared tomorrow

**Ben.** I would still photograph. I would still drive long distances to sit on someone's verandah. I would still write down what an Elder said while we both watched the sun drop. I would still build small tools that take administrative pain off people doing the real work. The org gives me leverage. The org does not give me the drive.

**Nic.** I would still build things. Whatever was rad. Whatever needed work. I would find people to have conversations with and listen to what they were trying to do. I would look for the things that were broken for no reason, and fix them so they worked again. Then I would sit with what I had fixed and think about how it could be built better next time. The van in the park was that. The Harvest is that. If ACT closed on Monday, by Tuesday I would be looking for the next thing that worked once, stopped working, and could work again.

---

## 3. Where the two souls overlap, and where they do not

**Ben.** We overlap on the ethic. Listen first. Build for handover. Refuse saviour framing. Refuse the deficit story. Where we differ: Nic moves through place and people. I move through systems and story. Nic puts a kettle on. I open a database. Both end up in the same kitchen, but we walk in through different doors. I am the one who builds the technology spine and holds the justice field. If I stepped out, the function could be replaced. The angle could not. The way I see Empathy Ledger and JusticeHub is mine because I came at them from photography and prison-room listening, not from product management. That angle does not transfer.

**Nic.** I am the one who holds the place and the hospitality. The Harvest is not a business plan, it is a way I treat a property and a town. Barry's shed only got into our founding document because I went and sat in it. The lease with Grant is held together by trust that took months. Someone could run the cafe after me. They could not run the room I made by sitting in it for two years. Ben can build infrastructure on top of any place, but he needs a place to build it on, and the places I bring to ACT are mine because of how I show up to them, not because I own them. We overlap on patience. We diverge on whether the work begins with a screen or with a kettle.

## 4. What it costs us, and what it gives back that nothing else does

**Ben.** It costs my time, my attention, and a steady erosion of the line between work and life. It costs me money. Nic and I have not paid each other enough this year. The entity is only just becoming the right shape and we can fix it, but the weirdness of running an organisation that pays everyone but its founders is real and it lands. It costs Carla time, and Pollyanna and Jolie time, and weekends other people spend at the beach. What it gives me back: I have stood in rooms where a young person told me their story for the first time, and I knew the room only existed because I had built the conditions for it. There is no other job that gives that.

**Nic.** It costs me the cleaner version of the life I could be living. Orange Sky was on a track I could have stayed on and grown. Choosing ACT meant choosing the ambiguous middle. It costs me certainty about how anything pays, and Ben and I have not paid each other enough this year, which is fixable but real. What it gives me back: I get to wake up on land I love, with people I trust, working on questions I actually care about. I am close to my parents and I want to stay that way. I want to be a father one day. The Harvest is partly a place I am building so the people I love most can be near it. Most people work to afford their life. I get to work as my life. That is the trade I made.

## 5. The first signal each of us would feel that we'd lost the soul of the work

**Ben.** When I stop being able to name the people the work is for. When the dashboards become the work. When I am writing a funder report I do not believe. When a community has to chase me for something they already gave me. That is the day I would know.

**Nic.** When the table at Witta has nobody at it I actually know. When the work becomes about scaling rather than showing up. When I am pitching The Harvest more than I am running it. When I find myself describing the place in slides rather than walking it. The first signal would be a quiet one. The room would still look like the room. It would just feel rented.

---

## 6. Build, connect, make art. Whose drives are they?

**Ben.** I do all three. The textures are different. Build is systems and data. JusticeHub, Empathy Ledger, the wiki, the agents, the dashboards. I build to move evidence faster than the system that resists it. Art is photography, older than the engineering. The picture came before the platform. Connect is the hardest one for me. I have to keep choosing it. I am better at building a platform that helps a community connect than I am at sitting in the room myself.

**Nic.** I do all three too, textured differently. Build is rooms, places, things that were broken and could work again. The van, the laundry rig, the property at Witta, the lease that took months. I build to make conditions for connection to land somewhere it can stay. Connect is what I do without trying. The kettle, the table, the long sit with Barry. Art I do quietly. Gold.Phone, The Confessional, the Compendium itself. These are art held under the surface of the work, not announced as art.

**Together.** Build. Connect. Make art. Both of us do all three. The differences are in the texture, not the verb. Ben's build moves evidence. Nic's build makes rooms. Ben's art is in the lens. Nic's art is in the gesture. Connect we share, but Ben has to choose it and Nic does it without trying. Listen, Curiosity, Action, Art did not come from a whiteboard. It is a description of what happens when we sit at the kitchen table and refuse to leave until the thing works. Curiosity is the bridge. Curiosity is what keeps each of us from getting stuck on our own texture.
