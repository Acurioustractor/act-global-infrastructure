# The Four Lanes

> ACT's internal economy. How money earned through the work flows back to the soul, the founders, the debts, the growth, and the communities. The body the soul lives in.

This file lives downstream of [[soul|Soul]]. It exists because the soul has a body, and the body needs feeding.

This is a draft. Edit freely. The shape has to be right before the wiring is.

---

## The Premise

Most organisations that work in the space ACT works in struggle to pay the people doing the work. The founders survive on grants, then burn out, then leave. The work either dies with them, or is absorbed by the next generation of consultants who do not carry the soul.

ACT's economic model rejects that pattern. Custodianship over ownership only works if the custodians can keep showing up. That means money has to flow not just to the work, but to the people doing it, to the debts being cleared, to the projects being grown, and to the communities being served. All four. Not one at the expense of the others.

We named four lanes. Every dollar that moves through ACT lands in one of them.

---

## The Four Lanes

### To Us
Director wages. Distributions through Knight Family Trust and Marchesi Family Trust. Anything that lands in Ben's life or Nic's life, or the lives of the people they go home to.

This lane exists because two humans hold the soul. If they cannot keep showing up, the soul has nowhere to live.

### To Down
Debts paid. Old liabilities cleared. Receivables collected. ATO obligations met. The $507K in outstanding invoices we are still chasing.

This lane exists because an org that owes money cannot move freely. Clearing the past is what frees the future.

### To Grow
Reinvestment into A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd, Harvest Pty Ltd, Farm Pty Ltd, and the projects each entity carries. New equipment for the Goods workshop. Site costs at Witta. Engineering hours for Empathy Ledger. Travel to Mount Isa.

This lane exists because the work has to keep getting better. Growth here is not scale. It is depth.

### To Others
Donations to A Kind Tractor when it activates. Fellowship payments. Anchor partner support. Community grants out. The hospitality money that lands in someone else's pocket because they were on the table when we earned it.

This lane exists because ACT exists in service of communities. A dollar that never reaches a community has not done its job.

## The Entity Flow

Money does not move in a single ledger. It moves through entities, and the structure is part of how the soul holds across the work.

### A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd
ACN 697 347 676. Registered 24 April 2026. Owned 50/50 by Knight Family Trust and Marchesi Family Trust. Directors: Ben and Nic. Bank: NAB. Accountant: Standard Ledger.

This is the trade muscle. Service revenue, contracts (QBE Catalysing Impact is the first), grants that need a corporate trustee land here. From here, distributions flow to the trusts, and from the trusts to the founders. The dual-trust ownership is what makes To Us clean instead of awkward.

### Harvest Pty Ltd (forming)
Standalone P&L for The Harvest. Lease, cafe, garden, table, events, retreats. Earns its own revenue. Reinvests its own growth. Distributes back when it makes sense.

The reason Harvest gets its own entity is so its story is legible. We can see what The Harvest cost, what it earned, what it bought, and what it gave away, without it mashing into a single ACT ledger that makes none of it readable.

### Farm Pty Ltd (forming)
Same logic. Standalone P&L for ACT Farm. Conservation work, land practice, residencies, the BCV arc. Earns its own. Grows its own.

### A Kind Tractor Ltd
ACN 669 029 341. Company Limited by Guarantee. ACNC-registered. Currently dormant. Not yet DGR-endorsed.

When A Kind Tractor activates, it becomes the channel for tax-deductible donations and DGR-only philanthropic capital. It holds the public-good cap. Until activation, it sits ready.

### Sole trader (Nic's ABN 21 591 780 066)
The legacy entity. Trading migrates out of here into A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd by 30 June 2026. Pre-cutover invoices stay with the sole trader. After cutover, no new work runs through it.

## Why Three Pty Ltds, Not One

Because each project earns the right to grow on its own revenue. The Harvest's money funds the Harvest's growth. Farm money funds Farm growth. A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd is the holding muscle that carries the founder relationship.

If we ran a single Pty Ltd with three project codes, the financial story would mash. To Grow at Harvest would be invisible to Farm. Founders would have no clean way to see whether each project pays its way.

The structure costs more in compliance. It saves more in legibility. Legibility is what makes the soul able to read its own body.

## LCAA in Money

Every dollar that moves through any ACT entity gets two tags: which project, and which Listen-Curiosity-Action-Art phase.

- **Listen** dollars: fieldwork, story collection, time spent on country, research with no deliverable yet.
- **Curiosity** dollars: prototypes, experiments, R&D, the engineering of things that have not been proven to work.
- **Action** dollars: delivery, services, operations, infrastructure, things shipping.
- **Art** dollars: artefacts, journals, public works, cultural production, the surface of the work.

A quarter's spend can be reported as: `Listen 28% / Curiosity 22% / Action 32% / Art 18%`. That ratio is the answer to "are we living the method, or just talking about it?"

When Listen drops below 20%, we have stopped sitting in rooms before we build. When Art drops below 10%, the work has gone quiet. When Action breaches 50%, we are scaling rather than seeding. The ratios are not rules. They are signals.

## Founder Sustainability is Method, Not Tax

The honest line, written into `soul.md` already: Ben and Nic have not paid each other enough this year. The entity is only just becoming the right shape. We can fix it. The weirdness of running something that pays everyone but its founders is real, and it lands.

This file exists partly so that line is taken seriously and not treated as a confession. Custodianship over ownership requires the custodians to keep showing up. A founder who cannot pay their mortgage cannot stay. A founder who burns out is not regenerative.

To Us is therefore not a tax on the work. It is part of the work. A dollar paid to Ben so he can be present with Carla, Pollyanna, and Jolie is the same kind of dollar as a fellowship paid to a community partner. A dollar paid to Nic so he can keep building places his parents recognise as his is the same kind of dollar as a donation to A Kind Tractor.

Both are how the soul keeps a body to live in.

## Beautiful Obsolescence Applied to Money

Same discipline as everything else ACT does. The point is not to build a financial empire. The point is to build a system that, if Ben and Nic stepped away, could still run.

That means:

- Standalone P&Ls so each project's economy is portable.
- Documentation good enough that someone else could read the books.
- Distributions structured so the founders are not the bottleneck.
- A charity arm ready to absorb the public-good elements when the time comes.

We are not building this to be inherited by family. We are building it to be handed back to the work itself.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

The four lanes show up in three places.

### Notion top card (daily)
A live snapshot of the current quarter. Updated weekly from Xero.

```
Q1 FY27 to date
  To Us      $X
  To Down    $Y
  To Grow    $Z
  To Others  $W

LCAA: Listen 28% · Curiosity 22% · Action 32% · Art 18%
Founders YTD: Ben $A · Nic $B
```

### Weekly digest (every Monday)
One line added to the existing digest:

> Lane most behind this week: To Us. Soul check: when did you last sit in a room with someone you did not have to convince of anything?

### Public translation (act.place/economy)
Not financials. Story told in money. A funder or community partner reads:

> Q4 To Grow funded the Goods pitch to Minderoo and the BG Fit fieldwork. Q4 To Others sent X to anchor partners. Q4 To Us paid the founders for the first time at the level the entity now allows.

The numbers do not need to be exact in public. The shape of the four lanes does.

---

## What This is Not

This is not a tax minimisation strategy. The structure is what the structure is because of how the work needs to be held, not because we optimised for tax.

This is not a financial transparency platform. CivicGraph does that for other people's public money. ACT's own transparency is for ACT's people first, and for our partners as a courtesy.

This is not a justification for under-paying anyone. It is the opposite. The lanes exist precisely so under-payment cannot hide.
