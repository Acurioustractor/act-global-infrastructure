# Indigenous Data Sovereignty

> The right of Indigenous peoples to govern the collection, ownership, and application of data about their communities, lands, and cultures.

## CARE Principles

The foundational framework, developed by the Global Indigenous Data Alliance (GIDA):

- **Collective benefit** — data ecosystems designed for Indigenous innovation and self-determination
- **Authority to control** — Indigenous peoples' rights to control and govern their data
- **Responsibility** — those working with Indigenous data have a responsibility to nurture respectful relationships
- **Ethics** — Indigenous peoples' rights and wellbeing must be the primary concern

## Mukurtu CMS

**Mukurtu** is a Warumungu word meaning "dilly bag" — a safe keeping place for sacred materials.

Created in 2007 as a grassroots project, Mukurtu supports Indigenous communities worldwide to safeguard cultural knowledge while respecting traditional protocols. Built on **Drupal**, which has recently integrated the **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** — enabling AI agents to securely connect while respecting access controls.

### Why Mukurtu Matters for ACT

- **TK Labels** embedded directly in metadata — restrictions based on gender, seasonal conditions, sacredness
- **Drupal + MCP** = AI agents can query the archive while mathematically obeying cultural protocols
- **Jumbunna Institute (UTS)** partnership for digitizing significant Aboriginal political history
- Perfect fit for PICC's 20-year archive and Empathy Ledger content

### Integration with ACT Stack

Mukurtu provides the **governance layer** for the [[local-ai-architecture|Local AI Architecture]]:
1. Raw cultural materials stored in Mukurtu with TK Labels
2. YAML frontmatter on every markdown file carries protocol metadata
3. AI agents read TK Labels before processing — cannot synthesize restricted content
4. Community retains absolute ownership of both data and intelligence

## Traditional Knowledge (TK) Labels

Digital tags from the [Local Contexts Hub](https://localcontexts.org/) that identify place-based knowledge carrying cultural rights:

- **TK Attribution** — credit to the community
- **TK Non-Commercial** — not for commercial use without consent
- **TK Culturally Sensitive** — contains sensitive material
- **TK Community Voice** — only community can speak to meaning
- **TK Seasonal** — access restricted to certain times
- **TK Men/Women Restricted** — gender-specific protocols

## Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC)

For Empathy Ledger narratives especially — consent must be:
- **Ongoing** (not one-time sign-off)
- **Revocable** (community can withdraw data at any stage)
- **Granular** (different levels for different uses)
- **Culturally appropriate** (consent processes designed with community)

## Sami Precedent (Sweden)

The Sami people (Sapmi region across Sweden, Norway, Finland) are pushing institutions to adopt CARE principles as a mandatory global standard through the GIDA-Sapmi network. The Sami Parliament's push for data rights demonstrates that sovereign storytelling can be formalized into national policy.
