# PPPP Operating Logic

> PPPP is a **regenerative field scan, not a project list**. ACT runs ~30 active threads at any time, and the real risk is never a shortage of ideas — it's that 30 projects become 30 liabilities. PPPP keeps the whole ecosystem legible by sorting everything into the few domains that decide whether impact grows, so the weekly question stays small: *what does the field need this week, and what can we stop carrying?*

The shared working surface is the Notion PPPP board (Ben + Nic). This page is the canonical source for the *logic* — the board is a view of it.

## The five Ps — what each really is, and where it lives

Each P already has a system home and a skill cluster. The board names the domain; the systems do the carrying.

| P | What it really is | System home | Skills that serve it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **People** 🔴 | The soil — the relational substrate. No impact grows without it. | [[the-field]] (Dunbar rings + community constellation), GHL, person pages | `orbit`, `consent-check`, `sync-storytellers` |
| **Project** 🟠 | The seeds being prototyped — where listen → prototype → handover actually happens | GHL opportunities + project codes + the Supabase ledger | `align-ghl`, `enrich-project`, `tag-transactions` |
| **Place** 🟡 | Country sets the pace. Witta, Palm Island, Mparntwe, Mt Isa, Brisbane. | Deliberately **not** systematised — geographies ground the data; automation stays out | none, on purpose |
| **Process** 🟣 | The operating spine — admin pushed into systems so humans return to relationship, ceremony, care, making | **The skill library itself**, plus crons, cockpits, the weekly scan | nearly everything else (`bas-cycle`, `reconcile-cycle`, `wiki`, `deploy`, …) |
| **Product** 🟢 | The artefacts that get handed over — tangible, ownable, obsolescence-ready | The wiki, Empathy Ledger, the physical Goods | `content-publisher`, `wiki`, the consent gates |

**The key asymmetry:** Process is not one of five equal columns. Process is the P that makes the other four legible *without consuming the humans*. Every skill written is a piece of admin moved into the system so a person gets their attention back. And Place is its mirror — the one P that can never become a skill. Five Ps where one is deliberately un-automatable is the design, not a gap.

## The skill-library theory — where ACT diverges

The 2026 framing (Hiten Shah, *"Every Company's First AI Strategy Should Be a Skill Library"*, 2026-06-05) says: connectors give agents access, skills give them judgment, and the library of private skills becomes the company's moat. ACT already runs this — ~65 skills across global and repo level, the rules files, this wiki as the brain. But ACT's theory diverges on three points, and the divergence is the strategy:

1. **The most important skills encode refusal, not performance.** The generic version of a skill library captures how the best performer works fastest. ACT's highest-value skills are *boundary* skills: `consent-check` blocks publishing when the OCAP trail is incomplete; the orbit model hard-codes that the community lane is never laddered, scored, or dripped — and it has already caught a live violation (community storytellers tagged into a funder drip). The deepest judgment made reusable is the judgment about what **not** to do. Automate admin, never relationship.

2. **The library is a handover, not a moat.** The generic thesis: "the best skills will be private" — package expertise to keep it. [[beautiful-obsolescence]] inverts this: a skill is the method made legible enough to *give away*. When Goods hands to The Butterfly Movement, when a community runs its own storytelling pipeline, the skill library is part of what transfers. Same mechanics, opposite teleology — success is when communities run it without us.

3. **Access + skills isn't enough — there's a third layer: standing.** Connectors give context, skills give method, but [[the-field]] gives *standing*: who this person is to us, which lane they're on, what we owe them. No skill fires safely without that. The relational substrate is hand-tended precisely so the automated layers can be trusted.

**The one-line theory:** everyone will have the same models. ACT's edge is a skill library that encodes not how we work fastest but where the lines are — and it's written to be handed over, not hoarded. PPPP is the field scan; the Field is who we're accountable to; skills are the method; the wiki is the memory. Place is the P we refuse to automate.

## The hidden engine — time and cadence

The Now / Next / Later columns and the monthly run turn each P into a time-phased rhythm rather than a static list — the sense → prototype → review → ship loop made visible (see [[lcaa-method]]). The weekly PPPP scan and weekly cockpit are the heartbeat: a triage that turns reflection into fewer commitments, clearer decisions, and visible proof. The desired shift it serves: from "20+ active threads, low completion" to "2 flagship pilots with clear outcomes."

## The Place-vs-Pulse fork (open decision, Ben + Nic)

The April–June Social Soil cycle named the five domains Projects · People · **Pulses** · Process · Products, where Pulses meant calendar/rhythm. The current board uses **Place** instead. The recommended position:

**Keep Place; absorb Pulse into Process.** Rhythm already lives in Process concretely — the weekly cockpit, the Monday crons, the morning Field read, the BAS quarter cycle are literally scheduled skills; a separate Pulse P would duplicate Process's heartbeat. Place, meanwhile, is the one P that can never be a skill: Country-led is the first principle, and Place is what keeps the whole board from collapsing into an automation diagram.

*Status: recommended, not yet agreed — settle with Nic before re-reading the board through it.*
