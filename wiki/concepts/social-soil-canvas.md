# Social Soil Canvas

> A founder-facing field diagnostic for tracing visible symptoms back to soil conditions, then choosing the smallest action that improves the whole system.

## Overview

The Social Soil Canvas is ACT's internal tool for founder-level diagnosis. It is used when the field feels noisy: too many live projects, blurred responsibilities, weak closure, or a sense that the visible work is drifting away from the conditions that actually sustain it.

It is not a brand exercise and it is not a journal prompt set. It is a working instrument for naming what is visible above ground, what is hidden beneath it, and what action will improve the field rather than just soothe the symptom.

The core premise is simple:

> *What grows above ground is a function of the soil below.*

Projects, revenue, partnerships, and public proof are the visible crop. Trust, role clarity, energy, infrastructure, and shared philosophy are the social soil. If the soil is compacted, the work above it eventually shows the damage.

## Why It Exists

ACT already has strong methods for community work — [[lcaa-method|LCAA]], [[alma|ALMA]], [[beautiful-obsolescence|Beautiful Obsolescence]], and the PTO discipline. The Social Soil Canvas exists because founders also need a method for diagnosing the field they themselves are standing in.

It is the internal, operator-facing companion to the public-facing **Field Canvas** in [[the-full-idea|The Full Idea]].

Where the STAY / JusticeHub field canvas asks a team or funder to look honestly at the field before changing it, the Social Soil Canvas asks the same thing of the founders:

- What is actually growing?
- What is draining energy?
- Which relationships or structures are carrying too much weight?
- Which projects belong in the tractor's drive lane now?
- What needs to be held, parked, or handed over?

## The Shape of the Canvas

The canvas has five layers.

### 1. North Star

The field you are trying to cultivate in this season.

This is the anti-reactivity move. It forces the week or quarter to orient around a field condition rather than a pile of incoming demands.

### 2. 5P Field Scan

The fast read of what is happening above ground:

| Domain | What it reads |
|---|---|
| **Projects** | What's active, stalled, overgrown, or close to proof |
| **People** | Who is carrying what, who decides, who is stretched |
| **Pulses** | The rhythm of the work: weekly, monthly, seasonal |
| **Process** | How work moves, closes, and hands over |
| **Products** | What is packaged, priced, legible, and reusable |

This is the layer outsiders usually see first. The canvas treats it as the starting point, not the whole truth.

### 3. Social Soil Grid

The layer beneath the visible field, read across **Self**, **Team**, and **Ecosystem**:

| Layer | What it asks |
|---|---|
| **Darkness / Friction** | Where is the field tight, avoidant, overburdened, or distorted? |
| **Compost / Learning** | What hard material is turning into signal, pattern, or intelligence? |
| **Rebirth / Repatterning** | What new behavior, structure, or commitment is ready to replace the old one? |

This is where the real work happens. Most strategy documents stay above ground. The Social Soil Canvas insists on reading the conditions beneath the crop.

### 4. Root System

The shared structures that hold the field together:

- infrastructure
- key relationships
- shared philosophy
- financial spine
- governance conditions

This is the layer that determines whether change can travel or whether it will snap at the first sign of stress.

### 5. Six Tracks

Every diagnostic ends in movement across six tracks:

1. **Identity**
2. **Co-founder**
3. **Core Venture**
4. **Ecosystem**
5. **Community**
6. **Next 96 Hours**

Each one ends in **Stop / Start / Proof**. If a reflection produces no proof line, it has not become operational yet.

## The Five Working Tags

The canvas uses a lightweight symbolic language:

- **SOIL** — hidden conditions, constraints, trust, and root causes
- **SEED** — emerging possibility, future pull, or honest opening
- **FURROW** — the next action that creates movement now
- **SPIRAL** — the recurring pattern or learning loop
- **SPROUT** — visible proof that something has shifted

These are not decorative symbols. They are compression tools. They let a founder move quickly from reflection to pattern recognition and from pattern recognition to action.

## Relationship to the Wiki and Tractorpedia

The Social Soil Canvas is not meant to replace Tractorpedia. It feeds it.

The relationship is:

- **Notion / live canvas** = working surface
- **wiki/** = durable memory
- **Tractorpedia** = human-readable browser over the wiki
- **/wiki synthesize** = compounding loop that saves non-trivial answers back into the system

Not every canvas cycle belongs in the wiki. Most of it is live thinking. What gets filed back are the durable parts:

- a **decision** goes to `wiki/decisions/`
- a **method or pattern** goes to `wiki/concepts/`
- a **proof point** goes to the relevant `wiki/projects/` or `wiki/stories/` article
- a **real synthesized answer** goes to `wiki/synthesis/`

This keeps Tractorpedia honest. The wiki should hold what compounds, not every raw founder feeling.

## Relationship to Existing ACT Methods

The Social Soil Canvas is not a new ACT methodology. It is a founder-facing expression of methods already present elsewhere in the ecosystem.

| Existing method | How the canvas relates |
|---|---|
| [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] | Gives the canvas its action discipline: listening before reaction, proof before story |

The PTO line from the Field Canvas still applies here:

> *the tractor powers one implement at a time.*

If the canvas cannot name what is actually in the drive lane, the portfolio is too diffuse.

## Field Anchor

The Social Soil Canvas belongs in the wiki because it is already shaping real founder decisions, especially for [[benjamin-knight|Benjamin Knight]] and the drive-lane cuts around JusticeHub, Empathy Ledger, and the flagship ACT stack. It should stay attached to those concrete decisions rather than drifting into abstract founder language.

## When To Use It

Use the Social Soil Canvas when:

- more than three projects feel live at once
- a founder feels trapped in support work
- the team cannot tell what is in drive, field, or shed
- a quarter ends with insight but little proof
- a major cut, hire, or strategic reset is needed

Do not use it as a substitute for project planning. It is for field diagnosis, not backlog management.

## Outputs It Should Produce

A good cycle produces three things:

1. one **decision**
2. one **pattern**
3. one **proof target**

If those three things do not emerge, the cycle stays in reflection and has not yet improved the field.
