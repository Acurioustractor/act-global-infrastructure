# FY26 Voice/Flow Gap Analysis

> **TL;DR.** Voice "overperformance" (24.5% vs 8% target) is not a storytelling motion working — it's one PICC photography contract carrying the entire pile. Flow "underperformance" (20% vs 56% target) is real and structural — **CivicGraph has zero commercial revenue in FY26 YTD**. The FY27 plan needs a hard look: either accept that Flow ramps later (FY28+) and rebalance FY27 expectations, or commit specific resourcing to CivicGraph commercialisation now.

## Strategic implications — three options for FY27

### Option A: Accept the data, rebalance FY27 expectations

- Voice realistic FY27: $400K-600K (PICC + 1-2 new commercial contracts), not $200K
- Flow realistic FY27: $500K-700K (Goods buyer-network maturity + SMART continuation), **excluding** CivicGraph
- Grants realistic FY27: $1.2M-1.5M (continue Centrecorp + targeted philanthropic)
- Ground realistic FY27: $200K-300K (Harvest + new programs)
- **Realistic FY27 total: $2.3M-3.1M, vs $2.6M plan** — close, but mix is different

**Implication:** R&DTI claim still valid; CivicGraph exit strategy moves out 1-2 years; commercial mix gets to 60% by FY29 not FY27.

### Option B: Commit specific resourcing to CivicGraph commercialisation now

Requires:
1. Productisation work — Stripe billing live, self-serve onboarding, public pricing
2. Sales capacity — at least 0.5 FTE dedicated to outbound (currently zero)
3. First customer goal — 5 paying customers by end FY27 (~$200K MRR target)
4. UK market entry timing — bring forward or accept it's FY28

**Implication:** founder time reallocates from Voice/Ground delivery to Flow build; near-term revenue dips before it rises; needs explicit sign-off from Nic on the trade.

### Option C: Defer CivicGraph commercialisation, lean into Goods scaling via IPP JV

The Goods Demand Register has $16M in open opportunities — orders of magnitude more than CivicGraph's hypothetical pipeline. The IPP JV with Oonchiumpa (framework E2) could unlock Federal/State procurement for that demand.

**Implication:** Flow target stays $1.45M but composition changes — IPP-JV-led Goods commercial replaces CivicGraph MRR. CivicGraph stays a research/proof asset funded by grants until commercial demand emerges.

## What the data is actually saying

1. **Voice mix-up:** the pile labelled "Voice" is actually "PICC photography." Either rename it or change what we're building toward. Don't confuse single-customer photography revenue with a scaled storytelling commercial motion.

2. **CivicGraph is at TRL-5 (working prototype), not TRL-9 (commercial product).** The FY27 plan implicitly assumes TRL-9. Either invest to close that gap, or accept the timeline is longer.

3. **The opportunity isn't missing — it's untriaged.** $16M Goods demand + $8.7M uncoded grants + 32K discovered grants. The bottleneck is conversion velocity, not opportunity discovery. Pile-tagging GHL deals at creation would materially help.

4. **Founder time is finite.** The FY27 plan implicitly asks Ben + Nic to deliver Voice (PICC + new), build Flow (CivicGraph + Goods), pursue grants ($1M target), AND incorporate Pty + handle cutover + lodge R&D claim. Pick three.
