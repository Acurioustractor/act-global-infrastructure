## Best Pay Stack for Ben

### 1. Primary: Salary or director wages from A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd

Use this for Ben's core recurring income.

**Why:** salary is the cleanest answer to ATO personal-services-income risk. It creates PAYG withholding, super guarantee, payslips, mortgage/serviceability evidence, and a clear record that Ben was paid for the work Ben actually performed.

**Working target:** about $120,000/year base salary, subject to ACT runway, R&D cashflow, Standard Ledger, and founder-pay parity with Nic.

**Guardrails:**

- Employment/director role description.
- Board/payroll approval.
- PAYG withholding.
- Single Touch Payroll.
- Super guarantee.
- Timesheet/R&D allocation evidence where relevant.
- Cashflow reserve before bonus/dividend decisions.

### 2. Secondary: planned super contributions

Once ACT cashflow is stable, use super as a boring wealth path.

**Why:** it separates household wealth from ACT trading risk and can be tax-effective if contribution caps allow.

**Guardrails:**

- Track employer super guarantee, salary sacrifice, and personal deductible contributions against caps.
- Do not lock cash in super if near-term Witta deposit, tax, or business runway is underfunded.
- Payday Super starts from 1 July 2026, so ACT payroll cash timing matters.

### 3. Secondary: franked dividends to Knight Family Trust

Use dividends only for true after-tax business profit or ACT equity return.

**Why:** this is the trust's best continuing role: receiving ACT dividends, holding family capital, and allowing adult-family distribution planning where lawful and documented.

**Do not use dividends/trust distributions to disguise underpaid Ben PSI.** ATO PCG 2025/5 says qualifying as a personal services business does not automatically make income splitting or profit retention low risk.

**Guardrails:**

- ACT has paid company tax and has franking capacity.
- Payroll, PAYG, super, GST/BAS, R&D evidence, and working capital are covered first.
- Board minutes document why profit is distributed versus retained.
- Trust resolution and beneficiary cash benefit are documented before 30 June.
- Minor-child distributions are avoided unless Standard Ledger confirms treatment.
- Review the Budget 2026-27 discretionary-trust minimum tax from 1 July 2028 before relying on low-rate beneficiary distributions.

### 4. Exception: company or director loans

Use loans only as a temporary bridge.

**Why:** loans can bridge timing gaps but are not a default pay mechanism.

**Guardrails:**

- Written Division 7A agreement.
- Benchmark interest.
- Required term.
- Minimum yearly repayments.
- Repayment source identified before drawdown.
- Accountant-approved before lodgement day.

### 5. Clean always: reimbursements

Use reimbursements when Ben, Carla, or a child personally pays a genuine business cost.

**Guardrails:**

- Receipt.
- Business purpose.
- Correct entity.
- GST/private-use split.
- Approval record.

## Entity Routing Rules

### A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd

Use for:

- ACT revenue and contracts.
- Founder payroll.
- ACT staff/contractors.
- Product/R&D spend.
- Retained working capital with commercial reason.
- Dividends to Knight/Marchesi trusts after tax and runway checks.

Avoid:

- Paying household costs directly.
- Paying Carla or children without real work, market-rate pay, and records.
- Retaining Ben's PSI just because company tax is lower than Ben's marginal rate.

### Knight Family Trust

Use for:

- Holding ACT shares.
- Receiving true ACT dividends.
- Long-term family capital planning.
- Adult-beneficiary distribution planning where documented and cash benefit is real.

Avoid:

- Routing Ben's personal exertion income.
- Minor-child distributions as tax planning.
- Circular distributions where cash returns to Ben or ACT without real beneficiary benefit.

### Knight Photography / Knight Photography Partnership

Use for:

- Real photography, media, storytelling, asset production, licensing, event shooting, or training.
- Arm's-length scoped media work for ACT/Harvest/Farm with invoice, deliverable, and market price.
- Employing Carla or children only where the work is genuinely for that media business.

Avoid:

- Redirecting ACT consulting income away from A Curious Tractor Pty Ltd.
- Using it as a family payroll shell.
- Mixing ACT R&D, Harvest/Farm trading, and household spending.

### Harvest/Farm Entities

Use for:

- Standalone P&L for Witta, accommodation, farm, events, cafe, workshops, residencies, and land-based programs.
- Wages and costs that belong to the operating entity receiving the benefit.
- Arm's-length services agreements with ACT where shared staff/IP/admin are used.

Avoid:

- Hiding private family housing costs in trading entities.
- Mixing charity/DGR money with trading or private-benefit activity.
- Launching accommodation before Sunshine Coast Council planning requirements are resolved.

### A Kind Tractor / DGR Channel

Use for:

- Endorsed public-benefit programs once DGR and governance settings are settled.
- Philanthropic capital that must sit outside private/trading benefit.

Avoid:

- Subsidising family land, accommodation assets, private living costs, or Harvest trading losses.
- Blurring charity grants/donations with ACT Pty or Harvest Pty revenue.

## Family Employment Rules

### Carla

Carla should stay primarily as separate PAYG nurse income unless ACT/Harvest/Farm/Knight Photography genuinely needs her work.

Potential legitimate roles:

- Clinical/community program design.
- Safeguarding or care-practice review.
- Workshop facilitation.
- Witta operations.
- Bookkeeping/admin only if hours and pay match a non-family market role.

Required evidence:

- Employment contract or contractor scope.
- Role description.
- Timesheets/roster.
- Pay slips.
- Bank payment to Carla.
- Market-rate basis.
- Work product.

### Daughters aged 13 and 14

They can be paid only for real, lawful, age-appropriate work. Pay from the entity that receives the benefit.

Potential roles:

- Event setup.
- Garden/cafe support.
- Photo assistant work.
- Asset tagging.
- Filing.
- Simple content/admin tasks.
- Inventory counts.
- Packing lists.
- Supervised school-holiday project work.

Required evidence:

- Queensland child-employment compliance.
- Parent/employer consent where required.
- No school interference.
- Safe work and supervision.
- TFN/PAYG/STP setup where required.
- Pay slips.
- Timesheets.
- Market junior rate.
- Clear task output.

Avoid:

- Adult-rate wages for token work.
- Payments unsupported by timesheets.
- Trust distributions to minors unless Standard Ledger confirms excepted-income/minor-tax treatment.

## Witta Wealth Path

The better family wealth mechanism is not "use the trust for everything." It is a stack:

1. ACT Pty compounds value through product, contracts, R&D, and Harvest/Farm options.
2. Ben receives clean salary/super for serviceability and family stability.
3. ACT distributes true after-tax profits to Knight Family Trust when ACT can afford it.
4. Personal/joint ownership may be better for the family home or principal residence component of Witta. Confirm duty, land tax, lending, CGT, and asset-protection tradeoffs before buying in a trust/company.
5. Accommodation/farm/Harvest trading sits in a separate operating entity with council/tax/insurance approval.
6. Charity/DGR remains separate from private and trading benefit.
