# ACT Money Philosophy + CY26 Plan

> **The philosophy in one paragraph.** Money in ACT serves three things: it lets us do the work, it pays the people doing the work, and it builds the asset base that funds the next chapter. The system shouldn't be cleverer than it needs to be — predictable salary covers life, trust distributions handle profit at year-end, R&D refunds compound the asset side, and the family relationships (Cameron, Pollyanna) are supported through legitimate roles in the work, not through informal money flow. Everything we build (the dashboards, the framework, the syncs) exists so that this stays visible and trustworthy at a glance.

---

## Part 1 — Philosophy

### What money in ACT is for, in priority order

1. **Survive** — pay rent, food, healthcare, super for Ben + Nic. Floor: ~$10K/mo each = $240K/yr in founder cash to live.
2. **Operate** — keep the work going (infra, contractors, travel, Goods materials, R&D spend that becomes refundable, etc).
3. **Build** — accumulate retained profit that funds CivicGraph commercialisation, Harvest infrastructure, family wealth via trust + small business CGT concessions over the long arc.
4. **Distribute** — to family, partners (e.g., Oonchiumpa JV), and into the philanthropy line (A Kind Tractor when it activates).

Order matters. The model breaks if we let "build" or "distribute" jump ahead of "survive" — e.g., if grant-funded work crowds out paid work and founders go unpaid for months waiting for an R&D refund.

### How money moves (the simple shape)

```
Customers/funders
    ↓
Trading entity (Nic's ST until 30 Jun 2026, then ACT Pty)
    ↓
    ├─→ Founder salary (default: $10K/mo each PAYG + super)
    ├─→ Operating expenses (most R&D-eligible)
    ├─→ Tax + super reserve (~25% of profit)
    └─→ Retained profit
            ↓
        Trust distributions at 30 June (Knight Trust 50% + Marchesi Trust 50%)
            ↓
            ├─→ Beneficiaries at marginal rate (Ben/Nic, spouses, adult children)
            └─→ Bucket company at 25% (excess profit)
```

Five rules to never break:

1. **No founder is unpaid.** $10K/mo salary runs first, before anything else, regardless of grant timing.
2. **Tax reserve is sacred.** 25% of profit goes to a separate buffer, untouched.
3. **Trust distribution by 30 June.** Skip this and the trust pays at 47% — never.
4. **Family payments are formal.** Cameron, Pollyanna get paid for actual work via contract or PAYG, never through informal cash.
5. **R&D documentation is contemporaneous.** If we don't track it as we spend, we can't claim it later.

## Part 3 — Founder payroll setup (post-cutover)

### The mechanism

ACT Pty pays Ben + Nic each as employees (PAYG salary):

| Component | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Base salary | $120,000/yr each ($10K/mo) | Set at incorporation, can adjust at EOY |
| Superannuation | 12% × $120K = $14,400/yr each | Concessional cap $30K/yr, well under |
| PAYG withholding | ~$28K/yr each at $120K bracket | Auto-deducted, remitted to ATO quarterly via BAS |

**Net to Ben/Nic personal account: ~$92K each per year, paid monthly $7,667 net.**

### Setup checklist (Standard Ledger configures this in May/June)

- [ ] ACT Pty registered as employer with ATO (PAYG withholding)
- [ ] Single Touch Payroll (STP) enabled
- [ ] Default super fund elected (or use stapled super for Ben + Nic)
- [ ] Workers' compensation insurance (WorkCover Queensland) — required from first employee
- [ ] Employment contracts: Ben as Director + employee; Nic as Director + employee
- [ ] Calendar reminders for monthly payroll runs (or use auto-recurring in Xero)

### Variations from the default

- **Increase salary** — possible but pay attention to top marginal rate. Sweet spot: keep total personal income under ~$135K to stay in 32.5% bracket where possible.
- **Bonus at year-end** — clears Director loan balance, settles Div 7A
- **Trust distribution instead** — for excess profit, keep salary at $120K and distribute remainder via trust (better tax outcome if franked)

## Part 4 — Trust distributions to family (Cameron, Pollyanna)

### The principle

Knight Family Trust is the Knight family's wealth vehicle. It can distribute to:
- **Ben** (settler/main beneficiary)
- **Cameron** (Ben's partner)
- **Pollyanna** (Ben's daughter, if listed in deed beneficiaries)
- **Other beneficiaries** named in deed (siblings, parents, etc — depends on deed)

Distributions are a **legitimate way to support family members** financially **AS LONG AS** they're documented as trust resolutions and run through the proper channels.

### Three legal channels for Cameron + Pollyanna

#### Channel 1: Trust beneficiary distribution (passive)
- Trust receives franked dividend from ACT Pty
- Trust distributes income to Cameron + Pollyanna at their marginal rates
- Cameron at her marginal rate, Pollyanna at hers (if she's a minor — kids' tax rate kicks in at 47% over $416/yr — usually NOT efficient unless adult)
- Documented in annual trust resolution
- **Best for:** tax-efficient income split if Cameron's marginal rate is lower than Ben's; large lump sums

#### Channel 2: Employment in ACT Pty (active)
- Cameron/Pollyanna do real work for ACT (administration, content production, photography assist, social, anything genuine)
- ACT Pty pays them as PAYG employees with super
- Salary at fair market rate for the work performed
- Counts as Pty operating expense (deductible)
- **Best for:** ongoing support tied to legitimate contribution; getting them on Pty payroll and into super

#### Channel 3: Contracting through their own ABN (independent)
- Cameron/Pollyanna register their own ABN
- They invoice ACT Pty for services
- Pty pays the invoice; their tax handled through their own books
- **Best for:** flexible, project-based work, if they have other income streams or want autonomy

### Pollyanna-specific considerations

If Pollyanna is a **minor** (under 18 living with parents):
- Trust distributions to her are taxed at penalty rates (47% over $416/yr) — NOT a tax-efficient channel
- Better: pay her direct for genuine work via Channel 2 (small jobs), or save the trust distribution for when she's 18+

If Pollyanna is **18+**:
- Trust distributions taxed at her marginal rate — usually low (student income or part-time) so highly efficient
- Can distribute up to ~$22,575 per year tax-free via tax-free threshold

### Cameron-specific considerations

Cameron is Ben's partner — assuming adult and tax-resident:
- Trust distributions taxed at Cameron's marginal rate
- If Cameron is on a lower marginal rate than Ben (e.g., works part-time, freelances), distributions to Cameron are tax-efficient income splitting
- Watch s100A — the "reasonable benefit" test. The ATO can attack distributions that don't reflect a real economic benefit to the beneficiary. Documentation matters: was Cameron actually able to spend the money or did it boomerang back to Ben? Modern ATO practice scrutinises this.

### Documentation needed

For each annual trust resolution:
1. Beneficiary list with proposed amounts (signed by trustee = corporate trustee Pty for trust)
2. Resolution dated **before 30 June** (this is non-negotiable — late resolution = trust pays at 47%)
3. Records of actual payment to beneficiaries' accounts (or formal "presently entitled" UPE management)
4. PAYG payment summary if any beneficiary is also paid as employee (separate income type)

**Standard Ledger sets up the resolution template, runs it each year.**

## Part 5 — Africa trip for Empathy Ledger (R&D alignment)

### What it is

Per memory: Empathy Ledger has a "World Tour" target list — 21 international prospects across Kenya, Uganda, Sweden, Netherlands, South Africa. The first major trip is Africa-focused.

### Why it's R&D-eligible (the case to make)

R&D Tax Incentive eligibility requires:
- **Core R&D activity:** experimental work whose outcome can't be known in advance, conducted to generate new knowledge
- **Supporting R&D activity:** activities directly related to a Core R&D activity

Empathy Ledger work in Africa potentially qualifies as:
- **Core:** if testing whether the Empathy Ledger storytelling protocol generalises to non-Australian First Nations / Indigenous community contexts (specifically Maasai, Karamojong, Acholi narrative + consent traditions). Hypothesis: the consent + provenance model needs adaptation. Test: deploy with at least one community, measure adaptation cost + acceptance.
- **Supporting:** travel, accommodation, equipment hire that directly enables the Core experiment

### What needs to be true for the spend to claim

1. **Pre-trip:** documented Core R&D activity description filed (or ready to file) with AusIndustry — what the experiment is, what knowledge gap it addresses, what success looks like
2. **During trip:** contemporaneous notes — Anthropic conversation logs, Empathy Ledger session captures, observation diary
3. **Post-trip:** technical report on what was learned — even if the answer is "doesn't generalise as-is, here's what would need to change"

### What's NOT R&D-eligible (stay clear)

- Tourism / cultural exposure that isn't tied to a stated experiment
- Marketing / business development meetings (those are commercial, not R&D)
- Time spent that's mostly relationship-building (those are partnership, not R&D)

The trick: separate the trip into **R&D days** (with documented activity) and **other days** (admin, partnership, downtime). Only R&D days/portion claimable.

### Suggested spend allocation framework

| Spend type | R&D-eligible % | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Travel (flights to Africa) | 70% | If 70% of trip days are R&D |
| Accommodation in fieldwork locations | 80-100% | If location specifically chosen for R&D activity |
| Equipment (camera, laptop, audio gear specifically for capture) | 100% | Instant asset write-off + R&D |
| Local transport in fieldwork days | 80-100% | When directly tied to R&D activity |
| Meals during R&D days | 50-70% | Apportionment haircut |
| Translation services / local guides | 100% | Direct R&D enablement |

**Standard Ledger / R&D consultant ratifies the percentages once they see the trip plan.**

### Practical workflow for the trip

1. **Before:** lock the R&D activity description (1-pager) in Notion. Get R&D consultant to sign off on the framing.
2. **During:** record-keep daily — what experiment did you run, what data captured, what initial findings. Photo each receipt as it lands. Use Empathy Ledger session captures themselves as evidence.
3. **After:** write the technical report (~2-3 pages — "what we tested, what we found, what generalises, what doesn't"). File with AusIndustry registration in October.

This becomes part of the FY27 R&D claim, refundable at 43.5% in late FY27 (paid out 2027 calendar year).

### The shape

```
Mon morning (auto)        Fri afternoon (review)
─────────────────         ──────────────────────
6:00 GHL cleanup          15:30 Review digest
6:30 Grant seed           - This week's wins
6:50 Payments sync        - This week's burns
7:00 Money in audit       - Pile shifts
7:05 Money out audit      - Action items captured

7:30 Notion → GHL changes - Decisions to log
8:00 Weekly reconciliation
8:15 Framework page
8:30 Opportunities DB
8:45 Pile pages
```

**Mon morning** = full data refresh, automated. By 9am, Notion is current.

**Fri afternoon** = human review. 30-45 minutes. Open the framework page, read the burning items, action what needs actioning, capture questions.

### What lives where

| Surface | Role | Updated by |
|---|---|---|

| **Money In Alignment** | Every $ in, payment hygiene | Auto Mon 7:10 |
| **Money Out Alignment** | Every $ out, R&D-eligible flagged | Auto Mon 7:10 |

| **Pile strategic pages** | Voice / Flow / Ground / Grants context | Auto Mon 8:45 |
