# Building Revolution in Shipping Containers: The Story of CONTAINED

<h2>The Problem We Can't Unsee</h2><p>Australia spends <strong>$1.1 million per child, per year</strong> to lock them up.<br>The "success rate"? <strong>15.5%.</strong><br>That means <strong>84.5% of kids reoffend within 12 months</strong> of release.</p><p>Meanwhile, community programs succeed at <strong>78%</strong> — and cost <strong>95% less</strong>.</p><p>Every statistic represents a child whose future hangs in the balance.<br>We can’t unknow what we know about what works.</p><hr><h2>What Is CONTAINED?</h2><p><strong>CONTAINED</strong> is not another awareness campaign with sad statistics on posters.</p><p>It’s an <em>immersive experience</em> built from shipping containers — transformation chambers that make the invisible visible and the abstract tangible.</p><p>We didn’t just design this. We <strong>built</strong> it. With our hands.</p><ul><li><p>Nicholas personally constructed the majority of the rooms, wired the electronics that make fluorescent despair tangible, and embedded the technology that bridges experience to action.</p></li><li><p>Benjamin followed paper trails that lead to kids in cages, transforming data into moral urgency.</p></li></ul><hr><h2>Why Shipping Containers?</h2><p>Because metaphors matter.</p><p>Young people in Australia’s justice system are literally <strong>contained</strong> — in cells, in systems, in cycles of harm.</p><p>By creating physical spaces that embody this containment, we invite people to step inside the experience, to <em>feel</em> what we’ve learned, to <em>understand</em> what must change.</p><hr><h2>The Revolution Happens at the Intersection</h2><p>Revolution doesn’t happen in boardrooms.<br>It happens when builders, dreamers, and survivors converge at the intersection of:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Moral urgency</strong> — We know this is wrong</p></li><li><p><strong>Practical possibility</strong> — We know what works</p></li><li><p><strong>Evidence-based action</strong> — We have the proof</p></li></ul><p><strong>CONTAINED</strong> bridges experience to policy, practice to transformation.<br>It’s infrastructure for change — not charity for problems.</p><hr><h2>What’s Inside CONTAINED?</h2><h3>Room 1: The Numbers Room</h3><p>Walk into a space filled with the data we can’t ignore.<br>Feel the weight of <strong>$1.1 million</strong> being spent per child while communities solve the problem for <strong>$58,000</strong>.</p><h3>Room 2: The Voices Room</h3><p>Hear directly from young people who’ve lived through the system.<br>Their stories — unfiltered, powerful, unforgettable.</p><h3>Room 3: The Solutions Room</h3><p>Discover the <strong>150+ community programs</strong> already working across Australia.<br>See the evidence. Meet the people making change happen.</p><h3>Room 4: The Action Room</h3><p>Your turn.<br>Connect with programs, support solutions, join the movement.</p><hr><h2>This Is Not a Gallery Piece</h2><p><strong>CONTAINED</strong> is a campaign — designed to move you:<br>from awareness to action,<br>from understanding to engagement,<br>from outrage to organization.</p><p>We’re building infrastructure for transformation:</p><ul><li><p><strong>JusticeHub Platform:</strong> Connecting young people to opportunities, communities to resources, evidence to action</p></li><li><p><strong>Community Partnerships:</strong> Amplifying grassroots solutions that already work</p></li><li><p><strong>Systems Advocacy:</strong> Using data and stories to shift policy and funding</p></li></ul><hr><h2>Join Us</h2><p>CONTAINED creates pathways from awareness to action.<br>We’re not asking you to feel bad about the problem — we’re inviting you to be part of the solution.</p><hr><h2>The Builders</h2><h3><strong>Benjamin Knight</strong> — Co-founder, <em>A Curious Tractor</em></h3><p>The one who stood in Madrid’s sunset-colored rooms and felt the weight of Australia’s failure in his bones.<br>Following paper trails that lead to kids in cages, transforming data into moral urgency.</p><h3><strong>Nicholas Marchesi</strong> — Co-founder, <em>A Curious Tractor</em></h3><p>The hands that built revolution.<br>A strategic architect who transformed shipping containers into transformation chambers — personally constructing the majority of the rooms, wiring the electronics, and embedding the technology that bridges experience to action.</p><hr><h2>Tags</h2><p>#YouthJustice #CONTAINED #SystemsChange #CommunityPrograms #EvidenceBasedAdvocacy #ACuriousTractor #ImmersiveExperience #TransformationNotPunishment</p><hr><p><em>Revolution doesn’t happen in boardrooms.
