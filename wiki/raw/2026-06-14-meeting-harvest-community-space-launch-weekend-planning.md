## The Harvest Founding Members Launch - Evening Session
An evening of live music, fire, pizza, community, and officially welcoming the first Harvest members.
Come for the afternoon, stay for the launch, and help mark the beginning of The Harvest as a member-led community space.
From 3:00–5:00pm, the space will be open for live music and an easy afternoon hang. Come when you can, bring the family, settle in, meet other members, and enjoy the place before the evening launch begins.
At 5:00pm, we’ll gather for the first Harvest Members Launch — a relaxed, celebratory welcome for the people helping bring The Harvest to life.
### What’s happening
3:00–5:00pm — Live music and afternoon hangs
Arrive anytime, listen to music, explore the space, meet other members, and ease into the evening.
5:00pm — Harvest Members Launch
We’ll come together to thank everyone who has helped get The Harvest to this point, share the bigger idea behind the space, and welcome the first circle of founding members.
Hear from the team behind The Harvest
We’ll share a little of the story behind The Harvest — what it is, why we’re creating it, and what we hope it becomes with members at the centre.
This will include:
- What The Harvest is all about — a place to make, grow, gather, eat, learn, contribute, and belong
- Why the milk crates — the thinking behind the simple, modular, playful materials we’re using to build the first version of the space together
- Where to from here — how members can help shape future offerings, events, workshops, meals, music, growing days, kids activities, and community rituals
As part of the welcome, we’ll do a simple whip-around:
- Your name
- What you’re harvesting this month
- Your Polaroid member badge 
5:30–7:30pm — Music and pizzas
The Pizza Guide Crew from the morning session will help bring the evening meal to life. We’ll make and share pizzas together, keep the music going, and enjoy the space as the sun goes down.
7:30pm — Closing provocation
We’ll close the formal part of the evening with a short reflection / provocation for the community: what do we want to grow here, and what are we each bringing to help make it real?
### Why we’re gathering
The Harvest is not just a venue or a program — it’s a place for people to belong, contribute, learn, make, grow, gather, and shape something together.
This evening is about marking the beginning. It’s a chance to meet the other founding members, understand the spirit of the place, share food, listen to music, and take your place in the first version of The Harvest.
### Cost
$30 per adult. Kids free.

## Community Notice Board Building
A hands-on welcome-making workshop for The Harvest
When: Friday, 3:00–5:00pm
Where: The Harvest
Purpose: Together, we’ll build the first version of our community notice board — a fun, practical welcome point that helps everyone know what’s happening when they arrive at The Harvest.[1]
### What we’re making
We’re creating a playful, useful arrival station made from timber, milk crates, found materials, and community ideas. It will become the place people naturally stop when they arrive — a kind of front porch, notice board, storage nook, and community exchange all in one.
The board might include:
- What’s on today — events, timings, workshops, meals, music, and gathering moments
- Member badge storage — a home for Polaroids, fabric badges, lanyards, or name cards
- Community notices — upcoming events, offers, requests, ideas, and messages
- Leave / take / share spots — milk crate cubbies for produce, tools, books, objects, seeds, or useful things people want to pass on
- Quote of the day / pizza of the week / harvest notes — a bit of fun and personality
- A “suggest an offering” area — a place for people to propose classes, workshops, talks, meals, music, kids activities, or future gatherings
### Why it matters
The Harvest is about more than events — it’s about helping people feel like they belong. This notice board will be one of the first things members and visitors see when they arrive, so we want it to feel warm, handmade, useful, a little bit weird, and full of possibility.
It should say:
“You’re welcome here. Something is happening. You can join in.”
### What we’ll do together
- Sketch fun ideas for how the board could work
- Build with timber, milk crates, screws, signs, shelves, hooks, and whatever useful materials we have
- Create spaces for badges, member names, notices, objects, and community contributions
- Make signs and labels so people understand how to use it
- Test how it feels when someone arrives: Where do they look? What do they need to know? What makes them smile?
No building experience needed — just bring energy, ideas, and a willingness to have a go.
### What to bring
- Closed-in shoes
- Fun ideas
- Clothes you don’t mind getting dusty
- Any spare timber, crates, hooks, signs, markers, paint, rope, clips, or odd useful things
- A thought, quote, notice, offering, or object you’d love to see on the first version of the board
### The vibe
Part working bee, part art project, part welcome ritual.
We’ll make something useful, imperfect, playful, and very Harvest.

## The Harvest Founding Members Day - Morning Session
A morning of fire, food, making, growing, gathering, and shaping what The Harvest becomes.
Join us for our first Founding Members Day — a free morning gathering for the people helping bring The Harvest to life.
This is more than an event. It’s a chance to come together as the first circle of members, connect with each other, get your hands into the place, and help shape the future of The Harvest from the very beginning.
The Harvest is grounded in three simple ideas: making, gathering, and growing. On the day, you’ll be invited to choose an activity, contribute to the space, share a meal, and be part of the first harvest lunch.
### What’s happening
10:00am — Members kick off: lighting of the fires and welcome
We’ll begin by lighting the fires, welcoming everyone in, and marking the start of The Harvest Founding Members Day.
10:30–11:00am — Welcome and Harvest overview
Ben and Nic will share the story of The Harvest — what it is, why we’re creating it, and how members can help shape the community, the space, and future gatherings. Oh and whats behind the milk creates. 
11:00am–12:00pm — Let’s Harvest
Pick one of three hands-on Harvest activities:
- Make — Table building
Help create useful, beautiful things for the space. We’ll be making tables and simple gathering infrastructure together with Thais and Joey
- Gather — Pizza Making Guide Crew
Join Nic, Eloise and Mum for a free pizza-making guide session. You’ll learn how the pizza flow works, make food together, and — if you’re available — we’d love you to come back in the evening as part of the Pizza Guide Crew.
- Grow — First harvest from the garden
Head into the garden with Suzie and Ben to pick produce for our first shared Harvest lunch.
12:00–1:00pm — First Harvest Lunch
We’ll come back together to make and share pizza and salad using what we’ve gathered from the garden.
### Why we’re gathering
We’re doing this because The Harvest is not something we want to build for people — it’s something we want to build with people.
As a founding member, you’re part of the first group helping shape the culture, rhythm, activities, and future of this place. This morning is about meeting each other, getting involved, sharing ideas, and beginning to grow as a community.
Come ready to join in, have a go, meet good people, and help make the first version of The Harvest real.
### Cost
Free for founding members.

## Kids of the Harvest - Design and Build Playground Workshop
A hands-on workshop where kids help imagine, design, and build the first version of The Harvest playground.
Kids are some of the most important members of The Harvest. This workshop invites them to become designers, builders, testers, artists, and inventors — helping shape a playful corner of the space from the very beginning.
Together, we’ll dream up what a Harvest playground could be: a place for climbing, hiding, building, drawing, making cubbies, balancing, storytelling, and creating games that can keep changing over time.
### What’s happening
Welcome and playground dreaming
We’ll start with a simple question: what makes a place fun to play in? Kids will share ideas, draw possibilities, and think about what they’d love to see at The Harvest.
Design with what we have
Using milk crates, timber, rope, fabric, cardboard, chalk, signs, found objects, and natural materials, we’ll explore how simple things can become playful things.
Build the first version
Kids and grown-ups will work together to create small playground elements — cubbies, balance paths, crate towers, signs, obstacle ideas, loose-parts play zones, or whatever the group imagines.
Test, name, and improve it
Once we’ve built a first version, the kids get to test it, give feedback, name different parts, and decide what should happen next.
### Why we’re gathering
The Harvest is a place we want to build with kids, not just for them.
This workshop is about giving kids a real voice in the space — letting them show us what play, belonging, adventure, and imagination can look like here. It’s also a way for families to meet each other, contribute to the first version of the place, and help create something that can grow over time.
### What kids will help shape
- The first playground ideas for The Harvest
- How milk crates and simple materials can become play structures
- Signs, names, rules, and rituals for the kids’ area
- Future kids workshops, making days, garden adventures, and school holiday activities
- What grown-ups need to understand about making The Harvest fun for kids
### What to bring
- Clothes that can get dusty or messy
- Closed-in shoes
- A hat and water bottle
- Big playground ideas
- Any safe, useful materials you’d love to contribute — crates, cardboard, rope, fabric, timber offcuts, paint, chalk, signs, or odd bits that could become something playful
### Good to know
This is a collaborative family workshop. Kids will need a grown-up with them, and any building will be simple, safe, supervised, and focused on imagination over perfection.
### Cost
Free for members / families.
