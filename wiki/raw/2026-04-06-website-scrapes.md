## JusticeHub (justicehub.com.au)

Digital platform transforming how grassroots organizations, communities, and governments collaborate to support young people in Australia. "Transparency engine for youth justice."

**Principles:** TRUTH - ACTION - JUSTICE

**Stats:**
- Detention: $1.3M/child/year ($3,634.90/day national average)
- Community models: $14,000/child — **97x cheaper**
- 1,000 verified alternative models tracked
- 430 models with evidence data
- $94.6B in funding tracked
- 98,418 organizations mapped
- Detention: 85% recidivism rate

**Three Pathways:**
1. Youth ("I need help"): Crisis support, legal assistance, mentorship, housing, peer connections via "Youth Scout"
2. Organizations ("I do the work"): ALMA Network — mapping, grant matching, peer connections, capacity building
3. Policymakers ("I fund or shape policy"): Evidence dashboards, funding tracking, cost calculators

**Features:** ALMA AI Chat, Cost Calculator, System Map (98,418+ orgs), Impact Dashboard, Learning Trips (including Spain's Diagrama centers)

**Content/Storytelling:**
- "Oonchiumpa: What Happens When Community Leads"
- "The Cure Already Exists"
- "Building Revolution in Shipping Containers: The Story of CONTAINED"

## Goods on Country (goodsoncountry.com)

Australian social enterprise delivering essential household items to remote Indigenous communities.

**Primary Product: Stretch Bed**
- 26kg weight, 200kg capacity, 10+ year lifespan
- Under 5 minutes assembly, no tools, flat-pack
- Recycled HDPE plastic legs (diverts 20kg plastic per bed)
- Galvanised steel poles (26.9mm OD)
- Heavy-duty Australian canvas (washable, repairable)

**Manufacturing:** On-country containerized plant. Five steps: collect plastic waste → shred → press into sheets → CNC routing → assemble. Capacity: ~30 beds/week.

**Impact:** 369+ beds delivered, 8 communities, 33 community storytellers

**Secondary product:** Washing machines ("Pakkimjalki Kari")

**Contact:** hello@goodsoncountry.org.au / goods.act.place
