## Major Impact Initiatives

### Goods.
Why: Urgent access gaps to basic household goods in First Nations communities, 55% experiencing overcrowding and poor living conditions driving health crises.
What: Building the "Toyota Troopy of household goods" — durable, repairable, culturally designed beds, washing machines, and fridges made with and by communities.
How: 4x3x3 model: 4 communities x 3 essential goods x 3 iterative prototyping cycles. Community-owned manufacturing facilities with skills transfer and training.
Needs: Scaling production (300 beds + 40 washing machines next 12 months), establishing manufacturing sovereignty in Tennant Creek and Palm Island.

### PICC - Storm Stories
Why: Palm Island's extraordinary response to the February 2025 flood revealed sophisticated community networks needing documentation to counter deficit narratives and support healing.
What: Community-controlled storytelling documenting 60+ stories of resilience and innovation, creating permanent database for future generations.
How: PICC-led story collection with full community ownership, multi-format capture (photography, audio, video), Elder-guided cultural protocols.
Needs: Complete database handover to PICC ownership, establish ongoing story collection capacity.

### JusticeHub
Why: Indigenous young people face 24x higher incarceration rates while community programs show 78% success at 95% lower cost than detention.
What: Community-owned platform connecting young people to proven programs, providing governance support, and mobilizing resources for Indigenous-led solutions.
How: Service matching, storytelling platform, transparency hub tracking funding flows, grassroots database of effective programs.
Needs: Secure $500K funding to scale platform.

### Empathy Ledger
Why: Stories of marginalised communities remain unheard while policies fail those they serve. Technology now enables ethical storytelling at scale.
What: Platform transforming lived experiences into catalysts for dignity, empathy, and systemic justice. Every story shapes policies and perceptions.
How: Secure storytelling platform with AI integration, ethical framework, community partnerships, feedback loops for real-time adaptation.
Needs: Beta platform deployment with 10+ community participants, establish ethical framework and community governance, demonstrate measurable policy impact.

## Youth Justice & Cultural Healing

### BG Fit
Why: Addressing overrepresentation of Indigenous youth in justice system through culturally grounded fitness, healing, and mentorship.
What: Fitness programs, annual on-country camps, cultural connection activities disrupting punitive youth justice cycle in Mount Isa and Doomadgee.
How: Pre-camp fitness, cultural camps with Elders, post-camp recovery. Partnerships with local Elders, police, community organisations.
Needs: Sustained funding for camp delivery, strengthened Elder partnerships, full community ownership by 2027.

### Oonchiumpa
Why: Rising youth crises, system failures, and risk of losing cultural knowledge in Central Australia requiring urgent culturally grounded response.
What: Cultural healing and empowerment for at-risk Aboriginal youth, creating confident navigation between cultural and contemporary worlds.
How: Traditional healing, holistic support across wellbeing domains, community-led governance, reclaiming heritage and leadership development.
Needs: Establish sustainable funding pathways, strengthen community leadership structures.

### MMEIC - Justice Projects
Why: Supporting alternatives to incarceration for local youth through culturally grounded storytelling and healing programs.
What: Justice reinvestment initiatives emphasizing community ownership, cultural protocols, advocacy for policy influence.

### Diagrama
Why: Australia's youth justice crisis requires transformation. Spain's therapeutic detention model offers alternatives.
What: Ethical, culturally appropriate transfer of therapeutic detention knowledge from Foundation Diagrama to Australia, prioritising Indigenous leadership.
How: Relationship building, cultural adaptation, community ownership development with goal of Indigenous-led implementation by 2027.

### Contained
Why: Challenging harmful narratives about young people in justice system through creative expression and truth-telling.
What: Art-based initiative involving youth with lived experiences, exploring themes of isolation and liberation.
How: Creative development, community engagement, connection to broader justice reform efforts, authentic dialogue.

## Community Regeneration

### Witta Harvest HQ
Why: Need for community connection, safety, and sustainability through collaborative stewardship on former Green Harvest site.
What: Regenerative hub fostering connection and sustainability, restoring site for community use, establishing sustainable governance.

### June's Patch
Why: Healthcare worker mental health crisis exacerbated by isolation and burnout, need for nature-based healing solutions.
What: Network of therapeutic gardens providing nature prescriptions for Queensland healthcare workers.

### Dad.Lab.25
Why: Men's health challenges requiring innovative, community-led approaches to support and connection.
What: Initiative supporting fathers through health and wellbeing programs, building community connections.

### Uncle Allan Palm Island Art
Why: Preserving and sharing cultural knowledge while supporting economic opportunities for Aboriginal artists.
What: Digital platform showcasing Uncle Allan's paintings inspired by reef life and Dreaming stories, supporting artist residency aspirations.
Website: burrgumanbarraart.com

### Designing for Obsolescence
Why: Challenge social innovation industrial complex fostering dependency, promote community empowerment and self-determination.

### Fishers Oysters

### Gold.Phone

### PICC Photo Kiosk / Server

### The Confessional
