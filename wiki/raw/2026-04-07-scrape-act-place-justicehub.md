# JusticeHub | A Curious Tractor Project Page

## Opening Framing

> The youth justice system is failing our young people, particularly Indigenous youth who are incarcerated at 24 times the rate of their non-Indigenous peers. This costly system ($1.5 billion annually) has a 70% recidivism rate, proving that punishment is not the answer. Community-led solutions *work*, but they are often isolated, underfunded, and overlooked.

## The Human Cost (Stats Used Publicly)

- First Nations children aged 10–13 were **46 times** as likely as non-Indigenous children to be in detention. (AIHW 2023)
- Males first sentenced between the ages of 10 and 14 have a reoffending rate of **86%**. (Sentencing Advisory Council)
- Mount Isa has the highest rate of youth crime per capita in Queensland.

## How It Works (Problem/Solution Pairs)

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Grassroots organisations are isolated | JusticeHub connects them, fostering collaboration and knowledge sharing |
| Powerful stories of lived experience are unheard | Empathy Ledger amplifies voices and uses tech-enhanced systems to generate insights for advocacy |
| Funding doesn't reach the most effective solutions | JusticeHub provides frameworks to support funding pathways to community-led initiatives |
| Solutions remain localised and do not scale | The ability to adapt and use what works in one community for another |

## Case Studies

**Confit Pathways** (Sydney & NSW) — Led by formerly incarcerated youth, uses fitness and mentorship to break cycles of recidivism. Reduced reoffending rates by 60%.

**BG Fit** (Mount Isa) — Transforming the health and well-being of Indigenous youth through fitness and cultural connection. Participants went from an average of five police contacts to less than one.
