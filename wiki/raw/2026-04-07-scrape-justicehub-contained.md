# THE CONTAINED: Australian Tour 2026 | JusticeHub

## Core Description

> One shipping container. Three rooms. Thirty minutes. Touring Australia. We are building it as we go because the people who need to see it cannot wait for perfect.

## The Evidence (As Displayed)

- **65%** — Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children make up 65% of those in detention. They are 6% of the population.
- **860** — Children locked up on any given night across Australia. Many are on remand. Not yet convicted of any crime.
- **$4,250** — Cost per day. Taxpayers spend $1.55M per child per year on detention. Community programs cost $75 a day and work better.
- **84%** — Recidivism rate of children who go through detention.

## Three Rooms

**Room 1: Current Reality** (10 minutes locked inside)
At every tour stop, young people from the local community design this room. They are the experts. They know what detention feels like. They decide what the public needs to see.
- Daily Cost: $4,250
- Annual Cost: $1.55M

**Room 2: What Works** (10 minutes of possibility)
We visited Diagrama's youth justice centres in Spain. Kids get education, therapy, and connection instead of concrete walls. 1:1 staffing, family visits every week.
- Diagrama recidivism: 13.6%
- Staff ratio: 1:1

**Room 3: The Organisations Already Doing It** (10 minutes to see what works)
This container celebrates grassroots organisations and people doing the work. Indigenous-led and community orgs that aren't being valued, reported on, or funded the way they should be. At each tour stop, the local host organisation fills this space with their story.
- Community Programs: $75/day
- Programs on ALMA: 1,387
