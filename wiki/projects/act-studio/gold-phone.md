# Gold.Phone

> A random connection voice platform and community installation tackling social isolation through authentic, unscripted human conversation — open-source, privacy-focused, and designed for local replication.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-GP | **Tier:** Studio

## Overview

Gold.Phone addresses the social isolation crisis with a deceptively simple intervention: connect two strangers by voice, at random, with no social media profile, no metrics, and no agenda. The platform creates conditions for genuine human connection that algorithmic social networks cannot — and deliberately will not — provide.

The project operates as both a digital platform and a physical community installation, making it accessible in contexts where smartphone apps are not the right interface. Its open-source foundation means any community can replicate and own their own version.

## Key Details

- **Format:** Voice platform + community installations
- **Technology:** Open-source, designed for local community replication
- **Core values:** Privacy-first, accessible, community ownership, no tracking or profiling
- **Primary problem:** Social isolation — the absence of authentic human connection in algorithmically mediated social environments

## How It Works

- **Random connection** — participants are connected by voice with a stranger, removing the friction and performance of curated social interaction
- **Community installations** — physical installations bring the experience to public spaces, community centres, and events where digital access may be limited
- **Open-source** — the technology is freely available for communities to deploy and own independently
- **Privacy by design** — no profiles, no recordings, no data retained beyond the call

## LCAA Phase

**Art** — Gold.Phone is a creative technology project where the medium (random voice connection) is the message. The act of connection is the art form. It sits within ACT's Art phase as an expression of the idea that human contact, stripped of performance, is itself transformative.

## Studio Context

Gold.Phone belongs in ACT's Studio line not as a side experiment but as one of the clearest examples of ACT's argument that communication infrastructure can itself be a work of art.
