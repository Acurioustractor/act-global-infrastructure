# Regional Arts Fellowship

> A fellowship for practitioners working at the intersection of art, technology, and agriculture — supporting the creative innovators regenerating regional Australia from the ground up.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-RA | **Tier:** Studio

## Overview

The Regional Arts Fellowship recognises a specific and underserved kind of innovator: the artist-farmer, the technologist-land-steward, the creative practitioner whose work is inseparable from the particular landscape they inhabit. These practitioners are doing some of the most consequential regenerative work in Australia, but they sit outside the usual categories — not quite arts, not quite agtech, not quite environmental science — and so fall through the gaps of standard fellowship and funding programs.

The fellowship creates dedicated support infrastructure for this work: time, resources, recognition, and connection to a wider network of peers and knowledge.

## The Intersection: Art, Technology, Agriculture

### Art
Creative practice as a primary mode of inquiry, communication, and transformation. Not art as decoration or documentation of "real" work, but art as a way of knowing and changing the world — making visible what data cannot, building the emotional and relational conditions that enable systemic change.

### Technology
Digital tools, sensing systems, data platforms, and emerging technologies deployed in service of land and community — not for efficiency gains divorced from ecological relationship, but as instruments in the hands of people who understand place.

### Agriculture
The land as the primary context and stakeholder. Agriculture not as industrial extraction but as regenerative practice — farming systems that restore soil, water, biodiversity, and community relationships simultaneously.

## Relationship to ACT's Ecosystem

The Regional Arts Fellowship connects to several threads in ACT's portfolio:

- The Harvest — regenerative food systems and regional innovation
- ACT Farm — land-based practice as community and research infrastructure
- Green Harvest Witta — regenerative agriculture in regional Queensland
- Goods on Country — First Nations-led land and supply chain work

The fellowship also reflects ACT's commitment to the Art phase of [[lcaa-method|LCAA]] as a site of genuine innovation, not just communication.

## LCAA Phase

**Art** — Creative practice is not a reporting mechanism here but the primary mode of work. The fellowship supports practitioners for whom making art and working the land are the same activity.

## Studio Context

The Regional Arts Fellowship is where ACT's art field becomes a repeatable support structure rather than a single artwork. It should be read as part of the Studio's innovation and business model: a way to host practice, build regional legitimacy, and tie art, agriculture, and technology into one field.
