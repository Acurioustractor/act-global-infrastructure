# ConFit Pathways

> A fitness and boxing-based youth transformation program using mentors with lived incarceration experience to build pathways out of the justice system.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-CP | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

ConFit Pathways is a community intervention program that uses physical fitness — primarily boxing — as a vehicle for personal transformation among young people in or at risk of entering the justice system. Its distinguishing feature is the deliberate recruitment of mentors who have been through the prison system themselves. This lived-experience model creates relationships based on credibility and genuine understanding, not institutional authority.

Founded by Joe Kwon, ConFit operates on a simple principle: the same discipline that builds a fighter can rebuild a life. Through structured training, mentoring, and community connection, it offers young people legitimate pathways to identity, income, and belonging — the things that keep people out of the system.

## Founder: Joe Kwon

Joe Kwon's transformation began inside prison, where he confronted the need for change and discovered fitness as a tool for personal rehabilitation. With community support, he turned this discovery outward — founding ConFit as a bridge for others navigating the same journey he had walked.

ConFit is not Joe telling young people what he learned. It is Joe and other mentors walking alongside, from a position of genuine shared experience. "It's not just about physical fitness; it is about reshaping futures and challenging societal stereotypes."

Hamilton is one participant whose story illustrates the programme's reach: a young man who came to ConFit carrying the weight of poor decisions and found, through the structure of training and consistent mentoring, a pathway to becoming a community role model himself.

## The Model

### Lived-Experience Mentorship

ConFit's mentors have been through the prison system and successfully reintegrated. This is not incidental — it is the core design choice. Young people who distrust institutions, who have been failed by systems, and who see no legitimate future for themselves are far more likely to engage with someone who has walked their exact path.

The programme builds community where young people feel genuinely understood. Trust is the precondition for change, and lived experience is the fastest way to establish it.

### Fitness as Framework

Physical training — particularly boxing — provides structure, discipline, and a goal. It is hard to fake. Progress in the gym is visible and earned. For young people whose environments have been chaotic and unpredictable, the gym offers a reliable system of cause and effect: effort produces result.

G-Code methodology runs through the program, focusing on three anchors: **Gratitude**, **Grounding**, and **Goals**. These are not abstract concepts — they are trained responses to the situations young people face every day.

### Breaking the Stigma of Incarceration

A significant function of ConFit is normalising the idea that incarceration is not a permanent identity. By centring mentors who have "been there and come back", the programme challenges the narrative that a criminal record defines a person's future. Young people can see — concretely — that a different path is possible.

## Bimberi Partnership

ConFit Pathways operates inside Bimberi Youth Justice Centre in the ACT in partnership with [[resoleution|ReSOLEution]]. The two programmes run on alternating days, creating a complementary rhythm: ConFit's G-Code methodology and fitness sessions alongside ReSOLEution's shoe-design workshops.

This partnership is deliberate. ConFit brings the internal work — values, discipline, identity. ReSOLEution brings the external expression — creativity, story, commercial possibility. Together they offer a more complete intervention than either could deliver alone.
