# Quandamooka Justice and Healing Strategy

> A justice reinvestment and community healing initiative grounded in Quandamooka Country — led by the Quandamooka people, focused on self-determination, and built on the understanding that healing and justice are inseparable.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-QD | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

The Quandamooka Justice and Healing Strategy is a place-specific, Indigenous-led response to the justice and wellbeing challenges facing the Quandamooka people of Moreton Bay and North Stradbroke Island (Minjerribah). It operates within the justice reinvestment framework — the evidence-based approach that redirects resources from incarceration toward early intervention, community support, and healing — but grounds that framework in Quandamooka knowledge, relationships, and self-determined priorities.

ACT's role is supportive and facilitative. The strategy belongs to the Quandamooka community, and long-term success is defined by the community's own capacity to own and direct the work without external intermediaries.

## The Place: Quandamooka Country

Quandamooka Country encompasses Moreton Bay and the islands of Minjerribah (North Stradbroke Island) and Mulgumpin (Moreton Island). The Quandamooka Yoolooburrabee Aboriginal Corporation (QYAC) is the recognised traditional owner body. The country is one of ecological richness and cultural depth — and, like much of Aboriginal Australia, carries the compounding legacies of dispossession, removal, and structural disadvantage that intersect with justice system involvement.

## Approach

### Justice Reinvestment
Redirecting resources from downstream responses (incarceration, child removal, crisis intervention) toward upstream investment in community wellbeing, cultural connection, economic participation, and early support. Justice reinvestment is most effective when it is community-led — and the Quandamooka strategy is built on this.

### Indigenous Leadership
Every element of the strategy is led by Quandamooka community members and organisations. ACT does not design the strategy — it supports the community to design, resource, and implement it. This is consistent with ACT's [[designing-for-obsolescence|Designing for Obsolescence]] philosophy.

### Community Healing
The strategy recognises that justice and healing cannot be separated. Addressing justice system involvement without addressing the underlying trauma, grief, and disconnection that drives it produces temporary statistics, not lasting change. Healing — cultural, relational, and psychological — is a strategic priority, not an afterthought.

### Self-Determination
The measure of success is increasing Quandamooka self-determination over justice and wellbeing outcomes on Country — not reduced dependence on a particular program, but reduced dependence on external systems altogether.

## LCAA Phase

**Listen + Action** — The strategy begins with deep listening to Quandamooka community priorities, knowledge, and experiences of the justice system. Action follows from that listening: strategy development, investment in community-led programs, and advocacy for the policy and resource shifts that make self-determination viable.
