# The Brave Ones

> Real portraits. Real stories. Real young people. The ones changing the system.

**Status:** Ideation (sketched 2026-04-07) | **Cluster:** Art (Studio) | **Tier:** Studio piece in development

## Overview

The Brave Ones is a sustained photographic and storytelling series sitting in the [[act-studio|ACT Studio cluster]] alongside [[contained|CONTAINED]]. Where CONTAINED puts you inside the *room* that youth detention is, The Brave Ones puts you in front of the *people* who walked out the other side. It is the deliberate counter-mugshot: studio-quality B&W portraits in the dignity register that policing and media have refused to give Indigenous and system-impacted young people for two centuries.

It is also the visual architecture of [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] — the human face of the world model — and the answer to the photo-credit problem every justice org runs into: the entire image library for the next decade of community-led justice work in Australia, with consent built in at the layer below.

## Cluster Context

The Brave Ones is a work-series inside the [[projects/justicehub/README|JusticeHub Cluster]], not a disconnected studio side project. The wiki should hold the philosophy, protocol, and role of the series in the justice constellation, [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] should hold the portraits, permissions, voice fragments, and future galleries, and the websites should compose those layers through the [[../../technical/wiki-project-and-work-sync-contract|Wiki Project and Work Sync Contract]] and [[../../concepts/living-website-operating-system|Living Website Operating System]].

## Why this exists

A diagram is read once and filed. A portrait is stood in front of. The CONTAINED tour proved that the move that breaks a funder, a judge, or a minister is not a stat — it is a body in a room. The Brave Ones is that move at scale and on repeat. It is also self-replicating: every new portrait is a new ALMA node, a new [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] story, a new Tractorpedia article. The system gets smarter every time someone stands in front of the lens.

The name does the inversion in three words. The dominant media calls these young people "at-risk youth", "offenders", "system-impacted". The Brave Ones says: *they are not the problem. They are the proof. And they are braver than anyone in the system that failed them.*

## Visual language

The reference is the Brian Duffy / *Station to Station*-era David Bowie portrait register, 1976:

- High-contrast black & white, soft halated key light from a single source
- Deep velvety blacks, silvery mid-tones, almost no pure whites except in the halation
- Film grain texture — never digital crispness
- Shallow depth of field, subject emerging from shadow
- Devotional close framing — chin/hand/contemplation
- Refuses the colour-coded infographic vocabulary of every other systems-change visual
- Negative space carries weight

Palette: bone white (#F4F0E8) and velvet black (#0E0E10). Optional single accent: blood ochre (#7A2F1A), used at most once per artefact.

Typography: refined serif, set small, set far from the centre. Numbers and statistics never appear on the portrait — they live in companion artefacts. The image is the argument; the data is the evidence.

## Components

|---|---|---|
| The portraits | Studio-quality B&W photographs, single visual continuity, life-size when printed | The photographer + each subject's editorial control |

| The stories | One-page case study per portrait: where they were, what changed, who held them | JusticeHub + community partner |

| The galleries | A touring physical exhibition — MCA, NGV, Powerhouse, regional galleries, ACCO halls, parliament foyers | ACT Studio + venue partners |
| The online hall | A permanent presence on JusticeHub — each portrait a node in ALMA | JusticeHub + Empathy Ledger |
| The book | Year three: the printed book | ACT publishing partner |

## Cultural protocol (non-negotiable)

Before a single Indigenous young person sits for a portrait:

1. **Aunty Bev, Uncle Terry, Tanya Turner, Kristy Bloomfield** at [[oonchiumpa|Oonchiumpa]] are consulted and the protocol is co-designed
2. **Empathy Ledger consent flow** is the legal and technical baseline — not aspirational
3. **Each subject has right of withdrawal at any point**, including after exhibition; portraits removed from all venues and digital instances within 7 days of request
4. **Subjects under 18** require guardian and cultural authority sign-off
5. **No exhibition opens without at least one subject (or a designated cultural representative) present** — this is their show, not ours
6. **Income** from commercial use (print sales, book sales, licensing) flows to subjects and their nominated community organisations on a transparent published split

The first consent conversation is more important than the first photograph. If we get the protocol wrong, the project should not exist.

## Stories already in the wiki worth photographing

These are the young people whose stories are already documented and consented at some level via [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] — the natural seed cohort:

- **Xavier** (Oonchiumpa) — built the Stretch Bed; was told by formal reports he had "limited ability to understand"; Oonchiumpa bet on his version
- **MS** (Oonchiumpa) — thought he was from Alice Springs; reconnected with family across Central Australia; building a tourism venture on his own Country
- **CB** (Oonchiumpa) — becoming a man, becoming a leader in Town Camp
- **Jackqwann** (Mparntwe) — rapper exploring identity, cultural heritage, belonging
- **A** (Oonchiumpa) — guarded to self-advocate after losing her mother
- **M** (Oonchiumpa) — homelessness to independent living
- **L** (Oonchiumpa, age 16) — named the cause of youth incarceration in one word: *"Oppression."*

Plus subjects to be drawn from Maranguka (Bourke), [[picc|Palm Island]], and [[confit-pathways|Confit Pathways]] as the cohort expands.

Use a **placeholder portrait** for the Three Ripples poster — pulled from Empathy Ledger's already-consented archive OR a public-domain reference image clearly marked as a stand-in.
