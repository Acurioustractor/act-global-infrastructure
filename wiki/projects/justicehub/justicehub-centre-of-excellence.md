# JusticeHub Centre of Excellence

> Research, training, and knowledge-sharing hub supporting the JusticeHub network with evidence-based practice and lived-experience expertise.

**Status:** Ideation | **Code:** ACT-JC | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

The JusticeHub Centre of Excellence is a planned extension of the [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] platform, designed to function as a dedicated hub for research, professional development, and knowledge exchange in youth justice reform.

Where JusticeHub provides the digital infrastructure for community-led justice, the Centre of Excellence provides the knowledge infrastructure: translating lived experience, academic research, and practice wisdom into usable resources for practitioners, policymakers, and community organisations working with young people in the justice system.

## Intended Functions

### Research Translation
Converting academic research and evaluation data into accessible, actionable resources for frontline workers and community organisations. Bridging the gap between what the evidence says works and what practitioners can actually use.

### Professional Development
Training and capability building for youth justice workers, community leaders, and allied professionals. Emphasis on lived-experience-informed practice and culturally safe approaches.

### Knowledge Sharing
A shared repository of case studies, practice frameworks, program evaluations, and community voice — drawn from across the JusticeHub network and partner organisations.

### Evidence Building
Systematic documentation of what works in community-led justice approaches, building the evidence base that funders and policymakers require while keeping community voice at the centre.

## Current Status

In ideation. The concept builds on JusticeHub's existing network and the evidence being generated through projects like [[campfire|CAMPFIRE]], [[confit-pathways|ConFit Pathways]], and [[contained|CONTAINED]]. The Centre of Excellence would formalise the knowledge-sharing that already happens informally across these projects.
