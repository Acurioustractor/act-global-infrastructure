# The Full Idea

> *A community-led infrastructure for youth justice in Australia. Three rings of work. Seven methods. Ten anchor communities. Three years to make it visible at scale.*

This document is the **complete idea**, in one place, written for walk-through review. Not a pitch to a specific funder. Not a deck. The whole architecture so it can be sat with, edited, and built from.

**How to use this document:** the headings below are the sections. Walk through them in order. At the end of each section there is an *Open questions* block — that is where to leave notes, corrections, and what should change. Each section is meant to be edited independently.

---

## §1 · What it is, in one paragraph

**STAY.** is a three-year program that leaves a library. Seven method books in **The STAY Series**, ten community volumes from ten anchor communities on Country, around fifty hand-stitched young-person Journals, and a national edition that goes to every electorate office in the country. The library is the proof — and it is the destination. A small team accompanies it into being by holding ten Indigenous-led communities over three years, wiring the infrastructure that makes their work legible (CivicGraph + ALMA-governed JusticeHub evidence + Empathy Ledger), and producing the artefacts the rest of the world will fly in to read.

The word **STAY** carries the whole methodology. *Stay where you are* — don't move children off Country to be punished. *Stay connected* — don't parachute in and leave. *Stay in the work* — don't file the artefact and forget. The library is the form all three meanings take.

## §2 · Why it exists

Australian youth justice has more reports, frameworks, evaluations, peak bodies, lived-experience panels, and government strategies than at any point in its history.

It also has more children in detention, more recidivism, and more lateral violence in the sector than at any point in recent memory.

**The problem is not absence of evidence.** It is that the evidence, the relationships, and the stories are all held in different rooms — and the rooms don't talk to each other.

- Frameworks live in consultancy decks.
- Stories live in unfunded community organisations.
- Funding flows live in spreadsheets nobody reads.
- Indigenous knowledge lives where it has always lived — in Country, in Elders, in Tuesday afternoon check-ins that no funder has ever counted as governance.

The cost of detaining one Australian child for one year: **$1.33M** *(ROGS 2024–25)*.
The projected detention rate for Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander children by 2030–31: **24.6 per 10,000** *(Closing the Gap)*.

We are not here to argue these numbers. We are here to **answer** them.

### §4.1 · The Three Circles

![](../output/three-circles-journal-spread.png)

**Subtitle:** *A community-led methodology for working with people the system has failed.*

**The model:**

| # | Circle | Who | When |
|---|---|---|---|

>
> The Three Circles is the methodology: start where the system has failed hardest, and let the work travel outward. Most programs do the opposite. Most programs fail.

### §4.2 · The Living Map Loop

![](../output/alma-loop-journal.png)

**Subtitle:** *How communities grow the ALMA-governed evidence commons.*

**The four moves:**

| # | Step | What happens | Who does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **WITNESS** | Someone notices a working model the formal system has never recognised. | A community member, a worker, anyone willing to look. |
| 2 | **DOCUMENT** | The model gets captured properly — story, mechanism, conditions, outcomes — in the witness's own voice. | Storyteller + the person running the model. |
| 3 | **VERIFY** | Community sovereignty over what counts as proof. Elder-, kin-, place-led validation. **Not** academic peer review. **Not** government audit. | The community itself, on its own terms. |
| 4 | **PUBLISH** | The entry joins the Map. Now searchable, citable, fundable. | The Map (the infrastructure does the rest). |

**Tagline:** *the things that work are already happening. we just have to write them down — properly.*

> 1,775 community-led models, and growing.
> 128 already verified by Elders, communities, or trials.
> The rest are the work — and the Loop is how we move them up the ladder.
>
> The system doesn't know what works because it never asked. The ALMA-governed evidence commons is the answer to that — built so communities can witness, document, verify and publish *what already works*, on their own terms.
>
> Every entry attracts the next witness. The library grows by recognition, not by extraction.

### §4.4 · I Have · I Am · I Can

![](../output/intentionality-canvas-journal-spread.png)

**Subtitle:** *Nine resources every child draws on to face adversity.*

**The nine resources:**

| | Category | What it is | Three items |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I HAVE** | external supports — what's around me | trusting, loving relationships · role models who show what resilient looks like · people who set limits with care |
| **I AM** | inner strengths — what I carry | respectful of myself · confident and optimistic · empathic and caring of others |
| **I CAN** | skills — what I do | solve problems · stay with a task / persevere · reach out for help when I need it |

**Tagline:** *resilience is contagious. its absence is also contagious.*

> Edith Grotberg studied street children in Lima — kids who'd lived on the streets for months or years, who'd used drugs, who'd been beaten by police, who'd sold sex for food. And she found the same thing she'd found in twenty other countries: the kids who *made it* drew on three pools of resources.
>
> **I have.** External supports — trusting relationships, role models, people who held the line.
> **I am.** Inner strengths — self-respect, confidence, empathy.
> **I can.** Skills — problem-solving, perseverance, knowing when to ask for help.
>
> Most programs design for the visible deficit. The resilience research says: design for the nine.
>
> And the hardest finding from Lima — from the families displaced by political violence: *resilience is contagious. Its absence is also contagious.* When the parents fled into trauma, the children fell with them. When the parents engaged the new world, the children rose with them. Whatever you build for a child, build the same thing for the adults around the child — or the child has no model to copy.
>
> *— after Edith Henderson Grotberg, Resilience in Street Children and in Victims of Political Violence in Peru, Lima 2006.*

### §4.5 · The Field Canvas

![](../output/field-diagnostic-journal-spread.png)

**Subtitle:** *Look honestly at the whole field — above the soil and below — before you change anything.*

**The 5 P scan:**

| Domain | What you read | Hidden pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **Projects** | What's active. What's earning. What's stalled. | Where money comes from vs where energy goes. |
| **People** | Who's here. Who's paid. Who decides. | The unspoken pay gap. The implicit operating agreement. |
| **Pulses** | Operating rhythm. Weekly / monthly / quarterly. | Whether urgency is driving priority instead of strategy. |
| **Process** | How work moves through. Closure rate. | Starting is easy, finishing is hard. WIP creep. |
| **Products** | What's packaged. What's priced. Go-to-market. | "If we build it well enough, they'll come" — they don't. |

**The PTO discipline:** *the tractor powers one implement at a time.*

| Lane | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Drive** | Connect the PTO now. Maximum 3 projects. |
| **Field** | Planted. Not powered yet. Hold the line. |
| 🔧 **Shed** | Park, sunset, or hand over. |

**The 90-day Furrow Plan** — six tracks every diagnostic ends in: Mission, Revenue, People, Product, Partnerships, Rhythm. Each one with an action verb, a target, and the proof it landed.

**Tagline:** *what grows above is a function of the soil below.*

> Most strategic plans fail because they describe the field above the ground and ignore the soil below. The Field Canvas is the tool that makes you look at both.
>
> Above: revenue, platforms, community, proof — the things outsiders see. Below: culture, operations, market — the soil that's either growing the visible work or rotting it. And under that, the root system: the infrastructure, relationships, philosophy and financial spine that connect everything.

## §5 · The proposition

A three-year program shaped as three movements. The movements come from Listen · Curiosity · Action · Art compressed into the program's whole arc:

| Year | Name | Where | What happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Year 1** | *Listen* | The ten anchor communities, in Australia | Heavy in-community engagement. Relationships. The first round of journals begins. The infrastructure layer (CivicGraph + Living Map + Empathy Ledger) is wired to the field arm so every story has a home. |
| **Year 2** | *Compare* | Distributed European exchange | Each community sends a young person + a support person to **one** European destination — Diagrama (Spain), Halt (Holland), or the Children's Hearings system (Scotland). Reciprocal European visits to Australia in the same year. Brings the international peer relationship into the journal. |
| **Year 3** | *Tell* | Back to the ten communities, then outward | Ten community-led launches in the ten places. A published book gathers the ten journals into a single national volume. The work goes home before it goes to the city. |

Three acts. Listen / Compare / Tell. Funders, audiences, and books all need three acts.

## §7 · The Library — *The STAY Series*

> *At the end of this program we have a library. Not a report. A library.*

Imagine a shelf. Aged cream spines, hand-set serif type, soft wear at the corners. Seven slim Penguin Modern Classics-style paperbacks lined up — **The STAY Series, No. 01 through No. 07** — and leaning against them at the end of the row, a stack of hand-stitched young-person Journals. Behind the shelf, ten community volumes. In front of the shelf, an open invitation: *come here to learn.*

This is the move that takes the program from **framework to action**, from *thinking about the work* to *the work itself in your hands*.

#### The other layers of the library

| Layer | What it is | How many |
|---|---|---|
| **Young-person Journals** | Bound book per young person — A5, hand-stitched, ~80–120 pages. ### What the library *does*

The shelf is not the end. It is the **beginning of the second program** — the one that runs when the books exist:

1. **People can read the work.** A magistrate in Hobart, a Minister in Canberra, an Aunty in Halls Creek, a youth worker in Birmingham — the methodology is now a thing they can hold, not a deck they have to sit through.
2. **People can buy the work.** The books go to bookshops, libraries, schools. The royalties flow back to communities and young people on a published split.
3. **People can come to the work.** The library becomes a tour: visitors fly into Australia to learn the methods *in the places they were built*. Mount Isa, Mparntwe, Palm Island, Halls Creek. Indigenous-led learning. The kind of civic-tech tourism Spain has been doing for thirty years through Diagrama — but here, on Country, with the people who built it.
4. **Young people can teach the work.** The young people whose journals are on the shelf become **the teachers**. They show their books. They walk visitors through their journey. They explain the methods in their own words. *The library makes them publishers and hosts, not subjects.*
5. **Other communities can write their own books.** The seven methods are forkable. The journal template is forkable. A community in Birmingham, in Lima, in Murcia, in Christchurch can pick up the series and add their own volumes — and the library grows.

### The macro reframe

This is what moves Australia from a country that **imports** youth justice frameworks to a country that **exports** them. From the place that has the highest Indigenous incarceration rate in the OECD to the place the rest of the world flies in to learn from.

> *Indigenous thinking. World model. Visitors invited in. Australia as the world-leading community model.*
>
> The library is not the report. The library is the proof — and the destination.

### The components, in detail

**The seven method books.** The Penguin-style series. Already designed. Live in `wiki/output/`. Each one has its own page in the journal (covers in §4 of this document). They are the *first run of the press.*

**The young-person Journals.** Year 3 delivery. Hand-stitched. The visual register is the **ledger page** — Christian Boltanski, Sophie Calle, Edmund Clark, Theaster Gates, Anselm Kiefer, Judy Watson, Daniel Boyd, Vernon Ah Kee, Tony Albert, Brook Andrew. Hand-made, lived-in, accumulated over time, the young person at the centre. See `tools/three-ripples-ledger.html` for the visual mockup.

**The community volumes.** One per anchor community, accumulating across all three years. Bound at the end of Year 3 alongside each community-led launch.

**The national edition.** The one volume that gathers the ten community volumes into a single national artefact. Sent to every electorate office. Sold publicly. The thing on the shelf in the public library.

**The companion exhibition (CONTAINED).** The shipping container on the road. Travels alongside the launches. The library you walk *into.*

**The platform underneath (CivicGraph + Living Map + JusticeHub + Empathy Ledger).** The infrastructure side of the library. Every voice in a journal traces back to a CivicGraph entity. Every model in a method book is verified through the Living Map. The library has receipts.

## §7.5 · The Travel Diary — *bidirectional, by design*

> *The same notebook a young person from Mparntwe takes to Murcia. The same notebook an Olga from Diagrama takes to Mount Isa.*

The Travel Diary is the meeting place. One physical object, one template, used in both directions. When the diary is on a desk in Spain it is the record of a young person from Country in Europe. When the diary is on a desk in Halls Creek it is the record of a European visitor learning at the source. The same notebook holds both.

This is the format that **operationalises *Australia as destination***. It's the artefact funders, magistrates, and the international civic-tech world walk away with — the proof that the traffic now flows both ways.

### The format

| | What's in it |
|---|---|
| **Cover** | Aged kraft, hand-stitched, with a small linocut symbol of the journey direction (an arrow, a plane, two sets of footprints) |
| **First spread** | Who is travelling, who is hosting, the dates, the Country at each end |
| **Daily entries** | Hand-written by the traveller. Photographs taped in. Receipts. Tickets. Pressed leaves. Notes on what was unexpected. Names of the people who held the room. |
| **The interview** | One long conversation with the host community, in their words. Voice transcript via Empathy Ledger sits alongside the handwritten version. |
| **The model encounter** | Which of the seven STAY Series methods showed up here? In what form? What did it look like in this place that it doesn't look like at home? |
| **What I'm taking back** | The traveller's own words on what they will carry home. |
| **What I'm leaving here** | A page the traveller writes for the host. Stays in the host's diary. |

### How it runs (bidirectional)

| Direction | Who travels | Who hosts | What gets made |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Outbound** *(AU → Europe)* | A young person + a support person from each of the ten anchor communities | Diagrama (Spain), Halt (Holland), Children's Hearings (Scotland) | Ten outbound diaries — one per community pairing |
| **Inbound** *(Europe → AU)* | Small delegations from each European host | The ten anchor communities, hosting on Country | Three inbound diaries — one per European delegation |

**Total: ~13 Travel Diaries per cycle.** Each one becomes a chapter in the relevant young-person Journal *and* a standalone publication that joins the library.

### Why this matters

It collapses the centre-and-periphery framing every philanthropic conversation about Indigenous justice gets stuck in. Spain has been receiving international visitors for thirty years through Diagrama. The Travel Diary is the move that turns Australia into the **other end** of that exchange — not the place that sends people away to learn, but the place people come *to.*

The diary makes the reciprocity legible in a way a report cannot. Two notebooks, sitting next to each other on the library shelf, one Australian and one Spanish — both filled out by the same kind of hand, both in the same template — is the entire argument made physical.

> *The traffic flows both ways. The proof is on the desk.*

**Lean by design.** Two people, not twenty.

- **1 FTE lead + 1 support person** — the core team that rotates through the ten communities
- **4 months on / 8 months off** rhythm — heavy in-community engagement when present, processing and journal production when home
- **One community close to Brisbane** for cheap, frequent low-friction visits and a shorter feedback loop
- **Cultural review fees and Elder governance** budgeted as line items, not absorbed as goodwill labour
- **The infrastructure layer** (CivicGraph, Living Map, Empathy Ledger, JusticeHub) is maintained by the existing ACT engineering team — not a new hire

This is the smallest possible team that can hold ten communities with depth. Anything bigger overwhelms a community when it arrives. Anything smaller can't sustain the cadence.

## §9 · The ask (in three tiers)

> *Fire does not arrive at bonfire stage. You build it, slowly, with attention.* — Model 07

The three tiers borrow the language of **Fire Crescendo** — the seventh book in the STAY Series — because the budget tells the same story as the methodology. A funder picks where they want to enter the fire.

## §10 · The lineage

This idea is not new. It is the synthesis of four lines of evidence that have all been on the table for a long time:

| Voice | Lineage | Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Tyson Yunkaporta** *(Sand Talk, 2019)* | Apalech (Wik), Nungar, Lost Mob | Aboriginal thinking is a structural refusal of hierarchy. Songlines as a working world model that has run for 65,000 years. **Authority sits with the people closest to the consequence.** |
| **Elinor Ostrom** *(Governing the Commons, 1990; Nobel 2009)* | Decades of fieldwork in Nepal, Maine, Spain, Swiss alps | Distributed governance *outperforms* hierarchy where authority sits with the people closest to the consequence and they can see each other's solutions. |
| **Fundación Diagrama** *(Spain, 30+ centres, 30 years)* | National-scale community-based youth justice | 20–30% reoffending vs Australia's 85%. Government-funded, community-run. The proof that this scales. |
| **Edith Henderson Grotberg** *(Resilience in Street Children and Victims of Political Violence in Peru, 2006)* | International resilience research | The I HAVE / I AM / I CAN framework. *Resilience is contagious. Its absence is also contagious.* |
| **Jack Dorsey / Block** *(From Hierarchy to Intelligence, 2026)* | Contemporary tech footnote | "Even fintech caught up." Capabilities + world model + intelligence layer + people at the edge. **Demoted from headline because the pattern is older than Block.** |

**The argument in one sentence:**

> Authority-at-the-edge is not new — Yunkaporta has been documenting it on this continent for thirty years, Ostrom proved it across five, Diagrama has been scaling it in Spain for thirty more, and Grotberg verified it in twenty countries with street children. What was always missing was the legibility infrastructure — the layer that lets capital, magistrates and the public see the work without going through the hierarchy. **We finally built it. It's called CivicGraph. The library on the next page is the proof.**

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], The Full Idea is the architecture document for **STAY** — the program that operationalises LCAA across three years and ten communities to leave a library. The arc is the program's three-act structure (Listen / Compare / Tell) extended to the full LCAA frame, with the artefacts (seven method books, ten community volumes, ~50 hand-stitched young-person Journals, the national edition) as the durable Art layer.

LCAA arc for The Full Idea / STAY:
- **Listen — Year 1** — small team rotates through ten Indigenous-led anchor communities; deep accompaniment without arrival-and-departure; the team that *stays* through the season the program is ostensibly about
- **Curiosity — Year 2 European exchange** — distributed travel to [[diagrama|Diagrama]] (Spain), Halt (Holland), and the Children's Hearings system (Scotland); each anchor community sends a focused pair, comes home with a chapter; reciprocal European visits to Australia in the same year
- **Action — Year 3 community-led launches** — ten launches in ten places; The STAY Series (seven method books) + ten community volumes published; a national edition delivered to every electorate office
- **Art** — the per-young-person Journals as the centre artefact; the library itself as the proof; the artefacts the rest of the world flies in to read

STAY's bet: in a sector with more reports, frameworks, and panels than ever — and more children in detention than ever — the missing infrastructure is *holding*, not scaling. The library is the form that holds.
