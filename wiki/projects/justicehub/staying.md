> *A three-year accompaniment of community-led youth justice in seven places.*

**Status:** ⚠️ Superseded by [[three-circles|Three Circles]] as a pitch — preserved here as the methodology + alternative-shape working draft | **Cluster:** Methodology + creative artefact (now lives inside Circle Two) | **Original envelope (no longer the ask):** Foundation $1.8M · Recommended $3.43M · Full ambition $6M (over 3 years)

## What it is

A 3-year program in which a small team rotates through 7 Australian communities — one in each state — accompanying community-led youth justice work, photographing it, journaling it, and producing a bound book per young person at the end. In Year 2, every community sends a young person and a support person on a focused exchange visit to **one** European country — [[diagrama|Diagrama]] in Spain, Halt in Holland, or the Children's Hearings system in Scotland — and brings the experience back. After 3 years it culminates in seven community-led launches and a published book — the largest collection of community-led youth justice evidence ever assembled in this country.

The word **Staying** is the methodology. Every other youth justice program leaves. Staying is the move.

The subtitle **Country & Council** is non-negotiable. *Country* is the place and the people on it. *Council* is the form of community-led decision-making. Both structures are the spine of the program.

## Why this exists

Australia spends $3.9M to detain a single child for 3 years. For roughly the cost of detaining 1.5 children, Staying runs for 3 years across 7 communities, reaches ~50 young people, takes Aboriginal Elders and tribal council members to Spain, and produces the largest evidence base of community-led youth justice ever assembled in this country.

The pattern Staying makes legible is older than fintech — Tyson Yunkaporta's *Sand Talk*, Elinor Ostrom's commons work, [[diagrama|Diagrama]]'s three decades in Murcia. [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] is the *world model* that holds it. [[the-brave-ones|The Brave Ones]] is its human face. [[contained|CONTAINED]] is the room you walk into. **Staying is the program that produces all of it.**

It is also the answer to the only question philanthropic capital ever actually asks: *does it work, and how do we know?* Staying answers by staying long enough to be in the proof.

See the synthesis: [[the-edge-is-where-the-healing-is-justicehub-as-the-world-model-for-community-led|The Edge Is Where the Healing Is]].

## Three movements

| Year | Name | Where | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Year 1** | *Listen* | 7 Australian communities | 4 months on (rotating visits) + 8 months virtual support |
| **Year 2** | *Compare* | Distributed European exchange — each community sends **2 people** (1 young person + 1 support) to **one** of [[diagrama|Diagrama]] (Spain), Halt (Holland), or Children's Hearings (Scotland) — focused short visit, full capture, return home | 4 months on in AU + the European exchange visits + reciprocal European visits to AU |
| **Year 3** | *Tell* | Back to the 7 communities + 7 community-led launches | 4 months on + 8 months journal production & launches |

Three acts. Funders, audiences, books all need three acts. Listen / Compare / Tell.

## Team

**1 FTE lead + 1 support person — two people.** Lean on purpose. Doesn't overwhelm a community when it arrives, and fits inside a $1M/year ceiling.

## Cadence

- **4 months on** — heavy engagement, rotating visits per community, in person, longer per-visit stays so each trip earns its travel cost
- **8 months off** — back home in Brisbane, processing, photographing, building journals, prepping next round, doing the European exchange logistics in Year 2
- **One community close to Brisbane** for cheap, frequent low-friction visits and a shorter feedback loop

## The journal — the centre artefact

> *If the program produces nothing else, the journals are the proof.*

- **Bound book per young person** — A5, hand-stitched, ~80–120 pages
- **One delivery, in Year 3** — the complete bound volume, accumulating quietly across all three years and only printed once everything is in. (Earlier versions of the design had three chapter deliveries; cut to fit the envelope without losing the artefact itself.)
- **Contents:** B&W portraits in [[the-brave-ones|Brave Ones]] discipline, the young person's own handwriting, voice transcripts from [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]], letters from Aunties and uncles, ledger entries of who showed up and when, drawings, marks, place — and the European exchange visit captured as its own chapter
- **Owned by the young person.** Not ACT. Not Minderoo. OCAP/CARE protocols at the data layer through Empathy Ledger. Right of withdrawal at any point.
- **One archival copy** (with explicit consent only) held by ACT for the program's evidence base.

Visual register: the **ledger page**, not the diagram. References — Christian Boltanski, Sophie Calle, Edmund Clark, Theaster Gates, Anselm Kiefer, Judy Watson, Daniel Boyd, Vernon Ah Kee, Tony Albert, Brook Andrew. Hand-made, lived-in, accumulated over time, system-as-marginalia, the young person at the centre. See `tools/three-ripples-ledger.html`.

## Year 3 launches (communities first)

**Seven launches in seven places.** The work goes home. Each of the seven communities hosts a launch on their own terms, in their own venues, with their own people standing in front of their own portraits and journals.

Accompanied by a **published book** — one printed volume that gathers the seven journals (with consent) into a single national artefact. Every electorate office gets a copy.

**National gallery tour deferred to a Year-4 stretch goal.** The institutional tour (NGA / MCA / NGV / regional) is the natural next chapter once Year 3 has landed and proven the work — but it sits outside this $3M envelope and would be raised separately, ideally with the young people and community representatives travelling with the work into the city institutions in Year 4.

## Year 2 — the European exchange

Year 2 is **distributed, not centralised.** No team-in-residence. No one community sends people to all three countries. Each community chooses one European destination that fits the work they're already doing, sends a focused pair, and brings the experience back into the journal and the home community.

**The shape:**

- **2 travellers per community: 1 young person + 1 support person** (Elder, family member, tribal council member, or program lead — chosen by the community, not by ACT)
- **One country per community**, not all three. Each pair goes deep into one place rather than skating across three.
- **Three host geographies**, chosen because they hold practice the rest of the world treats as impossible:
  - **Spain — [[diagrama|Diagrama]]** (Murcia and beyond). The thirty-year proof that community-based youth justice scales. Cornerstone partner; door is already open via the Olga meeting.
  - **Holland — Halt.** Restorative justice infrastructure and the diversion-first prevention model.
  - **Scotland — Children's Hearings system + Whole System Approach.** The political courage to legislate a minimum age of criminal responsibility worth importing.
- **Focused short visits** — long enough to sit inside the practice, short enough to honour the lives travellers leave at home and to keep the budget honest
- **Reciprocal European visits to Australia** — small delegations from each host (Diagrama, Halt, Scottish) travelling to a subset of the seven communities, walking Country with the people they only ever read about
- **Captured in full** — every visit documented end-to-end (photographs, voice, journal entries), and every traveller's experience becomes a chapter in their community's contribution to the Year 3 book

Why this still works as the masterstroke at a fraction of the original budget:

1. Puts a young person and a community Elder on a plane to Europe and into a Spanish, Dutch, or Scottish youth centre. **Those conversations have never happened in this country's history** — and they happen in seven communities at once because every community sends a pair.
2. Inspires the local communities to believe they are *already* doing world-class work — and to see themselves as international peers, not service recipients.
3. Brings Europe to Australia in the same year. The reciprocal visits collapse the centre/periphery framing every philanthropic conversation about Indigenous justice gets stuck in.
4. Turns [[diagrama|Diagrama]], Halt, and the Children's Hearings network from abstract precedents into living relationships, distributed across seven communities.
5. Gives the program its three-act arc — *Listen / Compare / Tell.*
6. Makes Minderoo's gift internationally legible — *"The Minderoo Australian Justice Cohort sent fourteen people to Europe and brought Europe back."*

## Three scales — same program, three sizes

Staying flexes. The architecture is the same at every scale; what moves is *how many communities*, *how big the team*, *how deep Europe goes*, and *what Year 3 produces*. Three honest tiers, so the conversation with a funder is about fit, not negotiation:

| | **Foundation** | **Recommended** | **Full ambition** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Annual flow** | ~$600K/year | ~$1.0M / $1.24M / $1.20M | ~$2.0M/year |
| **3-year total** | **~$1.8M** | **~$3.43M** | **~$6.0M** |
| **Communities** | 5 communities (drop hardest-to-reach 2 states) | 7 communities, one per state | 7 communities + annual national cohort gathering |
| **Team** | 1 FTE lead, no support | 1 FTE lead + 1 support | 2 FTE + 1 support |
| **Cadence** | 3 months on / 9 months virtual | 4 months on / 8 months virtual | 6 months on / 6 months virtual |
| **Year 2 Europe** | Deferred — small Spain scoping trip only (2 ACT staff, no community travellers) | Distributed exchange — 14 travellers (1 young person + 1 support per community), one country each (Spain / Holland / Scotland) | Distributed exchange + reciprocal European visits to AU + one full cohort immersion week per host country |
| **Journal** | Y3 only, simple binding, 1 copy per young person | Y3 only, hand-stitched, 1 copy per young person + 1 archive | **Three chapter deliveries** (Y1/Y2/Y3), hand-stitched, premium binding, multiple copies |
| **Year 3** | 5 community-led launches, no book | 7 community-led launches + 1 published book | 7 community-led launches + book + **national gallery tour** (NGA / MCA / NGV) + every-electorate-office distribution |

**How we find the 7 (locked):** there is no funnel. ## Cultural protocol (non-negotiable)

1. No community is approached without prior consultation with existing ACT relationships in that region
2. **Elder and Council sign-off** before any photographs, recordings, or journals begin
3. **OCAP/CARE protocols** at the data layer via [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] — the legal and technical baseline, not aspirational
4. **Young person owns their journal.** Right of withdrawal at any point, including post-exhibition; portraits and entries removed within 7 days of request
5. **Subjects under 18** require guardian and cultural authority sign-off
6. **Cultural review fees** are budgeted as line items, not absorbed as goodwill labour
7. **No exhibition opens** without at least one young person or cultural representative present
8. **Income from book/exhibition sales** flows on a published transparent split to subjects and their nominated community organisations

The first consent conversation matters more than the first photograph. Get the protocol wrong and the program should not exist.
