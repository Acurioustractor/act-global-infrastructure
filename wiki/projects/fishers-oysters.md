# Fishers Oysters

> A cultural-first aquaculture enterprise reclaiming and restoring Moreton Bay's oyster industry through Indigenous knowledge, community ownership, and regenerative practice.

**Status:** Transferred | **Code:** ACT-FO | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

Fishers Oysters is working to restore the oyster industry of Moreton Bay through a framework that places Indigenous knowledge first. The project treats ecological restoration and economic sovereignty as inseparable: the reef cannot be healthy without the cultural knowledge systems that have managed it for generations, and those knowledge systems cannot be sustained without the economic sovereignty that comes from community ownership of the enterprise.

The name "Fishers" signals both the people (fishing families and custodians) and the act — this is a project about reclaiming the right to fish, to own, and to steward.

## Key Details

- **Location:** Moreton Bay, Queensland
- **Website:** [fishersoysters.com](https://fishersoysters.com)
- **Core model:** Cultural-first aquaculture with community ownership structure
- **Focus:** Ecological justice, economic sovereignty, intergenerational knowledge transfer

## What Makes It Different

Most aquaculture operates as extractive industry. Fishers Oysters inverts this:

- **Indigenous knowledge drives the ecological model** — Traditional custodial knowledge of Moreton Bay's reef systems informs how the oyster beds are restored and managed
- **Community ownership** — the enterprise is structured for the community to own and benefit from the operation, not external investors
- **Regenerative practice** — the goal is not just commercial viability but ecological restoration and the health of the broader bay system
- **Intergenerational sovereignty** — knowledge and economic benefit are designed to transfer across generations, not be captured in a single cohort or business cycle

## LCAA Phase

**Action** — Fishers Oysters is on-the-ground ecological and economic work. Boats on water, reefs being seeded, knowledge being practised. The Listen phase lives in the deep Indigenous knowledge informing every operational decision.
