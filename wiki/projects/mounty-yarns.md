# Mounty Yarns

> A community storytelling project in Mount Gravatt — gathering and preserving local knowledge through yarning, oral history, and cultural exhibition.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-MY | **Tier:** Studio

## Overview

Mounty Yarns is rooted in a simple conviction: that communities hold extraordinary knowledge about themselves, and that preserving and sharing that knowledge is both an act of dignity and a source of community strength. Operating in the Mount Gravatt area of Brisbane, the project uses yarning circles, oral history methods, and community gathering to capture stories that would otherwise be lost.

The project operates at the intersection of community memory, cultural identity, and place — holding space for the stories that official histories overlook.

## Key Activity

### Yarning Circles
The yarning circle is both methodology and ethic: a form of conversation that values every voice, that proceeds at the pace of relationship, and that treats story as knowledge. Mounty Yarns uses this approach not as a cultural affectation but as a genuine epistemological commitment — the knowledge that matters is held by the people in the circle.

### Oral History Collection
Stories are gathered, documented, and preserved in forms that communities can access and own. The project is attentive to questions of consent, ownership, and the appropriate stewardship of community memory.

### Cultural Exhibition

The **CONTAINED** exhibition — a significant ACT art project — was shown at Mounty Yarns for $15,000, representing a meaningful intersection between the storytelling work of Mounty Yarns and ACT's broader creative practice. See [[contained|CONTAINED]] for the full exhibition context.
