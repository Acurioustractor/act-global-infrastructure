# ReSOLEution

> A shoe design and customisation program inside youth detention that transforms young people from sneaker consumers into sneaker creators, building identity, skills, and commercial pathways.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-RS | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

ReSOLEution is a creative intervention program operating inside Bimberi Youth Justice Centre in the ACT. It gives young people in detention the opportunity to design and customise their own sneakers — working from initial concept through to finished prototype across four structured workshop sessions.

The insight behind the program is simple but precise: in facilities where almost everything is stripped away and uniformity enforced, shoes remain a source of pride and personal identity for young people. ReSOLEution meets them there — not by importing an external interest, but by taking something they already care deeply about and making it a platform for creativity, skill development, and legitimate pathways to commercial opportunity.

The program's name holds a double meaning: resolution (finishing, completing, deciding) and sole (the foundation of both a shoe and a person's character). Craft your future, one sole at a time.

## Origin

The program was developed by a practitioner who began their justice work through a Snow Foundation-supported pilot exploring digital tools for the Drug and Alcohol Court (DASL). That experience — building relationships slowly, understanding what actually matters to young people inside — shaped ReSOLEution's design from the ground up.

The observation was direct: during visits to Bimberi, amid all the restrictions and uniformity of detention, shoes were consistently how young people expressed individuality and connection to life beyond the centre's walls. "They'd proudly show off their sneakers outside their units, sharing stories and connections through their footwear."

From this observation came a question: what if that pride became a skill? What if their stories became designs? What if their creativity had real commercial value?

## The Workshop Structure

ReSOLEution runs as four intensive 40-minute sessions, designed to build progressively:

**Session 1 — Brainstorming and Initial Design**
Participants explore the personal stories, interests, and themes they want to express. Initial sketches begin, followed by group sharing and feedback.

**Session 2 — Design Development**
A brief lesson in core design principles (colour theory, balance, composition), followed by refinement of initial sketches and development of the narrative behind each design — what story does the shoe tell?

**Session 3 — Prototyping and Making**
Introduction to materials and techniques, then 30 minutes of hands-on prototyping — creating a basic 3D model or detailed physical sketch. Design challenges are worked through in real time.

**Session 4 — Planning and Review**
Discussion of how the design could be produced at scale. Participants prepare and deliver a brief presentation of their design and its story. Group feedback closes the process.

By the end of the four sessions, each participant has: a fully developed shoe design concept, a prototype or detailed visual representation, a personal narrative explaining the meaning of their design, and experience presenting their work to a group.

## Key Relationships

**Jayke** — a mentor with lived experience of detention, introduced to the program through the DASL pilot. Jayke's involvement brings authenticity that no amount of professional training can provide. He helps participants see real possibilities, sharing his own journey and what changed for him.

**Lorana Bartels** — academic researcher and force for positive change in the Canberra justice space, whose support and insights helped shape the program's approach to innovation in a complex environment.

**Butter, Sydney** — ReSOLEution's commercial partnership with Butter (Sydney's sneaker culture hub) is what distinguishes this program from a craft activity. Participants' designs are showcased at Butter — connecting their creativity directly to one of Australia's most respected sneaker culture communities. For a young person in detention, knowing that their design could be displayed in that venue is not a small thing. It is proof that their creativity has real-world value.

## Partnership with ConFit Pathways

ReSOLEution operates at Bimberi on alternating days to [[confit-pathways|ConFit Pathways]]. The two programs are complementary by design: ConFit brings the internal work (G-Code methodology, physical discipline, values), ReSOLEution brings external expression (creativity, story, commercial possibility). Together they offer a more complete intervention — addressing who someone is on the inside and what they can build on the outside.

## Financial Empowerment Model

A core feature of ReSOLEution is that shoe sales generate revenue for designers upon release. This is not a symbolic gesture — it is a structural attempt to connect the creative work done inside to real economic outcomes outside. Young people who invest in their designs and share their stories have a legitimate pathway to earning from their creativity.

## Connection to ACT Ecosystem

ReSOLEution is tracked within the [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] ecosystem as a community-led innovation in youth justice. It embodies the ACT principle that programs that actually transform lives begin with what young people themselves identify as meaningful — not what adults decide they should care about.

The program also demonstrates the ACT methodology in action: meeting people where they are (sneaker culture, identity, pride), building trust through genuine engagement, and creating real pathways rather than instructional ones.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], ReSOLEution sits in the **Art** phase — a creative intervention inside detention that turns young people from sneaker consumers into sneaker creators. The program's design choice is to start with what young people already care about (sneakers, identity, pride) and let creativity become a legitimate pathway to identity, skills, and commercial possibility.

LCAA arc for ReSOLEution:
- **Listen** — the founding observation that in facilities where uniformity is enforced, shoes remain how young people express individuality and connection to life beyond the walls; conversations about the personal stories, interests, and themes participants want to express
- **Curiosity** — what if pride became a skill, stories became designs, creativity had real commercial value? Four 40-minute sessions designed to build progressively from sketch to prototype to presentation
- **Action** — fully developed designs and prototypes delivered by every participant; the partnership with Butter (Sydney's sneaker culture hub) where designs are showcased; a financial-empowerment model where shoe sales generate revenue for designers upon release
- **Art** — the program *is* the artwork — sneaker design as legitimate creative practice grounded in cultural pride; the double meaning of the name itself (resolution / sole) as the program's invitation: craft your future, one sole at a time

ReSOLEution and [[confit-pathways|ConFit Pathways]] alternate days at Bimberi by design — ConFit holds the internal work (values, discipline, identity), ReSOLEution holds the external expression (creativity, story, commercial possibility). Together they offer a more complete intervention.
