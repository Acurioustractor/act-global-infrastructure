# Feel Good Project

> A haircut is not a haircut. For a woman who has been invisible for months, it's someone saying: you are worth looking after.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FG

## What It Is

The Feel Good Project is an ACT-supported initiative providing mobile hair care and beauty services to women experiencing hardship — homelessness, domestic violence, poverty, or crisis. ACT's contribution was helping build a caravan: a mobile salon that brings the service to where women are, rather than asking women in difficult circumstances to navigate their way to a fixed location.

The project is active. Financial tracking in Xero under `ACT-FG — Feel Good Project` confirms it has incurred costs. Deeper operational detail — the partner organisation, service geography, and outcomes data — has not yet been fully ingested into the wiki.

## The Mobile Service Logic

The caravan is not incidental to this project. It is the argument.

Fixed-location services impose a friction cost on the people who most need them. A woman who is sleeping rough, or staying in emergency accommodation, or managing an unsafe home situation doesn't always have the capacity to travel to a service on a schedule that suits the service's hours. Fixed services serve the people who can reach them. Mobile services serve the people who can't.

Orange Sky — co-founded by ACT co-founder Nicholas Marchesi OAM — demonstrated this at scale: laundry services in a van, going where people experiencing homelessness actually are. The logic is the same: don't require dignity to be earned by navigating the system. Bring it.

The Feel Good Project applies this same logic to beauty and hair care — services that might seem non-essential until you understand what they actually do.

## Why Beauty Care Is Not a Luxury

For women experiencing hardship, access to hair care and beauty services carries practical and psychological weight:

**Practical:** Job interviews, Centrelink appointments, custody hearings, accommodation applications — all of them are easier when a woman feels presentable and is treated as someone worth taking seriously.

**Relational:** The act of someone washing your hair, cutting it, treating it with care — is an act of human regard. For women who have been in situations of control, violence, or invisibility, being touched gently, by someone who is there for them, is not trivial. It is sometimes transformative.

**Self-perception:** There is documented evidence in social service contexts that access to personal care — haircuts, clean clothes, showers — correlates with increased willingness to engage with other support services. The Feel Good Project is an entry point as much as an endpoint.

This is the same insight that runs through Orange Sky (laundry as conversation), [[the-confessional|The Confessional]] (horse trailer as mobile healing space), and ACT's broader approach: the physical thing creates the relational moment.

## Connection to ACT Ecosystem

### Nicholas Marchesi and the Orange Sky Pattern

Nicholas Marchesi OAM co-founded Orange Sky Australia in 2014, turning a van with two washing machines into a mobile laundry and conversation service for people experiencing homelessness. Orange Sky now operates in every Australian state and territory, has logged over 50,000 hours of conversation, and is one of the most recognised social enterprises in Australia.

The Feel Good Project carries Orange Sky's DNA: a vehicle, a service, dignity as infrastructure, meet people where they are. Nicholas's involvement in ACT means this instinct runs deep in how ACT thinks about what practical support looks like.

### Global Laundry Alliance

The Global Laundry Alliance is ACT's network of Orange Sky-model organisations operating in other countries — bringing the mobile laundry concept to new geographies. The Feel Good Project exists in the same conceptual family: mobile dignity infrastructure, designed around the reality of people's lives rather than the convenience of service delivery.

### The Confessional

The Confessional is an ACT project that converted a horse trailer into a mobile healing space — a place where people can come, sit, and be listened to. It and the Feel Good Project share the same physical grammar: a vehicle that becomes a sanctuary.

### Caring for Those Who Care

[[caring-for-those-who-care|Caring for Those Who Care]] — ACT's program supporting frontline workers — is the complement to the Feel Good Project. One supports the people using services; the other supports the people delivering them. Both operate on the premise that dignity and care are prerequisites for change, not rewards for it.

## The Caravan as Infrastructure

ACT's direct contribution to the Feel Good Project was practical: helping build out the caravan as a mobile service delivery platform. This fits ACT's pattern of providing physical infrastructure — vehicles, spaces, technology — that amplifies the work community organisations are already doing.

The caravan is not a metaphor. It is a specific piece of built infrastructure that allows a service to exist that couldn't otherwise exist in the same form. ACT's role here is the same as its role in Goods on Country manufacturing: building the physical thing that makes the community impact possible.
