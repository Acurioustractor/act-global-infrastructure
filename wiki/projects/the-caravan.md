# The Caravan

> A caravan as a venue, a dignity infrastructure prototype, and an art piece all at once. Born out of the Community Capital Leadership Retreat.

## Overview

The Caravan is an ACT studio piece that emerged from the **10x10 Community Capital Leadership Retreat** in Bowral (February 2026, see [[community-capital|Community Capital]]). It sits in the lineage of [[feel-good-project|Feel Good Project]] and [[global-laundry-alliance|Orange Sky's mobile dignity model]] — vehicles that turn up where the people are, instead of asking the people to come to them.

But it's also an art piece in the [[act-ecosystem|Studio cluster]] sense: the caravan is a thing that carries an argument. The argument is that infrastructure for community capital — leadership retreats, peer councils, dignity services — shouldn't live in conference rooms in capital cities. It should be towable.

## Origin: the Community Capital event

The Community Capital Leadership Retreat (Bowral, Feb 2026, funded by Social Impact Hub Foundation) brought ten community leaders together. The Caravan was the contribution ACT made to that event — both as a literal vehicle and as a statement about what happens when you invert "we built a building, come to us" into "we built a thing, we're coming to you."

[[nicholas-marchesi|Nicholas Marchesi]]'s Orange Sky pattern is the obvious precedent: a van turning up in a park is a more powerful equity intervention than a brochure. The Caravan extends that logic to leadership development.
