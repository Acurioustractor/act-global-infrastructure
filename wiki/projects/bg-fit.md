# BG Fit

> A culturally grounded fitness, healing, and mentorship program addressing the overrepresentation of Indigenous youth in the justice system — using movement and country to disrupt the punitive cycle.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-BG | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

BG Fit operates in Mount Isa and Doomadgee, communities where Indigenous youth face disproportionate contact with the justice system. The program works from a premise that physical fitness, cultural connection, and Elder mentorship — not punishment — are the most effective interventions. By grounding health and wellbeing in culture and country, BG Fit creates conditions for young people to build identity, purpose, and community belonging.

The results are striking: participating youth have reduced their average police contact from approximately 5 incidents per year to less than 1.

## Key Details

- **Locations:** Mount Isa and Doomadgee, Queensland
- **Target cohort:** Indigenous youth at risk of or with prior justice system involvement
- **Key partners:** Local Elders, community organisations, local police
- **Target:** Full community ownership by 2027
- **Current need:** Sustained funding, strengthened Elder partnerships

## Program Structure

The BG Fit model runs across three connected phases:

1. **Pre-camp fitness** — structured physical training building readiness and trust with participants
2. **On-country cultural camps** — annual camps with Elders integrating cultural knowledge, ceremony, and land connection
3. **Post-camp recovery and integration** — follow-up support maintaining the gains made on country

## Proof Points

- Youth police contact reduced from average 5 incidents per year to less than 1 per participant
- Annual on-country camps demonstrating measurable behaviour change
- Elder-led partnerships sustaining cultural credibility and community trust

## LCAA Phase

**Action** — direct programming through fitness, camps, and mentorship — with deep roots in **Listen**, expressed through ongoing Elder partnerships and community co-design.
