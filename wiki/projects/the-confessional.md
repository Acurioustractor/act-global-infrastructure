# The Confessional

> A horse trailer converted into a sacred healing space — a mobile art therapy installation making mental health support accessible in remote and underserved communities.

## Overview

The Confessional is a mobile therapeutic installation built inside a converted horse trailer. It travels to remote and underserved communities where traditional mental health infrastructure is absent or inaccessible, offering a safe, anonymous, and non-clinical space for people to engage with art as a therapeutic tool.

The design is intentional: a horse trailer is familiar in rural and remote contexts, functional, and carries no clinical or institutional associations. Inside, participants engage with art-making as a route to self-expression and emotional processing — with anonymity and psychological safety as core design principles. Crisis support pathways are embedded in the experience.

## Key Details

- **Format:** Mobile installation — horse trailer converted to therapeutic space
- **Primary modality:** Art therapy, creative expression
- **Target communities:** Remote and underserved areas without access to traditional mental health services
- **Website:** [act.place/project/the-confessional](https://act.place/project/the-confessional)
- **Core values:** Anonymity, safety, accessibility, non-clinical framing

## How It Works

The Confessional offers an alternative to traditional therapy by:

- Bringing the space to where people are, rather than requiring travel to services
- Using art-making as the primary therapeutic modality — lower barrier than talk therapy
- Providing anonymity as a structural guarantee, not just a promise
- Embedding crisis support access within the experience for participants who need it

## LCAA Phase

**Art** — The Confessional sits squarely in ACT's Art phase, where creative practice becomes the vehicle for healing, transformation, and community care. Art here is not decoration; it is the mechanism.
