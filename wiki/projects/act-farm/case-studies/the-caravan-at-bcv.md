# The Caravan at BCV

> The Caravan was built for the Community Capital retreat in Bowral. It now lives at Black Cockatoo Valley. A mobile dignity object, parked on Country, still working. This is the case study of what happens when a studio piece stops touring and takes up residence.

## What happened

[[the-caravan|The Caravan]] emerged from the 10x10 Community Capital Leadership Retreat in Bowral (Phase 1, 23–25 February 2025). It was part of the contribution ACT made to that event — both a literal vehicle and a statement: infrastructure for community capital shouldn't live in conference rooms in capital cities, it should be towable.

After Bowral, the Caravan came to BCV. It has been here since, parked and hosted at a notional $550/month that isn't being billed. The original touring logic — turn up where the people are, like [[global-laundry-alliance|Orange Sky's]] vans, like the [[feel-good-project|Feel Good Project]] — now runs alongside a second logic: sometimes the most useful move is to stay put, become part of a place, and let people come to it.

## What we did

- **We built a caravan that carries an argument.** Leadership development, capital convening, and community dignity services don't have to be office-bound. A caravan says that out loud.
- **We deployed it at Bowral.** The Caravan was part of the Phase 1 retreat infrastructure — present, used, photographed, felt.
- **We brought it home to BCV.** Instead of warehousing it or running it as a full tour between convenings, we installed it at Black Cockatoo Valley for a 12-month residency. It sits on Country now. Community Capital convenings that come to BCV can use it. So can visiting artists, storytelling residencies, and groups hiring the convening space.
- **We held it at zero billed cost** for the 12-month period. ACT carries the nominal $550/month; Community Capital funded its build and its Phase 1 use. The Caravan is paid forward into the ecosystem, not rented out.

## What the work produced

**A studio object that crossed registers.** The Caravan is art (it makes an argument), infrastructure (it hosts conversations), and case study (it's now evidence of what ACT studio practice produces). Most studio pieces settle in one of those registers. The Caravan stays in all three.

**A reusable dignity-infrastructure pattern.** The Caravan, the [[feel-good-project|Feel Good Project]], [[global-laundry-alliance|Global Laundry Alliance]], and [[gold-phone|Gold.Phone]] are now a named lineage: *mobile dignity infrastructure*. The pattern travels; the specific object at BCV is one instance of it.

**An anchor for the BCV convening offer.** Groups that hire [[experiences/the-space-at-bcv|The Space at BCV]] can choose to work with the Caravan as part of their convening. It's a gathering object that changes the shape of a conversation — small, enclosed, equal-footed — in a way a room can't.

**A living example of [[designing-for-obsolescence|beautiful obsolescence]].** The Caravan is designed to be handed over to whoever needs it next. The BCV residency is a way-station in that life, not a final destination.

## What we learned

- **Studio objects can have second careers.** The Caravan's first career was the Bowral retreat. Its second is BCV residency. Treating a studio piece as a one-shot is a category mistake — the object keeps working if we keep placing it.
- **Towable and rooted are not opposites.** The argument the Caravan makes (infrastructure should come to people) is not contradicted by it living on Country for a year. That residency is itself a form of placement.
- **Unbilled hosting is a form of investment.** Carrying $550/month in unbilled accommodation is ACT investing in the longevity of the object and the program, not a favour to Community Capital. It compounds.
