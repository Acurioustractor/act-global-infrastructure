## What happened

In February 2025, ACT convened the first Community Capital Leadership Retreat — branded internally as the **10x10 Community Capital Leadership Retreat** — in Bowral, NSW. The retreat brought community leaders and social-impact capital holders into the same room for three days, designed as a structured relational convening rather than a grant-application forum.

Bowral is a wealthy Southern Highlands town with a concentration of philanthropic and impact-investing activity. It was chosen not for convenience but for signal: the space, the town, and the format all said to participants that their work was worth serious capital attention. The meeting notes describe it, aside, as a "super fancy wedding venue." That detail is the method, not decoration.

The "10x10" framing is unresolved in the wiki — probably around ten community leaders meeting capital holders plus support, roughly 25 people in the room total. What's resolved is the outcome: relational ground laid, the Caravan built and deployed as part of the retreat infrastructure, and a follow-through funding arrangement that signed thirteen months later.

## What we did

- **We held the room, not the capital.** ACT did not sit on either side of the money table. We facilitated the convening, designed the time, and kept the focus on relationship rather than transaction. The verbatim framing: *"ACT does not hold the capital in Community Capital. It holds the room."*
- **We made candles while talking about money.** The retreat used candle-making kits (25 kits, line-itemed on INV-0289) as a shared making activity while the capital conversations happened. Hands-occupied talk loosens what heads-only talk can't. This is the method we now carry forward at BCV — it worked because shared making created the relational ground the capital talk needed.
- **We built and deployed [[the-caravan|the Caravan]].** The retreat was the first public deployment of the Caravan — mobile dignity infrastructure in the lineage of [[global-laundry-alliance|Orange Sky]] and the [[feel-good-project|Feel Good Project]]. The caravan reno is line-itemed on INV-0289. The argument-as-object was present in the room.
- **We produced a cookbook.** Also on INV-0289. A companion artefact, not a souvenir — documentation of the food that anchored the convening, as a way of returning the retreat to something usable afterwards.
- **We kept the facilitator economics honest.** Nic Marchesi facilitated as primary ($5,400). Ben Knight supported in-kind. The invoice reflects real cost, not inflated day-rates.

## What the work produced

**A relational base for Phase 2.** Phase 2 — **Catalysing Impact** — was signed on 17 March 2026, thirteen months after the Bowral convening. Up to $400K drawn from a $1M pool funded by QBE via the Social Impact Hub. The gap between Phase 1 and Phase 2 is what this retreat was designed to produce: a year of relationship before any formal capital committed.

**A new home for the program at BCV.** After Bowral, Community Capital funded a year of accommodation at Black Cockatoo Valley and relocated the Caravan there. The retreat came home. See [[case-studies/community-capital-at-bcv|Community Capital at BCV]] for the full case study.

**An object that kept working.** The Caravan did not return to storage after Bowral. It is now resident at BCV at a notional $550/month (unbilled). See [[case-studies/the-caravan-at-bcv|The Caravan at BCV]].

**Verbatim, to preserve:**

> "ACT does not hold the capital in Community Capital. It holds the room."

> "The gap is not a knowledge gap. It's a relationship gap."

## What we learned

- **Environment is part of facilitation.** Choosing the "super fancy wedding venue" was not indulgence. Participants arrived into a space that said their work was worth serious attention. That does work facilitation alone can't.
- **Shared making dissolves the pitch frame.** The candle-making was not an icebreaker. It was the format. Once people are making something together, the conversation about money stops being a pitch and starts being a relationship.
- **Relational programs need year-long runways.** Phase 2 signed in March 2026, on relationships that started in February 2025. A single-event convening followed by silence would have killed this. Moving the program to BCV was the structural answer to that problem.
- **The retreat's best output was the Caravan.** It outlived the weekend by design.
