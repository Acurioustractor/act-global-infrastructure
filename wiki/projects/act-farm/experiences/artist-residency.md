# Artist Residency at BCV

> We place artists on Country at Black Cockatoo Valley to make work that couldn't be made anywhere else. The land is the commission. We are the host, not the curator-in-chief.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-artist-residency | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

We run an artist residency at [[black-cockatoo-valley|Black Cockatoo Valley]] — 150 acres of Jinibara Country in the Sunshine Coast hinterland. We invite artists to live and work here for a defined stretch of time, with accommodation, studio space, meals from the garden, and honest time with the land.

We don't treat the residency as a gallery pipeline. We treat it as a placement on Country. What the artist makes, whether they finish it here, and where it ends up — those are their decisions. What we ask for in return is presence: that the artist actually spends time with this place, not just on it.

The residency sits inside ACT's wider studio practice — it's where [[act-studio|ACT Regenerative Studio]] meets land. It's also where the [[lcaa-method|LCAA method]] (Listen, Curiosity, Action, Art) becomes visible: the Art phase has to be grounded somewhere, and for us that somewhere is here.

## Who it's for

We host artists working across disciplines — visual art, writing, sound, film, performance, material practice, ecological and land art. We especially welcome artists whose practice involves listening to place rather than imposing on it.

We prioritise:
- Artists from communities ACT works alongside (First Nations, lived-experience, regional)
- Practitioners whose work is in dialogue with ecology, Country, or regenerative practice
- Collaborative pairings where a residency serves a longer project

We're not the right fit for artists looking for remote studio time detached from place. The residency asks you to be here, with us, with Country.

## What happens

A residency at BCV has a rhythm, not an itinerary. The length is negotiated — typically two to six weeks — and the artist sets the working pattern. We hold the frame; the artist does the work.

- Arrival and a walk with someone who knows the land
- Studio and accommodation set up — Pink Cabin, Train Carriage, or studio-adjacent space
- Shared meals where there's appetite for them; privacy where there isn't
- Open access to the land inside conservation boundaries we explain on arrival
- Optional conversations with ACT's wider network — [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] storytellers, [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]] makers, regional collaborators
- A closing conversation: what emerged, what wants to happen next, whether there's a case study or artefact worth holding in the wiki

## Price, duration, capacity

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Duration** | 2–6 weeks (negotiated) |
| **Capacity** | 1 artist or 1 small collaborative pairing at a time |
| **Fee** | Tiered — funded placements, supported placements, and self-funded residencies. We publish no single rack rate; we negotiate based on the artist's funding context |
| **Includes** | Accommodation, utilities, basic meals from the garden where season permits, introduction to Country and ecosystem, host time |
| **Doesn't include** | Travel, materials, artist fees outside funded placements |
| **Season** | Year-round, subject to land capacity and other program commitments |

Conservation-first is the baseline. If the creek is low or the season demands quiet, we slow residency intake rather than push through.

## How to inquire

Residency is by inquiry and conversation, not open application rounds. Tell us about your practice, what you're working on, roughly when you're looking to come, and what support you'd need. We reply within a few working days and typically run a short call before confirming. Inquiries go through `/experiences/artist-residency` on the ACT Farm site.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], the Artist Residency at BCV is the place where the **Art** phase has to be grounded somewhere — and for ACT, that somewhere is here. Here's how the four phases show up across a residency:

- **Listen** — arrival walk with someone who knows the land; honest time with Country before any work begins
- **Curiosity** — the residency placement as inquiry: what wants to be made here, that couldn't be made anywhere else?
- **Action** — studio practice in Pink Cabin, Train Carriage, or studio-adjacent space; optional conversations with [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] storytellers, [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]] makers, regional collaborators
- **Art** — the work itself, on the artist's terms; a closing conversation about what emerged and what wants to happen next

The land is the commission. ACT is the host, not the curator-in-chief.
