# Innovation Sessions

> We run focused one- to two-day innovation sessions across four practice areas — youth justice, AI and systems, data and evidence, storytelling. Small rooms, real problems, no theatre.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-innovation-sessions | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

Innovation Sessions are ACT-led working sessions around a specific problem a partner is trying to move. They're not strategy workshops in the generic sense and they're not innovation-theatre with post-its. We bring ACT's method, our ecosystem knowledge, and — when it helps — the right people from our network.

We run four distinct tracks:

### Youth justice
Sessions for organisations working inside or adjacent to the youth justice system — diversion programs, aftercare, family services, education-justice crossovers. We bring the [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] frame, [[custodian-economy|Custodian Economy]] thinking, and the lessons from on-Country work.

### AI and systems
Sessions for organisations grappling with AI as infrastructure — not as product. Includes LLM trust boundaries, consent-first data, [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] patterns, and the [[civicgraph|CivicGraph]] lens on sector-scale systems.

### Data and evidence
Sessions on evidence infrastructure — how to make the data legible to funders, communities, and regulators without flattening it. Covers [[consent-as-infrastructure|consent]], measurement, and how evidence becomes relational rather than extractive.

### Storytelling
Sessions on [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] method at program scale — consent, cultural sensitivity, elder approval, return-to-community publishing. Pairs naturally with the [[experiences/empathy-ledger-storytelling-residency|storytelling residency]].

## Who it's for

- Community-sector leadership teams with a specific, namable problem
- Funders wanting a working session rather than a pitch meeting
- Government and agency teams commissioning community-facing programs
- ACT partners needing a focused day on one of the four tracks

## What happens

One- or two-day intensive, usually 6–15 people in the room.

- Pre-session: a short brief back from us on how we'd frame the problem
- Day: short inputs, longer working blocks, honest conversation, decisions
- Post-session: a written note of decisions and next steps — not a deck

Can run at BCV, in your office, or hybrid. The format is the product, not the venue.
