# Goods on Country Makers' Weekend

> A weekend of making on Country — material practice, shared meals, and honest conversation about how we build durable things for communities that need them. Run with [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]].

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-makers-weekend | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

We run makers' weekends at [[black-cockatoo-valley|Black Cockatoo Valley]] in partnership with [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]] — ACT's recycled-plastic circular-economy enterprise. A group of makers comes to BCV for 2–3 days to work on material practice, learn from the Goods line, and sit in the longer conversation about manufacturing for place.

This isn't a craft weekend. It's material practice grounded in the same questions Goods asks daily: what holds up in remote communities, what breaks, what do we owe the people the object is for. Makers leave with work they've made and a frame for why that work matters.

## Who it's for

- Designers, makers, and material practitioners working with plastics, wood, metal, textile
- Students and early-career designers wanting grounded exposure to a working production line
- Community-sector practitioners interested in social-enterprise manufacturing
- Goods on Country's own network — makers already in or adjacent to the line

## What happens

A typical weekend:

- Friday evening arrival, shared meal, orientation
- Saturday: making sessions — material, prototyping, co-design methods from the Goods practice
- Saturday night: fire, conversation, the harder questions about ethics and manufacture
- Sunday: finishing, critique, shared lunch, close
- Optional Monday add-on: visit to Goods' manufacturing context where feasible

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], the [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]] Makers' Weekend is the material-practice version of Listen → Curiosity → Action → Art — making grounded in the same questions Goods asks daily. Here's how the four phases show up across a 2–3 day weekend:

- **Listen** — orientation on the Goods practice; honest exposure to what holds up in remote communities and what breaks
- **Curiosity** — what do we owe the people the object is for? The harder questions about ethics and manufacture, surfacing around the fire on Saturday night
- **Action** — Saturday's making sessions — material, prototyping, co-design methods from the Goods practice; Sunday's finishing and critique
- **Art** — work the makers leave with, plus a frame for why that work matters; optional Monday visit to Goods' manufacturing context to see the line in operation

Not a craft weekend. Material practice grounded in [[custodian-economy|custodian-economy]] thinking and [[designing-for-obsolescence|designing-for-obsolescence]] discipline.
