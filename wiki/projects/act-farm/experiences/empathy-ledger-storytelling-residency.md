# Empathy Ledger Storytelling Residency

> We place storytellers on Country to practice the Empathy Ledger method at field scale — listening, consent, capture, and return. The residency produces stories that live in the Ledger, not content that feeds a marketing calendar.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-empathy-ledger-storytelling-residency | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

We run an embedded storytelling residency at [[black-cockatoo-valley|Black Cockatoo Valley]] for practitioners who want to work inside the [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] method — consent-first capture, cultural sensitivity, elder approval workflows, and return-to-community publishing.

This is not a content residency. Storytellers don't come here to extract footage for their own portfolios. They come to practice a specific way of working with story that treats the source as author and the sensitivity filter as infrastructure, not an afterthought.

## Who it's for

- Journalists, filmmakers, photographers, audio producers, and writers who want to practice ethical capture
- First Nations storytellers and practitioners from communities ACT works alongside
- Research-adjacent storytellers doing participatory or community-led work
- Storytelling teams inside partner organisations learning the EL method before deploying it on their own field

## What happens

A residency runs 1–4 weeks, set to match a real story the Ledger is chasing or a cohort the storyteller is embedded with.

- Induction on the Empathy Ledger method: consent model, cultural sensitivity filter, elder-approval workflow, `culturalTags` handling
- Field time with ACT work on or near BCV — residencies, Harvest sessions, partner visits
- Capture workflow running through the Ledger from day one — no side-channel content
- Editorial conversations with ACT and, where relevant, with the communities the stories belong to
- Return: the storyteller leaves with their craft sharpened and the Ledger leaves with a story set that actually got consent
