# Workshops at BCV

> A rolling program of short-form workshops on Country — land practice, making, writing, systems. Hands in the soil by morning, a real conversation by afternoon. Come for one, come for a series.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-workshops-at-bcv | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

We run workshops at [[black-cockatoo-valley|Black Cockatoo Valley]] as the everyday way people drop into ACT's practice. Half-day and full-day sessions, usually open to public enrolment, priced to be reachable. Some are taught by ACT staff, some by visiting practitioners, some in partnership with [[the-harvest|The Harvest]] across the two sites.

The workshops are how we keep a porous edge to the farm — places where people who aren't yet in a residency, retreat, or cohort program can still meet the land practice and the method.

## Who it's for

- Locals from Witta, Maleny, and the Sunshine Coast hinterland
- Makers, writers, practitioners looking for short-form land time
- Community groups running single-day offsites
- People thinking about a longer engagement (residency, cohort, retreat) who want to try the gate first

## What happens

Content varies. A running program includes:

- **Land practice** — regenerative ag, native planting, weed management, water systems
- **Making** — pottery, food preserving, textile practice, material work in partnership with [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]]
- **Writing and story** — story craft sessions, [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] method intros
- **Systems and facilitation** — short sessions for people who want to learn how we hold rooms

Workshops typically run 3–6 hours with a shared meal. Some are single sessions; others are 3–6 week series.
