# June's Patch Healthcare Retreat

> We run healthcare-worker retreats at Black Cockatoo Valley as an extension of the [[junes-patch|June's Patch]] practice — nature prescription at residential scale, grounded in soil, quiet, and shared meals. No disclosure required. The work is the rest.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-junes-patch-healthcare-retreat | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

June's Patch is ACT's network of therapeutic gardens for healthcare workers — nature prescription, evidence-backed, phased to community ownership. The retreat offer at BCV extends that practice from day-visit gardens to residential time on Country.

Healthcare workers come here for 2–5 nights. We hold the space quietly. There's no therapy program, no group work we expect people to opt into, no disclosure required. People garden if they want to garden. They walk. They eat. They sleep. That is the intervention.

## Who it's for

- Nurses, doctors, paramedics, allied health workers, midwives, aged-care staff
- Teams from healthcare organisations running peer restoration programs
- Clinicians carrying moral injury, burnout, or compassion fatigue who need somewhere that isn't a clinical environment
- Cohorts referred via partner health services using nature-prescription pathways

We're not running a medical intervention. Anyone who needs acute mental health care needs that, not this.

## What happens

- Arrival late afternoon, a light meal, an early night
- Mornings are slow — garden time, a walk, breakfast whenever it happens
- Afternoons might include optional practice sessions (horticulture, food preserving, quiet making) or might not
- Shared evening meals using produce from here and from [[the-harvest|The Harvest]]
- Optional peer circles — opt in, not opt out
- Departure with no homework

The design principle: do less than a traditional retreat. The land does the work if we get out of its way.
