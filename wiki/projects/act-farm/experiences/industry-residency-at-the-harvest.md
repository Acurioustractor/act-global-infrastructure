# Industry Residency at The Harvest

> We host industry residencies at [[the-harvest|The Harvest]] — extended placements where artists, designers and ACT work alongside a regional industry and its people. Not an artist-in-residence at a company. A community treating its own industry, land and elders as the material, with us holding the studio.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-industry-residency-at-the-harvest | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

An industry residency is not an artist residency. We run both at ACT — the [[experiences/artist-residency|Artist Residency at BCV]] places an artist on Country and the land is the commission. An industry residency at [[the-harvest|The Harvest]] starts from the other end: a regional industry, its producers, processors, elders, its disappearing knowledge. Artists and designers come in alongside ACT to build the artefacts, structures and conversations that make the industry visible to itself.

Where the artist residency asks *what can an artist make here*, the industry residency asks *what does this industry want to see of itself, and who needs to be in the room for that to happen*.

We run this out of The Harvest — the former Green Harvest site in Witta, Kabi Kabi and Jinibara Country — because the site has the ground, the standing infrastructure, the kitchen, and the public-facing hours that this kind of work needs. [[case-studies/radical-scoops-at-the-harvest|Radical Scoops]] — our second-project selection in Regional Arts Australia's Industry Residencies Pilot — is the proof this works.

## Who it's for

- **Regional industries** that want to understand themselves through creative practice rather than consultancy decks — agriculture, timber, dairy, hospitality, fibre, fisheries
- **Arts-and-industry funding programs** — Regional Arts Australia, ANAT, Creative Australia, state arts bodies, regional arts development funds
- **Artists and designers** whose practice sits at the intersection of material, industry, community, and documentation
- **Philanthropic partners** looking for a placed, durable way into regional economic resilience
- **Communities** that want to commission work about their own unspoken histories — elders, industries, land changes, knowledge layers that were never asked about

We're not the right fit for activations, brand residencies, or short-form "artists visit industry" programs. An industry residency here is measured in months to years, not weeks.

## What happens

Each residency is shaped to its industry and community, but the backbone is shared:

- **A leased footprint.** We anchor the residency on a physical site at The Harvest — studio, workshop, pop-up spaces — held for the duration so the work can settle.
- **An industry proposition.** We identify the local industry, its producers, its unspoken knowledge, and the communities connected through it. This is not abstract — it's the dairy farmer across the road, the oyster farmer up the coast, the timber cutter who worked the region sixty years ago.
- **A circular-economy brief.** Every structure, every artefact, every output is designed with its next life specified. Temporary buildings become beds or housing for remote communities. Shells become paths. Non-permanence is a design constraint, not a slogan.
- **A documentation discipline.** Photography, audio, writing, [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] story capture — with consent — runs from day one. The residency produces a knowledge layer alongside the physical work.
- **Public-facing pop-ups and convenings** that bring the industry and community into the same room over the residency's duration, rather than staging a single exhibition at the end.
- **An adjacent capacity phase.** The residency funds delivery. We apply in parallel for capacity funding (governance, digital systems, volunteer pathways, replication toolkit) so the model doesn't end when the program money ends. This is how a one-site residency becomes a pattern other regions can adapt.

## Price, duration, capacity

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Duration** | 6–12 months on site minimum; Capacity Lab follow-on typically 12 months |
| **Capacity** | One industry proposition at a time per site; multiple artists/collaborators embedded |
| **Funding model** | Primarily grant-funded (RAA Industry Residencies, ANAT, state arts bodies, philanthropic partners); co-contributions from industry and community welcomed |
| **Cost structure** | Tiered — fully funded placements through program partners; co-designed with philanthropic or industry partners; ACT does not run a published rack rate |
| **Includes** | Site access at The Harvest, ACT co-production and governance, documentation discipline, ecosystem integration with [[black-cockatoo-valley|BCV]] and [[experiences/harvest-pairings|Harvest Pairings]] |
| **Doesn't include** | Artist fees (budgeted per residency), industry-partner time, participant travel |
| **Season** | Year-round, with seasonality baked into the program design (food, harvest cycles, industry rhythms) |

Pricing is genuinely per-residency. Bring a funded program, or come to us with an industry and a question; we'll help design the funding case.

## How to inquire

By conversation, not application round. Tell us the industry you want to work with, the community or place it connects to, the funding context you're working inside (or need help to build), and the artists or practitioners you'd want embedded. We reply within a few working days and usually run two or three calls before any proposal is drafted. Inquiries go through `/experiences/industry-residency-at-the-harvest` on the ACT Farm site.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], an Industry Residency at The Harvest extends the method across months and years — Listen → Curiosity → Action → Art applied to a regional industry rather than an artist's question. Here's how the four phases show up:

- **Listen** — to the local industry, its producers, processors, elders, and the disappearing knowledge layer; conversations with the dairy farmer across the road, the oyster farmer up the coast, the timber cutter who worked the region sixty years ago
- **Curiosity** — what does this industry want to see of itself, and who needs to be in the room for that to happen? An industry proposition co-shaped with community
- **Action** — leased footprint at The Harvest; circular-economy brief where every structure has its next life specified; documentation discipline running from day one; public-facing pop-ups and convenings rather than a single closing exhibition
- **Art** — the artefacts and structures the residency produces; the knowledge layer captured through [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]]; the Capacity Lab follow-on that turns a one-site residency into a pattern other regions can adapt

Where the [[experiences/artist-residency|artist residency]] asks *what can an artist make here*, the industry residency asks *what does this industry want to see of itself, and who needs to be in the room for that to happen*.
