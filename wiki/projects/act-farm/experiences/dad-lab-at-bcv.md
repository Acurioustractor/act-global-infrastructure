# DAD.LAB at BCV

> We host DAD.LAB intensives at Black Cockatoo Valley — residential time for fathers and men navigating health, identity, and connection, grounded in peer support and done in the bush. Not clinical. Not confessional. Just honest.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-FM-EXP-dad-lab-at-bcv | **Cluster:** [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]

## What it is

[[dad-lab-25|Dad.Lab.25]] is ACT's community-led men's health initiative for fathers. DAD.LAB at BCV is the residential strand: 2–3 day intensives on Country where the peer-support model gets room to breathe.

We run these because men's health — particularly for fathers — keeps failing the same way inside clinical and institutional frameworks. What works is peer connection and time, in an environment that doesn't feel like a workshop room. BCV is that environment.

## Who it's for

- Fathers across life stages — new dads, teenagers' fathers, grandfathers, step-dads
- Men's peer groups already meeting in their communities who want residential time together
- Partner organisations running fatherhood programs who need a retreat layer
- Individual fathers referred or self-referring, who want honest time with other men doing the same inquiry

This is not a therapy intensive. Men who need clinical support should access that in parallel.

## What happens

- Arrival, shared meal, a fire that first evening
- Mornings: work on the land — physical, shared, simple
- Afternoons: peer sessions, optional. Structured enough to go somewhere, open enough to breathe
- Evenings: food, fire, the kind of conversation that only shows up once you stop trying to have it
- A closing on the last morning — what each man is taking back

The design principle is Dad.Lab.25's: community-led, culturally appropriate, non-clinical. ACT hosts; the men do the work.
