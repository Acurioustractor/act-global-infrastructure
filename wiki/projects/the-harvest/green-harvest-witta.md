# Green Harvest Witta

> The bones of a nursery that became The Harvest — a specific piece of land, a specific man, and the archaeology of what working country looks like before someone turns it into a scenic drive.

**Status:** Active (as The Harvest) | **Code:** ACT-HV | **Tier:** Ecosystem

## The Property

Green Harvest Witta is the former organic seed and gardening business that operated at the Witta, QLD site (postcode 4552) that now houses [[the-harvest|The Harvest]]. The property sits on Jinibara Country in the Sunshine Coast hinterland — two thousand cars pass through Witta every weekend on their way somewhere else.

The Witta Farm property is held in Nicholas Marchesi's family trust and leased to ACT, making it both the physical home of The Harvest and a foundational commitment from one of ACT's co-founders. The site retains its Green Harvest infrastructure: shade cloth frames, irrigation lines, propagation benches. You don't build these things twice. You repair them.

## [[barry-rodgerig|Barry Rodgerig]] and the Archaeology of the Shed

The most important figure in understanding this property is not ACT. It is Barry Rodgerig — 80 years old, on this land since 1972. Before that, Peachester. Before that, 25 years of dairy, timber, and red soil. Barry drove tractors before he could see over the steering wheel.

His shed is full of machines that built the Sunshine Coast hinterland: an AB184 log truck from 1963, ex-army Blitz trucks, a little Italian Valpadana with a Lombardini diesel, a 1957 Land Rover he'd like to restore "if I live long enough." He starts them up sometimes, just to hear them run.

> *"Rust is a terrible thing. It's just like cancer in humans."*

Barry watched the hippies shut down the logging at Crystal Waters. He watched the government finish the job. He watched the hinterland turn from working country into scenic drive. His shed is archaeology — the living memory of what this place was.

Nicholas Marchesi's February 2026 Compendium document (see [[../research/harvest-property-arc|Harvest Property Arc]]) spends significant time with Barry before proposing a single building project. This is not background detail. It is LCAA Listen phase made visible: every place has a story already being told. The Harvest is built on ground that remembers something.

## Phase 1 Commitments

The lease between ACT and Grant (the property owner) was finalised on 16 February 2026, following the Compendium's vision. Phase 1 total: **$117,800**.

| Milestone | Amount | Timing | Scope |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------|
| 1 | $40,000 | Feb 2026 | Site establishment, community garden works commenced, access and amenities baseline |
| 2 | $33,900 | Apr–Jun 2026 | Outdoor builds — pavilion, timber/fire cooking, kids area |
| 3 | $33,900 | May–Jun 2026 | Phase 1 soft opening readiness |

Phase 2 total: $132,200. Staged approach confirmed by Grant, who pushed two constraints: anchor points (locals need a reason to come regularly, not just for one-off events) and simplicity first (coffee and straightforward food before scaling the program).

## The Lease Structure

Rather than a standard commercial lease, ACT proposed **revenue share** — a performance-based component up to an additional $50K/year — so the property owner shares in the upside. If The Harvest thrives, Grant benefits. This is ACT's commons model applied to a lease negotiation: shared skin in the game.

The initial proposal was $50,000 upfront to secure the space. The final Phase 1 total of $117,800 reflects the expanded scope agreed through the February 2026 negotiation.

## What This Place Was and What It Becomes

The former Green Harvest business — an organic seed and gardening operation — left the physical infrastructure that The Harvest is now inheriting: the nursery bones, the irrigation, the shade cloth. Witta has the highest homeschooling rate in Australia and no learning infrastructure. The garden-as-classroom is not a feature added for brand appeal; it is a direct response to what the listening revealed.

The property gives ACT something it hadn't had before: ground to stand on. Not metaphorically — literally. The February 2026 internal preparation meeting contained this sentence: *"What does having a property look like for ACT? $50,000 on the table and revenue share."* That sentence marks the shift from ACT as a mobile design-and-facilitation practice to ACT as a place-based organisation.
