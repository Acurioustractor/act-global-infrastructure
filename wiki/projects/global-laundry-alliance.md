# Global Laundry Alliance

> A dignity-centred international network connecting community laundry hubs — advocating for policy change that recognises clean clothing and access to basic hygiene as a matter of human dignity, not charity.

**Status:** Transferred | **Code:** ACT-GL | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

The Global Laundry Alliance grows from one of the most unexpected and powerful social innovations of recent Australian history: Orange Sky Laundry, co-founded by ACT co-founder [[nicholas-marchesi|Nicholas Marchesi]] OAM. Orange Sky demonstrated that a mobile laundry service for people experiencing homelessness was not primarily about clean clothes — it was about dignity, belonging, and the five-minute conversation that happened while the washing was on.

The Global Laundry Alliance takes that insight international: building a network of organisations running community laundry hubs worldwide, sharing knowledge and practice, and collectively advocating for the policy conditions that make this work sustainable and scalable.

## Origin: Orange Sky

Nicholas Marchesi co-founded Orange Sky Laundry in 2014 at age 20. What began as two friends with a borrowed washing machine and a van grew into a national service reaching tens of thousands of people experiencing homelessness across Australia and New Zealand. The insight at the heart of Orange Sky — that the laundry is a pretext for community — became foundational to how ACT thinks about service design more broadly.

See [[nicholas-marchesi|Nicholas Marchesi]] for the full founding story.

## The Alliance Model

### Community Hubs
The laundry hub is not just a utility — it is a community gathering point. The physical act of waiting together, of having the laundry done for you without judgment, creates conditions for trust, conversation, and connection that more formal services struggle to generate.

### Global Network
By connecting organisations running community laundry programs worldwide, the Alliance enables knowledge exchange, shared innovation, and collective advocacy that individual organisations cannot achieve alone. What works in Brisbane, London, or Nairobi can inform practice everywhere.

### Policy Change
The Alliance's advocacy work pushes toward systemic recognition: that access to clean clothing and basic hygiene is not a luxury or a charitable extra but a dimension of human dignity that public systems should support. This includes lobbying for funding models, public infrastructure, and social policy that treats hygiene as a right.

### Dignity as the Frame
Every element of the Alliance's work is oriented by dignity — not gratitude, not charity, not the transaction of service delivery, but the unconditional recognition of every person's inherent worth. This is the legacy of Orange Sky and the common thread connecting all member organisations.

## LCAA Phase

**Action** — The Alliance is network-building, hub-connecting, and policy-advocating. The listening happened in the years of Orange Sky operations that demonstrated what people actually need. The Global Laundry Alliance is the systemic action that follows.
