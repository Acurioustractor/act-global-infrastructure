# Cars and Microcontrollers

> Making things with your hands is not a hobby. For some young people, it's the first time their intelligence has been recognised.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-MC | **Tier:** Studio

## What It Is

Cars and Microcontrollers is an ACT project at the intersection of maker culture, electronics, and hands-on learning. The project puts young people in contact with physical machines — cars, engines, circuits, microcontrollers — and treats the work of understanding and building those machines as a legitimate site of learning, identity, and creative practice.

## The Logic Behind the Project

ACT returns consistently to one insight: that engagement follows relevance, and relevance in marginalised communities often looks like practical things that work.

A microcontroller is a small programmable chip embedded in everything from cars to washing machines to Arduino projects. Learning to wire one, program it, and watch it control something physical is — for a young person who has never been told they're the kind of person who does that — a significant event. Not because of the technology. Because of what it proves about them.

Cars occupy a different register. In many communities where ACT works — remote NT, outer suburban Queensland, Western Sydney — mechanical knowledge is intergenerational, practical, and status-bearing. Knowing how engines work is not abstract. It connects to work, to independence, to the skills of fathers and uncles and older brothers. Cars and Microcontrollers plants one foot in that world and extends the other into electronics and computational thinking.

## Relationship to ACT's Technology Thesis

Microcontrollers — the Arduino, the Raspberry Pi, the ESP32 — are the entry points into the physical computing world. They sit at the border between digital and physical: code that makes things move, sense, respond. For ACT, which builds technology platforms (JusticeHub, Empathy Ledger, CivicGraph) alongside community programs, a project that introduces young people to this same layer is meaningful beyond its immediate community impact.

The long-term question Cars and Microcontrollers implicitly poses: what if the young people in the program eventually build the technology that helps their own communities? This isn't a stated outcome — it's an aspiration encoded in the design.
