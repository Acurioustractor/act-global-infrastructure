# ACT Public Voice

> How A Curious Tractor describes itself to the world — in the words chosen for act.place.

**Sources:** act.place (home, about, about-new, action), captured 2026-04-07
**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-MISC

## The Farm as Public Metaphor

The public website's central metaphor is the farm — the same one used internally. Remarkably, act.place publishes the full internal glossary of farm metaphors as the "about" page. The Soil (knowledge network), The Seeds (active projects), The Campfire (conversation and ceremony), The Tractor (engine + PTO) — all public. ACT has chosen radical transparency about its internal operating system as a brand identity choice.

This is unusual. Most organisations keep their operational metaphors internal. ACT's bet is that publishing the metaphor system makes the work more legible, not more vulnerable.

## What ACT Calls Itself (Publicly)

The about-new page (the current, actively maintained About) defines ACT as:

> A Curious Tractor (ACT) fosters co-creation between communities impacted by injustice and those who hold resources, skills, or influence. We provide a space for deep listening, radical collaboration, and iterative prototyping, ensuring that solutions are rigorously tested and refined based on the lived experiences of those they are meant to serve, resulting in shared ownership and genuine impact.

This is more operational than the internal identity (LCAA, dual-entity, Third Reality framework). The public framing emphasises **co-creation** and **shared ownership** — not ACT's unique methodology.

## Four Public Values

Stated explicitly on the about-new page:

| Value | Statement |
|-------|-----------|
| **Radical Humility** | We listen, resource, and step back, prioritising community-identified needs. |
| **Decentralised Power** | We build systems that communities own, adapt, and govern. |
| **Creativity as Disruption** | We use art and storytelling to challenge apathy and centre community voices. |
| **Uncomfortable Truth-Telling** | We openly critique systems of oppression, including our own role. |

"Uncomfortable Truth-Telling" and the explicit self-critique ("including our own role") is notably rare for an organisation's public values page. It reads as genuine rather than performative.

## The Tractor Origin (Publicly Told)

The about page answers "What's the deal with tractors?" with a story about Nic riding tractors at the Witta farm:

> "Nic's tractor rides aren't just about plowing fields — they're journeys into the uncharted territories of ideas. With the wind on his face and the earth beneath his wheels, he finds a symphony of inspiration that only the rolling hills can provide."

The PTO (Power Take-Off) metaphor — how a tractor shares energy with external tools — is the core brand metaphor: ACT as the engine that powers others' work.

## Divergence: Public vs Internal

| Dimension | Internal (wiki) | Public (act.place) |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Identity framework | LCAA, Third Reality, dual-entity | Co-creation, shared ownership |
| Methodology name | LCAA, ALMA | Not named publicly |
| Primary projects | 7 core + satellites | Shows ~8 "seeds", not a fixed number |
| Founders named | Benjamin Knight + Nicholas Marchesi | Benjamin Knight (about-new), Nicholas Marchesi (farming, tractor stories) |
| "CivicGraph" | Core platform | Not mentioned on act.place |
| "Empathy Ledger" | Core platform | Listed as project under "seeds" |
| R&D, tax, finance | Rich internal docs | Absent from public site |

The most significant gap: **CivicGraph is not mentioned on act.place**. The organisation's largest data infrastructure project — 587K entities, $74B contracts tracked — has no presence on the parent website. Anyone researching ACT from the outside would not know it exists.
