# Place-Based Policy Lab

> An evidence-based, participatory research initiative co-designing community-led policy from the ground up — where expertise lives in the community, not the think tank.

## Overview

The Place-Based Policy Lab operates from a fundamental challenge to how policy is typically made: policy designed at a distance from the communities it affects tends to reproduce the same structural conditions that created the problems it claims to solve. The Lab inverts this — starting with community-held knowledge, using participatory research methods to surface and systematise that knowledge, and building evidence bases that communities own and can deploy.

"Place-based" is the operative commitment: policy designed for a specific place, with the people of that place, grounded in the particular history, ecology, economy, and social fabric of that location. Generic policy applied universally fails communities whose needs don't fit the template.

## Methodology

### Participatory Research
Community members are not research subjects or consultation participants — they are co-researchers. The Lab works with communities to design the questions, gather the data, interpret the findings, and communicate the recommendations. This is research as capacity-building, not extraction.

### Evidence-Based Practice
The Lab builds rigorous evidence bases, not just advocacy documents. This matters because policy change requires navigating institutional and bureaucratic environments that respond to evidence in particular forms. The Lab learns those forms and helps communities speak them fluently — without ceding ownership of the underlying knowledge.

### Community-Led Policy
The end goal is policy that communities have authored and own — not policy that was designed for them, however well-intentioned. This means the Lab's role is facilitative rather than directive.

## LCAA Phase

**Curiosity** — The Lab's primary mode is systematic inquiry: what do we actually know about this place, this problem, this community? What does the evidence say, and whose evidence counts? This inquiry phase is not passive — it is action-oriented curiosity that builds toward policy change.
