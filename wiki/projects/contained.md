# CONTAINED

> An experiential design campaign about youth detention — placing people inside the question of what we're actually paying for, and what we could choose instead.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-CN | **Tier:** Satellite

## What It Is

CONTAINED is ACT's immersive advocacy campaign at the intersection of experiential design and youth justice reform. It takes the evidence that sits inside [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] — the dry cost comparisons, the recidivism rates, the funding breakdowns — and transforms it into a felt experience. Something people walk through, not just read about.

The central provocation: **what if we spent $1.55M on keeping a child connected to family, culture, and community instead of locking them away?**

CONTAINED isn't a report. It isn't a petition. It's a designed environment that makes the economic and human cost of youth incarceration visceral and undeniable — then leaves people with the evidence that something better is not only possible, but already working.

## The Evidence Behind It

The numbers that power CONTAINED come directly from [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] data and national justice spending records:

| Approach | Annual Cost per Child | Success Rate |
|----------|-----------------------|-------------|
| Youth detention | $1.1–1.3 million ($3,320/day) | 15.5% |
| Community-led alternatives | ~$58,000 ($150/day) | 78% |

This isn't abstracted — it's drawn from 145,242 justice funding records and 570 evidence items in ALMA (Australian Living Map of Alternatives) tracked in [[civicgraph|CivicGraph]]. CONTAINED makes that data walk off the screen and into the room.

## The Form

CONTAINED takes a **touring exhibition format** — a physical installation that can be licensed and deployed by partner organisations. Venues rent the installation; it arrives, gets set up, runs for a season, and moves on.

The signature element is a **chalk question wall**: visitors are prompted with the incarceration cost figure, then invited to write their own answer to the question *"What could $1.55M do instead?"* The wall photographs — communities writing their own futures — become some of the most shared content the campaign generates.

At [[the-harvest|The Harvest Witta]], a permanent installation (the "Contained Wall") is planned as a standing fixture: a recurring provocation in a community space that changes and breathes.

The revenue model positions CONTAINED not as a cost centre but as a self-sustaining advocacy tool: the exhibition pays its own way while shifting the conversation in rooms that matter.

## Connection to JusticeHub

CONTAINED is the **campaign layer** of JusticeHub — the Art phase to JusticeHub's evidence-building Listen, Curiosity, and Action phases.

JusticeHub documents that:
- 1,004+ evidence-based community programs are already operating across Australia
- Programs like BG Fit (Mount Isa) reduced youth police contact from 5 incidents to under 1
- Oonchiumpa (Alice Springs) achieved 95% reduction in anti-social behaviour
- Confit Pathways (Sydney) delivered 60% reduction in recidivism

CONTAINED takes these proof points and the cost comparison data and turns them into the argument that audiences feel before they intellectualise. It answers: *why should someone who doesn't follow justice policy statistics care?*

## Connection to Empathy Ledger

[[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] is the consent-first storytelling platform that underpins CONTAINED's narrative layer. Where JusticeHub provides the systemic data, Empathy Ledger provides the human stories — lived experience captured with community consent and data sovereignty.

Stories gathered through Empathy Ledger can be woven into the CONTAINED installation: the person behind the statistic, the family alongside the cost figure. This is the core ACT methodology — evidence and story together create the conditions for empathy that leads to action.

## Australian Youth Justice Context

Australia's youth justice system is under sustained pressure. Key facts that frame CONTAINED:

- Youth detention costs $1.1–1.3 million per child per year, one of the highest rates in the developed world
- Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander young people are dramatically overrepresented — comprising roughly 60% of those in detention despite being ~6% of the youth population
- The 1991 Royal Commission into Aboriginal Deaths in Custody made 339 recommendations; most remain unimplemented
- Queensland, Northern Territory, and Western Australia have the worst detention rates and the worst overrepresentation of First Nations youth
- Community-controlled programs (see [[acco-sector-analysis|ACCO Sector Analysis]]) consistently outperform detention on every measurable outcome

CONTAINED enters this context as a public-facing advocacy intervention — designed to shift the frame from "law and order" to "what actually works."

## LCAA Phase: Art

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], CONTAINED sits squarely in the **Art** phase — the phase where evidence becomes experience, where data becomes story, where policy becomes something a person can feel in their body.

LCAA arc for CONTAINED:
- **Listen** — JusticeHub evidence-gathering; community testimony; ALMA mapping
- **Curiosity** — What does $1.1M/year actually look like? What are we choosing?
- **Action** — Divert funding; fund community programs; policy advocacy
- **Art** — CONTAINED installation; the chalk wall; the exhibition that travels

The Art phase is not the final step — it's the re-entry point. People who walk through CONTAINED are re-primed for Listen. The cycle continues.
