## Overview

Custodian Economy is ACT's exploration of economic models grounded in custodianship rather than ownership. The project asks a fundamental question: what does an economy look like when its participants see themselves as temporary stewards of resources, land, and knowledge rather than extractive owners?

This work draws on First Nations concepts of custodianship of Country, blended with contemporary thinking about [[community-capital|community capital]], regenerative economics, and mission-locked enterprise structures. It informs the dual-entity architecture that ACT itself uses (Foundation for charitable work, Ventures for mission-locked trading) and the broader ecosystem's approach to value creation that doesn't require value extraction.

## Themes

### Beyond Ownership

The dominant economic paradigm assumes ownership as the foundation of value. Custodian Economy challenges this by modelling alternatives where:

- Land is held in trust for future generations rather than speculated upon
- Knowledge is shared as commons rather than locked behind intellectual property
- Enterprise generates surplus for community benefit rather than shareholder extraction
- Success is measured by what you leave behind, not what you accumulate

### First Nations Foundations

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples have practised custodial economics for tens of thousands of years. The project doesn't appropriate these practices but learns from them, always in partnership and with proper protocols. The concept of caring for Country, where humans are embedded in reciprocal relationships with land, water, and other species, offers a living counter-model to extractive capitalism.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], Custodian Economy sits primarily in the **Curiosity** phase — it is the conceptual layer that asks what an economy looks like when participants see themselves as temporary stewards rather than extractive owners. It does not deliver a program directly; it shapes how ACT designs the programs it does deliver.

LCAA arc for Custodian Economy:
- **Listen** — to First Nations practice of custodianship of Country as a living, tens-of-thousands-of-years-old counter-model to extractive capitalism; learning in partnership rather than appropriating
- **Curiosity** — the central question: what does an economy look like when land is held in trust, knowledge is commons, enterprise generates surplus for community benefit, and success is measured by what you leave behind?
- **Action** — informs ACT's dual-entity architecture (Foundation for charitable purpose, Ventures for mission-locked trading); shapes [[black-cockatoo-valley|Black Cockatoo Valley]] as conservation-first land holding; underpins [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]]'s place-based supply chains and [[community-capital|Community Capital]]'s alternative capital structures
- **Art** — frames [[designing-for-obsolescence|Designing for Obsolescence]] as cultural practice — building organisations that hand power over rather than accumulate it

Custodian Economy is the principle that other ACT projects then enact in specific places.
