# Marriage Celebrant

> Ben Knight's civil marriage celebrant practice, connecting ceremony with community.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-CB | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

Marriage Celebrant is [[benjamin-knight|Benjamin Knight]]'s registered civil marriage celebrant practice. Operating as a satellite project within the [[act-ecosystem|ACT ecosystem]], it brings the same care for storytelling and human connection that runs through ACT's other work into the ceremony space.

Ben is a Commonwealth-registered civil celebrant. The practice sits outside ACT's core project portfolio but draws on the same foundations: deep listening, narrative craft, and the belief that gathering people around a moment of commitment is itself an act of social infrastructure.

## How It Works

Each ceremony is approached as a story worth telling well, with attention to the couple's own language, culture, and context rather than templated scripts. The process typically involves extended conversations with the couple to understand not just the logistics of the day but the shape of their relationship, the people who matter to them, and the tone they want to set.

The result is a ceremony that sounds like the couple, not like a celebrant. That same discipline of listening before composing is what drives ACT's broader work through the [[../concepts/lcaa-method|LCAA method]]: Listen, Curiosity, Action, Art.
