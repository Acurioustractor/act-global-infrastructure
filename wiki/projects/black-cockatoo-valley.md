# Black Cockatoo Valley

> A 150-acre property on Jinibara Country near Witta, Queensland. Conservation-first. The land sets the pace, not the calendar.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-BV | **Tier:** Satellite | **Country:** Jinibara

## Overview

Black Cockatoo Valley (BCV) is ACT's land-practice site. The work begins from a single rule: the land is the teacher and the limiter. Conservation-first is the baseline, always. When the creek is low or the grass is tired, the calendar yields. Capacity is a land decision before it is a business decision.

BCV is the quiet anchor of the ACT ecosystem. It is where residencies, workshops, regenerative R&D, and habitat restoration happen in a rhythm the property itself permits. Revenue from residencies and workshops is reinvested in restoration, weed management, and monitoring. No extractive tourism. Limited-volume operations. Long-term stewardship in community.

## Key Details

- **Country:** Jinibara
- **Location:** Near Witta, Queensland
- **Size:** 150 acres
- **Focus:** Conservation, land practice, R&D, residencies
- **Model:** Revenue reinvested into restoration and stewardship

## Land Practice Commitments

| Commitment | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Low-impact activity | No extractive tourism; limited-volume operations |
| Revenue reinvested | Residencies and workshops fund restoration, weed management, monitoring |
| Long-term stewardship | Community co-stewardship; [[beautiful-obsolescence|Beautiful Obsolescence]] as the goal |
| Conservation boundaries | Scale to land capacity and community readiness, not to demand |

## Relationship to The Harvest and ACT Farm

BCV sits inside the Place cluster alongside [[the-harvest|The Harvest Witta]] and [[act-farm|ACT Farm]]. The Valley is the conservation land. The Harvest is the small enterprise hub in Witta town — an old nursery repurposed for local markets and workshops. The two sites work together but carry different logics: BCV answers to land capacity; The Harvest answers to local enterprise rhythms.

## 2026 Focus

1. Protect land capacity — conservation-first guardrails maintained
2. Run The Harvest in Witta as a small, practical enterprise hub
3. Push [[goods|Goods on Country]] toward manufacturing and operational maturity
4. Treat stories and technology as core instruments of stewardship, not add-ons

Land practice remains central alongside innovation and enterprise. Every project should explain how it strengthens land practice.

## LCAA Phase

**Listen** — the land speaks first, and the practice is built from what it says. Curiosity follows in the form of restoration experiments and residency-led inquiry. Action is the weed management, the creek walks, the slow repair. Art lives in the stewardship itself.
