# Empathy Ledger Annual Field Service

> Y1 approach: bespoke engagement at ~$50K/year per org with four existing customers (JCF, PICC, Buttery, SMART Recovery). Learn what each org actually buys, then productise in Y2 based on real patterns.

This is NOT a three-tier product page. It is a scoping template for bespoke annual agreements that share a common backbone but flex per customer. The productised version comes after 12 months of delivery evidence.

## Common backbone (every engagement includes)

1. **Live Resonance Field** — the org's `/organisations/{slug}/observatory` page stays active with portrait-derived particle palettes, spiral resonance clustering, and real storyteller faces. Updated as new transcripts are ingested.
2. **Monthly editorial pass** — new quotes reviewed and cleaned via the MiniMax-M2.7 editorial pipeline. Cultural review gate enforced. Ben approves batches, not individual items.
3. **Annual Impact Atlas** — generated from the Resonance Field data. PDF + interactive web version. Includes spiral resonance profile, impact dimensions, top quotes, cultural markers, storyteller count, timeline.
4. **Storytelling infrastructure** — voice interview capture (Whisper transcription + Claude analysis), consent envelope per storyteller, storage in [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] with OCAP/CARE protocols.
5. **Cultural review gate** — maintained by ACT. No storyteller content goes public without consent and cultural protocol check.

## Y1 revenue target

4 customers × ~$50K average = **$200K annual recurring** (bespoke, not productised).

## What we learn in Y1 that informs Y2 productisation

Track these patterns across all four engagements:

1. **Which backbone elements do every customer actually use** (vs which do they ignore)?
2. **What's the real editorial workload per org per month** (hours, not estimates)?
3. **What artefact format do funders/boards actually respond to** (PDF atlas? live embed? printed book? video?)?
4. **What's the real cultural review overhead** (how many reviews, how many rejections, how long)?
5. **What would a customer pay 2x for** (the thing they'd upgrade without being asked)?
6. **What would make a customer refer another org** (the "hero moment" they tell their network about)?

Answers to these six questions define the Y2 productised tiers.
