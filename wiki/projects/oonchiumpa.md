# Oonchiumpa

> Aboriginal-led youth program in Mparntwe (Alice Springs) — grounding young people in culture, Country, and identity across seven language groups of Central Australia.

**Status:** Active | **Code:** ACT-OO | **Tier:** Satellite

## Overview

Oonchiumpa is a 100% Aboriginal-led youth civic agency based in Mparntwe (Alice Springs, NT), working across homeland communities within a 150km radius. The program serves young people from seven language groups — including Arrernte, Alyawarra, Luritja, and Warlpiri — connecting them to Country, culture, and civic agency through mentorship, on-Country healing, and cross-community exchange.

The name and program operate under the cultural authority of Aunty Bev and Uncle Terry. The central site is Atnarpa homestead on Loves Creek Station — the Bloomfield family's reclaimed ancestral Country.

Oonchiumpa is one of ACT's two primary community partnerships alongside [[picc|Palm Island Community Company (PICC)]]. It is also a key proof point within [[justicehub|JusticeHub]] for the effectiveness of community-led alternatives to youth detention.

## Programs

### Cultural Camps at Atnarpa
On-Country healing at the Bloomfield family's homestead on Loves Creek Station. Cultural camps connect youth to Country, language, and identity across Central Australian homelands under Elder guidance from Aunty Bev and Uncle Terry.

### True Justice (with ANU)
A program run in partnership with the Australian National University (ANU) that transforms law students through deep listening on Country. Law students come to Mparntwe and participate in structured learning with community leaders — building institutional understanding of community-led justice while generating research documentation.

### Youth Mentorship
21 active young people in programs designed by community. Mentorship is intergenerational and culturally grounded — not a Western service model, but a relational process rooted in obligation to Country and kin.

### Cross-Community Exchange
Oonchiumpa participates in cross-community exchange with [[picc|Palm Island Community Company]] and other First Nations programs across Queensland and the Northern Territory — including the ILA Grant knowledge exchange hosted at Atnarpa homestead.

## ACT's Role

ACT's relationship with Oonchiumpa spans several workstreams:

### Empathy Ledger Deployment
Oonchiumpa is one of the two primary deployment sites for [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]] — ACT's consent-first storytelling platform with sovereign data control. Oonchiumpa leaders use it to document youth mentorship and on-Country healing across seven language groups, with data fully under community control.

### JusticeHub Evidence
Oonchiumpa's outcomes are featured as a local proof point in JusticeHub's evidence base — alongside BG Fit (Mount Isa) and Confit Pathways (Sydney) — demonstrating the 78% average success rate of community programs versus 15.5% for detention.

### Goods on Country Manufacturing
A two-year co-design process between ACT and Oonchiumpa explored an Alice Springs production facility for [[goods-on-country|Goods on Country]] containerised manufacturing. The partnership is central to the REAL Innovation Fund EOI (submitted March 2025 to DEWR), seeking $1.2M over four years for a production facility pathway.

### R&D Tax (Supporting Activities)
Oonchiumpa is coded as project `ACT-OO` in ACT's financial systems. International research into social enterprise models (Portugal/Spain) conducted in relation to Oonchiumpa is classified as supporting R&D activity under ACT's R&D tax incentive claim.

## Place: Mparntwe (Alice Springs)

Mparntwe is the Arrernte name for Alice Springs, in the Northern Territory. It is the traditional Country of the Central and Eastern Arrernte peoples. The town sits at the geographic centre of Australia and is surrounded by the homeland communities that Oonchiumpa serves.

Atnarpa homestead (Loves Creek Station) is approximately 80km from Alice Springs — Bloomfield family's reclaimed ancestral Country, used as the base for cultural camps.

## LCAA Phase

**Action** — Oonchiumpa embodies the Action phase of the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]]: young people are walked into their identity and responsibility through culture, on Country, under Elder authority. The program is itself the methodology — not a service delivered to a community, but a living practice of cultural sovereignty.

## Connection to The Third Reality

Oonchiumpa is a primary evidence case for [[third-reality|The Third Reality]] — ACT's framework for proving that community-led alternatives to the current system produce better outcomes at lower cost.

The 95% reduction in anti-social behavior is the kind of result that makes the economic and moral case simultaneously: Oonchiumpa achieves outcomes that the $1.3M-per-year youth detention system cannot, at a fraction of the cost, while restoring culture rather than destroying it.
