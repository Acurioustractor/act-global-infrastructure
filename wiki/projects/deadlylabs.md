# DeadlyLabs

> STEM education meets youth detention — where sports science converges with cultural identity to redefine futures.

**Status:** Partner program | **Partner:** DeadlyScience

## Overview

DeadlyLabs is a project of DeadlyScience, an Indigenous-led initiative founded in 2019 by Corey Tutt (Kamilaroi man) to make STEM education accessible for remote Indigenous communities. The "Kickin' It" program brings sports science into youth detention centres, engaging detained young people as co-creators of STEM kits.

The partnership with Acmena Youth Justice Centre allows detained youth to become innovators — infusing their perspectives into STEM kits that celebrate the scientific legacy of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people.

## Corey Tutt

Corey Tutt, a Kamilaroi man, founded DeadlyScience with a vision to intertwine Indigenous children with their scientific heritage. His accolades include NSW Young Australian of the Year and the Eureka Prize for STEM Inclusion. DeadlyScience began as a dream and has blossomed into a national movement.

## The "Kickin' It" Program

### Activities
- **Biomechanics of Rugby** — applying physics (force, motion) to understand and enhance gameplay
- **Sports Nutrition Workshops** — biology of nutrition and its role in athletic performance
- **Mathematics in Game Strategy** — mathematical concepts applied to real-time rugby league decision-making

### Impact
The program addresses a pressing need: Indigenous youth comprise a substantial segment of those in detention centres. "Kickin' It" champions the belief that science and education serve as transformative forces.

> "Before Kickin' It, I didn't see the value in education. Now, I'm not just engaged; I'm excited about what I can learn and achieve." — Program Participant

> "I never saw the connection between the sports I love and science. Kickin' It changed that, making me see possibilities I never knew existed." — Participant, Acmena Youth Detention Centre

## Approach

DeadlyLabs represents a model that ACT advocates for in [[justicehub|JusticeHub]]: programs that meet young people where they are (in detention), build on their existing interests (sport), connect to cultural identity (Indigenous scientific heritage), and position them as creators rather than recipients.

## ACT Connection

DeadlyLabs is featured in ACT storytelling as evidence that creative, culturally-grounded programming in detention settings produces engagement and transformation that compliance-based approaches cannot. The program aligns with the [[../concepts/ai-community-engagement|AI and community engagement]] thesis — freeing practitioners from admin to focus on the relational and creative work that drives real change.
