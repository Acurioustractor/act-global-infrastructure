# PICC Centre Precinct

> A building is not community infrastructure. A community-controlled building is. The difference is who holds the keys.

**Status:** Active | **Canonical code:** `ACT-PI` | **Legacy PICC internal code:** `ACT-PC` | **Tier:** Studio
**Parent project:** [[picc|PICC (Palm Island Community Company)]]

## What It Is

The PICC Centre Precinct is the physical community centre on Palm Island — the hub where [[picc|PICC]]'s integrated service delivery operates. It covers fit-out, maintenance, utilities, and operational costs for the building that houses health services, family programs, employment support, and cultural activities under one roof.

This is not a government building managed by a contractor. It is community infrastructure, managed by community, for community — a physical expression of the transition from state-managed settlement to 100% community-controlled organisation.

## Why the Physical Space Matters

PICC's "whole-of-life" service model depends on integration: health, justice, family services, and employment are inextricably linked, and families shouldn't have to navigate to five different buildings to access five different services. The Centre Precinct is where this integration becomes physical.

For families on Palm Island, the Centre is the place where:
- The Bwgcolman Healing Service provides primary healthcare with integrated SEWB pathways
- The Children and Family Centre runs early childhood playgroups
- The Community Justice Group operates
- NDIS Community Connectors are accessible
- Employment pathways through the Telstra Call Centre can be found

Prior to community control, the equivalent services were delivered by external agencies with external staff. The Centre Precinct under PICC is the same building, different governance. Same walls — but now the decisions about what happens inside are made by the 176 people who work there, 78% of whom are local Palm Islanders.

## Infrastructure as Sovereignty

The maintenance and operational costs of the Centre Precinct are coded in ACT's project tracking as a distinct investment: not because maintaining a building is glamorous, but because infrastructure that breaks down is infrastructure that loses community trust. Keeping the lights on, the equipment working, and the space welcoming is part of the sovereignty work.

PICC Chair Luella Bligh and CEO Rachel Atkinson have been explicit: the organisation cannot credibly lead health and family services if its physical home is in disrepair. The Centre Precinct budget reflects this — it is not overhead to be minimised but mission-critical investment.

## Connection to the Digital Platform

The Centre Precinct houses (or is connected to) PICC's digital infrastructure — the digital platform featuring AI chat, story archive, and voice-sharing that enables community members to contribute and access knowledge without leaving country. The physical and digital reinforce each other: the Centre is where community members come to tell stories, and the platform ensures those stories remain under community control.

## LCAA Phase

In the [[lcaa-method|LCAA Method]], the PICC Centre Precinct sits in the **Action** phase — physical, community-controlled infrastructure as sovereignty made tangible. The argument the building carries is that a community-controlled service delivery model needs a community-controlled physical space to deliver it from; same walls under different governance produces a different organisation entirely.

LCAA arc for the PICC Centre Precinct:
- **Listen** — to families who shouldn't have to navigate to five different buildings to access five different services; to staff who are 78% local Palm Islanders and don't need to ask families to repeat their genealogies
- **Curiosity** — what does *whole-of-life* service delivery actually require physically? Health, family services, employment, and cultural programs under one roof, integrated not co-located
- **Action** — fit-out, maintenance, utilities, and operational costs for the Centre as ongoing community infrastructure; the digital platform (AI chat, story archive, voice-sharing) housed alongside or connected to it
- **Art** — the building as the visible, daily artefact of the transition from state-managed settlement to 100% community-controlled organisation

PICC Chair Luella Bligh and CEO [[rachel-atkinson|Rachel Atkinson]] have been explicit: the organisation cannot credibly lead health and family services if its physical home is in disrepair. The Centre Precinct budget is mission-critical, not overhead.
