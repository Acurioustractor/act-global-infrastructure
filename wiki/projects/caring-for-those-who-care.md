# Caring for Those Who Care

> A sector sustainability initiative identifying and filling the wellbeing gaps for NFP leaders and workers — research-led, storytelling-powered, and grounded in the conviction that sustaining the carers is inseparable from sustaining the communities they serve.

## Overview

Caring for Those Who Care addresses a structural blind spot in the social sector: the people who dedicate their working lives to holding communities together are themselves underserved, under-supported, and often operating at significant personal cost. NFP leaders in particular carry a particular burden — responsible for organisations, staff, and communities, expected to model resilience, and often without the peer support or institutional backing that leaders in other sectors take for granted.

The project combines rigorous research, community storytelling, and practical recommendations to identify the gaps and build the case for systemic change in how the sector cares for its own.

## NFP Leaders Interview Project

The research centred on the **NFP Leaders Interview Project**, conducted in partnership with **Bond University**. This multi-phase research project:

1. **Research Design** — developing interview frameworks that could surface the real experience of NFP leaders, including the dimensions of isolation, moral injury, financial precarity, and emotional labour that are rarely discussed publicly
2. **Data Collection** — in-depth interviews with NFP leaders across the sector, gathering qualitative evidence of the support gaps
3. **Analysis** — systematic analysis of themes, patterns, and the structural conditions that generate them
4. **Recommendations** — translating findings into actionable recommendations for funders, peak bodies, government, and the sector itself

The Bond University partnership brings academic rigour and credibility to findings that might otherwise be dismissed as anecdote or advocacy.

## Storytelling as Method and Output

Beyond the formal research, Caring for Those Who Care uses storytelling to communicate findings in ways that build empathy and shift the conditions for change. Stories from NFP leaders — told with care and appropriate consent — make visible the human cost of sector underinvestment in worker wellbeing.

This connects the project to ACT's broader storytelling infrastructure, particularly [[empathy-ledger|Empathy Ledger]].

## Relationship to Junes Patch

[[junes-patch|June's Patch]] is a natural companion to this project: where Caring for Those Who Care identifies the problem and builds the evidence base for healthcare worker wellbeing needs, June's Patch delivers a direct nature-based response. Together they represent ACT's commitment to both knowing and acting.

## LCAA Phase

**Listen** — The project's primary work is listening: to NFP leaders, to their experiences, to what the sector needs that it isn't getting. Research and storytelling are both forms of deep, structured listening. The recommendations that follow are grounded entirely in what was heard.
