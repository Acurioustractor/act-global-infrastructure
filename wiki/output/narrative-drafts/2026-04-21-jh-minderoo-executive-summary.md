# The Three Circles — Executive Summary
### A communities-first Minderoo partnership for early recognition
*Minderoo Foundation · FY2026–FY2028 · $2.9M over three years*

Minderoo has already helped Australia name the cost of late intervention. The 2024 *Cost of Late Intervention* update found Australia now spends **$22.3 billion** each year responding after children and young people are already in crisis, up from **$15.2 billion** in 2019. **Child protection accounts for $10.2 billion, or 43% of that spend. Youth crime accounts for $3.4 billion, or 14%.**

The proposition in this package is simple: if Minderoo has helped name the cost of acting too late, it can now help communities show what acting earlier, with dignity, culture, evidence, and relationship, looks like in a young person’s actual life.

The Brave Ones is the living response to that problem. The Third Reality is the method that makes it legible. The Three Circles is the funding envelope that holds it together.

| Layer | What it is | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **The number** | Minderoo-backed COLI evidence: **$22.3B** annually on late intervention, up from **$15.2B** in 2019 | The cost is not abstract. Australia is paying after harm compounds. |

## The doorway: from late intervention to early recognition

This pitch should not be read as “a youth justice program.”

It should be read as a practical, community-led response to the late-intervention crisis Minderoo has already helped quantify.

The phrase we are using is **early recognition**.

Early recognition is how this proposal translates Minderoo’s early-intervention logic into the lives of young people the system has already started to misrecognise.

It means:

- see the child before the label hardens
- see the community before the institution claims authority
- see the trusted adult before the court file becomes the only story
- see the cultural knowledge already holding the room together

Youth justice is where the failure of early support becomes visible. The Three Circles backs the communities already interrupting that failure every day.

---

## The method: The Third Reality

The core problem is not that Australia lacks evidence, care, or cultural authority. It is that these things are usually held in different rooms.

- A judge sees a file.
- A funder sees a budget line.
- A worker sees a young person.
- An Elder sees a child who belongs to Country.

The Third Reality is what happens when those partial views are brought together.

In ACT’s stack that means:

- **JusticeHub** holds the evidence spine.
- **CivicGraph** shows the organisational, funding, and decision-making context.
- **Empathy Ledger** protects consent, story sovereignty, and traceability.

When these layers are combined properly, a decision-maker can no longer hide inside one form of evidence. They have the data, the funding context, the story, the cultural authority, and the alternative in the same frame.

---

## The living response: The Brave Ones

The Brave Ones gives this whole argument a body.

It is not soft garnish on hard data. It is the public form that carries the truth outward without stripping it of dignity or context.

The Brave Ones does four jobs at once:

1. **Young people’s authorship**  
   A journal, a portrait, an exchange, and a public artefact that let a young person hold and shape their own story.

2. **Community labour made visible**  
   Aunties, Elders, ACCOs, and remote workers become visible as protagonists and governance, not background service delivery.

3. **Evidence with a human spine**  
   The story links back to the model, the funding environment, and the evidence for what works.

4. **A public argument that can travel**  
   Books, exhibitions, postcards, portraits, and other civic artefacts move the truth into rooms where the communities themselves are routinely excluded.

The Brave Ones sits **inside** the Three Circles ask. It is not a separate funding wedge.

## The Three Circles

### Circle One — The evidence spine

JusticeHub, CivicGraph, and Empathy Ledger already exist. They make it possible to connect:

- what works
- who is doing the work
- where the money is flowing
- what the policy system says
- what a young person and community are actually experiencing

This is not a speculative build. The infrastructure is live.

### Circle Two — The anchor-community hold

The active public cohort is:

- **Oonchiumpa** — Mparntwe (Alice Springs), NT
- **Palm Island Community Company** — Palm Island, QLD
- **BG Fit** — Mount Isa, QLD
- **MMEIC** — Minjerribah, QLD
- **2–3 additional relationships** to be co-selected carefully rather than named prematurely

This is the most meaningful oar in the whole package. Communities are not backdrop service providers. They are the people already doing the right thing every day with culture, relationship, and almost no structural support.

### Circle Three — The outward layer

This is where the work becomes harder to ignore:

- public artefacts
- exhibitions
- funder and judge-facing materials
- place-based evidence products
- the national library that makes the work citable and durable

The library is the destination, not the opening noun.

## What Minderoo is being asked to fund

The ask is **$2.9M over three years**, with **$780K in Year 1** as the clean entry point.

| Bucket | 3-year total | What it funds |
|---|---|---|
| **Partner / place-based hold** | **$1.4M** | Community relationships, local coordination, protocol, travel, and the time required to stay |
| **Generate evidence** | **$800K** | JusticeHub, CivicGraph, Empathy Ledger, journals, documentation, consent workflow, portraits, and traceability |
| **Develop + advocate** | **$700K** | Editorial production, public artefacts, exchanges, and outward-facing advocacy forms |

This remains less than the cost of detaining one Australian child for three years.

---

## Why now

This is not a concept waiting for permission.

- **Oonchiumpa is ready.**
- **The relationships already exist.**
- **JusticeHub, CivicGraph, and Empathy Ledger are already live.**
- **The Brave Ones gives the work a public form that does not yet exist at national scale.**

Minderoo would not be funding a blank-page idea. It would be helping turn an existing community-led proof point into a national mechanism for early recognition.

---

## The central line

Minderoo has named the cost of acting too late. The Three Circles is an opportunity to fund the next step: a living, Indigenous-led, evidence-backed demonstration of what Australia should be investing in instead.
