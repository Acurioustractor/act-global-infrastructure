# I am about to leave for six months. This is not a holiday.

I am about to do something I have been planning for two years. Six months. Twelve stops. Four continents.

I am calling it a field trip, not a tour.

A tour is performative. It has sponsors, scripts, and a stage. A field trip is curious, embodied, structured note-taking, and you come home with specimens. I am bringing a notebook, a recorder, a laptop, and the consent infrastructure I have been building for three years. I am going to listen, and I am going to log what I learn against a system that will outlive the trip.

Each chapter is a question, not a destination.

01. Palm Island — Whose history is this?
02. Alice Springs — What hasn't Country taught me yet?
03. Top End — What survives a monsoon?
04. Tanzania — Who do you call a beneficiary?
05. Rwanda — Can coffee grow forgiveness?
06. Lesotho — What does justice look like in a country that cannot afford to lock people up?
07. Uganda — What does dignity look like at 5 kg of laundry per day?
08. Sweden — What did fika know that we forgot?
09. Netherlands — Why do their kids come home?
10. Spain — What does Diagrama's loop tell us about ours?

(And six more I will share when they are confirmed.)

I want to be honest about what this is, because I am tired of nonprofits that travel and call it a documentary.

This is a working trip. I will be running A Curious Tractor from the road. The Empathy Ledger app will be in my pocket. The contact pipeline will be in my laptop. I will be meeting partners at every stop — community organisations, prison restorative-practice teams, founders of social enterprises that look exactly like the work we are doing in Australia. Some of them will become collaborators. Some will become customers. Some will become teachers.

The reason this is a working trip and not a holiday is because A Curious Tractor has earned $1.75 million in the last fourteen months across seven projects. We are not a hobby. We are not pre-revenue. The thing I have been building runs as one ecosystem: Empathy Ledger, JusticeHub, Goods on Country, The Harvest, ACT Farm, Black Cockatoo Valley, plus the data infrastructure that makes them legible to each other. The trip is the field research for what comes next.

If you are at any of these stops between now and October, I would love to meet you. If you have a story you want recorded with proper consent and sovereignty protocols, I am the person who carries the protocol. If you are running an organisation that has been doing the work alone and want to be in Room 3 of CONTAINED when it tours through Australia in 2027, this trip is how I find you.

The platform comes with me. The ecosystem comes with me. The questions come with me.

The first chapter starts in a tin shed in Witta, Sunshine Coast hinterland. Where it always starts.

The honest version is the only version. Polished is the lie.

— Ben
