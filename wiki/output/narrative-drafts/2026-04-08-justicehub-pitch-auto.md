### 2. Claim: Start at the edge. Work outward.

### 3. Claim: Every other youth justice program leaves. Staying is the move.

*File:* [`claim-staying-is-the-move.md`](../../narrative/justicehub/claim-staying-is-the-move.md) · *Frame:* confrontational · *Times deployed:* 1

**The argument:** The dominant pattern in Australian youth justice is the parachute. A consultancy lands, runs a 6-month pilot, files a report, leaves. A foundation funds for 12 months, asks for outcomes, defunds, moves on. A government program rebrands every three years. The communities it claims to serve learn to expect the leaving. Staying is the move that breaks the pattern. **Staying is the practice of an embedded storytelling team that does not leave.** It is what we call internally — and with communities — the answer to *"what is the team actually doing in those communities for three years?"* The word *STAY* carries three meanings at once: *Stay where you are* (don't move children off Country to be punished), *Stay connected* (don't parachute in and leave), *Stay in the work* (don't file the artefact and forget). The library is the form all three meanings take.

**What we have already said (do NOT repeat verbatim):**

- "Stay where you are — don't move children off Country to be punished. Stay connected — don't parachute in and leave. Stay in the work — don't file the artefact and forget."
- "Staying is the name we use internally and with communities for the practice of the embedded storytelling team."
- "It is what goes on the cover of every Journal."

**What we have NOT said (build the post here):**

- Never said as **a direct critique of named consultancies** that have parachuted into communities — there are obvious examples in the corpus
- Never paired with **the count of how many youth justice "pilots" have been run in Mparntwe in the last decade** — this number is brutal and we should know it

### 5. Claim: Authority-at-the-edge is not new. The infrastructure to make it legible is.

Resilience is contagious. ### 6. Claim: This is not ten grants. It is ten long-term relationships.

*File:* [`claim-ten-anchor-communities-filter-is-relationship.md`](../../narrative/justicehub/claim-ten-anchor-communities-filter-is-relationship.md) · *Frame:* structural · *Times deployed:* 1

**The argument:** Most "place-based" Australian programs run a competitive selection process — communities apply, criteria are weighted, an evaluation panel picks. The result is a contractual relationship between an institution and a community that has agreed to be measured. The Three Circles / STAY program runs the opposite: **The filter is relationship, not process.** Final selection is through assertive in-community visits — the only verification that works. There is no funnel. We start with the people we already know and trust, then radiate outward through trust, recommendation, and openness. Cultural protocol is non-negotiable: no community is approached without prior consultation with existing ACT relationships in that region; Elder and Council sign-off before any photographs, recordings or journals; subjects under 18 require guardian and cultural authority sign-off; income from book/exhibition sales flows on a published transparent split.

**What we have already said (do NOT repeat verbatim):**

- "The filter is relationship, not process."
- "This is not 10 grants. It is 10 long-term relationships the centre learns from, the international tour celebrates, and the artefact in Year 3 is built around."
- "Final selection through assertive in-community visits — the only verification that works."

**What we have NOT said (build the post here):**

- Never said as a **direct rebuke of competitive grant rounds** — the entire competitive grant infrastructure produces inverse selection: the communities best at proposal-writing get funded, not the communities best at the work
- Never paired with **the cultural protocol as the technical baseline** — *"OCAP / CARE is not aspirational. It is the data layer."* This is uncommonly explicit for a program of this size.
- Never positioned as **proof against scale arguments** — *"Yes, 10 communities. We could go to 50. We will not, because 50 means parachute."*

### 7. Claim: Fire does not arrive at bonfire stage. You build it slowly, with attention.

*File:* [`claim-fire-crescendo-build-slowly.md`](../../narrative/justicehub/claim-fire-crescendo-build-slowly.md) · *Frame:* structural · *Times deployed:* 1

**The argument:** Most youth justice programs fail because someone tries to light the bonfire in Year 1. Funders ask for bonfire-shaped outcomes from ember-stage work. Boards demand scale before the kindling has caught. Fire Crescendo names the six stages — **Ember** (Y1, a single relationship, one Elder, one yarn), **Tinder** (Y1, trust, showing up when you said you would), **Kindling** (Y2, small structured work, the first camp, the first cohort), **Campfire** (Y2, communities visit each other, storytellers tell their own stories), **Roaring fire** (Y3, the work spreads, other communities ask how), **Bonfire** (Y3, holding warmth for people who weren't there at the start). The diagram gives funders the language to fund Year 1 properly — and gives the team the language to say *"no, not yet."*

**What we have already said (do NOT repeat verbatim):**

- "fire does not arrive at bonfire stage. you build it, slowly, with attention."
- "Most programs fail because someone tries to light the bonfire in Year 1."
- "Funders ask for bonfire-shaped outcomes from ember-stage work. Boards demand scale before the kindling has even caught."
- "Fire Crescendo is the diagram that gives funders the language to fund Year 1 properly — and gives the team the language to say 'no, not yet.'"

**What we have NOT said (build the post here):**

- Never said as a **funder pitch line** at the moment of Year 1 ask — *"You are funding ember and tinder. You will not see bonfire-shaped outcomes for 24 months. That is the methodology, not a delay."*
- Never used as the **internal "no" tool** — *"No, not yet. We are at Kindling. Asking for Bonfire breaks the fire."*
- Never paired with the **CAMPFIRE worked example** publicly — Brodie Germaine's actual fire arc in Mount Isa is the proof that the diagram describes real time
- Never said as: *"Most foundation grant cycles are three years long. Fire Crescendo IS those three years, named correctly."*
- Never positioned as the **rebuttal to scale-first funders** — the diagram is the answer to "how do we know it scales" because the answer is *"because each stage produces the conditions for the next."*
