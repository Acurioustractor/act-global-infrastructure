# The Three Circles
### A communities-first partnership for early recognition
*A proposal to Minderoo Foundation · FY2026–FY2028*

---

## The proposition

Minderoo has already helped Australia name the cost of acting too late.

The 2024 *Cost of Late Intervention* update found Australia now spends **$22.3 billion** each year responding after children and young people are already in crisis, up from **$15.2 billion** in 2019. **Child protection accounts for $10.2 billion, or 43% of that spend. Youth crime accounts for $3.4 billion, or 14%.**

The Three Circles is a practical answer to that reality.

It is a three-year, communities-first partnership that helps Aboriginal community-controlled organisations stay with children and families, makes their work visible as evidence, and carries that proof outward in forms that funders, judges, journalists, and governments cannot easily ignore.

The phrase we are using is **early recognition**.

Early recognition is how this proposal translates Minderoo’s early-intervention logic into the lives of young people the system has already started to misrecognise.

---

## The Three Circles

### Circle One — The evidence spine

Circle One is the infrastructure that lets the work become legible without being flattened.

It includes:

- **JusticeHub** as the public evidence spine
- **CivicGraph** as the map of organisations, funding, and system context
- **Empathy Ledger** as the consent and story-sovereignty layer

This infrastructure already exists. Minderoo is not being asked to fund a speculative build.

Circle One makes it possible to hold:

- what works
- who is doing the work
- what funding is flowing
- what policy is being said
- what a young person and community are actually experiencing

### Circle Two — The anchor-community hold

Circle Two is the heart of the proposal.

It is the place-based hold around communities already doing the work:

- **Oonchiumpa** — Mparntwe (Alice Springs), NT
- **Palm Island Community Company** — Palm Island, QLD
- **BG Fit** — Mount Isa, QLD
- **MMEIC** — Minjerribah, QLD
- **2–3 additional relationships** to be co-selected carefully rather than named prematurely

This is not a grant round.

It is relationship-first support that gives communities:

- time
- travel
- protocol
- documentation
- journals and portraits
- communications support
- a visible place in a national body of proof

Circle Two is where the hidden labour of Aunties, Elders, ACCOs, and remote workers is recognised as governance, not background service delivery.

### Circle Three — The outward layer

Circle Three is where the work becomes harder to dismiss.

It includes:

- The Brave Ones
- books
- portraits
- exchanges
- exhibitions
- judge and funder-facing artefacts
- the living library that lets community-held proof travel

The library is the destination, not the opening noun.

---

## The Brave Ones inside the circles

The Brave Ones is not a separate funding wedge.

It sits inside the Three Circles as:

- the **human trace layer** that keeps the evidence honest
- the **public form** that carries Circle Two outward
- the **shared frame** in which a young person’s journey and the community labour around them remain visible together

This is the Third Reality in public form: systemic evidence, sovereign story, and community authority held together so decision-makers can no longer pretend a file is the whole truth.

---

## Why Minderoo fits

Minderoo’s public role language is the right fit for this proposal.

| Minderoo lever | How The Three Circles answers it |
|---|---|
| **Partner** | Backs communities already doing the work, with untied, relationship-first support |
| **Generate evidence** | Connects community-held knowledge to verified evidence, funding context, and traceable story |
| **Develop** | Strengthens a practical model through journals, documentation, exchanges, and local capability |
| **Advocate** | Carries the proof into rooms where communities are rarely centred, through public artefacts and civic argument |

---

## The ask

The ask is **$2.9M over three years**, with **$780K in Year 1** as the clean entry point.

| Bucket | 3-year total | What it funds |
|---|---|---|
| **Partner / place-based hold** | **$1.4M** | Community relationships, travel, local coordination, protocol, and the time required to stay |
| **Generate evidence** | **$800K** | JusticeHub, CivicGraph, Empathy Ledger, journals, documentation, consent workflow, and traceability |
| **Develop + advocate** | **$700K** | Editorial production, public artefacts, exchanges, and outward-facing advocacy |

This remains less than the cost of detaining one Australian child for three years.

---

## Why now

This is not a concept waiting for permission.

- **Oonchiumpa is ready.**
- **The relationships already exist.**
- **JusticeHub, CivicGraph, and Empathy Ledger are already live.**
- **The Brave Ones gives the work a public form it does not yet have at national scale.**

Minderoo would not be funding a blank-page idea. It would be helping turn an existing community-led proof point into a national mechanism for early recognition.

---

## The line to remember

Minderoo has named the cost of acting too late. The Three Circles is an opportunity to fund the next step: a living, Indigenous-led, evidence-backed demonstration of what Australia should be investing in instead.
