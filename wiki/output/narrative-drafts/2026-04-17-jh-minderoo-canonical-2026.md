## Opening proof · Murri Watch, 14 April 2026

### The claim we already made, in public

On Monday 14 April, we shipped one LinkedIn post, one A2 poster and one 16-page printed document. Source data: ABC News, Queensland state budget, Hansard, ROGS 2024-25. Assembly time: under one week. Cost: under $200.

The post named the trade: Queensland cut a $1,449,408 Aboriginal-led watch-house program that served 1,233 young people across 16 watch-houses in FY24-25, while the state's youth detention budget for the same period sat at $225,580,000. The ratio is 155:1. We named the minister. We named the 22-organisation claim she had not yet substantiated. We named the elder (Uncle Adrian Coolwell, 30 years QPS, first assistant watch-house officer). We named the Royal Commission year Murri Watch was founded in (1991). Every figure is traceable.

### What happened in 36 hours

144 reactions, 27 comments, 30 reposts. Inside the comments: George Newhouse AM (National Justice Project), Margaret O'Brien (Telstra Foundation), Adam Robinson (StreetSmart, existing Murri Watch funder since 2018), Sandra Phillips PhD. A lived-experience comment from Bernice Hookey — Murri Watch gave her family peace of mind when her relative was in the watch-house. Kylie Lower, actively emailing the minister for the list.

The minister has not named the 22 organisations. That silence is the proof. A policy substitution announced without a list of substitutes is not a re-investment. It is a trade no one can show the working for. The post forced the question into a format the minister's office cannot safely answer either way — if Murri Watch is one of the 22, the cancellation is not a re-investment; if it isn't, she needs to name the organisation that will sit with kids in watch-houses from 1 July.

### What this proves about the stack

This was not a campaign. This was a Tuesday afternoon query against a system we've been building for two years.

- **CivicGraph** surfaced the $225.58M detention line and the $1.45M Murri Watch contract from the same budget tree.
- **JusticeHub's evidence scatter** already knew Murri Watch was on the 34-year, community-governed, under-resourced end of the spectrum.
- **Empathy Ledger** holds the consent framework that made it safe to publish at speed without extracting from anyone.
- **Aesthetics of Asymmetry** is the output layer — the artefact series that turns the data into something a magistrate, a minister or a minister's staffer has to look at.

The Centre in the Three Circles proposal is the thing that makes this repeatable. Not once a month. Not once a quarter. Every time a state government announces a cut, a contract, a strategy or a silence, the system surfaces the shape within a week. The minister's silence becomes politically expensive. The concentration becomes visible. The community-led alternative becomes the case.

### The ask, in context

Minderoo is being asked to fund three things Murri Watch already demonstrates:

1. **The centre** — because the only reason the $1.45M vs $225.58M trade became visible at all is that we had already linked Hansard to ABNs to contracts to evaluations. Without that work, it takes a journalist three weeks. With it, it takes a morning. Queensland is one state. The centre lets us run the same surfacing across every state every week.
2. **The ten** — because the post only landed because Murri Watch is a named, known, community-governed organisation. The orgs that get quietly cut are the ones without the visibility. The ten are the ones we hold in relationship so the next cut has a named witness.
3. **The artefact layer** — because the post worked. 144 reactions in 36 hours with zero paid reach is not noise, it is the first use of a new campaign surface. Year 3's documentary + book + platform is the mature version of what Monday's post was the prototype of.

Murri Watch is the proof that the stack is live, the method is fast, and the political leverage is real. The Three Circles is the durable version.
