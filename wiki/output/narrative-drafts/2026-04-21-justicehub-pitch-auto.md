### 1. Claim: This is not a stack of grants. It is 7–8 long-term relationships.

*File:* [`claim-ten-anchor-communities-filter-is-relationship.md`](../../narrative/justicehub/claim-ten-anchor-communities-filter-is-relationship.md) · *Frame:* structural · *Times deployed:* 2

**The argument:** Most "place-based" Australian programs still behave like competitive grant rounds in disguise — communities apply, criteria are weighted, an evaluation panel picks, and a contractual relationship begins. ### 3. Claim: Fire does not arrive at bonfire stage. You build it slowly, with attention.

*File:* [`claim-fire-crescendo-build-slowly.md`](../../narrative/justicehub/claim-fire-crescendo-build-slowly.md) · *Frame:* structural · *Times deployed:* 2

Boards demand scale before the kindling has caught. Fire Crescendo names the six stages — **Ember** (Y1, a single relationship, one Elder, one yarn), **Tinder** (Y1, trust, showing up when you said you would), **Kindling** (Y2, small structured work, the first camp, the first cohort), **Campfire** (Y2, communities visit each other, storytellers tell their own stories), **Roaring fire** (Y3, the work spreads, other communities ask how), **Bonfire** (Y3, holding warmth for people who weren't there at the start). The diagram gives funders the language to fund Year 1 properly — and gives the team the language to say *"no, not yet."*

### 6. Claim: Authority-at-the-edge is not new. The infrastructure to make it legible is.

Resilience is contagious.
