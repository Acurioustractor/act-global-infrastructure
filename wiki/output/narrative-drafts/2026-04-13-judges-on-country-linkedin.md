# Draft: Judges on Country — LinkedIn

---

**Option A — The photo in their hands**

55 judges came to Mparntwe.

Traditional Owners put a photo and a story in their hands. A 14-year-old. A quote. A moment from a life the court file will never capture.

Then they said: decide.

They decided.

Then they were told what they didn't know.

He hasn't been home in three weeks. There's no transport to the nearest program. This is his third breach — not his third offence.

Would you make the same decision now?

Australia spends $1.3 million a year to detain one child. 80% reoffend. 60% are Indigenous.

In the same town, Oonchiumpa — a 100% Aboriginal-led program run by Traditional Owners — diverts 95% of young people from the justice system. At 97.6% less cost.

A judge can only refer to a program they know exists.

So we built postcards. Real photos. Real young people. Real stories told with consent. QR codes on the back that open JusticeHub — filtered to that region, showing real alternatives.

The experience isn't information. It's a felt shift.

The question isn't whether the alternatives work. The evidence is already in.

The question is whether the people making the decisions have ever been in a room where they had to feel what those decisions produce.

→ justicehub.com.au

**Option B — Shorter, sharper**

A 14-year-old in Alice Springs told us:

"When I'm talking to the judge, I feel like I'm panicking."

55 judges came to his Country this week.

Traditional Owners put his photo in their hands and said: decide. Then told them what the court file never will.

Australia spends $1.3M per year to detain one child. 80% reoffend.

The community-led alternative costs 97.6% less. 95% diversion.

But a judge can only refer to a program they know exists.

That's why we built the postcards. Real stories. Real faces. QR to real programs on JusticeHub.

The postcard is the entry point.
JusticeHub is the answer.
The young people are the proof.

→ justicehub.com.au

---

**Option C — Laquisha's voice**

Laquisha was 16 when they sent her to Darwin.

1,500 kilometres from home. Twelve-minute phone calls. No family.

"Court is scary because you don't know whether you're getting out or not."

When we asked her why young people end up in the system, she said one word:

"Oppression."

This week, 55 judges came to Mparntwe — Alice Springs — to hear what Laquisha already knows.

Traditional Owners ran the room. Not as guests. As authority.

They put a photo in each judge's hands. A story. A young person who could appear in their court tomorrow.

Then they said: decide.

Then they revealed what the court file doesn't contain.

The shift wasn't cognitive. It was felt.

We can't make judges feel differently by giving them more reports. We can make them feel differently by putting them in the room.

The postcards are the room.
JusticeHub is the map.
The young people are the compass.

→ justicehub.com.au
