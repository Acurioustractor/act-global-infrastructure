# JCF Final 5 — Story Infrastructure for the Last Five Years

**To:** [JCF primary contact — TBC]
**From:** Benjamin Knight, A Curious Tractor
**Subject:** A story product for the next five years
**Length:** read in 4 minutes; one ask at the bottom

---

There is something I want to bring to JCF that I have been building for two years and have not put on the table yet. Now is the right time, because of where JCF is in its arc.

JCF has five years left. Fifteen down, five to go. That is not a small number. Most foundations close without anyone able to write down what actually happened — the receipts survive, the photos survive, the grant reports survive, but the things people said about why it mattered are usually lost the day the foundation winds up. I want to make sure that does not happen here.

## What I am offering

A multi-year story infrastructure retainer. Specifically:

- **The platform.** Empathy Ledger is a sovereignty-first storytelling system I have been building since 2023. It now runs across 18 partner organisations with 342 named storytellers and 2,569 edited quotes. Every story has consent metadata, every storyteller can revoke at any time, every quote is traceable to a date, a place, and a relationship. JCF gets a private tenant on the same platform for the duration of the remaining five years.

- **The editorial pipeline.** I run a four-layer transcript-to-story workflow that I will run for JCF beneficiaries — Tanzania, Rwanda, Australia, the African legacy programs, the local Australian work — with the same care I bring to the rest of the corpus.

- **The annual report you cannot currently produce.** Every year I will deliver a JCF Final-Five impact report that is built from the storytellers' words, not from grant officer summaries. Funders, trustees, and the families of June Canavan will be able to read what people actually said about what JCF did for them.

- **The cross-jurisdictional spine.** My world tour is heading to Tanzania (St Jude, Naramatisho) and Rwanda (Kula Project) in the next twelve months. I am visiting JCF beneficiaries directly, with the platform on the laptop, recording stories at no marginal cost to JCF. The same trip extends into Lesotho once we close the prison contact and the JCF Lesotho program details (I am asking about both separately).

## Why the cost is reasonable

The work I am proposing is the kind of work most foundations would have to commission three different vendors for: a platform, an editor, and a researcher. JCF would get all three from the same person, on a system that already exists, with a corpus that already proves the method works.

Specific terms:

- **$60,000 / year for five years** — fixed
- Includes platform tenancy, editorial pipeline, annual report, and the African leg of the world tour fieldwork as bundled fieldwork
- Excludes printed-book production (separate quote if you want it) and any documentary licensing (separate again)
- I take a 20% deposit on the first year. The remaining four years are billed annually and are cancellable each year if the work is not delivering.

That is $300,000 across five years for a complete impact-storytelling infrastructure for the last quarter of JCF's mandate. For comparison, a single boutique impact-evaluation contractor would charge $80,000–$150,000 per year for a fraction of what I am describing.

## Why this is not a charity ask

I want to be direct because the relationship deserves it. A Curious Tractor has earned $1.75M across the ecosystem in the last fourteen months. We are not pre-revenue. We are running a working venture that does not yet know how to talk about itself. This is not a grant. This is a contract for a product I have already built and that I am asking JCF to be the reference customer for.

There is a long version of why this matters — I am happy to send the strategy document I have been building, which lays out the closed-loop ecosystem the platform sits inside (LISTEN → DOCUMENT → STRUCTURE → DECIDE → BUILD → MEASURE → LISTEN). I will not push it on you in a first email. It is there if you want it.

## Why JCF specifically

Three reasons.

1. **JCF is the philanthropic spine of the ACT international leg.** The Tanzania and Rwanda partners are already in JCF's portfolio. I am visiting them. If JCF is the storytelling sponsor, the same fieldwork serves both organisations at zero duplication.

2. **JCF has a hard wind-down date.** Most foundations have no incentive to capture their own legacy because they are not going anywhere. JCF is. The work has a deadline, and that deadline makes the storytelling more urgent and more honest than it would be for a perpetual trust.

3. **The next decade of social-sector funders are going to ask "how do you measure impact" with a level of rigour that is going to embarrass most current foundations.** JCF, with this product on the table, will be one of the few foundations whose final report is built from real consented voice rather than from re-purposed marketing copy. That is a legacy worth paying for.

## What I need from you

One thirty-minute phone call to walk through the platform live. I will show you a real per-org branded experience (PICC, Snow Foundation, June Canavan, Diagrama already exist as tenants) so you can see exactly what JCF would look like inside the system before you commit to anything.

If the call goes well, I will draft a one-page statement of work and send it to JCF's nominated decision-maker by the end of next week.
