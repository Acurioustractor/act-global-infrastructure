# Reports that have already said this

If you work in Australian youth justice, you have read these.

If you make policy in Australian youth justice, your office commissioned at least one of them.

If you fund Australian youth justice work, you cite them in your grant criteria.

I am going to list them anyway, because the question we are about to walk into for the next decade is whether anyone is going to actually do anything with what they say.

---

**1991** — Royal Commission into Aboriginal Deaths in Custody. 339 recommendations. Most are not implemented.

**2017** — Royal Commission into the Protection and Detention of Children in the Northern Territory. The conditions Don Dale described still exist.

**2018, 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024** — Productivity Commission, Report on Government Services, Youth Justice. Same conclusions, every year.

**2022** — Banksia Hill Inquiry, Western Australia. Findings: not implemented.

**2024** — Cleveland Youth Detention Centre coronial inquest, Queensland. Findings: pending implementation.

**Every state child commissioner, every year, since the position has existed.**

**Every coroner who has ever sat over the body of a child who died inside the Australian youth justice system.**

**The Australian Human Rights Commission, in five separate national reports.**

**The UN Committee on the Rights of the Child, in three separate periodic reviews.**

**Closing the Gap, in every annual report since 2009.**

---

This is not a complete list. I stopped because the point is made.

The evidence is settled. Every inquiry has said the same thing. Every report has confirmed it. Every coroner has named it.

What we have run out of is not evidence. What we have run out of is the political will to act on the evidence we already have.

I am asking one question on the record. If you are a Premier, a Minister, a senior bureaucrat in a Department of Youth Justice, or a senior policy officer in any of the agencies above — when you are next asked at a press conference whether we need "more research" before reforming youth justice in Australia, are you going to say yes?

Because if you do, you are not doing youth justice policy.

You are postponing the decision to act on what you already know.

CONTAINED is a shipping container with three rooms. It launches in Mount Druitt on April 20. It does not contain any new research. It contains the research that has been on the desk for thirty-five years.

If you would like to walk through it before you give that next press conference, the door is open.

— Ben
