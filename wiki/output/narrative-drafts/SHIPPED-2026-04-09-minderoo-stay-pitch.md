# STAY — A Three-Year Library

**To:** Lucy Stronach, Minderoo Foundation
**From:** Benjamin Knight, A Curious Tractor
**Date:** April 2026
**Length:** A six-minute read. One ask at the end.

---

## Lucy

I want to bring you a version of the Three Circles pitch you have been generous to since October — but rebuilt around a single conceptual hook that I have not yet put on paper for you, and updated with one piece of news from yesterday that I think changes the conversation.

**At the end of this program, we have a library.**

Not a report. Not an evaluation. A library — Penguin-style, hand-set serif, aged cream spines, on a shelf you can stand in front of. Seven slim method books. Fifty hand-stitched young-person Journals. Ten community volumes from ten anchor communities on Country. And one national edition that goes to every electorate office in Australia.

The library is not the documentation of the program. **The library *is* the program.** It is the proof, and it is the destination — the thing the rest of the world will fly in to read.

I am writing to ask Minderoo to commission that library.

I want to put this on the table early because it changes the framing of the rest of the document.

**Yesterday I received a confirmed yes from Ludmila Andrade at YOPE International in Amsterdam.** YOPE is the Dutch youth-justice organisation that works directly with system-impacted young people. Ludmila and her colleague Veerle have agreed to host a Travel Diary exchange between Australian young people from Mounty Yarns and Oonchiumpa, and the YOPE Amsterdam cohort. I will be in the Netherlands in July to begin the first cohort visit.

This matters for the pitch because the Travel Diary methodology — which sits in Year 2 of the STAY program proper — is **already happening in Year 0**. The first real cohort lands in Amsterdam in three months. Mounty Yarns young people, Oonchiumpa young people, and YOPE young people will each receive a hand-stitched Travel Diary book — the same template, used in both directions — to fill with reflections, photographs, voices, and the small everyday observations that come from being a fifteen-year-old in a country that is not your own, on Country that has held its own children for thousands of years.

What this changes for Minderoo: **you are not being asked to fund a hypothesis.** The proof-of-concept is in motion. The methodology is being tested before the program begins, with a confirmed European partner, with consent infrastructure already running, and with named young people from existing partner communities. The Crescendo tier funds the *scaling* of a methodology that has already produced its first cohort by the time the funding lands.

The full planning for the YOPE July visit is at `wiki/library/locations/yope-amsterdam-july-2026.md` in the project file system. I can send you the planning page directly if it would help the conversation.

## What we are not asking

Before the proposition, three things this is not.

**This is not a report grant.** Three years of consultancy reports about Indigenous-led youth justice cost roughly the same as one Crescendo tier of this program and produce nothing the communities can hold afterwards. We are not asking Minderoo to fund a report.

**This is not a competitive funnel.** We are not running a place-based competition where ten communities apply, criteria are weighted, and a panel selects winners. *The filter is relationship, not process.* Final selection is through assertive in-community visits — the only verification that works. We start with the people we already know and trust (Aunty Bev, Tanya Turner, Kristy Bloomfield at Oonchiumpa; Richard Cassidy and the Bwgcolman families at PICC; Brodie Germaine at CAMPFIRE; Mounty Yarns; Maranguka in Bourke), then radiate outward through trust, recommendation, and openness. Cultural protocol is not aspirational. OCAP and CARE protocols are the data layer.

**This is not "scaling."** Ten communities is not a number we picked because ten was funder-friendly. Ten is the number we can hold with depth across three years without parachuting. Anything bigger overwhelms a community when it arrives. We will not scale the methodology until the methodology has been built right at ten — and we will tell you when, not the other way around.

What we are asking instead is more interesting and, I think, harder to ignore.

## The methodology in one paragraph

The seven method books on the shelf are not invented. They are the synthesis of work that has been on the table for decades and was never wired together into a single legible structure.

**Tyson Yunkaporta** has been documenting authority-at-the-edge on this continent for sixty-five thousand years — Songlines as a working world model, governance distributed by relationship rather than hierarchy. **Elinor Ostrom** won a Nobel Prize in 2009 for proving the same pattern across five continents and forty years of fieldwork. **Fundación Diagrama** in Spain has been running national-scale community-based youth justice for thirty years, with reoffending rates around thirteen per cent — against Australia's eighty-five. **Edith Henderson Grotberg**, working in Lima with street children in 2006 and across twenty other countries before that, named the three pools of resource that resilient children draw on (*I have, I am, I can*) and made the harder finding: resilience is contagious, and so is its absence.

Yunkaporta, Ostrom, Diagrama, Grotberg. The research is done. What was always missing was the legibility infrastructure — the layer that lets capital, magistrates and the public *see* the work without going through the hierarchy.

We finally built it. It is called CivicGraph. The library on the shelf is the proof.

## What is already shipping

This program is not starting from zero. The infrastructure layer is already live and Minderoo has been generous about funding the next layer, not the foundation.

| Layer | What it is | Live numbers (as of April 2026) |

| **CivicGraph** | The legibility layer. Entities, relationships, place-mapping, daily syncs from public sources. | 100,036 entities · 199,001 relationships · 94.1% entity-match accuracy · 96% postcode coverage |

| **JusticeHub** | The public-facing front end — search, dossiers, MCP agent tools, place pages. Live at justicehub.com.au. | 6 live agent tools |

The four layers share a database. They reference one another by foreign key. When a young person from Oonchiumpa speaks into Empathy Ledger, the quote can be cited inside JusticeHub, mapped against the funding flows in CivicGraph, and used as evidence in the next ALMA verification round. **This is the rare social-venture stack where the listening, the doing, and the money-and-power all share a database.** Nobody else in Australia has this. We are not building it. We have built it. We are running it. The earned revenue across the seven ACT projects is $1.75 million in the last fourteen months — this is a working venture, not a grant proposal.

What we have not yet built — and what STAY is asking Minderoo to fund — is the *editorial and relational* layer that turns this infrastructure into a national advocacy artefact you can hold in your hand.

## The proposition

A three-year program shaped as three movements.

**Year 1 — Listen.** The team rotates through ten anchor communities on Country. Heavy in-community engagement. Relationships first, methodology second. The first round of journals begins. The infrastructure layer (CivicGraph + Living Map + Empathy Ledger) is wired to the field arm so every story has a home the moment it is told.

**Year 2 — Compare.** A distributed European exchange. Each community sends a young person plus a support person to one of three European destinations: Fundación Diagrama in Spain (the international anchor for the youth-justice methodology), YOPE International in Amsterdam under Ludmila Andrade and Veerle (the confirmed first partner — see *What is already in motion* above), and the Children's Hearings system in Scotland. *And the diary travels back.* The same notebook a young person from Mparntwe takes to Murcia is the same kind of notebook an Olga from Diagrama brings to Mount Isa, and the same kind of notebook a YOPE young person carries from Amsterdam to Mount Druitt the year after that. Reciprocal European visits to Australia in the same year, hosted on Country. The traffic flows both ways. The proof is on the desk.

**And in Africa.** The JCF Africa visit (Tanzania and Rwanda, with Lesotho pending the prison contact and the JCF Lesotho program details) brings the African leg into the same Travel Diary methodology. The School of St Jude in Tanzania, the Naramatisho Women's Centre, and the Kula Project in Rwanda are existing JCF beneficiary partnerships that the same notebooks can travel through. This is the leg where the "world tour" framing meets the "library" framing — every JCF beneficiary visit in Year 2 produces material that goes into the African chapter of the master book and into the relevant community volumes.

**Year 3 — Tell.** Ten community-led launches in the ten places. A published book gathers the ten community volumes into a single national edition. The work goes home before it goes to the city. Then the library opens publicly — and a magistrate in Hobart, a Minister in Canberra, a youth worker in Birmingham can read the methodology in the form they actually read things in: a slim Penguin-style paperback, on a shelf, with a hand-stitched journal beside it.

Three acts. Listen, Compare, Tell. This is the same arc the methodology runs on at every scale — the LCAA loop of a Curious Tractor compressed into the program's whole shape.

## Staying is the move

The dominant pattern in Australian youth justice is the parachute. A consultancy lands, runs a six-month pilot, files a report, leaves. A foundation funds for twelve months, asks for outcomes, defunds, moves on. A government program rebrands every three years. The communities the work claims to serve have learned to expect the leaving. Aunty Bev has lived this pattern for forty years. Tanya Turner has lived it. Brodie has lived it.

Staying is the move that breaks the pattern. It is what we call internally — and with the communities themselves — the practice of an embedded storytelling team that does not leave. Not a value statement. Not a brand line. The actual deliverable. We are selling presence.

The word *STAY* carries three meanings at once:

> *Stay where you are* — don't move children off Country to be punished.
>
> *Stay connected* — don't parachute in and leave.
>
> *Stay in the work* — don't file the artefact and forget.

The library is the form all three meanings take. Each of the ten community volumes is a record of a relationship that did not leave. Each young-person journal is a record of a child whose name was held across three years by people who showed up.

## The fire metaphor (and the budget that mirrors it)

Most youth justice programs fail because someone tries to light the bonfire in Year 1. Funders ask for bonfire-shaped outcomes from ember-stage work. Boards demand scale before the kindling has caught.

The seventh book in the STAY Series is called Fire Crescendo and it names the six stages: **Ember** (a single relationship, one Elder, one yarn), **Tinder** (trust, showing up when you said you would), **Kindling** (small structured work, the first camp, the first cohort), **Campfire** (communities visit each other, storytellers tell their own stories), **Roaring fire** (the work spreads, other communities ask how), **Bonfire** (holding warmth for people who weren't there at the start).

The budget tells the same story as the methodology. The three tiers borrow the fire language because the budget shape and the work shape are the same shape:

| | 🔥 **Foundation** | 🔥🔥 **Crescendo** | 🔥🔥🔥 **Bonfire** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **3-year total** | ~$1.8M | **~$3.43M** | ~$6.0M |
| **Communities** | 5 anchor | **10 anchor** | 10 + annual national gathering |
| **Team** | 1 FTE lead | 1 FTE + 1 support | 2 FTE + 1 support |
| **Cadence** | 3 months on / 9 off | 4 / 8 | 6 / 6 |
| **Year 2 international exchange** | Deferred — one scoping trip | Distributed exchange, ~14 travellers | Full exchange + reciprocal visits + cohort weeks |
| **Library — community volumes** | 5 | **10** | 10 + slipcase boxed sets in every State Library |
| **Library — national edition** | — | 1 printed volume to every electorate office | 1 + national gallery tour (NGA / MCA / NGV / regional) |
| **Versus detaining one child for 3 years** *($3.9M, ROGS 2024–25)* | 46% of cost | **88% of cost** | 154% of cost |

**Crescendo is the default.** It is the shape that *moves*: small enough to fund without negotiation, big enough to do the work, structured so every line earns its place — and big enough that the library at the end is the real artefact, not a deliverable.

The line I want sitting alone on the page:

> **Detaining one Australian child for three years costs $3.9 million.**
> **The Crescendo tier runs the entire program for less than that.**
> **Across ten Indigenous-led communities, sending fourteen people to Europe and bringing Europe back, and producing the seven-book STAY Series plus ten community volumes plus around fifty young-person journals plus the national edition.**

That is the arithmetic. It does not need defending.
