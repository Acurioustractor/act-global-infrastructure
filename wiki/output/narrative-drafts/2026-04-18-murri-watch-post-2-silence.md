# Post 2 — the silence post (AutoReason Round 2 winner)

## Draft (ship candidate)

Four days ago, the Queensland government said $560 million is going to 22 First Nations-led organisations delivering 28 new services.

She has not named them.

Murri Watch served 1,233 young people across 16 watch-houses last financial year, at a cost of $1,449,408. The state's youth detention budget for the same year was $225,580,000. The ratio is 155:1. Murri Watch's contract ends 30 June.

Uncle Adrian Coolwell spent 30 years in Queensland Police, including time as the state's first assistant watch-house officer. He said the cancellation puts "a lot of weight" back on general duties police. Bernice Hookey wrote that Murri Watch gave her peace of mind when her own family member was in the watch-house. Adam Robinson at StreetSmart Australia has funded Murri Watch with small grants since 2018, because larger ones never came. This week he called the decision terrible.

If Murri Watch is one of the 22 organisations, Laura Gerber is cancelling a service she counts as an investment. The government would owe an explanation for why re-investment looks like closure.

If Murri Watch is not one of the 22, there is a watch-house service gap opening on 1 July that no named organisation has been assigned to fill. The average stay for a child in a Queensland watch-house is 161 hours. Some run past 14 days.

Either answer exists. Neither has been made public.

Murri Watch is 34 years old and Aboriginal-led. The list of 22 organisations should not be hard to produce. If it exists, post it. If it does not exist yet, the Premier's office needs to know before Murri Watch's doors close.

Email the Premier at premier@premiers.qld.gov.au and ask for the list by name.
