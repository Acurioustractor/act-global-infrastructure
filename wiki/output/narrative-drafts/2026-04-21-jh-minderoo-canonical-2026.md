# Minderoo canonical pitch package — FY2026–FY2028

*Single source of truth as of 21 April 2026.*

## What this package is

One argument. One ask. One disciplined opening.

Minderoo has already helped Australia name the cost of late intervention. The 2024 update put the annual bill at **$22.3 billion**, up from **$15.2 billion** in 2019. The package below is the next sentence after that finding: what does it look like to back communities who are already doing the opposite of late intervention?

The answer in this package is:

- **The number:** the Cost of Late Intervention
- **The method:** The Third Reality
- **The living response:** The Brave Ones
- **The envelope:** The Three Circles

The live ask remains **$2.9M over 3 years**, with **$780K for Year 1** as the clean entry point.

---

## The opening logic

This is not a platform-first pitch and not a generic youth justice program ask.

It is a communities-first proposal that says:

> Minderoo has helped Australia see the cost of intervening too late. Will it now help communities show what it looks like to intervene with dignity, culture, evidence, and relationship before the system swallows another child?

The phrase we are using is **early recognition**.

Early recognition is how this proposal translates Minderoo’s early-intervention logic into the lives of young people the system has already started to misrecognise.

## What Lucy is being asked to back

The ask is not:

- fund our youth justice program
- fund our platform
- fund our AI

The ask is:

> Help turn the evidence Minderoo already supported into a living, Indigenous-led mechanism for recognition, redirection, and community-led proof.

The money story remains:

| Bucket | 3-year total |
|---|---|
| **Partner / place-based hold** | **$1.4M** |
| **Generate evidence** | **$800K** |
| **Develop + advocate** | **$700K** |

## Why this is credible now

This is not a blank-page idea.

- **Oonchiumpa is ready.**
- **The relationships already exist.**
- **JusticeHub, CivicGraph, and Empathy Ledger are already live.**
- **The Brave Ones is the public form that lets the work travel without flattening it.**

Minderoo would be helping turn an existing proof point into a national mechanism for early recognition.

## What goes in the room

For the Lucy conversation and any internal review pack, the package should be assembled in this order:

1. **Canonical cover** — this file
2. **Executive summary** — the policy bridge
3. **Brave Ones insert** — the living response
4. **Three Circles spine** — the funding/program detail

The opening noun in the room stays **community**, not platform.

The emotional centre stays the same:

- the people already doing the right thing every day with culture, relationship, and almost no structural support are the heroes
- the platform matters only because it makes their work visible and harder to dismiss
- the artefact matters only because it carries their truth into rooms they are normally excluded from

## The line to close on

Minderoo has named the cost of acting too late. This package is an opportunity to fund the next step: a living, Indigenous-led, evidence-backed demonstration of what Australia should be investing in instead.
