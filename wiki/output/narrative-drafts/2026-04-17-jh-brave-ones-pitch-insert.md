# The Brave Ones

*A journal, journey and exchange program for young people in youth justice*

---

[PHOTO: Judges on Country origin moment — to be captured April 21, 2026. Real photo only.]

---

In Alice Springs last week, we sat with Kristy and Tanya from Oonchiumpa and talked about what happens on a Tuesday afternoon when a young person walks through the door.

The girls will sit and paint for two hours. Hudson sat on a computer for two hours yesterday. They engage when the thing in front of them is theirs — personal, tactile, real.

So we asked: what if every young person in the program got two things? An iPad locked to a drawing app. And a physical journal — A5, hardcover, theirs to keep. Family can write in it. Workers can write in it. The young person draws, paints, records their voice, pastes in photos. Every entry stays theirs. They choose what to share and with whom.

The goal state is simple. A young person walks in and says: *"Can I grab my journal?"*

That's it. That's the program.

---

## How it works

**The journal** is the daily practice. iPads with Procreate for drawing and Voice Memos for audio (most of these kids prefer talking or painting over writing). Physical A5 books alongside. Workers upload content weekly to the Empathy Ledger — the consent and story sovereignty layer — where each young person controls what is shared.

**The exchange** is the reward. Journal practice unlocks travel between communities. Alice Springs to Mount Isa. Mount Isa to Sydney. Four kids and two workers, three days. By Year 2, the exchanges go international — to Diagrama in Spain (85% non-recidivism), to YOPE in Amsterdam (youth-led policy advocacy). These kids have never been on an aeroplane. The trip itself is transformative.

**The book** is the artifact. Journal entries — drawings, audio transcripts, paintings, words — compiled into physical books. One per community. One national collection. Not written about young people. Made BY young people. Goes to magistrates, ministers, funders. A physical object no one can look away from.

**The alumni** carry it forward. Graduates become peer mentors for the next cohort. Over three years, a national network of young advocates emerges — people who went through the program and now speak from experience.

---

## The pilot

Oonchiumpa, Mparntwe (Alice Springs). June 2026. Proceeding on existing budget — this is a proof point, not a promise.

- Journals start June
- Brisbane inspiration trip (June 8-13) is the first domestic exchange
- First data flows to Empathy Ledger by August
- Pilot review in September
- First inter-community exchange by December

---

## The cost

One iPad costs **$1,500**. One night in youth detention costs **$3,644**.

For the price of one week in detention, you equip a community with journals for a year.

The Brave Ones sits within the $2.9M Three Circles envelope — not on top of it. It is the daily practice that makes Circle 2 tangible, the source material that makes Circle 3 inevitable, and the evidence that the program is real.

*The Brave Ones is the human spine of the Three Circles proposal. The data infrastructure makes the case. The communities live the case. The books tell the case in the voices of the young people who lived it.*
