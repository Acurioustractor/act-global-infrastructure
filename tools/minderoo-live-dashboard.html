The heart of the program

What young people get from this

The Minderoo ask is for a national change. But the people inside the program are not changing the country for the country's sake. They are changing something for themselves — their future, their agency, the daily feeling that an adult showed up and kept showing up. The national outcome is what happens when you take care of the smaller one first.

What every young person in the program gets — regardless

Daily agency
A journal that's theirs. An iPad loaded with Procreate and Voice Memos. A physical A5 hardcover book no one else opens without permission. Family can write in it. Workers can. The young person decides what is shared.
Being listened to, not assessed
The program is designed around the young person's own theory of change. Their explanation of what works for them is the design principle, not an aftermath.
Tangible rewards
Journal practice unlocks travel. Domestic trips Year 1. International Year 2. Paid peer-mentor roles Year 2 onward for alumni. First aeroplane. First stamp on a passport.
Long-run record
Their voice sits in a consented, withdrawable wiki that a national book draws on in Year 3. They are cited by name. They are the evidence. They are the authors.

Books + journals — how the corpus becomes national evidence

Every book is built from what the young people have already recorded, with their consent, in their words.

Year 1 · The zines

Per-community books

One printed book per anchor community. Oonchiumpa, BG Fit, Mounty Yarns launch first. Each carries the community's young people's drawings, writing, photos, audio transcripts. Distributed to the community first. Then to local magistrates + state ministers.

First zine target: September 2026. Budget allowance in Three Circles Year 1.
Year 2 · Cross-community volumes

Paired chapters

Documentary-quality compilations. Mparntwe paired with Mount Druitt. Mount Isa paired with Palm Island. Young people from each community travel to the other as part of the Brave Ones exchange and co-write the chapter together.

Target: 4 cross-community volumes across FY27.
Year 3 · National collection

The Country We're Building

One 400-page bound volume. Launches at a national event. Every magistrate, every state attorney-general, every First Nations MP receives a copy. Every young person who contributed is named, credited, paid an authorship fee.

Launch target: FY28. Minderoo Year 3 deliverable.
The individual journal — what each young person keeps

A5 hardcover. Theirs. Forever.

The journal isn't just an input to the books — it's the thing the young person takes home at the end of the program. Family writes in it. Workers write in it. The young person paints, pastes, records voice memos linked by QR code. Worth nothing to the system. Worth everything to them. The goal state is simple: a young person walks in and says, "Can I grab my journal?"

National + international travel — how stories earn movement

Journal kept → exchange eligible. The travel is both a reward and a capture — young people come back with footage and writing that becomes the next year's book material.

Each international trip returns with footage, field journals, and cross-context evidence that feeds the Year 3 national collection.

Diagrama + YOPE — what the young people encounter overseas

The international leg is not tourism. It is evidence-gathering in communities running a cousin of the method at a different scale of recognition.

Spain

Diagrama · Murcia

Cost-benefit
€31K
Per young person / yr

Diagrama runs a community-led educational model for young people in the justice system in Spain. Over thirty years of operation. Thirty years of better outcomes at a fraction of detention cost. Every Australian state that has looked at Diagrama has found a reason not to replicate it.

What a young person does there
  • Week-long residential with their worker. Shadow a Diagrama practitioner
  • Sit alongside Spanish young people in the same shoes — literally same age, same story arcs
  • Visit 2–3 Diagrama sites, see the everyday-ness of the method
  • Keep filming. Keep journaling. Co-write a chapter of the Year 2 cross-context book
Why it matters: the Australian policy argument that "community-led models don't scale" breaks when a young person comes home and says "I met Spanish kids doing exactly what we're doing — for thirty years."
Netherlands

YOPE · Amsterdam

Youth Organised Policy Engagement. A Dutch framework where young people affected by policy design the policy. Young people sit at tables that in Australia are still closed to them. The mayor of Amsterdam convenes them. They set agendas. They measure outcomes. The policy is legitimately co-authored.
What a young person does there
  • Attend a YOPE policy workshop. Observe. Ask.
  • Co-author a short position paper with Dutch peers on a shared issue (youth sentencing, foster-care churn, policing)
  • Meet Dutch city councillors. Watch what "young people at the table" looks like when it is normal, not performative
  • Come home with a practice model Australian youth can adapt for local council or state parliamentary engagement
Why it matters: Taleigha's question — "why are the older fellas making choices for the younger fellas?" — is the exact question YOPE answers in practice every week in Amsterdam. Her trip becomes the blueprint for the ACT version.
Budget frame: Each international leg costs roughly $25K for 4 young people + 2 workers including flights, accommodation, program fees, filming costs. Per Year 2, the Three Circles envelope supports 3–4 international trips (Diagrama + YOPE + one exploratory). Paid for out of Circle Three's media + book production line, because each trip is primary research for the artefact.

What's missing — without the hold

The gaps the $2.9M closes. Named honestly. Not guilt; shape.

Stories that stay in EL v2 without the editorial work

440+ stories already in Empathy Ledger v2 across communities ACT is in relationship with. Without sustained editorial staffing, most stay raw ingest — voice captured, but never compiled into anything the country sees.

Magistrates who don't receive the tool

The JusticeHub magistrate-facing service directory is prototyped. Without the relational work to put it in front of every sentencing magistrate in the country, it sits live but unseen.

Young people who don't get on the plane

The Brave Ones exchange requires per-trip funding. Without Year 1 travel budget, 12+ young people don't see Brisbane, Sydney, or Palm Island. Year 2: no one sees Spain or Amsterdam.

Communities who stay at "in conversation"

Three anchors are active today. Seven more are in relationship-building or selection-pending status. Without Year 2 convening and relational work, those seven stay at 2026-04-18 state for the next three years.
